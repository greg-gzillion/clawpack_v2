# Chicago Manual of Style (17th Edition)

## Notes-Bibliography System (Humanities)

### Book Citations

**Bibliography Format:**
`{author}. {title}. {place}: {publisher}, {year}.`

**Example:**
`Smith, John. Legal Research Methods. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2020.`

**Footnote Format:**
`{number}. {author}, {title} ({place}: {publisher}, {year}), {page}.`

**Example:**
`1. John Smith, Legal Research Methods (Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2020), 45.`

### Journal Articles

**Bibliography Format:**
`{author}. "{title}." {journal} {volume}, no. {issue} ({year}): {pages}.`

**Example:**
`Taylor, Robert. "Artificial Intelligence in Legal Practice." Stanford Law Review 73, no. 4 (2021): 891-945.`

**Footnote Format:**
`{number}. {author}, "{title}," {journal} {volume}, no. {issue} ({year}): {page}.`

**Example:**
`1. Robert Taylor, "Artificial Intelligence in Legal Practice," Stanford Law Review 73, no. 4 (2021): 895.`

### Case Law

**Bibliography Format:**
`{case_name}, {volume} {reporter} {page} ({court} {year}).`

**Example:**
`Brown v. Board of Education, 347 U.S. 483 (1954).`

**Footnote Format:**
`{number}. {case_name}, {volume} {reporter} {page} ({court} {year}).`

**Example:**
`1. Brown v. Board of Education, 347 U.S. 483 (1954).`

### Websites

**Bibliography Format:**
`{author}. "{title}." {website}. Last modified {date}. {URL}.`

**Example:**
`U.S. Courts. "About Federal Courts." United States Courts. Last modified April 6, 2026. https://www.uscourts.gov/about-federal-courts.`

**Footnote Format:**
`{number}. {author}, "{title}," {website}, accessed {date}, {URL}.`

### Author-Date System (Sciences)

**In-Text:** `(Smith 2020, 45)`

**Reference List:** `Smith, John. 2020. Legal Research Methods. Oxford: Oxford University Press.`

### Quick Comparison

| Element | Notes-Bibliography | Author-Date |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| In-text | Superscript numbers | (Author year, page) |
| Citations | Footnotes/Endnotes | Parenthetical |
| Bibliography | Alphabetical | Alphabetical |
