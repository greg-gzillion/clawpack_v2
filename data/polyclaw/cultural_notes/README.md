# Cultural Notes for Translation

## Regional Variations

### Spanish
- **Spain**: vosotros (informal plural), distinción (c/z vs s)
- **Mexico/Latin America**: ustedes (formal plural), seseo (s/c/z same)

### Portuguese
- **Portugal**: European Portuguese (closed vowels, different pronoun placement)
- **Brazil**: Brazilian Portuguese (open vowels, different vocabulary)

### Chinese
- **Simplified** (China, Singapore): 你好
- **Traditional** (Taiwan, Hong Kong): 你好

### English
- **US**: color, center, apartment
- **UK**: colour, centre, flat

## Cultural Considerations

### Formality in Business
- **Japan/Korea**: Extremely hierarchical, use titles
- **Germany**: Formal "Sie" until invited to use "du"
- **Nordic countries**: More egalitarian, first names common

### Taboos to Avoid
- Never assume gender-neutral language exists in all languages
- Avoid idioms that don't translate (e.g., "it's raining cats and dogs")
- Be careful with humor and sarcasm across cultures

## Date/Time Formats
- **US**: MM/DD/YYYY, 12-hour clock
- **Europe**: DD/MM/YYYY, 24-hour clock
- **Japan/Korea**: YYYY/MM/DD
