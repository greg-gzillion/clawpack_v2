# Psychiatry - Forensic Psychiatry

## Forensic Psychiatry - Key Concepts
| Concept | Description | URL |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| **Competence to Stand Trial** | Ability to **understand charges**, **Assist in defense**, **Make decisions** about plea/sentencing | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/medical-ethics |
| **Criminal Responsibility (Insanity Defense)** | **M'Naghten Rule**: Due to mental illness, Did not **know nature/quality of act** or **know it was wrong** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/medical-ethics |
| **Involuntary Commitment** | **Danger to self**, **Danger to others**, **Grave disability** | https://www.samhsa.gov/serious-mental-illness |
| **Capacity** | **Medical decision-making**: Ability to communicate choice, Understand, Appreciate, Reason | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/medical-ethics |
| **Guardianship/Conservatorship** | Court-appointed surrogate decision-maker for incapacitated person | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/medical-ethics |
| **Tarasoff Duty** | **Duty to protect** identifiable third party from **credible threat of violence** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/medical-ethics |
| **Mandated Reporting** | **Child abuse**, **Elder abuse**, **Dependent adult abuse** | https://www.childwelfare.gov/topics/systemwide/laws-policies/state/ |
