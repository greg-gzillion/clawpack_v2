# Psychiatry - Dissociative Disorders

## Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Diagnostic Criteria** | **Disruption of identity** characterized by **=2 distinct personality states**; **Recurrent gaps in recall** of everyday events, Personal information, or traumatic events | https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/dissociative-disorders |
| **Etiology** | **Severe childhood trauma** (abuse, Neglect) | https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/dissociative-disorders |
| **Treatment** | **Trauma-focused psychotherapy**, **Phase-oriented treatment** (stabilization, Trauma processing, Integration), **Hypnosis**, **EMDR** | https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/dissociative-disorders |

## Dissociative Amnesia
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Diagnostic Criteria** | **Inability to recall important autobiographical information**, Usually of **traumatic or stressful nature**; **Not attributable to ordinary forgetfulness** | https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/dissociative-disorders |
| **Dissociative Fugue** | **Apparently purposeful travel or bewildered wandering** associated with amnesia for identity or other important information | https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/dissociative-disorders |
| **Treatment** | **Supportive psychotherapy**, **Hypnosis**, **Cognitive therapy**, **Treat underlying trauma** | https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/dissociative-disorders |

## Depersonalization/Derealization Disorder
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Diagnostic Criteria** | **Persistent or recurrent experiences of depersonalization** (feeling detached from one's mind/body), **Derealization** (feeling detached from surroundings), or **both**; **Reality testing intact** | https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/dissociative-disorders |
| **Treatment** | **CBT**, **SSRIs**, **Lamotrigine**, **Naltrexone** | https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/dissociative-disorders |
