# Palliative Care - End of Life Care

## Recognizing the Dying Patient - Signs of Imminent Death (Days to Hours)
| Sign | Description | URL |
|------|-------------|-----|
| **Decreased Level of Consciousness** | Lethargy ? Obtundation ? Coma | https://www.nhpco.org/end-of-life-signs |
| **Decreased Oral Intake** | Anorexia, Dysphagia | https://www.nhpco.org/end-of-life-signs |
| **Terminal Secretions** | **"Death rattle"** | https://www.nhpco.org/end-of-life-signs |
| **Changes in Breathing** | **Cheyne-Stokes respiration**, Apnea, Irregular pattern | https://www.nhpco.org/end-of-life-signs |
| **Changes in Perfusion** | **Mottling** of extremities, Cool skin, Cyanosis | https://www.nhpco.org/end-of-life-signs |
| **Changes in Urine Output** | Oliguria ? Anuria | https://www.nhpco.org/end-of-life-signs |
| **"Terminal Rally"** | Brief period of lucidity/energy before death | https://www.nhpco.org/end-of-life-signs |

## Symptom Management at End of Life
| Symptom | Management | URL |
|---------|------------|-----|
| **Pain** | **Opioids** (morphine, Hydromorphone) SC; **Scheduled + PRN** | https://www.capc.org/toolkits/last-days-of-life/ |
| **Dyspnea** | **Opioids** (first-line), **Fan**, **Positioning**, **Benzodiazepines** (if anxiety) | https://www.capc.org/toolkits/last-days-of-life/ |
| **Terminal Secretions** | **Glycopyrrolate** 0.2 mg SC q4h PRN, **Scopolamine** 0.4 mg SC q4h PRN, **Atropine** 1% ophthalmic drops SL q4h PRN | https://www.capc.org/toolkits/last-days-of-life/ |
| **Delirium** | **Haloperidol** 0.5-2 mg SC q1-4h PRN, **Chlorpromazine** 12.5-25 mg SC/PR q4-6h PRN | https://www.capc.org/toolkits/last-days-of-life/ |
| **Nausea/Vomiting** | **Haloperidol** 0.5-2 mg SC q6-8h, **Metoclopramide** 10 mg SC q6h, **Ondansetron** 4-8 mg ODT q8h | https://www.capc.org/toolkits/last-days-of-life/ |
| **Anxiety** | **Lorazepam** 0.5-1 mg SL/SC q4-6h PRN, **Midazolam** 2.5-5 mg SC q1-2h PRN | https://www.capc.org/toolkits/last-days-of-life/ |

## Non-Essential Medications at End of Life
| Medication Class | Rationale for Discontinuation | URL |
|------------------|------------------------------|-----|
| **Statins** | No benefit in last weeks-months | https://www.capc.org/toolkits/last-days-of-life/ |
| **Antihypertensives** | May cause hypotension; Goal is comfort | https://www.capc.org/toolkits/last-days-of-life/ |
| **Oral Hypoglycemics** | Decreased oral intake ? Hypoglycemia risk | https://www.capc.org/toolkits/last-days-of-life/ |
| **Vitamins/Supplements** | No benefit | https://www.capc.org/toolkits/last-days-of-life/ |
| **Bisphosphonates/Denosumab** | No benefit in last weeks | https://www.capc.org/toolkits/last-days-of-life/ |
| **Antibiotics** | Consider comfort vs burden; May discontinue if not providing comfort | https://www.capc.org/toolkits/last-days-of-life/ |

| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Pronouncement** | Confirm absence of heartbeat, Respirations, Pupillary response | https://www.ama-assn.org/delivering-care/ethics/care-after-death |

| **Documentation** | Time of death, Pronouncing provider, Notification, Disposition | https://www.ama-assn.org/delivering-care/ethics/care-after-death |
| **Organ/Tissue Donation** | Notify Organ Procurement Organization (OPO) | https://www.organdonor.gov |
| **Autopsy** | Offer to family; Required for certain circumstances | https://www.ama-assn.org/delivering-care/ethics/care-after-death |
| **Bereavement Support** | Offer resources; Follow-up with family | https://www.nhpco.org/bereavement-support |
