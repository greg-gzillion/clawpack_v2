# Palliative Care - Overview

## What is Palliative Care?
Palliative care is specialized medical care for people living with serious illness. It focuses on providing relief from the symptoms and stress of the illness, with the goal of improving quality of life for both the patient and the family. Palliative care is provided by a specially-trained team of doctors, nurses, and other specialists who work together with a patient's other doctors to provide an extra layer of support. It is appropriate at any age and at any stage in a serious illness and can be provided along with curative treatment.

## Palliative Care vs Hospice
| Feature | Palliative Care | Hospice Care | URL |
|---------|-----------------|--------------|-----|
| **Timing** | **Any stage** of serious illness | **Life expectancy =6 months** | https://www.nia.nih.gov/health/hospice-care |
| **Curative Treatment** | **Can be provided alongside** | **Focus on comfort, Not cure** | https://www.nia.nih.gov/health/hospice-care |
| **Location** | Hospital, Clinic, Home | **Home**, Hospice facility, Nursing home | https://www.nia.nih.gov/health/hospice-care |
| **Reimbursement** | **Fee-for-service** | **Medicare Hospice Benefit** | https://www.cms.gov/medicare/palliative-care |
