# Plastic Surgery - Burn Care

## Burn Classification
| Degree | Depth | Appearance | Sensation | Healing | URL |
|--------|-------|------------|-----------|---------|-----|
| **Superficial (1st)** | **Epidermis only** | **Erythema**, **Dry**, **Blanching** | **Painful** | **3-7 days**, No scarring | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Superficial Partial-Thickness (2nd)** | **Epidermis + superficial dermis** | **Blisters**, **Moist**, **Blanching** | **Very painful** | **1-3 weeks**, Minimal scarring | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Deep Partial-Thickness (2nd)** | **Epidermis + deep dermis** | **White**, **Non-blanching**, **Less moist** | **Less painful** | **3-6 weeks**, Scarring | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Full-Thickness (3rd)** | **Epidermis + entire dermis** | **White**, **Leathery**, **Waxy**, **Charred**, **Non-blanching** | **Painless** | **Requires excision and grafting** | https://ameriburn.org |
| **4th Degree** | **Through dermis into fat/muscle/bone** | **Charred**, **Exposed deeper structures** | **Painless** | **Requires extensive debridement and reconstruction** | https://ameriburn.org |

## Burn Size Estimation
| Method | Description | URL |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| **Rule of Nines (Adult)** | **Head 9%**, **Each arm 9%**, **Anterior trunk 18%**, **Posterior trunk 18%**, **Each leg 18%**, **Perineum 1%** | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Rule of Nines (Child)** | **Head 18%** (age <1), Decreases with age; **Legs 14%** each (age <1), Increases with age | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Palmar Surface** | **Patient's palm = 1% TBSA** | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Lund-Browder Chart** | **Most accurate**, Accounts for age-related changes | https://ameriburn.org |

## Burn Resuscitation (Parkland Formula)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Formula** | **4 mL x weight (kg) x %TBSA burned** | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Fluid** | **Lactated Ringer's** (preferred) | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Timing** | **Half over first 8 hours**, **Half over next 16 hours** (from time of burn) | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Goal** | **UOP 0.5 mL/kg/h** (adults), **1 mL/kg/h** (children <30 kg) | https://ameriburn.org |

## Inhalation Injury
| Sign | Management | URL |
|------|------------|-----|
| **Facial burns**, **Singed nasal hairs**, **Carbonaceous sputum**, **Hoarseness**, **Stridor** | **Early intubation** | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Carboxyhemoglobin** | **100% O2** (until COHb <3%), **Hyperbaric oxygen** | https://www.cdc.gov/co |
| **Cyanide toxicity** | **Hydroxocobalamin (Cyanokit)** | https://www.cdc.gov/chemicalemergencies/factsheets/cyanide.html |

## Burn Wound Management
| Depth | Management | URL |
|-------|------------|-----|
| **Superficial** | **Moisturizers**, **Aloe vera**, **Oral analgesics** | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Superficial Partial-Thickness** | **Topical antimicrobial** (silver sulfadiazine, Bacitracin), **Non-adherent dressing** | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Deep Partial-Thickness** | **Excision and grafting** | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Full-Thickness** | **Early excision and grafting** | https://ameriburn.org |

## Escharotomy
| Indication | Technique | URL |
|------------|-----------|-----|
| **Circumferential full-thickness burn** with **compromised distal perfusion** | **Incision through eschar** to release pressure | https://ameriburn.org |
| **Circumferential chest burn** with **compromised ventilation** | **Incision through eschar** to allow chest expansion | https://ameriburn.org |
