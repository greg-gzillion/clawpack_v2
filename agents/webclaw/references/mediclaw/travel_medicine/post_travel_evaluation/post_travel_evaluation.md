# Travel Medicine - Post-Travel Evaluation

## Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **When to Evaluate** | **Any traveler with fever**, **Diarrhea**, **Skin lesions**, **Eosinophilia**, or **other concerning symptoms** after travel | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/evaluating-ill-returning-traveler |
| **Key History** | **Itinerary** (dates, locations, urban/rural), **Activities**, **Exposures** (food, water, insects, animals, sexual), **Vaccinations**, **Malaria prophylaxis** (adherence) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/evaluating-ill-returning-traveler |

## Fever in the Returning Traveler
| Timing | Common Causes | URL |
|--------|---------------|-----|
| **<21 days** | **Malaria**, **Dengue**, **Enteric fever** (typhoid), **Rickettsial diseases**, **Leptospirosis**, **Viral hemorrhagic fevers** (rare) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/evaluating-ill-returning-traveler |
| **>21 days** | **Malaria** (P. vivax/ovale), **Tuberculosis**, **Hepatitis A/B/E**, **Amebic liver abscess**, **HIV**, **Visceral leishmaniasis** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/evaluating-ill-returning-traveler |

## Malaria Evaluation
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Suspect Malaria in ANY febrile traveler from endemic area** (regardless of prophylaxis) | **Thick and thin blood smears** (=3 over 48-72h), **Rapid diagnostic test (RDT)** , **PCR** | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/diagnosis_treatment/diagnosis.html |
| **P. falciparum** | **Medical emergency** (can progress rapidly) | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/about/biology/index.html |

## Diarrhea in the Returning Traveler
| Duration | Common Causes | Evaluation | URL |
|----------|---------------|------------|-----|
| **Acute (<14 days)** | **Bacterial** (Campylobacter, Salmonella, Shigella, ETEC), **Viral** (norovirus) | **Stool culture**, **PCR panel**, **Consider empiric antibiotics** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/evaluating-ill-returning-traveler |
| **Persistent (=14 days)** | **Parasitic** (Giardia, Cryptosporidium, Cyclospora, Entamoeba), **Post-infectious IBS** | **Stool O&P x3**, **PCR panel**, **Consider empiric treatment** (tinidazole, nitazoxanide) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/evaluating-ill-returning-traveler |

## Eosinophilia in the Returning Traveler
| Definition | Common Causes | Evaluation | URL |
|-----------|---------------|------------|-----|
| **Absolute eosinophil count >450-500/µL** | **Helminthic infections**: **Strongyloides**, **Schistosomiasis**, **Hookworm**, **Ascaris**, **Filaria**, **Trichinella** | **Stool O&P x3**, **Strongyloides serology**, **Schistosomiasis serology**, **Filarial serology** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/evaluating-ill-returning-traveler |

## Skin Lesions in the Returning Traveler
| Lesion | Common Causes | URL |
|--------|---------------|-----|
| **Maculopapular Rash** | **Dengue**, **Chikungunya**, **Zika**, **Measles**, **Rickettsial diseases**, **Drug reaction** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/evaluating-ill-returning-traveler |
| **Eschar** | **Rickettsial diseases** (African tick bite fever, Mediterranean spotted fever, Scrub typhus) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/evaluating-ill-returning-traveler |
| **Cutaneous Larva Migrans** | **Serpiginous, pruritic track** (hookworm larvae) | https://www.cdc.gov/parasites/larva-migrans/index.html |
| **Myiasis** | **Botfly larva** (painful nodule with central punctum) | https://www.cdc.gov/parasites/myiasis/index.html |
| **Leishmaniasis** | **Painless ulcer** with raised borders | https://www.cdc.gov/leishmaniasis/index.html |
