# Travel Medicine - Immunocompromised Travelers

## General Considerations
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **HIV**, **Active malignancy**, **Chemotherapy**, **Transplant recipients**, **Immunosuppressive medications** (corticosteroids =20 mg/day prednisone x=2 weeks, Biologics, DMARDs) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/immunocompromised-travelers |
| **Pre-Travel Consultation** | **Essential** (ideally with travel medicine + specialist) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/immunocompromised-travelers |
| **Vaccine Response** | **May be diminished**; **Check titers** if possible | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/immunocompromised-travelers |
| **Travel Insurance** | **Essential** (including medical evacuation) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/immunocompromised-travelers |

## Vaccines in Immunocompromised Travelers
| Vaccine | Recommendation | URL |
|---------|----------------|-----|
| **Inactivated Vaccines** | **Safe** (may have diminished response) | https://www.cdc.gov/vaccines/hcp/acip-recs/general-recs/immunocompetence.html |
| **Live Vaccines (MMR, Varicella, Yellow Fever, Oral Typhoid)** | **Generally contraindicated** (depending on degree of immunosuppression) | https://www.cdc.gov/vaccines/hcp/acip-recs/general-recs/immunocompetence.html |
| **Yellow Fever** | **Contraindicated**; **Waiver letter** may be issued | https://www.cdc.gov/yellowfever/vaccine/contraindications.html |

## Degree of Immunosuppression (IDSA Guidelines)
| Category | Examples | Live Vaccine Recommendation | URL |
|----------|----------|---------------------------|-----|
| **High-Level** | **HIV with CD4 <200**, **Active leukemia/lymphoma**, **Chemotherapy**, **Transplant <2 months**, **High-dose corticosteroids** | **Contraindicated** | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vaccination-of-the-immunocompromised-host/ |
| **Low-Level** | **HIV with CD4 200-499**, **Transplant >2 months** (stable), **Low-dose immunosuppression** | **Consider** (case-by-case) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vaccination-of-the-immunocompromised-host/ |
| **Not Immunosuppressed** | **HIV with CD4 =500**, **Transplant >1 year** (no rejection), **No immunosuppression** | **Safe** | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vaccination-of-the-immunocompromised-host/ |

## Malaria Prophylaxis in Immunocompromised
| Consideration | Recommendation | URL |
|---------------|----------------|-----|
| **Efficacy** | **Same as immunocompetent** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/immunocompromised-travelers |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Check interactions** with immunosuppressive medications | https://www.hiv-druginteractions.org |
| **Mosquito Avoidance** | **Even more critical** | https://www.cdc.gov/mosquitoes/mosquito-bites/prevent-mosquito-bites.html |

## Traveler's Diarrhea in Immunocompromised
| Treatment | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Oral Rehydration** | **Essential** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/immunocompromised-travelers |
| **Antibiotics** | **Lower threshold for empiric treatment**; **Azithromycin preferred** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/immunocompromised-travelers |
| **Prolonged Diarrhea** | **Evaluate for opportunistic infections** (Cryptosporidium, Microsporidia, CMV) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/immunocompromised-travelers |
