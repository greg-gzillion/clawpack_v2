# Travel Medicine - Traveler's Diarrhea

## Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **=3 unformed stools in 24h** + **=1 enteric symptom** (nausea, vomiting, cramping, fever, tenesmus) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Incidence** | **30-70%** of travelers to developing countries (depending on destination) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **High-Risk Destinations** | **South/Southeast Asia**, **Africa**, **Middle East**, **Central/South America** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Etiology** | **Bacterial** (80-90%): **ETEC** (most common), **Campylobacter**, **Shigella**, **Salmonella**; **Viral** (5-10%): Norovirus, Rotavirus; **Parasitic** (<5%): Giardia, Cryptosporidium, Entamoeba | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |

## Prevention of Traveler's Diarrhea
| Measure | Recommendation | URL |
|---------|----------------|-----|
| **Food/Water Precautions** | **"Boil it, cook it, peel it, or forget it"** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/food-water-safety |
| **Hand Hygiene** | **Soap and water** or **Alcohol-based hand sanitizer** (=60% alcohol) | https://www.cdc.gov/handwashing/index.html |
| **Prophylactic Antibiotics** | **NOT routinely recommended** (risk of adverse effects, C. diff, Resistance); **Consider** for high-stakes travel (athletes, politicians) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Bismuth Subsalicylate** | **2 tablets QID** reduces incidence by **~50%** (black tongue/stool, Avoid with aspirin, anticoagulants) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |

## Treatment of Traveler's Diarrhea
| Severity | Definition | Treatment | URL |
|----------|------------|-----------|-----|
| **Mild** | **1-2 loose stools/24h**, **No distressing symptoms** | **Oral rehydration**, **Loperamide** PRN | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Moderate** | **Distressing symptoms** (cramping, nausea, Interferes with activities) | **Loperamide** + **Antibiotic** (azithromycin preferred) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Severe** | **Dysentery** (bloody diarrhea, Fever), **>6 stools/24h**, **Incapacitating** | **Antibiotic** (azithromycin), **Avoid loperamide** (if dysentery) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |

## Antibiotics for Traveler's Diarrhea
| Antibiotic | Dose | Duration | Notes | URL |
|------------|------|----------|-------|-----|
| **Azithromycin** | **500 mg daily** | **1-3 days** | **Preferred** (covers Campylobacter, Fluoroquinolone-resistant pathogens) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Ciprofloxacin** | **500 mg BID** | **1-3 days** | **Increasing resistance** (especially South/Southeast Asia); **Avoid in pregnancy/children** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Levofloxacin** | **500 mg daily** | **1-3 days** | Same as ciprofloxacin | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Rifaximin** | **200 mg TID** | **3 days** | **Non-absorbed**, **Only for non-invasive diarrhea** (no fever/bloody stools); **Safe in pregnancy** (Category C) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |

## Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS)
| Component | Recipe | URL |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| **WHO ORS** | **1 liter clean water + 6 tsp sugar + 0.5 tsp salt** | https://www.cdc.gov/cholera/treatment/rehydration.html |
| **Commercial ORS** | **Pedialyte**, **Ceralyte**, **DripDrop** | https://www.cdc.gov/cholera/treatment/rehydration.html |
| **Sports Drinks** | **NOT adequate** (too much sugar, Not enough electrolytes) | https://www.cdc.gov/cholera/treatment/rehydration.html |

## Post-Infectious Irritable Bowel Syndrome (PI-IBS)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **IBS symptoms** persisting **>6 months** after acute gastroenteritis | https://gi.org/topics/irritable-bowel-syndrome/ |
| **Incidence** | **5-15%** after traveler's diarrhea | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Risk Factors** | **Severe/prolonged diarrhea**, **Dysentery**, **Female sex**, **Younger age**, **Psychological stress** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Management** | **Same as IBS** (dietary modification, Antispasmodics, Neuromodulators) | https://gi.org/topics/irritable-bowel-syndrome/ |
