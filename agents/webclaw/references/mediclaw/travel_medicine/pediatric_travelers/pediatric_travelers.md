# Travel Medicine - Pediatric Travelers

## General Considerations
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Air Travel** | **Generally safe after 48 hours** of life (full-term); **Check airline policy** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/children |
| **Car Seats** | **FAA-approved car seat** for infants/toddlers on planes | https://www.faa.gov/travelers/fly_children |
| **Ear Pain** | **Feed during ascent/descent** (infants), **Chew gum/swallow** (older children) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/children |

## Vaccines in Pediatric Travelers
| Vaccine | Recommendation | URL |
|---------|----------------|-----|
| **Routine Vaccines** | **Accelerated schedule** possible for infants =6 weeks | https://www.cdc.gov/vaccines/schedules/hcp/imz/child-adolescent.html |
| **MMR** | **Can give as early as 6 months** (but still needs 2 doses after 12 months) | https://www.cdc.gov/vaccines/vpd/mmr/hcp/recommendations.html |
| **Hepatitis A** | **Can give as early as 6 months** (but still needs 2 doses after 12 months) | https://www.cdc.gov/vaccines/vpd/hepa/hcp/recommendations.html |
| **Yellow Fever** | **Contraindicated <6 months** (encephalitis risk); **Precaution 6-8 months** | https://www.cdc.gov/yellowfever/vaccine/contraindications.html |
| **Typhoid** | **IM vaccine**: =2 years; **Oral vaccine**: =6 years | https://www.cdc.gov/typhoid-fever/typhoid-vaccine.html |
| **Japanese Encephalitis** | **=2 months** | https://www.cdc.gov/japaneseencephalitis/vaccine/index.html |

## Malaria Prophylaxis in Children
| Drug | Age | Notes | URL |
|------|-----|-------|-----|
| **Atovaquone-Proguanil** | **=5 kg** | **Weight-based dosing** | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/travelers/drugs.html |
| **Mefloquine** | **=5 kg** | Weight-based dosing | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/travelers/drugs.html |
| **Doxycycline** | **=8 years** | **Contraindicated <8 years** (tooth discoloration) | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/travelers/drugs.html |
| **Primaquine** | **Any age** (with G6PD testing) | **Requires G6PD testing** | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/travelers/drugs.html |
| **Chloroquine** | **Any age** | Weight-based dosing | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/travelers/drugs.html |

## Traveler's Diarrhea in Children
| Treatment | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Oral Rehydration** | **First-line** (ORS preferred) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/children |
| **Loperamide** | **Avoid <2 years**; **Use with caution** in older children | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/children |
| **Azithromycin** | **Preferred antibiotic** (10 mg/kg daily x3 days) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/children |
| **Fluoroquinolones** | **Avoid <18 years** (arthropathy risk) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/children |
| **Rifaximin** | **=12 years** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/children |

## Altitude Illness in Children
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Risk** | **Similar to adults** (no increased risk) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/altitude-illness |
| **Diagnosis** | **Difficult in preverbal children** (fussiness, Poor feeding, Vomiting) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/altitude-illness |
| **Acetazolamide** | **2.5 mg/kg BID** (max 125 mg BID) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/altitude-illness |
