# Pediatrics - Newborn Care

## Routine Newborn Care
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Vitamin K** | **1 mg IM** at birth (prevents hemorrhagic disease of newborn) | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/newborn-care/ |
| **Erythromycin Ointment** | **Ophthalmic prophylaxis** (prevents gonococcal ophthalmia) | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/newborn-care/ |
| **Hepatitis B Vaccine** | **First dose** within 24 hours of birth | https://www.cdc.gov/vaccines/schedules/hcp/imz/child-adolescent.html |
| **Newborn Screening** | **Heel stick** at 24-48 hours: Metabolic, Endocrine, Hemoglobinopathies, SCID, Hearing screen | https://www.cdc.gov/newbornscreening/index.html |
| **Umbilical Cord Care** | **Keep clean and dry**, **No alcohol or antiseptics** (in developed countries) | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/newborn-care/ |
| **Circumcision** | Parental choice; **Analgesia required** (dorsal penile nerve block, EMLA) | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/circumcision/ |

## Neonatal Jaundice
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Physiologic Jaundice** | **Unconjugated hyperbilirubinemia** appearing **after 24 hours**, Peaks day 3-5 | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/neonatal-jaundice/ |
| **Pathologic Jaundice** | Appears **<24 hours**, **Rapid rise** (>0.2 mg/dL/h), **Conjugated hyperbilirubinemia** (>2 mg/dL or >20% of total) | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/neonatal-jaundice/ |
| **Risk Factors** | **Prematurity**, **ABO/Rh incompatibility**, **G6PD deficiency**, **Breastfeeding jaundice**, **Cephalohematoma**, **Family history** | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/neonatal-jaundice/ |
| **Kernicterus** | **Unconjugated bilirubin crosses blood-brain barrier** ? **Encephalopathy**, **Athetoid cerebral palsy**, **Hearing loss**, **Dental enamel dysplasia** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/kernicterus |
| **Treatment** | **Phototherapy** (based on bilirubin level, Age, Risk factors), **Exchange transfusion** (severe) | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/neonatal-jaundice/ |

## Newborn Respiratory Distress
| Cause | Onset | Key Features | Diagnosis | Treatment | URL |
|-------|-------|--------------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Transient Tachypnea of Newborn (TTN)** | **Immediately after birth** | **Delayed clearance of fetal lung fluid**, **Term or late preterm**, **C-section** | **CXR**: Perihilar streaking, Fluid in fissures, Hyperinflation | **Supportive** (oxygen, CPAP), Resolves in 24-72h | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/transient-tachypnea-newborn |
| **Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS)** | **Within hours** | **Surfactant deficiency**, **Prematurity** | **CXR**: **Ground-glass appearance**, **Air bronchograms**, **Low lung volumes** | **CPAP**, **Surfactant**, **Mechanical ventilation** | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/respiratory-distress-syndrome |
| **Meconium Aspiration Syndrome** | **At birth** | **Meconium-stained amniotic fluid**, **Term/post-term** | **CXR**: Patchy infiltrates, Hyperinflation, Pneumothorax | **Supportive**, **Antibiotics**, **Surfactant**, **Inhaled nitric oxide** | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/meconium-aspiration-syndrome |
| **Congenital Pneumonia** | Variable | **GBS**, **E. coli**, **Listeria** | **CXR**: Infiltrates | **Antibiotics** | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/neonatal-pneumonia/ |

## Neonatal Sepsis
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Early-Onset** | **<72 hours**; Vertical transmission (GBS, E. coli, Listeria) | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/neonatal-sepsis/ |
| **Late-Onset** | **>72 hours**; Nosocomial or community-acquired (CoNS, S. aureus, GBS, Gram-negatives) | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/neonatal-sepsis/ |
| **Evaluation** | **CBC with differential**, **CRP**, **Blood culture**, **LP**, **CXR** (if respiratory symptoms) | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/neonatal-sepsis/ |
| **Empiric Treatment** | **Ampicillin + Gentamicin** or **Cefotaxime** | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/neonatal-sepsis/ |

## Neonatal Abstinence Syndrome (NAS)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|

| **Symptoms** | **Irritability**, **High-pitched cry**, **Tremors**, **Hypertonia**, **Poor feeding**, **Diarrhea**, **Seizures** | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/neonatal-abstinence-syndrome/ |
