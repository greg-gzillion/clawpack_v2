# Pediatrics - Ophthalmology

## Amblyopia
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Unilateral or bilateral decreased vision** due to abnormal visual development | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/amblyopia |
| **Types** | **Strabismic**, **Refractive** (anisometropia), **Deprivation** (cataract, Ptosis) | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/amblyopia |
| **Treatment** | **Correct refractive error**, **Patching**, **Atropine penalization**, **Treat underlying cause** | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/amblyopia |

## Strabismus
| Type | Features | Treatment | URL |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| **Infantile Esotropia** | <6 months, Large angle, Cross-fixation | **Surgery** by 6-12 months | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/strabismus/ |
| **Accommodative Esotropia** | 6 months - 7 years, Hyperopia | **Full cycloplegic correction** (glasses) | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/strabismus/ |
| **Intermittent Exotropia** | Deviates with fatigue, Bright light | **Observation**, **Patching**, **Surgery** | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/strabismus/ |

## Retinopathy of Prematurity (ROP)
| Stage | Findings | Management | URL |
|-------|----------|------------|-----|
| **Stage 1-2** | Demarcation line, Ridge | **Observation** | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/retinopathy-prematurity |
| **Stage 3** | Ridge with extraretinal proliferation | **Laser** or **Anti-VEGF** if threshold | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/retinopathy-prematurity |
| **Stage 4-5** | Retinal detachment | **Vitrectomy** | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/retinopathy-prematurity |

## Congenital Cataract
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Etiology** | **Idiopathic**, **Hereditary**, **TORCH**, **Metabolic** (galactosemia), **Chromosomal** | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/congenital-cataract/ |
