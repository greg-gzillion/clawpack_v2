# Pediatrics - Neurology

## Febrile Seizures
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | Seizure in child **6 months - 5 years** with **fever =38°C**, No CNS infection, No prior afebrile seizures | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/febrile-seizures |
| **Simple** | **Generalized**, **<15 minutes**, **Once per 24 hours**, **No focal features** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/febrile-seizures |
| **Complex** | **Focal**, **>15 minutes**, **Recurs within 24 hours** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/febrile-seizures |
| **Management** | **Reassurance**, **Antipyretics** (do not prevent), **Rescue benzodiazepines**; **No prophylactic ASMs** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/febrile-seizures |

## Infantile Spasms (West Syndrome)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Triad** | **Epileptic spasms**, **Hypsarrhythmia** on EEG, **Developmental regression** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/infantile-spasms |

| **Treatment** | **ACTH** (first-line), **Vigabatrin** (especially tuberous sclerosis), **Corticosteroids** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/infantile-spasms |

## Pediatric Epilepsy Syndromes
| Syndrome | Age | Key Features | Treatment | URL |
|----------|-----|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Childhood Absence Epilepsy** | 4-10 years | **Frequent brief absence seizures**, **3 Hz spike-wave** | **Ethosuximide**, Valproate, Lamotrigine | https://www.epilepsy.com/what-is-epilepsy/syndromes/childhood-absence-epilepsy |
| **Juvenile Myoclonic Epilepsy** | 12-18 years | **Myoclonic jerks** (morning), GTCs, **4-6 Hz polyspike-wave** | **Valproate**, Levetiracetam, Lamotrigine | https://www.epilepsy.com/what-is-epilepsy/syndromes/juvenile-myoclonic-epilepsy |
| **Lennox-Gastaut Syndrome** | 1-8 years | **Multiple seizure types**, **Slow spike-wave**, Intellectual disability | Valproate, Lamotrigine, Topiramate, Clobazam, Cannabidiol | https://www.epilepsy.com/what-is-epilepsy/syndromes/lennox-gastaut-syndrome |
| **Dravet Syndrome** | <1 year | **Prolonged febrile seizures**, **SCN1A mutation** | Valproate, Clobazam, Stiripentol, Cannabidiol, **AVOID sodium channel blockers** | https://www.epilepsy.com/what-is-epilepsy/syndromes/dravet-syndrome |
