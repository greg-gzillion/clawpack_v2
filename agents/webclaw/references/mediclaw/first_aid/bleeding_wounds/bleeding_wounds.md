# First Aid - Bleeding and Wounds

## Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **External Bleeding** | **Visible blood loss** from wound | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |
| **Internal Bleeding** | **Blood loss into body cavities** (not visible) | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |

## Control of External Bleeding
| Step | Action | URL |
|------|--------|-----|
| **1** | **Apply direct pressure** with sterile gauze or clean cloth | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |
| **2** | **If blood soaks through**, **Add more gauze** (do NOT remove original) | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |
| **3** | **Apply pressure bandage** to maintain pressure | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |
| **4** | **If severe extremity bleeding**: **Apply tourniquet** if direct pressure fails | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |
| **5** | **Elevate extremity** (if no fracture) above heart level | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |
| **6** | **Apply pressure to arterial pressure point** (brachial, femoral) if needed | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |

## Tourniquet Application
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Indications** | **Life-threatening extremity bleeding** uncontrolled by direct pressure | https://www.stopthebleed.org |
| **Placement** | **2-3 inches above wound** (not over joint) | https://www.stopthebleed.org |
| **Tightening** | **Tighten until bleeding stops** and **distal pulse disappears** | https://www.stopthebleed.org |
| **Time** | **Note time of application** | https://www.stopthebleed.org |
| **Do NOT Remove** | **Leave in place** for EMS; **Only remove in hospital setting** | https://www.stopthebleed.org |

## Wound Care
| Wound Type | Management | URL |
|------------|------------|-----|
| **Abrasion** | **Clean with soap and water**, **Apply antibiotic ointment**, **Cover with bandage** | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/wounds |
| **Laceration** | **Control bleeding**, **Clean wound**, **Close with steri-strips** (if small), **Seek medical care** (if deep, gaping, or >6-12h old) | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/wounds |
| **Puncture** | **Clean thoroughly**, **Do NOT remove impaled object**, **Tetanus prophylaxis** | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/wounds |
| **Avulsion** | **Control bleeding**, **Preserve avulsed tissue** (wrap in moist gauze, Place in plastic bag, Keep cool), **Seek medical care** | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/wounds |
| **Amputation** | **Control bleeding** (tourniquet if needed), **Preserve amputated part** (wrap in moist gauze, Place in plastic bag, Place bag in ice water - do NOT freeze), **Transport with victim** | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/wounds |

## Impaled Object
| Step | Action | URL |
|------|--------|-----|
| **1** | **Do NOT remove** object (may tamponade bleeding) | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/wounds |
| **2** | **Stabilize object** with bulky dressing | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/wounds |
| **3** | **Control bleeding** around object with direct pressure | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/wounds |
| **4** | **Seek immediate medical care** | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/wounds |
| **Exception** | **Remove if object obstructs airway** (cheek, neck) | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/wounds |

## Signs of Internal Bleeding
| Sign | URL |
|------|-----|
| **Bruising**, **Swelling**, **Pain**, **Rigidity** over injured area | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |
| **Signs of shock**: **Pale/cool/clammy skin**, **Rapid weak pulse**, **Rapid breathing**, **Altered mental status** | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |
| **Bleeding from body openings** (mouth, nose, ears, rectum, vagina) | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |
| **Vomiting blood** (hematemesis) or **Coffee-ground emesis** | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |
| **Black tarry stool** (melena) or **Bright red blood per rectum** | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/bleeding |

## Nosebleed (Epistaxis)
| Step | Action | URL |
|------|--------|-----|
| **1** | **Sit upright**, **Lean forward** (prevents swallowing blood) | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/nosebleed |
| **2** | **Pinch soft part of nose** for **10-15 minutes** continuously | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/nosebleed |
| **3** | **Apply cold compress** to bridge of nose | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/nosebleed |
| **4** | **If bleeding persists >20 min**, **Seek medical care** | https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid/nosebleed |
