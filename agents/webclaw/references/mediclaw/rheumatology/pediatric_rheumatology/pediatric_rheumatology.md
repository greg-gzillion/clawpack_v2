# Rheumatology - Pediatric Rheumatology

## Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Arthritis of unknown etiology** beginning **before age 16** and persisting for **=6 weeks** | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Juvenile-Arthritis |
| **ILAR Classification** | **Oligoarticular** (=4 joints), **Polyarticular RF-negative**, **Polyarticular RF-positive**, **Systemic**, **Psoriatic**, **Enthesitis-related**, **Undifferentiated** | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Juvenile-Arthritis |
| **Prevalence** | **~1 in 1,000** children | https://www.arthritis.org/diseases/juvenile-arthritis |

## JIA Subtypes
| Subtype | Key Features | Treatment | URL |
|---------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Oligoarticular** (50-60%) | **=4 joints** (knees, ankles), **Girls > Boys**, **High risk of uveitis** (screen q3-4 months) | **NSAIDs**, **Intraarticular CS**, **Methotrexate** | https://www.rheumatology.org/Portals/0/Files/JIA-Guideline-2019.pdf |
| **Polyarticular RF-Negative** (20-30%) | **=5 joints**, Symmetric or asymmetric | **Methotrexate**, **TNF inhibitor** | https://www.rheumatology.org/Portals/0/Files/JIA-Guideline-2019.pdf |
| **Polyarticular RF-Positive** (5-10%) | Similar to adult RA, **Erosions**, **Rheumatoid nodules** | **Methotrexate**, **TNF inhibitor** | https://www.rheumatology.org/Portals/0/Files/JIA-Guideline-2019.pdf |
| **Systemic** (5-15%) | **Quotidian fever**, **Evanescent rash**, **Hepatosplenomegaly**, **Serositis**, **? Ferritin** | **NSAIDs**, **Corticosteroids**, **IL-1 inhibitors** (anakinra, canakinumab), **IL-6 inhibitor** (tocilizumab) | https://www.rheumatology.org/Portals/0/Files/JIA-Guideline-2019.pdf |
| **Enthesitis-Related** (5-10%) | **Enthesitis**, **Axial involvement**, **HLA-B27+**, **Boys > Girls** | **NSAIDs**, **TNF inhibitor**, **Sulfasalazine** | https://www.rheumatology.org/Portals/0/Files/JIA-Guideline-2019.pdf |
| **Psoriatic** (5-10%) | Arthritis + **Psoriasis** OR =2 of: **Dactylitis**, **Nail pitting**, **Family history of psoriasis** | **Methotrexate**, **TNF inhibitor** | https://www.rheumatology.org/Portals/0/Files/JIA-Guideline-2019.pdf |

## Kawasaki Disease
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Acute, self-limited vasculitis of medium-sized arteries** (especially coronary) | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Kawasaki-Disease |
| **Demographics** | **Children <5 years** (peak 1-2 years), **Asian > other ethnicities** | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Kawasaki-Disease |
| **Diagnostic Criteria** | **Fever =5 days** + **=4 of**: **Bilateral conjunctival injection**, **Oral mucous membrane changes**, **Polymorphous rash**, **Cervical lymphadenopathy** (>1.5 cm), **Extremity changes** (erythema, edema, desquamation) | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Kawasaki-Disease |
| **Treatment** | **IVIG** 2 g/kg + **High-dose aspirin** (80-100 mg/kg/day) ? Low-dose aspirin (3-5 mg/kg/day) | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Kawasaki-Disease |
| **Refractory Disease** | **Second dose IVIG**, **Corticosteroids**, **Infliximab**, **Cyclosporine**, **Anakinra** | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Kawasaki-Disease |
| **Long-Term Follow-Up** | **Echocardiogram** at diagnosis, 2 weeks, 6-8 weeks; **Long-term cardiology follow-up** if coronary abnormalities | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Kawasaki-Disease |
