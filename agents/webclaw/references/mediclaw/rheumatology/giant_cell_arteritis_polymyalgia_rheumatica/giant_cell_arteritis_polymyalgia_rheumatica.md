# Rheumatology - Giant Cell Arteritis (GCA) and Polymyalgia Rheumatica (PMR)

## Giant Cell Arteritis (Temporal Arteritis)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Granulomatous vasculitis of large and medium arteries** (especially cranial branches) | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Giant-Cell-Arteritis |
| **Demographics** | **Age >50** (peak 70-80), **Women > Men (2-3:1)** , **Northern European ancestry** | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Giant-Cell-Arteritis |
| **Clinical Features** | **New headache** (temporal), **Scalp tenderness**, **Jaw claudication**, **Visual symptoms** (diplopia, Amaurosis fugax, **Sudden vision loss**), **PMR symptoms** (40-60%) | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Giant-Cell-Arteritis |
| **Laboratory** | **?? ESR** (often >100), **? CRP**, **Normocytic anemia**, **Thrombocytosis** | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Giant-Cell-Arteritis |
| **Diagnosis** | **Temporal artery biopsy** (gold standard) OR **Temporal artery ultrasound** (halo sign) OR **MRI/PET-CT** | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Giant-Cell-Arteritis |

## Polymyalgia Rheumatica (PMR)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Inflammatory condition causing proximal muscle pain and stiffness** | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Polymyalgia-Rheumatica |
| **Demographics** | **Age >50** (peak 70-80), **Women > Men (2:1)** | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Polymyalgia-Rheumatica |
| **Clinical Features** | **Bilateral shoulder and hip girdle pain/stiffness**, **Morning stiffness >45 min**, **Constitutional symptoms** | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Polymyalgia-Rheumatica |
| **Laboratory** | **? ESR** (often >40), **? CRP**, **Normal CK** (distinguishes from myositis) | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Polymyalgia-Rheumatica |
| **Association** | **15-20% have GCA**; **40-60% of GCA have PMR symptoms** | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Polymyalgia-Rheumatica |

## Treatment of GCA and PMR
| Condition | Initial Therapy | Taper | Refractory/Relapse | URL |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|-------------------|-----|
| **PMR** | **Prednisone 12.5-25 mg/day** | **Slow taper** over 12-18 months (reduce by 1-2.5 mg/month) | **Methotrexate** (steroid-sparing) | https://www.rheumatology.org/Portals/0/Files/PMR-Guideline-2015.pdf |
| **GCA (No Visual Symptoms)** | **Prednisone 40-60 mg/day** | **Slow taper** over 12-18 months | **Tocilizumab** (FDA-approved), **Methotrexate** | https://www.rheumatology.org/Portals/0/Files/GCA-Guideline-2021.pdf |
| **GCA (With Visual Symptoms)** | **IV Methylprednisolone 1g/day x3d** ? Prednisone 60-80 mg/day | **Slow taper** | **Tocilizumab** | https://www.rheumatology.org/Portals/0/Files/GCA-Guideline-2021.pdf |
| **GCA with Tocilizumab** | **Prednisone + Tocilizumab 162 mg SC weekly** | **Rapid taper** (off prednisone by 6-12 months) | Continue tocilizumab | https://www.rheumatology.org/Portals/0/Files/GCA-Guideline-2021.pdf |
