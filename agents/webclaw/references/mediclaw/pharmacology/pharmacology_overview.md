# Pharmacology - Overview

## What is Pharmacology?
Pharmacology is the science of drugs, including their origin, composition, pharmacokinetics, pharmacodynamics, therapeutic use, and toxicology. It encompasses the study of how drugs interact with biological systems to produce therapeutic effects.

## Core Principles of Pharmacology
| Principle | Definition | URL |
|-----------|------------|-----|
| **Pharmacokinetics** | What the body does to the drug (Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/pharmacokinetics |
| **Pharmacodynamics** | What the drug does to the body (Mechanism of action, Receptor binding, Dose-response) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/pharmacodynamics |
| **Therapeutic Index** | Ratio of toxic dose to effective dose (TI = TD50/ED50) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK557829/ |
| **Bioavailability** | Fraction of administered drug that reaches systemic circulation | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/bioavailability |
| **Half-Life** | Time required for drug concentration to decrease by 50% | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK557829/ |
| **Volume of Distribution** | Theoretical volume in which drug is distributed | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK557829/ |
| **Clearance** | Volume of plasma cleared of drug per unit time | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK557829/ |
