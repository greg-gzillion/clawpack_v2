## Bioavailability (F)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Fraction of administered dose reaching systemic circulation** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/bioavailability |
| **IV Administration** | **F = 1** (100% bioavailability) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/bioavailability |
| **Oral Administration** | **F < 1** due to incomplete absorption and first-pass metabolism | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/bioavailability |

## Zero-Order vs First-Order Kinetics
| Kinetics | Description | Examples | URL |
|----------|-------------|----------|-----|
| **First-Order** | **Constant fraction** of drug eliminated per unit time (most drugs) | Most drugs | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK557829/ |
| **Zero-Order** | **Constant amount** of drug eliminated per unit time (saturable) | **Phenytoin**, **Ethanol**, **Aspirin** (high dose) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK557829/ |
