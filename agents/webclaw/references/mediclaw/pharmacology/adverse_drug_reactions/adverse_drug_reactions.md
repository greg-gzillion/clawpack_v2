# Pharmacology - Adverse Drug Reactions

## Types of Adverse Drug Reactions
| Type | Description | Examples | URL |
|------|-------------|----------|-----|
| **Type A (Augmented)** | **Dose-dependent**, Predictable, Extension of pharmacologic effect | **Hypoglycemia** from insulin, **Bleeding** from warfarin | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/finding-and-learning-about-side-effects-adverse-reactions |
| **Type B (Bizarre)** | **Dose-independent**, Unpredictable, Idiosyncratic | **Anaphylaxis** from penicillin, **SJS/TEN** from anticonvulsants | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/finding-and-learning-about-side-effects-adverse-reactions |
| **Type C (Chronic)** | **Dose and time-dependent**, Cumulative | **Osteoporosis** from corticosteroids, **Tardive dyskinesia** from antipsychotics | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/finding-and-learning-about-side-effects-adverse-reactions |
| **Type D (Delayed)** | **Delayed onset**, Teratogenicity, Carcinogenicity | **Clear cell adenocarcinoma** from DES, **Secondary malignancy** from chemotherapy | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/finding-and-learning-about-side-effects-adverse-reactions |
| **Type E (End of use)** | **Withdrawal** reactions | **Opioid withdrawal**, **Benzodiazepine withdrawal**, **Beta-blocker withdrawal** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/finding-and-learning-about-side-effects-adverse-reactions |
| **Type F (Failure)** | **Unexpected failure of therapy** | **Antibiotic resistance**, **Drug interactions** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-consumers-and-patients-drugs/finding-and-learning-about-side-effects-adverse-reactions |

## Severe Cutaneous Adverse Reactions (SCAR)
| Reaction | Onset | Features | Offending Drugs | URL |
|----------|-------|----------|-----------------|-----|
| **DRESS** | 2-6 weeks | **Fever**, **Eosinophilia**, **Rash**, **Organ involvement** | **Aromatic anticonvulsants** (phenytoin, Carbamazepine), **Allopurinol**, **Sulfonamides** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-drug-class/drug-induced-liver-injury |
| **SJS/TEN** | 1-4 weeks | **Mucosal erosions**, **Epidermal detachment** (<10% SJS, >30% TEN) | **Aromatic anticonvulsants**, **Allopurinol**, **Sulfonamides**, **NSAIDs** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-drug-class/drug-induced-liver-injury |
| **AGEP** | 1-2 weeks | **Non-follicular pustules** on erythematous base | **Antibiotics** (aminopenicillins), **CCBs** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-drug-class/drug-induced-liver-injury |

## Drug-Induced Liver Injury (DILI)
| Pattern | AST/ALT | Alk Phos | Bilirubin | Examples | URL |
|---------|---------|----------|-----------|----------|-----|
| **Hepatocellular** | **>3x ULN** | <2x ULN | Variable | **Acetaminophen**, **Isoniazid**, **Valproate** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-drug-class/drug-induced-liver-injury |
| **Cholestatic** | <3x ULN | **>2x ULN** | Elevated | **Amoxicillin-clavulanate**, **Anabolic steroids**, **Chlorpromazine** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-drug-class/drug-induced-liver-injury |
| **Mixed** | >3x ULN | >2x ULN | Variable | **Phenytoin**, **TMP-SMX**, **Macrolides** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/information-drug-class/drug-induced-liver-injury |
