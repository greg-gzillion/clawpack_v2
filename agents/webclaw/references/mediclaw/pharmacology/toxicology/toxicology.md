# Pharmacology - Toxicology

## Common Antidotes
| Toxin | Antidote | Dose | URL |
|-------|----------|------|-----|
| **Acetaminophen** | **N-Acetylcysteine (NAC)** | 150 mg/kg over 1h ? 50 mg/kg over 4h ? 100 mg/kg over 16h | https://www.poison.org/articles/acetaminophen |
| **Opioids** | **Naloxone** | 0.4-2 mg IV/IM/IN; Repeat q2-3min | https://www.cdc.gov/stop-overdose/index.html |
| **Benzodiazepines** | **Flumazenil** | 0.2 mg IV; Repeat 0.3 mg; Max 3 mg (AVOID in chronic use) | https://www.poison.org/articles/benzodiazepines |
| **Organophosphates** | **Atropine + Pralidoxime (2-PAM)** | Atropine 2-5 mg IV q5min; 2-PAM 1-2g IV | https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/topics/pesticides |
| **Beta-Blockers** | **Glucagon** | 3-10 mg IV; Then infusion 1-5 mg/hr | https://www.poison.org/articles/beta-blockers |
| **Calcium Channel Blockers** | **Calcium gluconate + High-dose insulin** | Calcium 1-2g IV; Insulin 1 unit/kg bolus + 1 unit/kg/hr | https://www.poison.org/articles/calcium-channel-blockers |
| **Digoxin** | **Digoxin Immune Fab (Digibind)** | Dose based on ingested amount or serum level | https://www.poison.org/articles/digoxin |
| **Ethylene Glycol/Methanol** | **Fomepizole** or **Ethanol** | Fomepizole 15 mg/kg load; 10 mg/kg q12h | https://www.poison.org/articles/antifreeze |
| **Cyanide** | **Hydroxocobalamin (Cyanokit)** | 5g IV over 15 min | https://www.cdc.gov/chemicalemergencies/factsheets/cyanide.html |
| **Carbon Monoxide** | **Oxygen 100%** or **Hyperbaric oxygen** | Until COHb <3% | https://www.cdc.gov/co/guidelines.htm |
| **Warfarin** | **Vitamin K + 4-Factor PCC** | Vitamin K 10 mg IV; PCC based on INR | https://www.cdc.gov/ncbddd/hemophilia/treatment.html |
| **Heparin** | **Protamine** | 1 mg per 100 units heparin (max 50 mg) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |
| **Iron** | **Deferoxamine** | 15 mg/kg/hr IV infusion | https://www.poison.org/articles/iron |
| **Tricyclic Antidepressants** | **Sodium Bicarbonate** | 1-2 mEq/kg bolus for wide QRS or hypotension | https://www.poison.org/articles/tricyclic-antidepressants |
| **Sulfonylureas** | **Octreotide** | 50-100 mcg SC q6-12h | https://www.poison.org/articles/sulfonylureas |
| **Methemoglobinemia** | **Methylene Blue** | 1-2 mg/kg IV over 5 min | https://www.poison.org/articles/methemoglobinemia |

## Common Toxidromes
| Toxidrome | Signs | Common Agents | URL |
|-----------|-------|---------------|-----|
| **Anticholinergic** | "Hot as a hare, Blind as a bat, Dry as a bone, Red as a beet, Mad as a hatter" | Antihistamines, TCAs, Atropine | https://www.poison.org/articles/anticholinergic |
| **Cholinergic** | **SLUDGE** (Salivation, Lacrimation, Urination, Defecation, GI distress, Emesis) + **DUMBELS** | Organophosphates, Carbamates | https://www.poison.org/articles/cholinergic |
| **Opioid** | **CNS depression**, **Miosis**, **Respiratory depression** | Heroin, Fentanyl, Oxycodone | https://www.cdc.gov/opioids/patients/index.html |
| **Sympathomimetic** | **Tachycardia**, **Hypertension**, **Mydriasis**, **Diaphoresis** | Cocaine, Amphetamines | https://www.poison.org/articles/sympathomimetic |
| **Sedative-Hypnotic** | **CNS depression**, **Respiratory depression** | Benzodiazepines, Barbiturates | https://www.poison.org/articles/sedative-hypnotic |
