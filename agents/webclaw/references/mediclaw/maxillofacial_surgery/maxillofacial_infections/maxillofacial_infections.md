## Ludwig Angina
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Bilateral infection of submandibular, sublingual, and submental spaces** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-infections |
| **Etiology** | **Odontogenic** (mandibular molars) | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-infections |
| **Clinical Features** | **Firm, brawny edema** of floor of mouth, **Elevated tongue**, **Drooling**, **Stridor**, **Airway compromise** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-infections |
| **Management** | **Airway securement** (awake fiberoptic intubation), **IV antibiotics**, **Surgical drainage** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-infections |

| **Severe** | **Above + S. aureus**, **Gram-negatives** | **Ampicillin-sulbactam**, **Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole**, **Piperacillin-tazobactam** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-infections |

## Surgical Drainage Principles
| Principle | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Indications** | **Fluctuance**, **Airway compromise**, **No response to antibiotics** (48-72h) | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-infections |
| **Approach** | **Dependent drainage**, **Extraoral** (submandibular, Submental) or **Intraoral** (vestibular) | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-infections |
| **Drain Placement** | **Penrose** or **Jackson-Pratt drain** until output <25 mL/day | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-infections |
| **Culture** | **Aerobic and anaerobic** cultures | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-infections |
