# Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery - Maxillofacial Pathology

## Odontogenic Cysts
| Cyst | Origin | Clinical Features | Treatment | URL |
|------|--------|------------------|-----------|-----|
| **Radicular (Periapical)** | **Inflammatory** (non-vital tooth) | **Most common**, **Asymptomatic**, **Radiolucency at apex** | **Endodontic treatment** or **Extraction + Curettage** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |
| **Dentigerous** | **Developmental** (crown of unerupted tooth) | **Second most common**, **Mandibular third molar** | **Enucleation** + **Extraction** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |
| **Odontogenic Keratocyst (OKC)** | **Developmental** (dental lamina) | **High recurrence** (30-50%), **Posterior mandible**, **PTCH1 mutation** | **Enucleation + Carnoy's solution** or **Peripheral ostectomy** or **Resection** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |
| **Lateral Periodontal** | **Developmental** | **Lateral to vital tooth**, **Mandibular premolar** | **Enucleation** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |
| **Calcifying Odontogenic Cyst (Gorlin)** | **Developmental** | **Mixed radiolucent/radiopaque**, **Ghost cells** | **Enucleation** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |

## Odontogenic Tumors
| Tumor | Behavior | Clinical Features | Treatment | URL |
|-------|----------|------------------|-----------|-----|
| **Ameloblastoma** | **Benign but locally aggressive** | **Multilocular radiolucency** ("soap bubble"), **Posterior mandible** | **Resection with 1-1.5 cm margins** (high recurrence with enucleation) | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |
| **Odontoma** | **Hamartoma** (most common odontogenic tumor) | **Compound** (tooth-like structures), **Complex** (disorganized) | **Enucleation** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |
| **Odontogenic Myxoma** | **Benign but infiltrative** | **Multilocular radiolucency**, **Mandible** | **Resection** (infiltrative) | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |
| **Cementoblastoma** | **Benign** | **Radiolucency with central opacity** fused to root, **Mandibular molar** | **Extraction + Enucleation** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |

## Non-Odontogenic Cysts and Tumors
| Lesion | Clinical Features | Treatment | URL |
|--------|------------------|-----------|-----|
| **Nasopalatine Duct Cyst** | **Heart-shaped radiolucency** between central incisors, **Anterior maxilla** | **Enucleation** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |
| **Central Giant Cell Granuloma** | **Multilocular radiolucency**, **Anterior mandible**, **Young females** | **Curettage**, **Intralesional corticosteroids**, **Calcitonin**, **Interferon** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |
| **Ossifying Fibroma** | **Well-circumscribed mixed lesion**, **Mandibular premolar/molar** | **Enucleation or Resection** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |
| **Fibrous Dysplasia** | **Ill-defined radiopacity** ("ground glass"), **Monostotic > Polyostotic** (McCune-Albright) | **Observation** (arrests after puberty), **Contouring surgery** | https://www.aaoms.org/procedures/oral-pathology |

## Medication-Related Osteonecrosis of the Jaws (MRONJ)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Exposed necrotic bone** for **>8 weeks** in patient on **antiresorptive/antiangiogenic medication**, **No history of radiation** | https://www.aaoms.org/practice-resources/position-papers |
| **Risk Factors** | **IV bisphosphonates** (highest), **Oral bisphosphonates**, **Denosumab**, **Anti-angiogenics**, **Dental extraction**, **Poor oral hygiene** | https://www.aaoms.org/practice-resources/position-papers |
| **Staging** | **Stage 0**: No exposed bone, Symptoms; **Stage 1**: Exposed bone, Asymptomatic; **Stage 2**: Exposed bone + Infection; **Stage 3**: Exposed bone + Pathologic fracture/Extraoral fistula | https://www.aaoms.org/practice-resources/position-papers |
| **Management** | **Conservative** (chlorhexidine, Antibiotics), **Avoid surgery** unless Stage 3 or refractory | https://www.aaoms.org/practice-resources/position-papers |

## Osteoradionecrosis (ORN)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Exposed necrotic bone** for **>3 months** in **irradiated field**, **No tumor recurrence** | https://www.aaoms.org/practice-resources/position-papers |
| **Pathophysiology** | **Hypoxia**, **Hypocellularity**, **Hypovascularity** | https://www.aaoms.org/practice-resources/position-papers |
| **Prevention** | **Dental evaluation before radiation**, **Extract diseased teeth =2 weeks before**, **Fluoride** | https://www.aaoms.org/practice-resources/position-papers |
| **Treatment** | **Conservative** (chlorhexidine, Antibiotics), **Hyperbaric oxygen**, **Pentoxifylline + Tocopherol**, **Surgical debridement/Resection + Free flap** | https://www.aaoms.org/practice-resources/position-papers |
