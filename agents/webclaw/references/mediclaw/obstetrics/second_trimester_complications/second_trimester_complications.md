# Obstetrics - Second Trimester Complications

## Cervical Insufficiency
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Painless cervical dilation** leading to **second trimester pregnancy loss** or **preterm birth** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2014/02/cerclage-for-the-management-of-cervical-insufficiency |
| **Risk Factors** | **Prior cervical surgery** (cone biopsy, LEEP), **Prior second trimester loss**, **Uterine anomalies**, **Connective tissue disorders** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2014/02/cerclage-for-the-management-of-cervical-insufficiency |
| **Diagnosis** | **History of painless cervical dilation** OR **Cervical length <25 mm** before 24 weeks in high-risk patients | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2014/02/cerclage-for-the-management-of-cervical-insufficiency |

## Cerclage Indications
| Indication | Timing | URL |
|------------|--------|-----|
| **History-Indicated** | **=3 prior second trimester losses** or **prior cerclage**; Place at **12-14 weeks** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2014/02/cerclage-for-the-management-of-cervical-insufficiency |
| **Ultrasound-Indicated** | **Prior spontaneous PTB <34 weeks** + **Cervical length <25 mm** before 24 weeks | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2014/02/cerclage-for-the-management-of-cervical-insufficiency |
| **Physical Exam-Indicated (Rescue)** | **Cervical dilation** with **visible membranes** before 24 weeks (no infection, No bleeding, No contractions) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2014/02/cerclage-for-the-management-of-cervical-insufficiency |
| **Contraindications** | **Infection**, **Bleeding**, **Preterm labor**, **Ruptured membranes**, **Lethal fetal anomaly**, **Fetal demise** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2014/02/cerclage-for-the-management-of-cervical-insufficiency |

## Second Trimester Pregnancy Loss Evaluation
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Fetal Evaluation** | **Karyotype** or **Microarray** from products of conception | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/02/second-trimester-abortion |
| **Placental Pathology** | Evaluate for **infection**, **Infarction**, **Abruption** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/02/second-trimester-abortion |
| **Maternal Evaluation** | **Antiphospholipid antibodies**, **Thrombophilia** (controversial), **Uterine cavity evaluation** (saline sonogram, Hysteroscopy) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/02/second-trimester-abortion |

## Fetal Demise (Stillbirth)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Fetal death at =20 weeks gestation** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/obstetric-care-consensus/articles/2020/03/management-of-stillbirth |
| **Evaluation** | **Karyotype/Microarray**, **Placental pathology**, **Autopsy** (offer), **Maternal labs** (CBC, Kleihauer-Betke, Antiphospholipid antibodies, Syphilis, Thyroid) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/obstetric-care-consensus/articles/2020/03/management-of-stillbirth |
| **Delivery Timing** | **Individualized**; Most deliver within 2 weeks; **Expectant management** reasonable if stable | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/obstetric-care-consensus/articles/2020/03/management-of-stillbirth |
| **Mode of Delivery** | **Vaginal** preferred (induction); Cesarean for obstetric indications | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/obstetric-care-consensus/articles/2020/03/management-of-stillbirth |
| **Suppression of Lactation** | **Cabergoline** (preferred), **Dopamine agonists**, **Supportive measures** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/obstetric-care-consensus/articles/2020/03/management-of-stillbirth |
