# Obstetrics - Intrapartum Fetal Monitoring

## Fetal Heart Rate (FHR) Interpretation (NICHD)
| Component | Definition | URL |
|-----------|------------|-----|
| **Baseline** | Mean FHR over **10-minute segment** (rounded to 5 bpm) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Normal Baseline** | **110-160 bpm** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Tachycardia** | **>160 bpm** for =10 min | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Bradycardia** | **<110 bpm** for =10 min | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Variability** | Fluctuations in baseline FHR | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Absent Variability** | **Undetectable** amplitude | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Minimal Variability** | **=5 bpm** amplitude | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Moderate Variability** | **6-25 bpm** amplitude (**normal**) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Marked Variability** | **>25 bpm** amplitude | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |

## FHR Accelerations and Decelerations
| Pattern | Definition | Significance | URL |
|---------|------------|--------------|-----|
| **Acceleration** | **Abrupt increase** =15 bpm for =15 sec (term) or =10 bpm for =10 sec (preterm) | **Reassuring** (indicates fetal well-being) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Early Deceleration** | **Gradual decrease** with **nadir at peak of contraction** | **Head compression** (benign) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Late Deceleration** | **Gradual decrease** with **nadir after peak of contraction** | **Uteroplacental insufficiency** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Variable Deceleration** | **Abrupt decrease** (=15 bpm for =15 sec) | **Umbilical cord compression** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Prolonged Deceleration** | **=15 bpm decrease** lasting **=2 min but <10 min** | **Cord compression**, **Maternal hypotension**, **Uterine tachysystole** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |

## Three-Tier Fetal Heart Rate Interpretation (ACOG)
| Category | Description | Management | URL |
|----------|-------------|------------|-----|
| **Category I (Normal)** | **Baseline 110-160**, **Moderate variability**, **No late/variable decelerations**, **Accelerations present or absent** | **Continue routine monitoring** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Category II (Indeterminate)** | **All tracings not Category I or III** | **Evaluation and surveillance**, **Intrauterine resuscitation** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Category III (Abnormal)** | **Absent variability + Recurrent late/variable decelerations** OR **Bradycardia** OR **Sinusoidal pattern** | **Prompt evaluation**, **Intrauterine resuscitation**, **Expeditious delivery** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |

## Intrauterine Resuscitation Measures
| Measure | Purpose | URL |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **Maternal Position Change** | Left lateral, Knee-chest, Hands and knees | **Relieve cord compression**, **Improve uterine blood flow** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **IV Fluid Bolus** | 500-1,000 mL LR or NS | **Improve uterine blood flow** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Discontinue Oxytocin** | Stop infusion | **Reduce uterine contractions** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Administer Oxygen** | 10 L/min non-rebreather (controversial) | **Improve fetal oxygenation** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Terbutaline** | 0.25 mg SC | **Acute tocolysis** for tachysystole | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
| **Amnioinfusion** | Warm saline into uterine cavity | **Relieve cord compression** (variable decelerations) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2009/07/intrapartum-fetal-heart-rate-monitoring |
