## Diagnostic Criteria for Early Pregnancy Loss (ACOG)
| Finding | Criteria | URL |
|---------|----------|-----|
| **Crown-Rump Length =7 mm** | **No cardiac activity** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/11/early-pregnancy-loss |
| **Mean Sac Diameter =25 mm** | **No embryo** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/11/early-pregnancy-loss |
| **Absence of embryo with cardiac activity** | **=2 weeks** after scan showing gestational sac without yolk sac | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/11/early-pregnancy-loss |
| **Absence of embryo with cardiac activity** | **=11 days** after scan showing gestational sac with yolk sac | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/11/early-pregnancy-loss |

## Management of Early Pregnancy Loss
| Option | Description | URL |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| **Expectant Management** | **Wait for spontaneous passage** (up to 4-8 weeks) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/11/early-pregnancy-loss |
| **Medical Management** | **Misoprostol 800 mcg vaginally** (repeat if needed) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/11/early-pregnancy-loss |
| **Surgical Management** | **Dilation and Curettage (D&C)** or **Dilation and Evacuation (D&E)** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/11/early-pregnancy-loss |

## Ectopic Pregnancy
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Risk Factors** | **Prior ectopic**, **PID**, **Tubal surgery**, **IUD**, **IVF**, **Smoking** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/03/tubal-ectopic-pregnancy |
| **Symptoms** | **Abdominal pain**, **Vaginal bleeding**, **Amenorrhea** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/03/tubal-ectopic-pregnancy |
| **Diagnosis** | **hCG >1,500-2,000** + **No intrauterine pregnancy on ultrasound** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/03/tubal-ectopic-pregnancy |
| **hCG Discriminatory Zone** | **1,500-2,000 mIU/mL** (transvaginal ultrasound) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/03/tubal-ectopic-pregnancy |

## Ectopic Pregnancy Management
| Option | Criteria | URL |
|--------|----------|-----|
| **Expectant Management** | **Hemodynamically stable**, **hCG <200 and declining**, **No evidence of rupture** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/03/tubal-ectopic-pregnancy |
| **Medical Management (Methotrexate)** | **Hemodynamically stable**, **hCG <5,000**, **Mass <3.5 cm**, **No cardiac activity**, **No contraindications** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/03/tubal-ectopic-pregnancy |
| **Methotrexate Dosing** | **Single-dose**: 50 mg/m˛ IM; **Two-dose**: 50 mg/m˛ IM days 1 and 4 | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/03/tubal-ectopic-pregnancy |
| **Surgical Management** | **Hemodynamically unstable**, **Rupture**, **Failed medical**, **Contraindication to methotrexate** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/03/tubal-ectopic-pregnancy |
| **Salpingostomy vs Salpingectomy** | **Salpingostomy**: Desires future fertility, Normal contralateral tube; **Salpingectomy**: Damaged tube, Recurrent ectopic, Completed childbearing | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/03/tubal-ectopic-pregnancy |

## Hyperemesis Gravidarum
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Persistent vomiting** with **weight loss (>5% pre-pregnancy weight)** , **Dehydration**, **Ketosis**, **Electrolyte abnormalities** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/01/nausea-and-vomiting-of-pregnancy |
| **First-Line Treatment** | **Doxylamine-Pyridoxine** (Diclegis, Bonjesta) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/01/nausea-and-vomiting-of-pregnancy |
| **Additional Therapies** | **Ondansetron**, **Metoclopramide**, **Promethazine**, **Corticosteroids** (severe) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/01/nausea-and-vomiting-of-pregnancy |
| **Thiamine Supplementation** | **100 mg/day** to prevent **Wernicke encephalopathy** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/01/nausea-and-vomiting-of-pregnancy |
| **Hospitalization Criteria** | **Inability to tolerate PO**, **Weight loss**, **Electrolyte abnormalities**, **Ketosis** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/01/nausea-and-vomiting-of-pregnancy |
