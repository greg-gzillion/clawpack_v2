# Obstetrics - Infectious Diseases in Pregnancy

## TORCH Infections
| Infection | Transmission | Fetal Effects | Screening | URL |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|-----------|-----|
| **Toxoplasmosis** | **Undercooked meat**, **Cat feces** | **Hydrocephalus**, **Intracranial calcifications**, **Chorioretinitis** | Not routine (counsel prevention) | https://www.cdc.gov/parasites/toxoplasmosis/index.html |
| **Rubella** | Respiratory droplets | **Congenital rubella syndrome** (deafness, Cataracts, Heart defects) | **Universal** (immunize non-immune postpartum) | https://www.cdc.gov/rubella/index.html |
| **Cytomegalovirus (CMV)** | Body fluids (saliva, urine) | **Sensorineural hearing loss**, **Microcephaly**, **Hepatosplenomegaly** | **Not routine** (counsel hygiene) | https://www.cdc.gov/cmv/index.html |
| **Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV)** | **Vaginal delivery** | **Neonatal HSV** (disseminated, CNS, SEM) | **Cesarean** if active lesions at delivery | https://www.cdc.gov/std/treatment-guidelines/herpes.htm |
| **Parvovirus B19** | Respiratory droplets | **Hydrops fetalis**, **Anemia** (infects RBC precursors) | Not routine (evaluate if exposed) | https://www.cdc.gov/parvovirusb19/pregnancy.html |
| **Varicella-Zoster Virus (VZV)** | Respiratory droplets, Contact | **Congenital varicella syndrome** (limb hypoplasia, Skin scarring, CNS) | **Immunize non-immune postpartum** | https://www.cdc.gov/chickenpox/index.html |

## Group B Streptococcus (GBS)
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Universal Screening** | **Vaginal-rectal culture at 36 0/7 - 37 6/7 weeks** | https://www.cdc.gov/groupbstrep/guidelines/index.html |
| **Intrapartum Prophylaxis Indications** | **Positive GBS culture**, **GBS bacteriuria**, **Prior infant with GBS disease**, **Unknown status + risk factors** (PTB, PROM >18h, Fever =38.0°C, NAAT positive) | https://www.cdc.gov/groupbstrep/guidelines/index.html |
| **Antibiotic Prophylaxis** | **Penicillin G** 5 million units IV load ? 2.5-3 million units q4h OR **Ampicillin** 2g IV load ? 1g q4h | https://www.cdc.gov/groupbstrep/guidelines/index.html |
| **Penicillin Allergy (Low Risk)** | **Cefazolin** 2g IV load ? 1g q8h | https://www.cdc.gov/groupbstrep/guidelines/index.html |
| **Penicillin Allergy (High Risk)** | **Clindamycin** 900 mg IV q8h (if susceptible) OR **Vancomycin** (if resistant or unknown) | https://www.cdc.gov/groupbstrep/guidelines/index.html |

## HIV in Pregnancy
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Universal Screening** | **Opt-out testing** at first prenatal visit and third trimester | https://clinicalinfo.hiv.gov/en/guidelines/perinatal |
| **Antiretroviral Therapy** | **All pregnant women with HIV** (regardless of CD4 or viral load) | https://clinicalinfo.hiv.gov/en/guidelines/perinatal |
| **Preferred Regimen** | **Dolutegravir + Tenofovir DF/TAF + Emtricitabine or Lamivudine** | https://clinicalinfo.hiv.gov/en/guidelines/perinatal |
| **Mode of Delivery** | **Cesarean at 38 weeks** if viral load **>1,000 copies/mL** near delivery; **Vaginal delivery** if viral load **=1,000 copies/mL** | https://clinicalinfo.hiv.gov/en/guidelines/perinatal |
| **IV Zidovudine** | **Intrapartum** if viral load >1,000 copies/mL | https://clinicalinfo.hiv.gov/en/guidelines/perinatal |
| **Neonatal Prophylaxis** | **Zidovudine x4-6 weeks** (with additional agents if high-risk) | https://clinicalinfo.hiv.gov/en/guidelines/perinatal |
| **Avoid Breastfeeding** | **In the United States** (safe infant formula available) | https://clinicalinfo.hiv.gov/en/guidelines/perinatal |

## Hepatitis B in Pregnancy
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Universal Screening** | **HBsAg** at first prenatal visit | https://www.cdc.gov/hepatitis/hbv/perinatalxmtn.htm |
| **Maternal Treatment** | **Tenofovir DF** 300 mg daily starting at **28-32 weeks** if **HBV DNA >200,000 IU/mL** | https://www.cdc.gov/hepatitis/hbv/perinatalxmtn.htm |
| **Neonatal Prophylaxis** | **Hepatitis B vaccine + HBIG** within **12 hours of birth** | https://www.cdc.gov/hepatitis/hbv/perinatalxmtn.htm |
| **Breastfeeding** | **Safe** (after infant receives vaccine + HBIG) | https://www.cdc.gov/hepatitis/hbv/perinatalxmtn.htm |

## Syphilis in Pregnancy
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Universal Screening** | **RPR/VDRL** at first prenatal visit, third trimester, and delivery | https://www.cdc.gov/std/treatment-guidelines/syphilis-pregnancy.htm |
| **Treatment** | **Penicillin G benzathine** 2.4 million units IM x1 (early syphilis) or **weekly x3** (late latent/unknown duration) | https://www.cdc.gov/std/treatment-guidelines/syphilis-pregnancy.htm |
| **Penicillin Allergy** | **Desensitization** (no alternative in pregnancy) | https://www.cdc.gov/std/treatment-guidelines/syphilis-pregnancy.htm |
| **Jarisch-Herxheimer Reaction** | **Fever**, **Myalgias**, **Hypotension** within 24h; **Monitor fetus** | https://www.cdc.gov/std/treatment-guidelines/syphilis-pregnancy.htm |
