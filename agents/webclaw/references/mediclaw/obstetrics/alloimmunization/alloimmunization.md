# Obstetrics - Alloimmunization

## Red Blood Cell Alloimmunization
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Maternal antibodies against fetal RBC antigens** (most commonly **RhD**, also Rhc, Kell, Duffy, Kidd) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/06/management-of-alloimmunization-during-pregnancy |
| **Pathophysiology** | **Fetomaternal hemorrhage** ? Maternal sensitization ? IgG antibodies cross placenta ? **Fetal hemolytic anemia** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/06/management-of-alloimmunization-during-pregnancy |
| **RhD Prevention** | **Rhogam** (anti-D immune globulin) at **28 weeks** and **within 72h of delivery** (if Rh-negative, Unsensitized) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/06/management-of-alloimmunization-during-pregnancy |
| **Rhogam Dose** | **300 mcg** (covers up to 30 mL fetomaternal hemorrhage) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/06/management-of-alloimmunization-during-pregnancy |

## Management of Alloimmunized Pregnancy
| Antibody Titer | Management | URL |
|----------------|------------|-----|
| **Anti-D <1:16** (or Critical Titer) | **Repeat titer q4 weeks**; No further intervention unless titer rises | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/06/management-of-alloimmunization-during-pregnancy |
| **Anti-D =1:16** (or Critical Titer) | **Serial MCA-PSV Doppler** q1-2 weeks starting at 16-20 weeks | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/06/management-of-alloimmunization-during-pregnancy |
| **MCA-PSV =1.5 MoM** | **Cordocentesis** or **Delivery** (depending on gestational age) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/06/management-of-alloimmunization-during-pregnancy |

## Middle Cerebral Artery Peak Systolic Velocity (MCA-PSV)
| MCA-PSV (MoM) | Interpretation | Management | URL |
|----------------|----------------|------------|-----|
| **<1.5** | **No or mild anemia** | Repeat in 1-2 weeks | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/06/management-of-alloimmunization-during-pregnancy |
| **=1.5** | **Moderate-severe anemia** | **Cordocentesis** or **Delivery** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/06/management-of-alloimmunization-during-pregnancy |

## Intrauterine Transfusion (IUT)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Indication** | **Fetal anemia** with **Hct <30%** (or Hgb <10 g/dL) before 34-35 weeks | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/06/management-of-alloimmunization-during-pregnancy |
| **Procedure** | **Ultrasound-guided transfusion** of **O-negative, Irradiated, CMV-negative, Cross-matched** packed RBCs into **umbilical vein** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/06/management-of-alloimmunization-during-pregnancy |
| **Complications** | **Fetal bradycardia**, **Infection**, **PPROM**, **Fetal demise** (1-3% per procedure) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/06/management-of-alloimmunization-during-pregnancy |

## Fetal and Neonatal Alloimmune Thrombocytopenia (FNAIT)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Maternal antibodies against fetal platelet antigens** (most commonly **HPA-1a**) ? **Fetal/neonatal thrombocytopenia** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/03/fetal-and-neonatal-alloimmune-thrombocytopenia |
| **Presentation** | **Intracranial hemorrhage** (10-20%), **Petechiae**, **Purpura** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/03/fetal-and-neonatal-alloimmune-thrombocytopenia |
| **Management** | **IVIG ± Corticosteroids** (antenatal), **Matched platelet transfusion** (neonatal) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/03/fetal-and-neonatal-alloimmune-thrombocytopenia |
