# Obstetrics - Cesarean Delivery

## Indications for Cesarean Delivery
| Category | Examples | URL |
|----------|----------|-----|
| **Fetal** | **Non-reassuring fetal status**, **Malpresentation** (breech, Transverse), **Macrosomia** (suspected), **Multiple gestation** (non-vertex first twin) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |
| **Maternal** | **Prior uterine surgery** (classical, TOLAC refusal), **Active genital herpes**, **Placenta previa**, **Vasa previa**, **Obstructive pelvic mass** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |
| **Obstetric** | **Labor dystocia**, **Failed induction**, **Placental abruption**, **Umbilical cord prolapse**, **Uterine rupture** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |

## Surgical Technique
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Skin Incision** | **Pfannenstiel** (suprapubic transverse) or **Joel-Cohen** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |
| **Uterine Incision** | **Low transverse** (preferred) or **Classical** (vertical) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |
| **Placental Removal** | **Spontaneous** (reduces blood loss, Endometritis) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |
| **Uterine Closure** | **Single-layer vs Double-layer** (both acceptable) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |
| **Subcutaneous Closure** | **If =2 cm thickness** (reduces wound complications) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |
| **Skin Closure** | **Subcuticular suture** preferred (? infection, ? cosmetic) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |

## Antibiotic Prophylaxis for Cesarean
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Timing** | **Within 60 minutes before incision** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |
| **Agent** | **Cefazolin 2g IV** (or 3g if =120 kg) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |
| **Penicillin Allergy** | **Clindamycin 900 mg + Gentamicin 1.5 mg/kg** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |
| **Adjunctive Azithromycin** | **500 mg IV** in labor or after ROM (reduces infection) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/cesarean-delivery |

## Vaginal Birth After Cesarean (VBAC/TOLAC)
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Success Rate** | **60-80%** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/vaginal-birth-after-cesarean-delivery |
| **Contraindications** | **Prior classical/T-incision**, **Prior uterine rupture**, **Contraindication to labor** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/vaginal-birth-after-cesarean-delivery |
| **Uterine Rupture Risk** | **0.5-1.0%** (spontaneous labor), **1.5-2.0%** (induced/augmented) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/vaginal-birth-after-cesarean-delivery |
| **Monitoring** | **Continuous fetal monitoring**; **Immediate availability of cesarean** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/vaginal-birth-after-cesarean-delivery |
