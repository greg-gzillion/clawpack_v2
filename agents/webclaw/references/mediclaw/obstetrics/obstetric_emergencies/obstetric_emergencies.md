# Obstetrics - Obstetric Emergencies

## Shoulder Dystocia
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Inability to deliver shoulders** after head delivery, requiring **additional obstetric maneuvers** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2021/05/shoulder-dystocia |
| **Risk Factors** | **Macrosomia**, **Diabetes**, **Prior shoulder dystocia**, **Operative vaginal delivery**, **Prolonged second stage** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2021/05/shoulder-dystocia |
| **HELPERR Mnemonic** | **H**elp, **E**valuate for episiotomy, **L**egs (McRoberts), **P**ressure (suprapubic), **E**nter (rotational maneuvers), **R**emove posterior arm, **R**oll (Gaskin/all-fours) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2021/05/shoulder-dystocia |
| **McRoberts Maneuver** | **Hyperflexion of maternal hips** (thighs to abdomen) + **Suprapubic pressure** (Rubin I) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2021/05/shoulder-dystocia |
| **Documentation** | **Time of head delivery**, **Maneuvers performed**, **Time of body delivery**, **Cord gases**, **Neonatal evaluation** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2021/05/shoulder-dystocia |

## Postpartum Hemorrhage (PPH)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Cumulative blood loss =1,000 mL** OR **Blood loss with signs/symptoms of hypovolemia** (regardless of mode of delivery) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **Primary PPH** | **Within 24 hours** of delivery | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **Secondary PPH** | **24 hours - 12 weeks** postpartum | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |

## Causes of PPH (4 T's)
| Cause | Frequency | Risk Factors | Management | URL |
|-------|-----------|--------------|------------|-----|
| **Tone (Uterine Atony)** | **70%** | **Prolonged labor**, **Chorioamnionitis**, **Uterine overdistention** (twins, Polyhydramnios), **Oxytocin**, **Magnesium** | **Uterine massage**, **Uterotonics** (Oxytocin, Methylergonovine, Carboprost, Misoprostol), **Bakri balloon**, **B-Lynch suture**, **Hysterectomy** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **Trauma** | **20%** | **Lacerations** (cervical, vaginal, perineal), **Uterine rupture**, **Uterine inversion** | **Repair lacerations**, **Laparotomy** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **Tissue (Retained Placenta)** | **10%** | **Placenta accreta spectrum**, **Succenturiate lobe**, **Prior uterine surgery** | **Manual removal**, **Curettage**, **Hysterectomy** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **Thrombin (Coagulopathy)** | **<1%** | **HELLP syndrome**, **DIC**, **Anticoagulation**, **Von Willebrand disease** | **Transfuse blood products**, **Treat underlying cause** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |

## Uterotonics for PPH
| Medication | Dose | Contraindications | URL |
|------------|------|-------------------|-----|
| **Oxytocin** | **10-40 units in 1L IV** (or 10 units IM) | None | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **Methylergonovine (Methergine)** | **0.2 mg IM** q2-4h (max 5 doses) | **Hypertension**, **Preeclampsia** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **Carboprost (Hemabate)** | **0.25 mg IM** q15-90min (max 8 doses) | **Asthma** (bronchospasm) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **Misoprostol (Cytotec)** | **800-1,000 mcg PR** (or sublingual/buccal) | **Prior cesarean** (caution with uterine rupture) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **Tranexamic Acid (TXA)** | **1 g IV** over 10 min (within 3h of delivery) | **Active thromboembolic disease** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |

## Massive Transfusion Protocol in Obstetrics
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Activation Criteria** | **Blood loss >1,500 mL**, **Hemodynamic instability**, **Ongoing bleeding** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **Ratio** | **1:1:1** (pRBC:FFP:Platelets) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **TXA** | **1 g IV** within 3 hours of delivery | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **Fibrinogen** | **Cryoprecipitate** if fibrinogen <200 mg/dL | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |

## Umbilical Cord Prolapse
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Umbilical cord presents before fetal presenting part** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/01/umbilical-cord-prolapse |
| **Management** | **Elevate presenting part** (manual vaginal elevation, Knee-chest, Trendelenburg), **Avoid cord manipulation**, **Emergent cesarean delivery** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/01/umbilical-cord-prolapse |
| **Funic Reduction** | **Replace cord into uterus** while preparing for cesarean (controversial) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/01/umbilical-cord-prolapse |

## Uterine Inversion
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Uterus turns inside out** (fundus prolapses through cervix) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
| **Management** | **Immediate manual replacement** (push fundus through cervix toward umbilicus), **Uterotonics AFTER replacement**, **Terbutaline or Nitroglycerin** for relaxation | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/12/postpartum-hemorrhage |
