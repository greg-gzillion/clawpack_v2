# Obstetrics - Third Trimester Complications

## Antepartum Hemorrhage
| Cause | Features | Diagnosis | Management | URL |
|-------|----------|-----------|------------|-----|
| **Placental Abruption** | **Painful vaginal bleeding**, **Uterine tenderness**, **Contractions**, **Fetal distress** | **Clinical** (ultrasound low sensitivity) | **Emergent delivery** if fetal distress or maternal instability; Expectant if stable and preterm | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/10/antepartum-fetal-surveillance |
| **Placenta Previa** | **Painless vaginal bleeding** (often after 28 weeks) | **Ultrasound** (placenta overlying os) | **Cesarean delivery** (36-37 weeks); Avoid vaginal exams; Hospitalize if bleeding | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/10/ultrasound-in-pregnancy |
| **Vasa Previa** | **Painless vaginal bleeding** with **fetal bradycardia** (rupture of fetal vessels) | **Ultrasound** (color Doppler shows vessels over os) | **Cesarean delivery at 34-35 weeks** (before ROM); Corticosteroids | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2018/10/ultrasound-in-pregnancy |
| **Uterine Rupture** | **Severe abdominal pain**, **Vaginal bleeding**, **Fetal bradycardia**, **Loss of station** | **Clinical** (surgical emergency) | **Emergent laparotomy** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/vaginal-birth-after-cesarean-delivery |

## Decreased Fetal Movement
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Definition** | Maternal perception of **reduced fetal activity** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2021/06/antepartum-fetal-surveillance |
| **Evaluation** | **Nonstress Test (NST)** or **Biophysical Profile (BPP)** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2021/06/antepartum-fetal-surveillance |
| **Kick Counts** | **10 movements in 2 hours** (if <10, further evaluation) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2021/06/antepartum-fetal-surveillance |
| **Counseling** | Educate patients to **report decreased movement**; Routine kick counts **not recommended** (insufficient evidence) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2021/06/antepartum-fetal-surveillance |

## Acute Fatty Liver of Pregnancy (AFLP)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Rare, life-threatening** liver disease in third trimester | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/liver-disease-in-pregnancy |
| **Presentation** | **Nausea/Vomiting**, **Abdominal pain**, **Jaundice**, **Hypoglycemia**, **Coagulopathy**, **Encephalopathy** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/liver-disease-in-pregnancy |
| **Diagnosis** | **Swansea Criteria** (=6 criteria) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/liver-disease-in-pregnancy |
| **Management** | **Prompt delivery** (regardless of gestational age) + **Supportive care** (correct hypoglycemia, coagulopathy) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2019/02/liver-disease-in-pregnancy |
