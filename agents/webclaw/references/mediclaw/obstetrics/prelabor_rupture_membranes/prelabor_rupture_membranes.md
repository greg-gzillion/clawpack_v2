# Obstetrics - Prelabor Rupture of Membranes

## Definitions
| Term | Definition | URL |
|------|------------|-----|
| **PROM** | **Rupture of membranes before onset of labor** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **Preterm PROM (PPROM)** | PROM at **<37 weeks** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **Prolonged PROM** | **>18 hours** between ROM and delivery | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **Latency Period** | Time from **ROM to delivery** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |

## Diagnosis of PROM
| Method | Findings | URL |
|--------|----------|-----|
| **Sterile Speculum Exam** | **Pooling** of amniotic fluid, **Ferning** on slide, **Nitrazine test** (blue = alkaline) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **Amnisure / ROM Plus** | **Placental Alpha Microglobulin-1 (PAMG-1)** or **Insulin-like Growth Factor Binding Protein-1 (IGFBP-1)** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **Ultrasound** | **Oligohydramnios** (supportive, not diagnostic) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **Avoid Digital Exam** | **? Infection risk**, Shortens latency | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |

## Management of Term PROM (=37 weeks)
| Approach | Recommendation | URL |
|----------|----------------|-----|
| **Immediate Induction** | **Preferred** (reduces infection, NICU admissions) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **Expectant Management** | **12-24 hours** if patient desires; **GBS prophylaxis** if indicated | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **GBS Prophylaxis** | If **GBS positive**, **Unknown status with risk factors** (PTB, PROM >18h, Fever), or **Prior GBS infant** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |

## Management of Preterm PROM (24-33 6/7 weeks)
| Gestational Age | Management | URL |
|-----------------|------------|-----|
| **<24 weeks** | **Expectant management** OR **Induction** (counsel on poor prognosis) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **24-33 6/7 weeks** | **Expectant management** (unless infection, Abruption, Non-reassuring fetal status) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **34-36 6/7 weeks** | **Delivery** (no clear benefit to expectant management) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |

## PPROM Expectant Management Components
| Intervention | Recommendation | URL |
|--------------|----------------|-----|
| **Corticosteroids** | **Betamethasone or Dexamethasone** (24-34 weeks) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **Antibiotics** | **Latency antibiotics** to prolong pregnancy: **Ampicillin 2g IV q6h + Azithromycin 1g PO x1** (or 500 mg IV) ? **Amoxicillin 250 mg PO q8h** x5 days | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **Magnesium Sulfate** | **Fetal neuroprotection** if <32 weeks and delivery anticipated | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **GBS Prophylaxis** | If **GBS positive**, **Unknown status**, or **Prior GBS infant** | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |
| **Monitoring** | **Maternal**: Fever, Tachycardia, Uterine tenderness, Purulent discharge; **Fetal**: NST, BPP | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2020/03/prelabor-rupture-of-membranes |

## Chorioamnionitis (Intraamniotic Infection)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Diagnostic Criteria** | **Fever =39.0°C once** OR **Fever =38.0°C + =1 of**: **Maternal tachycardia** (>100), **Fetal tachycardia** (>160), **Uterine tenderness**, **Purulent discharge**, **Leukocytosis** (>15,000) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/08/intrapartum-management-of-intraamniotic-infection |
| **Treatment** | **Ampicillin 2g IV q6h + Gentamicin 1.5 mg/kg q8h** (or 5 mg/kg daily) ± **Clindamycin** or **Metronidazole** (if cesarean) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/08/intrapartum-management-of-intraamniotic-infection |
| **Delivery** | **Prompt delivery** (regardless of gestational age) | https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/practice-bulletin/articles/2017/08/intrapartum-management-of-intraamniotic-infection |
