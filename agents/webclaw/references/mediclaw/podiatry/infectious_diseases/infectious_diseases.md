# Podiatry - Infectious Diseases of the Foot

## Bacterial Infections
| Infection | Pathogens | Clinical Features | Treatment | URL |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|-----------|-----|
| **Cellulitis** | **S. aureus**, **S. pyogenes** | **Erythema**, **Warmth**, **Edema**, **Pain** | **Cephalexin**, **Clindamycin**, **TMP-SMX** (if MRSA) | https://www.cdc.gov/groupastrep/diseases-hcp/cellulitis.html |
| **Erysipelas** | **S. pyogenes** | **Sharply demarcated**, **Raised**, **Indurated** | **Penicillin**, **Cephalexin** | https://www.cdc.gov/groupastrep/diseases-hcp/erysipelas.html |
| **Paronychia** | **S. aureus**, **Streptococcus**, **Anaerobes** | **Painful swelling** of **nail fold** | **Warm soaks**, **Incision and drainage**, **Antibiotics** | https://www.aad.org/public/diseases/a-z/paronychia |
| **Puncture Wound Infection** | **Pseudomonas** (sneaker), **S. aureus** | **Pain**, **Swelling**, **Drainage** | **Antibiotics** (ciprofloxacin for Pseudomonas), **Tetanus prophylaxis** | https://www.acfas.org/footankleinfo/Puncture-Wounds.htm |
| **Diabetic Foot Infection** | **Polymicrobial** (aerobes + anaerobes) | **Ulcer with purulence**, **Erythema**, **Probing to bone** | **Broad-spectrum antibiotics**, **Debridement**, **Assess for osteomyelitis** | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/diabetic-foot-infections/ |

## Fungal Infections (Tinea Pedis)
| Type | Characteristics | Treatment | URL |
|------|-----------------|-----------|-----|
| **Interdigital** | **Maceration**, **Scaling**, **Fissures** between toes (most common) | **Topical antifungals** (clotrimazole, terbinafine) | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/diseases/ringworm/index.html |
| **Moccasin** | **Diffuse scaling**, **Hyperkeratosis** of soles | **Topical antifungals** ｱ **Oral terbinafine** | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/diseases/ringworm/index.html |
| **Vesiculobullous** | **Vesicles/bullae** on instep | **Topical antifungals**, **Burrow's solution** | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/diseases/ringworm/index.html |
| **Ulcerative** | **Ulcerations**, **Secondary bacterial infection** | **Antifungals + Antibiotics**, **Wound care** | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/diseases/ringworm/index.html |

## Osteomyelitis of the Foot
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Etiology** | **Contiguous spread** (most common - diabetic foot ulcer), **Hematogenous**, **Post-traumatic/Post-surgical** | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vertebral-osteomyelitis-and-discitis/ |
| **Common Pathogens** | **S. aureus** (most common), **Polymicrobial** (diabetic foot) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vertebral-osteomyelitis-and-discitis/ |
| **Diagnosis** | **Probe-to-bone test**, **MRI** (gold standard), **Bone biopsy** (definitive), **X-ray** (late findings) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vertebral-osteomyelitis-and-discitis/ |
| **Treatment** | **IV antibiotics** (6 weeks), **Surgical debridement** (often required) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vertebral-osteomyelitis-and-discitis/ |
