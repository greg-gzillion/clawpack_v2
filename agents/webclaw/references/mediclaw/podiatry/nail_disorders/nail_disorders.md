# Podiatry - Nail Disorders

## Onychomycosis (Fungal Nail Infection)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Pathogens** | **Trichophyton rubrum** (most common), T. mentagrophytes, Candida | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/nail-infections.html |
| **Subtypes** | **Distal lateral subungual** (most common), **Superficial white**, **Proximal subungual**, **Candidal**, **Total dystrophic** | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/nail-infections.html |
| **Diagnosis** | **KOH preparation**, **Fungal culture**, **PAS stain**, **PCR** | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/nail-infections.html |
| **Oral Treatment** | **Terbinafine** 250 mg daily x12 weeks (toenails), x6 weeks (fingernails); **Itraconazole** (pulse dosing) | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/nail-infections.html |
| **Topical Treatment** | **Efinaconazole 10%**, **Tavaborole 5%**, **Ciclopirox 8%** (lower efficacy) | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/nail-infections.html |
| **Laser Therapy** | **Limited evidence**; May be adjunctive | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/nail-infections.html |

## Other Nail Disorders
| Condition | Description | Treatment | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|-----|
| **Onychocryptosis (Ingrown Toenail)** | **Nail plate penetrates lateral nail fold** | **Conservative** (cotton wick, Gutter splint), **Partial nail avulsion** ± **Chemical matrixectomy** (phenol, NaOH) | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2019/0801/p158.html |
| **Onychogryphosis (Ram's Horn Nail)** | **Thickened, curved nail** (often great toe) | **Debridement**, **Nail avulsion** ± **Matrixectomy** | https://www.apma.org/Patients/content.cfm?ItemNumber=992 |
| **Subungual Hematoma** | **Blood under nail** from trauma | **Observation** (<25% nail), **Trephination** (painful, >25-50%), **Nail removal** (if nail plate disrupted) | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2019/0801/p158.html |
| **Subungual Exostosis** | **Benign bony growth** under nail | **Surgical excision** | https://www.apma.org/Patients/content.cfm?ItemNumber=992 |
| **Paronychia** | **Infection of nail fold** (acute: S. aureus; chronic: Candida) | **Acute**: Warm soaks, I&D, Antibiotics; **Chronic**: Avoid moisture, Topical antifungals, Marsupialization | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2017/0701/p44.html |
| **Nail Psoriasis** | **Pitting**, **Oil spots**, **Onycholysis**, **Subungual hyperkeratosis** | **Topical corticosteroids**, **Vitamin D analogs**, **Systemic therapy** | https://www.psoriasis.org/nails/ |
| **Melanonychia** | **Brown/black pigmentation** of nail | **Biopsy** if concerning features (wide, Irregular, Hutchinson sign) | https://www.aad.org/public/diseases/a-z/melanonychia |
