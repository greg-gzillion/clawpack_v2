# Podiatry - Infections in Podiatry

## Soft Tissue Infections
| Infection | Pathogens | Treatment | URL |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Cellulitis** | **S. aureus**, **S. pyogenes** | **Cephalexin**, **Clindamycin**, **TMP-SMX** (if MRSA) | https://www.cdc.gov/groupastrep/diseases-hcp/cellulitis.html |
| **Erysipelas** | **S. pyogenes** | **Penicillin**, **Cephalexin** | https://www.cdc.gov/groupastrep/diseases-hcp/erysipelas.html |
| **Paronychia** | **S. aureus** (acute), **Candida** (chronic) | **Warm soaks**, **I&D**, **Antibiotics** (acute); **Avoid moisture**, **Antifungals** (chronic) | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2017/0701/p44.html |
| **Felon** | **S. aureus** | **I&D** (through pulp) | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2017/0701/p44.html |
| **Necrotizing Fasciitis** | **Polymicrobial** or **S. pyogenes** | **Emergent surgical debridement**, **Broad-spectrum antibiotics** | https://www.cdc.gov/groupastrep/diseases-hcp/necrotizing-fasciitis.html |
| **Gas Gangrene** | **Clostridium perfringens** | **Emergent surgical debridement**, **Penicillin + Clindamycin**, **HBOT** | https://www.cdc.gov/clostridium-perfringens/index.html |

## Bone and Joint Infections
| Infection | Pathogens | Diagnosis | Treatment | URL |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Osteomyelitis** | **S. aureus** (most common), **Pseudomonas** (puncture wounds), **Polymicrobial** (diabetic foot) | **MRI** (gold standard), **Bone biopsy** (definitive) | **IV antibiotics** (4-6 weeks), **Surgical debridement** | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vertebral-osteomyelitis-and-discitis/ |
| **Septic Arthritis** | **S. aureus**, **Neisseria gonorrhoeae** (young, sexually active) | **Joint aspiration** (WBC >50,000, PMN >75%), **Gram stain/culture** | **Emergent surgical drainage**, **IV antibiotics** | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/native-joint-infections/ |

## Puncture Wounds
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Common Pathogens** | **S. aureus**, **Pseudomonas aeruginosa** (especially through sneakers) | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2020/0801/p177.html |
| **Retained Foreign Body** | **X-ray** (radiopaque), **Ultrasound** (radiolucent) | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2020/0801/p177.html |
| **Prophylactic Antibiotics** | **Not routinely recommended**; Consider for **immunocompromised**, **Deep/contaminated wounds** | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2020/0801/p177.html |
| **Tetanus Prophylaxis** | **Tdap** if >5 years since last dose (contaminated wound) | https://www.cdc.gov/vaccines/vpd/tetanus/index.html |

## Fungal Infections
| Infection | Pathogens | Treatment | URL |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Tinea Pedis** | **Trichophyton rubrum** | **Topical antifungals** (clotrimazole, terbinafine), **Oral terbinafine** (severe) | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/diseases/ringworm/index.html |
| **Onychomycosis** | **T. rubrum**, **T. mentagrophytes** | **Oral terbinafine**, **Topical efinaconazole/tavaborole** | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/nail-infections.html |
| **Candidiasis** | **Candida albicans** | **Topical nystatin**, **Clotrimazole**, **Keep interdigital spaces dry** | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/diseases/candidiasis/index.html |
