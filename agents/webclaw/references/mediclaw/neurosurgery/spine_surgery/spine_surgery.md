## Surgical Approaches to the Spine
| Approach | Indications | Advantages | Disadvantages | URL |
|----------|-------------|------------|---------------|-----|
| **Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF)** | Central/paracentral disc, Myelopathy, Radiculopathy | Direct decompression, Restores lordosis | Dysphagia, Recurrent laryngeal nerve injury, Adjacent segment disease | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Anterior-Cervical-Discectomy-and-Fusion |
| **Posterior Cervical Laminectomy/Fusion** | Multilevel stenosis, Lordotic spine | Avoids anterior structures | Posterior neck pain, C5 palsy | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Posterior-Cervical-Laminectomy-and-Fusion |
| **Lumbar Microdiscectomy** | Disc herniation with radiculopathy | Minimally invasive, Preserves motion | Recurrent herniation, Dural tear | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Lumbar-Discectomy |
| **Lumbar Laminectomy** | Central stenosis, Neurogenic claudication | Decompresses neural elements | Instability (may require fusion), Dural tear | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Lumbar-Laminectomy |
| **Transforaminal Lumbar Interbody Fusion (TLIF)** | Spondylolisthesis, Degenerative disc disease, Foraminal stenosis | Circumferential fusion, Restores disc height | Nerve root injury, Pseudarthrosis | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Lumbar-Fusion |
