## aSAH Complications
| Complication | Timing | Prevention/Treatment | URL |
|--------------|--------|---------------------|-----|
| **Rebleeding** | **First 24-72h** (highest risk) | **Early aneurysm securement** (clipping or coiling), **BP control** (SBP <160) | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Cerebral-Aneurysm |
| **Vasospasm** | **Days 4-14** (peak day 7) | **Nimodipine** 60 mg q4h x21 days (improves outcomes, does not prevent vasospasm), **Euvolemia**, **Induced hypertension** | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Cerebral-Aneurysm |
| **Hydrocephalus** | Acute or chronic | **EVD** (acute), **Ventriculoperitoneal shunt** (chronic) | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Cerebral-Aneurysm |
| **Hyponatremia** | Days 2-10 | **Cerebral salt wasting** (volume depletion) vs **SIADH** (euvolemic); Fluid restriction ONLY for SIADH | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Cerebral-Aneurysm |
| **Seizures** | Acute | Prophylactic antiepileptics (controversial); Treat if occurs | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Cerebral-Aneurysm |

## Aneurysm Treatment
| Method | Indications | Advantages | Disadvantages | URL |
|--------|-------------|------------|---------------|-----|
| **Microsurgical Clipping** | **Younger patients**, **MCA aneurysms**, **Wide-neck aneurysms**, **Hematoma requiring evacuation** | **Durable**, Lower retreatment rate, Immediate occlusion | **Craniotomy**, Longer recovery | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Cerebral-Aneurysm-Clipping |
| **Endovascular Coiling** | **Older patients**, **Posterior circulation**, **Basilar apex**, **Poor surgical candidates** | **Less invasive**, Shorter recovery | **Higher retreatment rate**, Requires long-term follow-up imaging | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Cerebral-Aneurysm-Coiling |
| **Flow Diversion** | **Large/giant aneurysms**, **Fusiform aneurysms** | Reconstructs parent vessel | **Dual antiplatelet therapy required**, Delayed aneurysm occlusion | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Cerebral-Aneurysm-Flow-Diversion |
| **ISAT Trial** | Coiling vs Clipping for ruptured aneurysms | **Coiling showed better 1-year outcomes** (mRS 0-2); Similar long-term mortality | https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736(05)67214-5/fulltext |
| **BRAT Trial** | Clipping vs Coiling | **No significant difference** in outcomes at 10 years | https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamaneurology/fullarticle/2734811 |

## Arteriovenous Malformation (AVM)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | Congenital tangle of abnormal arteries and veins **without intervening capillary bed** | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Arteriovenous-Malformation |
| **Presentation** | **Hemorrhage** (50%), **Seizure** (25%), **Headache**, **Focal neurologic deficit** | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Arteriovenous-Malformation |
| **Annual Hemorrhage Risk** | **2-4%** per year (unruptured); **4.5-34%** for ruptured | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Arteriovenous-Malformation |
| **Spetzler-Martin Grading** | **Size** (<3cm=1, 3-6cm=2, >6cm=3), **Eloquence** (non-eloquent=0, eloquent=1), **Venous drainage** (superficial=0, deep=1) | https://www.mdcalc.com/calc/10430/spetzler-martin-avm-grade |
| **ARUBA Trial** | **Medical management superior to intervention** for unruptured AVMs (controversial, limited follow-up) | https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736(13)62302-8/fulltext |
| **Treatment** | **Microsurgical resection**, **Stereotactic radiosurgery**, **Endovascular embolization**, **Multimodal** | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Arteriovenous-Malformation |

## Cavernous Malformation
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Pathology** | **Mulberry-like** cluster of **thin-walled sinusoidal vessels** without intervening brain parenchyma | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Cavernous-Malformation |
| **MRI Appearance** | **"Popcorn" appearance** with **hemosiderin ring** (T2 GRE) | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Cavernous-Malformation |
| **Annual Hemorrhage Risk** | **0.6-1.1%** (non-brainstem); **2.5-4.1%** (brainstem); **Higher** if prior hemorrhage or **familial** (CCM1-3 mutations) | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Cavernous-Malformation |
| **Indications for Surgery** | **Recurrent hemorrhage**, **Progressive neurologic deficit**, **Medically refractory seizures**, **Brainstem lesions** with accessible pial surface | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Cavernous-Malformation |
| **Familial Syndromes** | **CCM1 (KRIT1)** , **CCM2**, **CCM3 (PDCD10)** ; **Multiple lesions**, **Autosomal dominant** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK537247/ |

## Carotid Stenosis
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | Atherosclerotic narrowing of carotid artery | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Carotid-Artery-Stenosis |
| **Symptomatic** | **TIA or stroke** within 6 months | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Carotid-Artery-Stenosis |
| **Asymptomatic** | No symptoms | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Carotid-Artery-Stenosis |
| **NASCET Trial** | **CEA superior to medical therapy** for **symptomatic 50-69% stenosis** (moderate benefit) and **=70% stenosis** (substantial benefit) | https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJM199108153250701 |
| **ACAS/ACST Trials** | **CEA superior to medical therapy** for **asymptomatic =60% stenosis** (modest benefit) | https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/388344 |
| **CREST Trial** | **CEA vs CAS**: **No difference** in composite outcome; **CEA**: More MI, Cranial nerve injury; **CAS**: More stroke (especially in elderly) | https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa0912321 |
| **Treatment** | **Carotid Endarterectomy (CEA)** or **Carotid Artery Stenting (CAS)** + **Antiplatelet + Statin + Risk factor modification** | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Carotid-Artery-Stenosis |
