## Brain Abscess
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Source** | **Contiguous spread** (sinusitis, otitis, dental), **Hematogenous**, **Trauma/Post-neurosurgical**, **Cryptogenic** | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/healthcare-associated-ventriculitis-and-meningitis/ |
| **Common Pathogens** | **Streptococcus** (aerobic and anaerobic), **Staphylococcus**, **Gram-negatives**, **Toxoplasma** (HIV), **Nocardia** (immunocompromised) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/healthcare-associated-ventriculitis-and-meningitis/ |
| **Imaging** | **Ring-enhancing lesion** with **restricted diffusion** (DWI) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/healthcare-associated-ventriculitis-and-meningitis/ |
| **Stages** | **Early cerebritis** (days 1-3) ? **Late cerebritis** (days 4-9) ? **Early capsule** (days 10-13) ? **Late capsule** (=14 days) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/healthcare-associated-ventriculitis-and-meningitis/ |
| **Treatment** | **Empiric antibiotics** (Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole ± Vancomycin) + **Stereotactic aspiration or excision** (if >2.5 cm or mass effect) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/healthcare-associated-ventriculitis-and-meningitis/ |

## Spinal Epidural Abscess
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Risk Factors** | **IV drug use**, **Diabetes**, **Immunocompromise**, **Spinal instrumentation**, **Hemodialysis** | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vertebral-osteomyelitis-and-discitis/ |
| **Pathogens** | **S. aureus** (most common), Gram-negatives (IVDU), Coagulase-negative Staph (post-op) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vertebral-osteomyelitis-and-discitis/ |
| **Symptoms** | **Back pain**, **Fever**, **Neurologic deficit** (weakness, sensory loss, Bowel/bladder) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vertebral-osteomyelitis-and-discitis/ |
| **Diagnosis** | **MRI with contrast** (gold standard), ESR/CRP, Blood cultures | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vertebral-osteomyelitis-and-discitis/ |
| **Treatment** | **Emergent surgical decompression** if neurologic deficit OR **IV antibiotics alone** if no deficit and responding | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vertebral-osteomyelitis-and-discitis/ |

## Postoperative Infections
| Infection | Timing | Pathogens | Management | URL |
|-----------|--------|-----------|------------|-----|
| **Superficial Wound Infection** | POD 3-7 | **S. aureus**, S. epidermidis | **Antibiotics** ± Wound drainage | https://www.cdc.gov/infection-control/hcp/ssi/index.html |
| **Bone Flap Osteomyelitis** | Weeks-months | **S. aureus**, **Cutibacterium acnes** (indolent) | **Bone flap removal** + Antibiotics + **Delayed cranioplasty** | https://www.cdc.gov/infection-control/hcp/ssi/index.html |
| **Post-Craniotomy Meningitis** | POD 3-7 | **S. aureus**, Gram-negatives | **IV antibiotics** (penetrate CSF) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/healthcare-associated-ventriculitis-and-meningitis/ |
| **Ventriculostomy-Related Infection** | >5 days of EVD | **S. epidermidis**, **S. aureus** | **Remove EVD** + Antibiotics ± New EVD | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/healthcare-associated-ventriculitis-and-meningitis/ |
| **Shunt Infection** | <2 months post-op | **S. epidermidis**, **S. aureus** | **Shunt externalization/removal + EVD + Antibiotics** | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/healthcare-associated-ventriculitis-and-meningitis/ |
