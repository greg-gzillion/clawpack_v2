## Chiari Malformations
| Type | Definition | Associated Conditions | Treatment | URL |
|------|------------|----------------------|-----------|-----|
| **Chiari I** | **Cerebellar tonsillar descent =5mm below foramen magnum** | **Syringomyelia** (50-75%), **Scoliosis** | **Posterior fossa decompression** if symptomatic or syrinx | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Chiari-Malformation |
| **Chiari II** | **Cerebellar vermis + Brainstem + Fourth ventricle descent** | **Myelomeningocele** (always), **Hydrocephalus** (90%) | **Myelomeningocele closure**, **VP shunt**, Posterior fossa decompression if symptomatic | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Chiari-Malformation |
| **Chiari III** | **Occipital encephalocele** containing cerebellum/brainstem | Severe neurologic deficits | Surgical closure of encephalocele | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Chiari-Malformation |
| **Chiari IV** | **Cerebellar hypoplasia/aplasia** | Severe developmental delay | Supportive | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Chiari-Malformation |
