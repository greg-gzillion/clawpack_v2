# Neurosurgery - Functional Neurosurgery

## Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS)
| Indication | Target | Effect | URL |
|------------|--------|--------|-----|
| **Parkinson Disease** | **Subthalamic nucleus (STN)** or **Globus pallidus internus (GPi)** | Improves tremor, Bradykinesia, Rigidity, Dyskinesia | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Deep-Brain-Stimulation |
| **Essential Tremor** | **Ventral intermediate nucleus (VIM)** of thalamus | **Marked improvement in tremor** | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Deep-Brain-Stimulation |
| **Dystonia** | **GPi** | Improves sustained muscle contractions | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Deep-Brain-Stimulation |
| **Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)** | **Ventral capsule/Ventral striatum** | Improves refractory OCD | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Deep-Brain-Stimulation |
| **Epilepsy** | **Anterior nucleus of thalamus (ANT)** | ? Seizure frequency | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Deep-Brain-Stimulation |

## Epilepsy Surgery
| Procedure | Indication | Description | URL |
|-----------|------------|-------------|-----|
| **Anterior Temporal Lobectomy** | **Mesial temporal sclerosis** | Most common and effective epilepsy surgery | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Epilepsy-Surgery |
| **Lesionectomy** | Focal lesion (tumor, cavernoma) | Resection of epileptogenic lesion | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Epilepsy-Surgery |
| **Corpus Callosotomy** | **Drop attacks** (atonic seizures) | Disconnects hemispheres to prevent seizure spread | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Epilepsy-Surgery |
| **Hemispherectomy** | **Rasmussen encephalitis**, **Hemimegalencephaly**, **Sturge-Weber** | Removal/disconnection of entire hemisphere (usually in children) | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Epilepsy-Surgery |
| **Responsive Neurostimulation (RNS)** | **Bitemporal or eloquent cortex** | Implanted device detects and interrupts seizures | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Epilepsy-Surgery |
| **Vagus Nerve Stimulation (VNS)** | Medically refractory epilepsy | Intermittent stimulation of left vagus nerve | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Epilepsy-Surgery |

## Trigeminal Neuralgia
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Type** | **Type 1**: >50% paroxysmal pain; **Type 2**: >50% constant pain | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Trigeminal-Neuralgia |
| **Characteristics** | **Lancinating**, **Electric shock-like** pain in **V2/V3** distribution, **Triggered by light touch** (chewing, talking, wind) | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Trigeminal-Neuralgia |
| **Medical Treatment** | **Carbamazepine** (first-line), Oxcarbazepine, Gabapentin, Baclofen | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Trigeminal-Neuralgia |
| **Surgical Options** | **Microvascular decompression** (younger, healthy), **Stereotactic radiosurgery**, **Percutaneous rhizotomy** (balloon compression, glycerol, radiofrequency) | https://www.aans.org/Patients/Neurosurgical-Conditions-and-Treatments/Trigeminal-Neuralgia |
