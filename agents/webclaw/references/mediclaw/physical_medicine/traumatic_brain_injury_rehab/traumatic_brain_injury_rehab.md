## Rancho Los Amigos Levels of Cognitive Functioning
| Level | Description | Management Approach | URL |
|-------|-------------|---------------------|-----|
| **I** | **No Response** | **Sensory stimulation** | https://www.rancho.org/research/rancho-levels-cognitive-functioning |
| **II** | **Generalized Response** | **Sensory stimulation**, **Range of motion** | https://www.rancho.org/research/rancho-levels-cognitive-functioning |
| **III** | **Localized Response** | **Consistent stimulation**, **Begin positioning** | https://www.rancho.org/research/rancho-levels-cognitive-functioning |
| **IV** | **Confused-Agitated** | **Structure**, **Redirect**, **Safety**, **Low stimulation environment** | https://www.rancho.org/research/rancho-levels-cognitive-functioning |
| **V** | **Confused-Inappropriate, Non-Agitated** | **Structure**, **Cueing**, **Simple tasks** | https://www.rancho.org/research/rancho-levels-cognitive-functioning |
| **VI** | **Confused-Appropriate** | **Supervision**, **Routine**, **Compensatory strategies** | https://www.rancho.org/research/rancho-levels-cognitive-functioning |
| **VII** | **Automatic-Appropriate** | **Independence with structure**, **Community outings** | https://www.rancho.org/research/rancho-levels-cognitive-functioning |
| **VIII** | **Purposeful-Appropriate** | **Community reintegration**, **Vocational training** | https://www.rancho.org/research/rancho-levels-cognitive-functioning |

## Common TBI Sequelae and Rehabilitation
| Impairment | Intervention | URL |
|------------|--------------|-----|
| **Cognitive Deficits** (attention, memory, executive function) | **Cognitive rehabilitation**, **Compensatory strategies** (external aids, Routines), **Environmental modifications** | https://www.biausa.org/brain-injury/about-brain-injury/rehabilitation |
| **Behavioral Dysregulation** | **Behavioral management**, **Structure/Routine**, **Medications** (SSRI, Mood stabilizers, Antipsychotics - caution) | https://www.biausa.org/brain-injury/about-brain-injury/rehabilitation |
| **Post-Traumatic Amnesia (PTA)** | **Orientation**, **Structure**, **Avoid overstimulation** | https://www.biausa.org/brain-injury/about-brain-injury/rehabilitation |
| **Headache** | **Medications** (avoid opioids), **PT**, **Trigger management** | https://www.biausa.org/brain-injury/about-brain-injury/rehabilitation |
| **Sleep Disturbance** | **Sleep hygiene**, **Melatonin**, **Trazodone**, **Avoid benzodiazepines** | https://www.biausa.org/brain-injury/about-brain-injury/rehabilitation |
| **Heterotopic Ossification (HO)** | **Early ROM**, **Etidronate**, **NSAIDs**, **Surgical excision** (mature) | https://www.aapmr.org/about-physiatry/conditions-treatments/rehabilitation/traumatic-brain-injury |
| **Paroxysmal Sympathetic Hyperactivity (PSH)** | **Benzodiazepines**, **Beta-blockers**, **Gabapentin**, **Baclofen**, **Clonidine**, **Bromocriptine** | https://www.aapmr.org/about-physiatry/conditions-treatments/rehabilitation/traumatic-brain-injury |

## Disorders of Consciousness
| Condition | Features | Management | URL |
|-----------|----------|------------|-----|
| **Coma** | **No eye opening**, **No command following**, **No verbalization** | **Medical stabilization**, **Prevent complications** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/coma |
| **Vegetative State** | **Eye opening**, **Sleep-wake cycles**, **No awareness** | **Sensory stimulation**, **Prevent complications** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/vegetative-state |
| **Minimally Conscious State** | **Inconsistent but reproducible evidence of awareness** | **Intensive rehabilitation**, **Amantadine** (improves recovery) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/minimally-conscious-state |
