## Oral Antispasticity Medications
| Medication | Dose | Mechanism | Adverse Effects | URL |
|------------|------|-----------|-----------------|-----|
| **Baclofen** | 5-80 mg/day | **GABA-B agonist** | **Sedation**, **Weakness**, **Withdrawal** (seizures, Hallucinations) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |
| **Tizanidine** | 2-36 mg/day | **Alpha-2 agonist** | **Sedation**, **Hypotension**, **Dry mouth**, **Hepatotoxicity** (monitor LFTs) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |
| **Dantrolene** | 25-400 mg/day | **Ryanodine receptor antagonist** (peripheral) | **Weakness**, **Hepatotoxicity** (monitor LFTs) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |
| **Gabapentin** | 300-3,600 mg/day | **Calcium channel modulation** | **Sedation**, **Dizziness**, **Peripheral edema** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |

## Intrathecal Baclofen (ITB)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Indications** | **Severe generalized spasticity** (SCI, TBI, MS, CP) refractory to oral medications | https://www.aapmr.org/about-physiatry/conditions-treatments/rehabilitation/spasticity |
| **Screening Trial** | **Single ITB bolus** via lumbar puncture (assess response) | https://www.aapmr.org/about-physiatry/conditions-treatments/rehabilitation/spasticity |
| **Pump Placement** | **Subcutaneous abdominal pocket** with **intrathecal catheter** | https://www.aapmr.org/about-physiatry/conditions-treatments/rehabilitation/spasticity |
| **Dosing** | **Continuous infusion** (1/100th of oral dose) | https://www.aapmr.org/about-physiatry/conditions-treatments/rehabilitation/spasticity |
| **Withdrawal** | **Life-threatening** (seizures, Rhabdomyolysis, Multiorgan failure) - **Never abruptly stop** | https://www.aapmr.org/about-physiatry/conditions-treatments/rehabilitation/spasticity |
