# Asthma

## Definition
Chronic inflammatory airway disease with reversible airflow obstruction, bronchial hyperresponsiveness

## Classification
- Intermittent: symptoms ≤2 days/week, night awakenings ≤2/month
- Mild persistent: symptoms >2 days/week, night 3-4/month
- Moderate persistent: daily symptoms, night ≥1/week
- Severe persistent: continuous symptoms, frequent night

## Pathophysiology
- Airway inflammation (eosinophils, mast cells, Th2 cells)
- Bronchoconstriction
- Mucus hypersecretion
- Airway remodeling (chronic)

## Triggers
- Allergens (pollen, dust mites, pet dander)
- Respiratory infections (viral)
- Exercise (especially cold air)
- Irritants (smoke, pollution, strong odors)
- Medications (NSAIDs, beta-blockers)
- GERD
- Stress

## Symptoms
- Wheezing
- Dyspnea
- Chest tightness
- Cough (nocturnal, exercise-induced)

## Diagnosis
- Spirometry (pre/post bronchodilator)
  - FEV1/FVC <0.70 (obstruction)
  - FEV1 increase ≥12% and ≥200mL (reversibility)
- Bronchial challenge testing (methacholine, exercise)
- FeNO (elevated in eosinophilic inflammation)
- Peak flow monitoring (variability >20%)

## Treatment

### Stepwise approach (GINA)

**Step 1 (intermittent):**
- Low-dose ICS-formoterol as needed (preferred)
- or SABA as needed (albuterol)

**Step 2 (mild persistent):**
- Low-dose ICS daily + as-needed SABA

**Step 3 (moderate persistent):**
- Low-dose ICS-LABA daily
- or medium-dose ICS

**Step 4 (severe persistent):**
- Medium-dose ICS-LABA
- Add-on: tiotropium, anti-IgE (omalizumab), anti-IL5 (mepolizumab), anti-IL4/13 (dupilumab)

**Step 5 (refractory):**
- High-dose ICS-LABA + add-on therapy
- Oral corticosteroids (lowest effective dose)
- Bronchial thermoplasty (selected patients)

### Acute exacerbation

**Mild-moderate:**
- SABA 4-8 puffs every 20 min (first hour)
- Oral corticosteroids (prednisone 40-50mg x 5-7 days)

**Severe (ED setting):**
- Oxygen (goal SpO2 93-95%)
- Nebulized SABA + ipratropium
- IV corticosteroids (methylprednisolone 125mg)
- Magnesium sulfate IV
- Consider non-invasive ventilation

## Monitoring
- Symptom control (ACT score)
- Spirometry annually
- Inhaler technique check
- Exacerbation frequency
- Medication adherence
