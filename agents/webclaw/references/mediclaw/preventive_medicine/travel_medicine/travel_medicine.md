## Travel Vaccines (CDC Yellow Book)
| Vaccine | Indication | Schedule | URL |
|---------|------------|----------|-----|
| **Hepatitis A** | **Most travelers** | 2 doses (0, 6-12 months) | https://www.cdc.gov/vaccines/vpd/hepa/index.html |
| **Typhoid** | South Asia, Africa, Latin America | IM once (booster q2y) or PO (booster q5y) | https://www.cdc.gov/typhoid-fever/index.html |
| **Yellow Fever** | Sub-Saharan Africa, South America | Once (lifelong for most) | https://www.cdc.gov/yellowfever/index.html |
| **Japanese Encephalitis** | Rural Asia, =1 month stay | 2 doses (0, 28 days) | https://www.cdc.gov/japaneseencephalitis/index.html |
| **Meningococcal** | **Hajj/Umrah**, Meningitis belt | MenACWY once | https://www.cdc.gov/meningococcal/index.html |
| **Rabies** | High-risk activities, Remote areas | 2 doses (0, 7 days) pre-exposure | https://www.cdc.gov/rabies/index.html |
| **Cholera** | Humanitarian workers | Single dose oral (CVD 103-HgR) | https://www.cdc.gov/cholera/index.html |
| **Polio** | Endemic countries (Afghanistan, Pakistan) | Single booster for adults | https://www.cdc.gov/polio/index.html |

## Malaria Prophylaxis
| Drug | Regimen | Notes | URL |
|------|---------|-------|-----|
| **Atovaquone-Proguanil (Malarone)** | Daily, Start 1-2d before, Continue 7d after | **Best tolerated**, Daily dosing | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/travelers/drugs.html |
| **Doxycycline** | Daily, Start 1-2d before, Continue 4 weeks after | **Photosensitivity**, GI upset, Vaginal yeast | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/travelers/drugs.html |
| **Mefloquine** | Weekly, Start 2 weeks before, Continue 4 weeks after | **Neuropsychiatric effects**, Contraindicated in psychiatric/seizure history | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/travelers/drugs.html |
| **Primaquine** | Daily, Start 1-2d before, Continue 7d after | Requires **G6PD testing** (hemolysis if deficient) | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/travelers/drugs.html |
| **Tafenoquine (Arakoda)** | Weekly, Start 3d before, Weekly during, Single dose week after | Requires **G6PD testing** | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/travelers/drugs.html |

## Traveler's Diarrhea
| Severity | Treatment | URL |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| **Mild** (1-2 loose stools/24h, No distressing symptoms) | **Loperamide** PRN, Hydration | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Moderate** (Distressing symptoms) | **Loperamide** + **Antibiotic** (azithromycin preferred) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Severe** (Dysentery, Fever) | **Antibiotic** (azithromycin), Avoid loperamide | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |
| **Antibiotic Options** | **Azithromycin** 500 mg daily x1-3d (preferred), **Ciprofloxacin** 500 mg BID x1-3d, **Rifaximin** 200 mg TID x3d (non-invasive only) | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/travelers-diarrhea |

## Altitude Illness
| Condition | Symptoms | Management | URL |
|-----------|----------|------------|-----|
| **Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS)** | Headache + =1: Nausea, Fatigue, Dizziness, Insomnia | **Stop ascent**, Acetazolamide, Dexamethasone (severe), **Descend if worsening** | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/altitude-illness |
| **High-Altitude Cerebral Edema (HACE)** | **Ataxia**, Confusion, Altered mental status | **Immediate descent**, Dexamethasone, Oxygen | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/altitude-illness |
| **High-Altitude Pulmonary Edema (HAPE)** | **Dyspnea at rest**, Dry cough, Crackles | **Immediate descent**, Nifedipine, Oxygen | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/altitude-illness |
| **Prophylaxis** | **Acetazolamide** 125 mg BID starting day before ascent | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/altitude-illness |
