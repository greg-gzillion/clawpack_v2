# Urology - Kidney Cancer (Renal Cell Carcinoma)

## RCC - Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Epidemiology** | 3% of adult malignancies; Male > Female (2:1) | https://www.cancer.gov/types/kidney |
| **Risk Factors** | **Smoking**, **Obesity**, **Hypertension**, **VHL disease**, **Hereditary papillary RCC**, **Birt-Hogg-Dubé** | https://www.cancer.gov/types/kidney |
| **Classic Triad** | **Flank pain**, **Hematuria**, **Palpable mass** (<10%) | https://www.cancer.gov/types/kidney |

## RCC - Histologic Subtypes
| Subtype | Frequency | Genetics | Prognosis | URL |
|---------|-----------|----------|-----------|-----|
| **Clear Cell RCC** | 75% | **VHL** (3p25) | Intermediate | https://www.cancer.gov/types/kidney |
| **Papillary RCC** | 15% | MET, Hereditary papillary RCC | Favorable (type 1), Worse (type 2) | https://www.cancer.gov/types/kidney |
| **Chromophobe RCC** | 5% | Multiple chromosomal losses | **Favorable** | https://www.cancer.gov/types/kidney |
| **Collecting Duct RCC** | <1% | - | **Poor** | https://www.cancer.gov/types/kidney |
| **Renal Medullary Carcinoma** | <1% | **Sickle cell trait** | **Very poor** | https://www.cancer.gov/types/kidney |

## Localized RCC - Treatment
| Tumor Size | Treatment | URL |
|------------|-----------|-----|
| **<3 cm** | **Active surveillance** (elderly, Comorbidities), **Partial nephrectomy**, **Thermal ablation** (RFA, Cryo) | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/kidney |
| **3-7 cm** | **Partial nephrectomy** (preferred), Radical nephrectomy, Thermal ablation | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/kidney |
| **>7 cm** | **Radical nephrectomy** | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/kidney |

## Advanced RCC - Systemic Therapy (IMDC Risk Groups)
| Risk Group | First-Line Therapy | URL |
|------------|---------------------|-----|
| **Favorable Risk** | **ICI + TKI** (Pembrolizumab + Axitinib, Pembrolizumab + Lenvatinib, Nivolumab + Cabozantinib) or **TKI alone** | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/kidney |
| **Intermediate/Poor Risk** | **ICI + TKI** or **IO/IO** (Nivolumab + Ipilimumab) | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/kidney |
