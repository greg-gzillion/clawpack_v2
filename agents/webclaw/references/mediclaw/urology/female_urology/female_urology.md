## Overactive Bladder (OAB) / Urgency Incontinence
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Urinary urgency**, Usually with **frequency** and **nocturia**, With or without **urgency incontinence** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/bladder-control-problems-women |
| **Non-Pharmacologic** | **Bladder training**, **Pelvic floor PT**, **Fluid management**, **Avoid bladder irritants** | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/overactive-bladder |
| **Pharmacologic** | **Beta-3 agonists** (mirabegron, Vibegron - preferred in elderly), **Antimuscarinics** (oxybutynin - avoid elderly, Tolterodine, Solifenacin, Darifenacin, Trospium) | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/overactive-bladder |
| **Advanced** | **Percutaneous tibial nerve stimulation (PTNS)** , **Sacral neuromodulation** (InterStim), **Intradetrusor onabotulinumtoxinA (Botox)** | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/overactive-bladder |

## Interstitial Cystitis/Bladder Pain Syndrome (IC/BPS)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Chronic bladder pain/pressure** with urinary symptoms >6 weeks without identifiable cause | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/interstitial-cystitis-painful-bladder-syndrome |
| **Diagnosis** | **Clinical** (rule out infection, Malignancy); Cystoscopy with hydrodistention (Hunner lesions in 10-20%) | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/interstitial-cystitis |
| **Treatment** | **Multimodal**: Dietary modification, Pelvic floor PT, Amitriptyline, Pentosan polysulfate, Hydroxyzine, Bladder instillations (DMSO, Heparin, Lidocaine), Cyclosporine | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/interstitial-cystitis |
