# Urology - Nephrolithiasis (Kidney Stones)

## Kidney Stones - Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Epidemiology** | Lifetime prevalence 10-15%; Male > Female; 50% recurrence at 10 years | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/kidney-stones |
| **Stone Types** | **Calcium oxalate** (70-80%), **Calcium phosphate** (10-15%), **Uric acid** (5-10%), **Struvite** (5-10%), **Cystine** (1%) | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/kidney-stones |

## Acute Stone Episode - Management
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Imaging** | **Non-contrast CT** (gold standard), **Ultrasound** (pregnancy, Pediatrics) | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
| **Pain Control** | **NSAIDs** (ketorolac) first-line; **Opioids** if refractory | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
| **Medical Expulsive Therapy (MET)** | **Tamsulosin** 0.4 mg daily for **distal ureteral stones 5-10 mm** | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
| **Indications for Admission** | **Intractable pain**, **Nausea/Vomiting**, **Infection**, **AKI**, **Solitary kidney**, **Bilateral obstruction** | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |

## Surgical Management of Stones
| Procedure | Indications | Stone-Free Rate | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Shock Wave Lithotripsy (SWL)** | **Proximal ureteral <10 mm**, **Renal <20 mm** | 60-80% | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
| **Ureteroscopy with Laser Lithotripsy** | **Distal ureteral**, **SWL failures**, **Cystine stones** | 85-95% | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
| **Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy (PCNL)** | **Renal >20 mm**, **Staghorn**, **SWL/URS failures** | 90-95% | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |

## Stone-Specific Prevention
| Stone Type | Dietary/Lifestyle | Medications | URL |
|------------|------------------|-------------|-----|
| **Calcium Oxalate** | **High fluid** (>2.5 L/day), **Low sodium** (<2 g/day), **Normal calcium** (1000-1200 mg/day), **Low oxalate** | **Thiazide diuretics**, **Potassium citrate**, **Allopurinol** (if hyperuricosuria) | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/kidney-stones |
| **Uric Acid** | High fluid, Low purine, **Urinary alkalinization** (goal pH 6.5-7.0) | **Potassium citrate**, **Allopurinol** | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/kidney-stones |
| **Cystine** | **Very high fluid** (>3-4 L/day), **Urinary alkalinization** (pH >7.5) | **Potassium citrate**, **Tiopronin**, **D-Penicillamine** | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/kidney-stones |
