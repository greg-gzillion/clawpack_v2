# Anatomy - Overview

## What is Anatomy?
Anatomy is the branch of biology concerned with the study of the structure of organisms and their parts. Human anatomy is the scientific study of the morphology of the adult human body, divided into gross (macroscopic) anatomy and microscopic anatomy (histology).

## Subdivisions of Anatomy
| Subdivision | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Gross Anatomy | Study of anatomical structures visible to the naked eye |
| Neuroanatomy | Study of the nervous system structure |
| Histology | Microscopic study of tissues |
| Embryology | Study of prenatal development |
| Surface Anatomy | Study of external features and landmarks |
| Radiological Anatomy | Study of anatomy through imaging modalities |
| Clinical Anatomy | Application of anatomical knowledge to clinical practice |

## Anatomical Position and Planes
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Anatomical Position | Body erect, feet together, palms facing forward, thumbs outward |
| Sagittal Plane | Divides body into left and right |
| Coronal (Frontal) Plane | Divides body into anterior and posterior |
| Transverse (Axial) Plane | Divides body into superior and inferior |

## Directional Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Superior/Cranial | Toward the head |
| Inferior/Caudal | Toward the feet |
| Anterior/Ventral | Toward the front |
| Posterior/Dorsal | Toward the back |
| Medial | Toward the midline |
| Lateral | Away from the midline |
| Proximal | Closer to the trunk |
| Distal | Farther from the trunk |
| Superficial | Closer to the surface |
| Deep | Farther from the surface |
