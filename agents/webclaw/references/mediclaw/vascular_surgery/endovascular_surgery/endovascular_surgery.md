## Access Sites
| Site | Advantages | Disadvantages | URL |
|------|------------|---------------|-----|
| **Common Femoral Artery** | **Most common**, **Large caliber**, **Easy compression** | **Retroperitoneal bleeding**, **Pseudoaneurysm** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/endovascular-surgery |
| **Radial Artery** | **Lower bleeding risk**, **Early ambulation**, **Patient preference** | **Smaller caliber**, **Spasm**, **Learning curve** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/endovascular-surgery |
| **Brachial Artery** | **Alternative to femoral** | **Nerve injury**, **Hematoma** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/endovascular-surgery |
| **Popliteal Artery** | **Retrograde access** for SFA occlusions | **Prone position** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/endovascular-surgery |
| **Pedal Access** | **Retrograde access** for tibial occlusions | **Small caliber** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/endovascular-surgery |

## Access Complications
| Complication | Management | URL |
|--------------|------------|-----|
| **Hematoma** | **Manual compression**, **Observation**, **Evacuation** (expanding) | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/endovascular-surgery |
| **Pseudoaneurysm** | **Ultrasound-guided compression**, **Thrombin injection**, **Surgical repair** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/endovascular-surgery |
| **Arteriovenous Fistula** | **Observation** (most resolve), **Surgical repair** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/endovascular-surgery |
| **Retroperitoneal Hemorrhage** | **Fluid resuscitation**, **Transfusion**, **Covered stent** or **Surgical repair** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/endovascular-surgery |
| **Thrombosis** | **Thrombectomy** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/endovascular-surgery |
| **Dissection** | **Stenting** (if flow-limiting) | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/endovascular-surgery |

## Contrast-Induced Nephropathy (CIN)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **AKI** (? Cr =0.5 mg/dL or =25%) within **48-72 hours** of contrast | https://www.acr.org/Clinical-Resources/Contrast-Manual |
| **Risk Factors** | **CKD** (Cr >1.5, eGFR <60), **Diabetes**, **CHF**, **Volume depletion**, **High contrast volume** | https://www.acr.org/Clinical-Resources/Contrast-Manual |
| **Prevention** | **IV hydration** (NS 1 mL/kg/h x12h pre/post), **Minimize contrast**, **Hold nephrotoxins** | https://www.acr.org/Clinical-Resources/Contrast-Manual |
| **N-Acetylcysteine** | **Not recommended** (PRESERVE trial) | https://www.acr.org/Clinical-Resources/Contrast-Manual |
