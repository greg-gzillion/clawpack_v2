# Vascular Surgery - Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm (AAA)

## Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Focal dilation of abdominal aorta =3.0 cm** (or =1.5x normal diameter) | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Prevalence** | **5-10%** of men >65 years | https://www.cdc.gov/heartdisease/aortic_aneurysm.htm |
| **Risk Factors** | **Smoking** (strongest), **Male sex**, **Age >65**, **Family history**, **Caucasian**, **Hypertension**, **Atherosclerosis** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Screening** | **One-time abdominal ultrasound** for **men 65-75 with smoking history** (USPSTF Grade B) | https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm-screening |

## Indications for Repair (SVS Guidelines)
| Indication | Threshold | URL |
|------------|-----------|-----|
| **AAA Diameter (Men)** | **=5.5 cm** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **AAA Diameter (Women)** | **=5.0 cm** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Rapid Expansion** | **=0.5 cm in 6 months** or **=1.0 cm in 1 year** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Symptomatic** | **Any size** (pain, tenderness, Embolization) | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Ruptured** | **Emergent repair** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |

## EVAR vs Open Repair
| Feature | EVAR | Open Repair | URL |
|---------|------|-------------|-----|
| **Perioperative Mortality** | **Lower** (1-2%) | **Higher** (3-5%) | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Length of Stay** | **Shorter** (1-3 days) | **Longer** (7-10 days) | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Long-Term Survival** | **Similar** | **Similar** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Reintervention Rate** | **Higher** (20-30% at 5 years) | **Lower** (5-10% at 5 years) | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Surveillance** | **Lifelong annual CTA or Duplex** | **Intermittent** (q5 years) | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Anatomic Suitability** | **Requires adequate neck, Access vessels** | **Any anatomy** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |

## Medical Management of AAA
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Smoking Cessation** | **Single most important intervention** | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Antiplatelet** | **Aspirin** or **Clopidogrel** (for cardiovascular risk reduction) | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Statin** | **High-intensity** (reduces expansion and rupture risk) | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Blood Pressure** | **<130/80 mmHg** (beta-blockers may reduce expansion) | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
| **Exercise** | **No restrictions** (does not increase rupture risk) | https://vascular.org/patients/vascular-conditions/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm |
