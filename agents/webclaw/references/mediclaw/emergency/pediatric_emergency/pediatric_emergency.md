# Emergency Medicine - Pediatric Emergencies

## Pediatric Assessment Triangle (PAT)
| Component | Normal | Abnormal |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Appearance | Alert, Interactive | Lethargic, Irritable, Inconsolable |
| Work of Breathing | Normal, Quiet | Retractions, Nasal flaring, Head bobbing |
| Circulation | Pink, Normal cap refill | Pale, Mottled, Cyanotic, Delayed refill |

## Pediatric Vital Signs
| Age | HR (awake) | RR | SBP (5th %ile) |
|-----|------------|-----|----------------|
| Newborn | 100-205 | 30-60 | >60 |
| Infant (1-12 mo) | 100-180 | 30-50 | >70 |
| Toddler (1-3 yr) | 80-150 | 24-40 | >70 + (2 x age) |
| Preschool (3-5 yr) | 70-140 | 22-34 | >70 + (2 x age) |
| School Age (6-12 yr) | 65-120 | 18-30 | >70 + (2 x age) |
| Adolescent | 60-100 | 12-16 | >90 |

## Pediatric Weight Estimation
| Method | Formula |
|--------|---------|
| 1-12 months | (Age in months + 9) / 2 = weight in kg |
| 1-8 years | (Age x 2) + 8 = weight in kg |
| 9-14 years | (Age x 3) + 7 = weight in kg |
| Broselow Tape | Length-based weight estimation |

## Pediatric Fever
| Age | Temperature Threshold | Management |
|-----|----------------------|------------|
| <28 days | >=38.0C (100.4F) | **Full sepsis workup**; Admit; IV antibiotics |
| 29-60 days | >=38.0C (100.4F) | Labs; Consider LP; Antibiotics; Admit vs observe |
| 61-90 days | >=38.0C (100.4F) | Labs; UA; Discharge if low-risk |
| >90 days | >=38.0C (100.4F) | Clinical judgment; UA if indicated |

## Pediatric Respiratory Emergencies
| Condition | Key Features | Management |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Croup | Barking cough, Stridor | Dexamethasone 0.6 mg/kg; Nebulized epinephrine |
| Bronchiolitis | Wheezing, Crackles, <2 years | Supportive; Suctioning; Oxygen if needed |
| Asthma | Wheezing, Prolonged expiration | Albuterol; Ipratropium; Steroids; Magnesium |
| Foreign Body Aspiration | Sudden choking, Asymmetric breath sounds | Bronchoscopy |
| Epiglottitis | Tripod position, Drooling | **Do NOT examine**; Secure airway |
| Pertussis | Paroxysmal cough, Whoop | Azithromycin; Isolation |

## Pediatric Dehydration
| Severity | Signs | Management |
|----------|-------|------------|
| Mild (3-5%) | Slightly dry mucosa | Oral rehydration |
| Moderate (6-9%) | Dry mucosa, Decreased tears, Sunken fontanelle | Oral or IV rehydration |
| Severe (>=10%) | Very dry, Cap refill >2 sec, Lethargy | **IV fluid bolus** 20 mL/kg; Repeat as needed |

## Pediatric Seizures
| Condition | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Febrile Seizure (Simple) | Reassurance; Antipyretics; No anticonvulsants |
| Febrile Seizure (Complex) | Consider workup; Benzodiazepines if prolonged |
| Status Epilepticus | Lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg IV; Fosphenytoin 20 PE/kg; Levetiracetam 60 mg/kg |

## BRUE (Brief Resolved Unexplained Event)
| Low Risk | High Risk |
|----------|-----------|
| Age >60 days | Age <60 days |
| Born >=32 weeks | Born <32 weeks |
| First event | Multiple events |
| Event <1 minute | Event >=1 minute |
| No CPR required | CPR required |
| Normal exam | Abnormal exam |

## Non-Accidental Trauma (Child Abuse)
| Red Flag | Finding |
|----------|---------|
| History | Changing story, Delay in seeking care, Injury inconsistent with development |
| Bruises | Patterned, Multiple stages, Non-mobile infant |
| Burns | Stocking-glove pattern, Symmetric, Cigarette burns |
| Fractures | Metaphyseal corner, Posterior rib, Multiple stages |
| Head Trauma | Subdural hematoma, Retinal hemorrhages |
| Workup | Skeletal survey; Head CT if <6 months; Labs; Social work |

## Common Pediatric Rashes
| Rash | Features | Management |
|------|----------|------------|
| Hand-Foot-Mouth | Vesicles on hands/feet/mouth | Supportive |
| Fifth Disease | Slapped cheeks | Supportive |
| Roseola | High fever then rash | Supportive |
| Scarlet Fever | Sandpaper rash, Strawberry tongue | Penicillin |
| Varicella | Vesicles in different stages | Supportive; Isolation |
| Measles | Cough, Coryza, Conjunctivitis, Koplik spots | Supportive; Vitamin A |
| Kawasaki Disease | Fever >5 days, Rash, Conjunctivitis, Strawberry tongue | **IVIG + Aspirin**; Echo |

## Pediatric Dosing Pearls
| Medication | Dose |
|------------|------|
| Acetaminophen | 15 mg/kg q4-6h (max 75 mg/kg/day) |
| Ibuprofen | 10 mg/kg q6-8h (max 40 mg/kg/day) |
