# Emergency Medicine - Psychiatric Emergencies

## Suicide Risk Assessment
| Risk Factor | High-Risk Features |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Ideation | Active plan, Intent, Means available |
| History | Prior attempts, Self-harm |
| Demographics | Male, Elderly, Adolescent, LGBTQ+ |
| Comorbidities | Depression, Substance use, Chronic pain |
| Psychosocial | Recent loss, Isolation, Unemployment |

## Medical Clearance for Psychiatric Patients
| Component | Evaluation |
|-----------|-----------|
| Vital Signs | HR, BP, RR, Temp, SpO2 |
| Glucose | Fingerstick glucose |
| Toxicology | UDS, ETOH level if indicated |
| Pregnancy | hCG in women of childbearing age |
| Head CT | New-onset psychosis, Trauma, Focal deficit |
| Additional Labs | Based on presentation (CBC, CMP, TSH) |

## Agitation Management
| Agent | Dose | Onset |
|-------|------|-------|
| Midazolam IM | 5-10 mg | 5-10 min |
| Lorazepam PO/IM | 1-2 mg | 15-30 min |
| Haloperidol IM | 5-10 mg | 20-30 min |
| Olanzapine IM/ODT | 5-10 mg | 15-30 min |
| Ziprasidone IM | 10-20 mg | 15-30 min |
| Ketamine IM | 2-4 mg/kg | 2-5 min |
| Droperidol IM | 2.5-5 mg | 10-15 min |

## Chemical Restraint Combinations
| Combination | Doses |
|-------------|-------|
| B-52 | Haloperidol 5 mg + Lorazepam 2 mg + Diphenhydramine 50 mg IM |
| Midazolam + Haloperidol | Midazolam 5 mg + Haloperidol 5 mg IM |
| Ketamine + Midazolam | Ketamine 2 mg/kg + Midazolam 2 mg IM |

## Psychiatric Holds
| Type | Duration | Criteria |
|------|----------|----------|
| Emergency Hold | 72 hours | Danger to self, Danger to others, Grave disability |
| Involuntary Commitment | Varies by state | Requires court hearing |

## Depression
| Severity | Management |
|----------|------------|
| Mild-Moderate | Outpatient referral; SSRI |
| Severe (with SI) | Psychiatric admission; Consider ECT |
| Catatonia | **Lorazepam challenge**; ECT |

## Bipolar Disorder
| Phase | Management |
|-------|------------|
| Mania | Antipsychotics (Olanzapine, Risperidone); Mood stabilizers; Admission |
| Depression | Mood stabilizers; Avoid antidepressants alone (risk of switching) |
| Mixed | Antipsychotics; Mood stabilizers |

## Psychosis
| Type | Management |
|------|------------|
| First-Episode | Medical workup; Antipsychotics; Psychiatry consultation |
| Schizophrenia Exacerbation | Antipsychotics; Evaluate for non-adherence |
| Substance-Induced | Supportive; Antipsychotics if needed |

## Anxiety and Panic Disorder
| Condition | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Panic Attack | Benzodiazepines (short-term); Reassurance |
| Generalized Anxiety | Outpatient referral; SSRI |

## Substance Use Disorders
| Substance | Withdrawal Management |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Alcohol | Benzodiazepines (CIWA protocol); Thiamine; Folate |
| Opioids | Clonidine; Buprenorphine (if waivered); Methadone referral |
| Benzodiazepines | Taper with long-acting benzodiazepine |
| Stimulants | Supportive; Benzodiazepines for agitation |

## CIWA-Ar Protocol (Alcohol Withdrawal)
| Score | Benzodiazepine Dose |
|-------|---------------------|
| <8 | No medication |
| 8-15 | Lorazepam 2 mg or Diazepam 10 mg |
| 16-20 | Lorazepam 4 mg or Diazepam 20 mg |
| >20 | Lorazepam 6-8 mg or Diazepam 30-40 mg |

## Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS)
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Presentation | Rigidity, Hyperthermia, Autonomic instability, AMS |
| Labs | Elevated CK, Leukocytosis |
| Treatment | Stop offending drug; **Bromocriptine**; **Dantrolene**; Supportive |

## Serotonin Syndrome
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Presentation | Clonus, Hyperreflexia, Tremor, Hyperthermia |
| Causes | SSRIs, MAOIs, Linezolid, MDMA |
| Treatment | Stop offending drug; **Benzodiazepines**; **Cyproheptadine**; Supportive |
