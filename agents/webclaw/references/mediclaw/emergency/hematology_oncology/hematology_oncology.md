# Emergency Medicine - Hematology and Oncology Emergencies

## Oncologic Emergencies
| Emergency | Presentation | Management |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Tumor Lysis Syndrome | Hyperkalemia, Hyperphosphatemia, Hypocalcemia, Hyperuricemia | Aggressive hydration; Rasburicase; Dialysis |
| Hypercalcemia of Malignancy | AMS, Constipation, Renal failure | Aggressive IVF; Calcitonin; Bisphosphonates |
| Superior Vena Cava Syndrome | Facial swelling, Dyspnea, Venous distention | Steroids; Radiation; Stenting |
| Spinal Cord Compression | Back pain, Weakness, Sensory level, Incontinence | **Steroids (Dexamethasone 10 mg IV)**; Emergent MRI; Radiation |
| Febrile Neutropenia | Fever + ANC <500 | Broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour |
| Malignant Pericardial Effusion | Dyspnea, Tamponade | Pericardiocentesis; Pericardial window |

## Sickle Cell Emergencies
| Emergency | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Vaso-occlusive Crisis | Aggressive pain control (opioids); Hydration |
| Acute Chest Syndrome | Oxygen; Antibiotics (Ceftriaxone + Azithromycin); Exchange transfusion |
| Splenic Sequestration | Fluid resuscitation; Transfusion; Splenectomy |
| Aplastic Crisis | Supportive; Transfusion; Treat parvovirus |
| Priapism | Hydration; Analgesia; Aspiration; Exchange transfusion |

## Transfusion Reactions
| Type | Timing | Management |
|------|--------|------------|
| Acute Hemolytic | Immediate | Stop transfusion; IVF; Diuretics |
| Febrile Non-Hemolytic | 1-6 hrs | Antipyretics; Slow transfusion |
| Allergic | Immediate | Antihistamines; Steroids |
| Anaphylactic | Immediate | Epinephrine; Stop transfusion |
| TRALI | 1-6 hrs | Supportive; Mechanical ventilation |
| TACO | During/After | Diuretics; Oxygen |

## Coagulation Emergencies
| Condition | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Warfarin Reversal (Life-threatening) | 4-Factor PCC (Kcentra) + Vitamin K 10 mg IV |
| Warfarin Reversal (Non-urgent) | Vitamin K PO/IV; Hold warfarin |
| DOAC Reversal | Andexanet alfa (Xa inhibitors); Idarucizumab (Dabigatran) |
| Heparin Reversal | Protamine 1 mg per 100 units heparin |
| DIC | Treat underlying cause; Cryoprecipitate; Platelets |

## Thrombocytopenia
| Severity | Management |
|----------|------------|
| Platelets <10,000 | Transfuse (even without bleeding) |
| Platelets 10,000-20,000 | Transfuse if bleeding or procedure |
| Platelets <50,000 + Bleeding | Transfuse |
| ITP | Steroids; IVIG; Avoid platelet transfusion unless life-threatening |

## Bleeding Disorders
| Condition | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Hemophilia A | Factor VIII concentrate; DDAVP for mild |
| Hemophilia B | Factor IX concentrate |
| von Willebrand Disease | DDAVP; vWF concentrate |
| Acquired Hemophilia | Factor VIIa; Immunosuppression |
