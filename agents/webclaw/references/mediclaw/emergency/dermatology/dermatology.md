# Emergency Medicine - Dermatologic Emergencies

## Life-Threatening Rashes
| Condition | Presentation | Management |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Stevens-Johnson Syndrome (SJS) | <10% BSA detachment; Mucosal involvement | Stop offending drug; Supportive care; Consider steroids/IVIG |
| Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN) | >30% BSA detachment; Positive Nikolsky sign | Stop drug; Burn unit; Supportive care |
| SJS/TEN Overlap | 10-30% BSA detachment | Same as TEN |
| Erythema Multiforme | Target lesions; Minimal mucosal involvement | Supportive; Treat underlying infection |
| DRESS Syndrome | Fever, Eosinophilia, Organ involvement | Stop drug; Steroids |

## Necrotizing Soft Tissue Infections
| Condition | Features | Management |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| Necrotizing Fasciitis | Pain out of proportion; Crepitus; Rapid spread | Broad-spectrum antibiotics; **Emergent surgical debridement** |
| Fournier's Gangrene | Perineal necrotizing fasciitis | Broad-spectrum antibiotics; **Emergent debridement** |
| Clostridial Myonecrosis | Gas gangrene; Crepitus | Antibiotics (Penicillin + Clindamycin); Debridement; HBO |

## Stevens-Johnson Syndrome / TEN
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Common Causes | Sulfa drugs, Anticonvulsants, NSAIDs, Allopurinol |
| SCORTEN Score | Predicts mortality in TEN |
| Treatment | Stop offending drug; Supportive; Burn unit if >10% BSA |
| Controversial Therapies | IVIG, Cyclosporine, Steroids |

## Erythema Multiforme
| Type | Features | Management |
|------|----------|------------|
| Minor | Target lesions; Acral distribution | Supportive; Treat HSV if present |
| Major | + Mucosal involvement (usually oral only) | Supportive; Steroids controversial |

## Staphylococcal Scalded Skin Syndrome (SSSS)
| Feature | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Presentation | Diffuse erythema; Superficial blistering; Nikolsky positive |
| Cause | S. aureus exfoliative toxin |
| Treatment | IV antibiotics (Nafcillin or Vancomycin); Wound care |

## Purpura Fulminans
| Feature | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Presentation | Rapidly progressive purpura; DIC; Shock |
| Causes | Meningococcemia, Sepsis, Protein C/S deficiency |
| Treatment | Antibiotics; Supportive; Activated Protein C |

## Common Rashes in the ED
| Rash | Features | Treatment |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Urticaria | Wheals; Pruritic | Antihistamines; Steroids |
| Angioedema | Swelling of lips/tongue/airway | Epinephrine; Antihistamines; Steroids |
| Cellulitis | Erythema, Warmth, Tenderness | Antibiotics (Cephalexin or Clindamycin) |
| Erysipelas | Raised, Demarcated, Facial | Antibiotics (Penicillin) |
| Herpes Zoster | Dermatomal vesicles | Antivirals (Acyclovir/Valacyclovir) |
| Erythema Migrans | Bull's-eye rash | Doxycycline |

## Drug Eruptions
| Type | Timing | Management |
|------|--------|------------|
| Morbilliform | 7-14 days after starting drug | Stop drug; Antihistamines |
| Fixed Drug Eruption | Hours; Recurrence at same site | Stop drug |
| Urticarial | Immediate or delayed | Antihistamines |
| Photosensitivity | Sun-exposed areas | Sun protection; Stop drug |
