# Emergency Medicine - Overview

## What is Emergency Medicine?
Emergency Medicine is the medical specialty concerned with the acute care of patients with urgent, undifferentiated illness or injury. It encompasses the initial evaluation, diagnosis, stabilization, treatment, and disposition of patients across all age groups.

## Core Principles of Emergency Medicine
| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Triage | Prioritizing patients based on acuity |
| Primary Survey | ABCDE approach to immediate life threats |
| Resuscitation | Stabilization of critically ill patients |
| Diagnosis | Rapid evaluation and testing |
| Disposition | Admission, observation, transfer, or discharge |

## Primary Survey (ABCDE Approach)
| Component | Assessment | Intervention |
|-----------|------------|--------------|
| **A**irway | Patency, obstruction, secretions | Jaw thrust, suction, OPA/NPA, intubation |
| **B**reathing | Rate, depth, breath sounds, SpO2 | Oxygen, BVM, chest tube, needle decompression |
| **C**irculation | Pulse, BP, capillary refill, skin | IV access, fluids, blood products, vasopressors |
| **D**isability | GCS, pupils, glucose, focal deficits | Glucose, naloxone, ICP management |
| **E**xposure | Full body exam, temperature | Warm blankets, hypothermia prevention |

## Secondary Survey (AMPLE History)
| Letter | Component |

| **L** | Last meal |
| **E** | Events preceding illness/injury |

## ESI Triage System (Emergency Severity Index)
| Level | Acuity | Response Time | Examples |
|-------|--------|---------------|----------|
| Level 1 | Immediate life threat | Immediate | Cardiac arrest, severe respiratory distress |
| Level 2 | High risk | <10 minutes | Chest pain, stroke, severe pain |
| Level 3 | Multiple resources needed | <30 minutes | Abdominal pain, fracture |
| Level 4 | One resource needed | <60 minutes | Laceration, UTI |
| Level 5 | No resources needed | <120 minutes | Sore throat, medication refill |
