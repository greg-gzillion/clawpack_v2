# Emergency Medicine - ENT and Dental Emergencies

## Ear Emergencies
| Condition | Presentation | Management |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Acute Otitis Media | Ear pain, Bulging TM | Amoxicillin 500 mg TID x 5-7 days |
| Otitis Externa | Ear pain with tragus traction | Ciprofloxacin/Hydrocortisone otic drops |
| Malignant Otitis Externa | Diabetes, Severe pain, Granulation tissue | **IV antibiotics**; CT; ENT consult |
| Mastoiditis | Postauricular swelling, Protruding ear | IV antibiotics; CT; ENT consult |
| Tympanic Membrane Perforation | Hearing loss, Bloody drainage | Dry ear precautions; Antibiotics if contaminated |
| Auricular Hematoma | Trauma, Swelling | **Drainage + Pressure dressing** (prevent cauliflower ear) |
| Foreign Body | Often in children | Removal; Consider ENT if unable |

## Nose and Sinus Emergencies
| Condition | Presentation | Management |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Epistaxis | Nasal bleeding | Anterior: Pressure, Oxymetazoline, Cautery, Packing |
| Posterior Epistaxis | Bleeding despite anterior packing | Posterior pack; ENT consult; Embolization |
| Acute Sinusitis | Facial pain, Purulent drainage | Amoxicillin-clavulanate; Decongestants |
| Complicated Sinusitis | Visual changes, Neurologic signs | **Emergent**; IV antibiotics; CT; ENT consult |
| Nasal Fracture | Trauma, Deformity | ENT follow-up in 5-7 days (after swelling resolves) |
| Septal Hematoma | Bilateral swelling, Nasal obstruction | **Immediate drainage** (risk of septal necrosis) |
| CSF Leak | Clear rhinorrhea, Halo sign | Head elevation; Antibiotics; Neurosurgery consult |

## Throat Emergencies
| Condition | Presentation | Management |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Streptococcal Pharyngitis | Sore throat, Exudates, Fever, No cough | Penicillin or Amoxicillin |
| Peritonsillar Abscess | Trismus, Hot potato voice, Uvular deviation | Needle aspiration/I&D; Antibiotics; Steroids |
| Retropharyngeal Abscess | Stridor, Neck stiffness, Fever | **Emergent**; IV antibiotics; CT; OR drainage |
| Epiglottitis | Tripod position, Drooling, Stridor | **Do NOT examine**; Secure airway; Antibiotics |
| Ludwig's Angina | Bilateral submandibular swelling, Elevated tongue | **Airway emergency**; IV antibiotics; OR drainage |
| Deep Neck Space Infection | Fever, Neck swelling, Dysphagia | IV antibiotics; CT; ENT consult |
| Foreign Body | Sensation of something stuck | Endoscopy; ENT consult |

## Dental Emergencies
| Condition | Presentation | Management |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Dental Caries | Pain with sweets/temperature | Analgesia; Dental referral |
| Reversible Pulpitis | Sharp pain; Resolves quickly | NSAIDs; Dental referral |
| Irreversible Pulpitis | Lingering pain; Spontaneous | NSAIDs; Dental referral for root canal |
| Periapical Abscess | Tooth pain, Swelling, Fever | Antibiotics (Penicillin or Clindamycin); I&D; Dental referral |
| Periodontal Abscess | Gum swelling, Pocketing | I&D; Antibiotics; Dental referral |
| Alveolar Osteitis | Pain 2-4 days after extraction | Irrigation; Packing with eugenol |
| Dental Avulsion | Tooth completely out | Replace in socket (if permanent); Milk/saline; **Immediate dental** |
| Dental Fracture | Ellis Classification | Ellis I: Smooth edges; Ellis II: Cover with CaOH; Ellis III: Dental referral |
| Post-Extraction Bleeding | Oozing from socket | Pressure with gauze; Gelfoam; Suture |

## Temporomandibular Joint (TMJ) Dislocation
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Presentation | Inability to close mouth; Jaw deviation |
| Reduction | Massage masseter; Downward and backward pressure |
| Post-Reduction | Soft diet; Avoid wide opening; ENT/OMFS follow-up |

## Salivary Gland Emergencies
| Condition | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Sialolithiasis | Hydration; Sialogogues; Massage; ENT referral |
| Sialadenitis | Antibiotics (S. aureus coverage); Hydration |
| Parotitis | Supportive; Antibiotics if bacterial |
