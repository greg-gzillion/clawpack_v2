# Emergency Medicine - Obstetric and Gynecologic Emergencies

## Ectopic Pregnancy
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Presentation | Abdominal pain, Vaginal bleeding, Amenorrhea |
| Risk Factors | PID, Prior ectopic, IUD, Tubal surgery |
| Diagnosis | Positive hCG; No intrauterine pregnancy on US; Adnexal mass |
| Management (Stable) | Methotrexate (if hCG <5,000, <3.5 cm, no fetal heartbeat) |
| Management (Unstable) | **Emergent surgery** |

## Spontaneous Abortion (Miscarriage)
| Type | Cervix | US Findings | Management |
|------|--------|-------------|------------|
| Threatened | Closed | IUP with cardiac activity | Expectant; RhoGAM if Rh negative |
| Inevitable | Open | IUP | Expectant vs D&C |
| Incomplete | Open | Products of conception | D&C |
| Complete | Closed | Empty uterus | Expectant |
| Missed | Closed | IUP without cardiac activity | Expectant vs D&C vs Misoprostol |
| Septic | Open/Closed | Products + Fever | **IV antibiotics**; Emergent D&C |

## Vaginal Bleeding in Pregnancy
| Trimester | Differential | Management |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| First | Ectopic, Miscarriage, Molar pregnancy | hCG, US, Rh type |
| Second/Third | Placenta previa, Abruptio placentae, Vasa previa | **Do NOT perform vaginal exam**; US; OB consult |

## Hypertensive Disorders of Pregnancy
| Condition | Criteria | Management |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| Gestational Hypertension | BP >=140/90 after 20 weeks; No proteinuria | Monitor; Antihypertensives if severe |
| Preeclampsia | BP >=140/90 + Proteinuria or end-organ damage | Delivery; Magnesium for seizure prophylaxis |
| Severe Preeclampsia | BP >=160/110; Thrombocytopenia; Renal failure; Pulmonary edema | **Magnesium**; Antihypertensives; Delivery |
| Eclampsia | Seizures in setting of preeclampsia | **Magnesium**; Airway management; Delivery |
| HELLP Syndrome | Hemolysis, Elevated LFTs, Low Platelets | Delivery; Steroids if <34 weeks |

## Magnesium Sulfate for Preeclampsia/Eclampsia
| Component | Dose |
|-----------|------|
| Loading Dose | 4-6 g IV over 15-20 min |
| Maintenance | 1-2 g/hr infusion |
| Therapeutic Level | 4-8 mg/dL |
| Toxicity | Loss of reflexes (>10 mg/dL), Respiratory depression (>12 mg/dL), Cardiac arrest (>15 mg/dL) |
| Antidote | Calcium gluconate 1 g IV over 5-10 min |

## Postpartum Hemorrhage
| Cause (4 T's) | Management |
|---------------|------------|
| **T**one (Uterine atony) | Uterine massage; Oxytocin; Methylergonovine; Carboprost; Misoprostol |
| **T**rauma | Repair lacerations; Evaluate for uterine rupture |
| **T**issue | Remove retained products; D&C |
| **T**hrombin | Treat coagulopathy; TXA |

## Ovarian Torsion
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Presentation | Sudden severe unilateral pelvic pain; N/V |
| Diagnosis | US with Doppler (absent flow) |
| Treatment | **Emergent surgery** (detorsion within 6-8 hours) |

## Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Presentation | Pelvic pain, Cervical motion tenderness, Fever |
| Diagnosis | Clinical; Consider US for abscess |
| Treatment | Ceftriaxone 1g IM + Doxycycline 100 mg BID x 14 days + Metronidazole 500 mg BID x 14 days |
| Admission Indications | Pregnancy, Failed outpatient, Abscess, Severe illness |

## Sexual Assault
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Medical Care | Treat injuries; Forensic exam (SANE if available) |
| Pregnancy Prophylaxis | Levonorgestrel 1.5 mg PO or Ulipristal 30 mg PO |
| STI Prophylaxis | Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM + Azithromycin 1g PO + Metronidazole 2g PO |
| HIV PEP | Consider if high risk; 28-day course |
| Follow-up | Mental health resources; Repeat testing |

## Bartholin's Gland Abscess
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Presentation | Painful labial swelling |
| Treatment | I&D; Word catheter placement; Antibiotics |

## Vaginitis
| Type | Discharge | pH | Treatment |
|------|-----------|-----|-----------|
| Bacterial Vaginosis | Thin, gray, fishy odor | >4.5 | Metronidazole 500 mg BID x 7 days |
| Candidiasis | Thick, white, curd-like | <4.5 | Fluconazole 150 mg PO x 1 |
| Trichomoniasis | Frothy, yellow-green | >4.5 | Metronidazole 2g PO x 1 |
