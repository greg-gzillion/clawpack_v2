# Emergency Medicine - Trauma

## Trauma Primary Survey (ATLS)
| Component | Assessment | Life-Threatening Injuries |
|-----------|------------|---------------------------|
| **A**irway | Patency, obstruction | Airway obstruction, facial trauma |
| **B**reathing | Rate, breath sounds | Tension pneumothorax, open pneumothorax, flail chest, massive hemothorax |
| **C**irculation | Pulse, BP, bleeding | Hemorrhagic shock, cardiac tamponade |
| **D**isability | GCS, pupils, motor | TBI, spinal cord injury |
| **E**xposure | Full body exam | Hypothermia, missed injuries |

## eFAST Exam
| View | Assesses | Positive Finding |
|------|----------|------------------|
| Right Upper Quadrant | Hepatorenal recess (Morison's pouch) | Free fluid (blood) |
| Left Upper Quadrant | Splenorenal recess | Free fluid (blood) |
| Subxiphoid | Pericardium | Pericardial effusion/tamponade |
| Suprapubic | Pelvis (Pouch of Douglas) | Free fluid (blood) |
| Anterior Chest | Pleural space | Pneumothorax |

## Hemorrhagic Shock Classification (ATLS)
| Class | Blood Loss | HR | BP | Mental Status | Fluid |
|-------|------------|-----|-----|---------------|-------|
| I | <15% (<750 mL) | <100 | Normal | Anxious | Crystalloid |
| II | 15-30% (750-1500 mL) | 100-120 | Normal | Anxious | Crystalloid |
| III | 30-40% (1500-2000 mL) | 120-140 | Low | Confused | Crystalloid + Blood |
| IV | >40% (>2000 mL) | >140 | Very Low | Lethargic | Massive Transfusion |

## Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)
| Severity | GCS | Management |
|----------|-----|------------|
| Mild | 13-15 | Observation; CT if indicated |
| Moderate | 9-12 | CT head; admit; repeat imaging |
| Severe | <=8 | Intubation; ICP monitoring; CPP 60-70 mmHg |

| High Risk (CT Required) | Medium Risk (CT Considered) |
|-------------------------|---------------------------|

## Spinal Cord Injury Syndromes
| Syndrome | Mechanism | Findings |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| Central Cord | Hyperextension | Upper extremity > lower extremity weakness |
| Anterior Cord | Flexion/compression | Motor loss, pain/temp loss; preserved proprioception |
| Brown-Sequard | Hemisection | Ipsilateral motor/proprioception loss; Contralateral pain/temp loss |
| Cauda Equina | Lumbosacral injury | Saddle anesthesia, incontinence, LE weakness |

## Chest Trauma
| Injury | Signs | Management |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Tension Pneumothorax | Hypotension, JVD, tracheal deviation | Needle decompression -> Chest tube |
| Open Pneumothorax | Sucking chest wound | Three-sided occlusive dressing; Chest tube |
| Massive Hemothorax | >1500 mL output or >200 mL/hr | Chest tube; Thoracotomy if unstable |
| Flail Chest | Paradoxical chest wall movement | Analgesia; PPV if respiratory failure |
| Cardiac Tamponade | Beck's Triad | Pericardiocentesis; Thoracotomy |

## Abdominal Trauma
| Type | Management |
|------|------------|
| Blunt Trauma - Unstable | Positive FAST -> OR |
| Blunt Trauma - Stable | CT abdomen/pelvis |
| Penetrating Trauma - Unstable | OR exploration |

## Extremity Trauma
| Injury | Signs | Management |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Compartment Syndrome | Pain out of proportion; Pain with passive stretch | Fasciotomy if pressure >30 mmHg |
| Open Fracture | Bone exposed | Antibiotics; Tetanus; I&D |
| Knee Dislocation | Gross deformity | Assess popliteal artery (ABI <0.9 -> CTA) |

## Burns - Rule of Nines (Adult)
| Body Part | %TBSA |
|-----------|-------|
| Head | 9% |
| Each Arm | 9% |
| Anterior Trunk | 18% |
| Posterior Trunk | 18% |
| Each Leg | 18% |
| Perineum | 1% |

## Parkland Formula
| Component | Calculation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Fluid Requirement | 4 mL x weight (kg) x %TBSA burned |
| Timing | Half over first 8 hrs; Half over next 16 hrs |
