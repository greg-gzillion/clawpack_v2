# Emergency Medicine - Environmental Emergencies

## Hypothermia
| Severity | Core Temperature | Clinical Features | Management |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|------------|
| Mild | 32-35C (90-95F) | Shivering, Tachycardia | Passive rewarming; Warm blankets |
| Moderate | 28-32C (82-90F) | Shivering stops, Bradycardia, AMS | Active external rewarming |
| Severe | <28C (<82F) | Coma, Apnea, VF arrest risk | Active internal rewarming; ECMO/CPB |

## Hypothermia and Cardiac Arrest
| Rhythm | Management |
|--------|------------|
| VF/VT | Defibrillate ONCE; Resume CPR; Transport to ECMO |
| Asystole/PEA | CPR; Rewarming |
| Epinephrine | Avoid if temp <30C |
| Termination | "Not dead until warm and dead" |

## Hyperthermia and Heat Illness
| Condition | Core Temp | Management |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Heat Cramps | Normal | Rest, Oral hydration |
| Heat Exhaustion | <40C (<104F) | Rest, Cool environment, IVF |
| Heat Stroke | >40C (>104F) | Rapid cooling (Ice water immersion); IVF |

## Drowning
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Airway/Breathing | Oxygen; CPAP/BiPAP; Intubation for hypoxia |
| CPR | Start with 5 rescue breaths |
| C-Spine | Immobilize if high-risk mechanism |
| Antibiotics/Steroids | Not routinely indicated |

## Electrical Injuries
| Type | Management |
|------|------------|
| Low-Voltage (<1000V) | ECG; Cardiac monitoring if abnormal |
| High-Voltage (>1000V) | ECG; Monitor for compartment syndrome, rhabdomyolysis |
| Lightning Strike | Cardiac arrest; Keraunoparalysis |

## Altitude Illness
| Condition | Altitude | Management |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| Acute Mountain Sickness | >2500m | Rest; Acetazolamide; Descend if severe |
| HAPE | >3000m | **Immediate descent**; Oxygen; Nifedipine |
| HACE | >4000m | **Immediate descent**; Dexamethasone |

## Diving Emergencies
| Condition | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Decompression Sickness | 100% O2; Hyperbaric oxygen therapy |
| Arterial Gas Embolism | 100% O2; Hyperbaric oxygen therapy |
| Barotrauma | Supportive; Avoid diving until healed |

## Snake Envenomations (North America)
| Species | Features | Antivenom |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Crotalinae (Pit Vipers) | Local tissue injury, Coagulopathy | CroFab or Anavip |
| Elapidae (Coral Snakes) | Neurotoxicity | Coral snake antivenom |

## Spider Envenomations (North America)
| Species | Features | Management |
|---------|----------|------------|
| Latrodectus (Black Widow) | Abdominal pain, Muscle cramps | Antivenom (rarely needed) |
| Loxosceles (Brown Recluse) | Necrotic skin lesion | Supportive |

## Marine Envenomations
| Species | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Cnidaria (Jellyfish) | Vinegar (box jellyfish); Hot water immersion |
| Stingray | Hot water immersion; Wound care; Tetanus |
| Scorpaenidae (Lionfish) | Hot water immersion |
