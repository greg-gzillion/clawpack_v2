# Emergency Medicine - Cardiovascular Emergencies

## Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)
| Type | ECG | Initial Management |
|------|-----|---------------------|
| STEMI | ST elevation >=1 mm | Aspirin; P2Y12 inhibitor; Emergent PCI <90 min |
| NSTEMI | ST depression, T wave inversion | Aspirin; P2Y12 inhibitor; PCI within 24-48 hrs |
| Unstable Angina | ST depression, T wave inversion | Same as NSTEMI |

## STEMI Localization
| ECG Leads | Affected Wall | Culprit Artery |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| V1-V4 | Anterior | LAD |
| II, III, aVF | Inferior | RCA (80%) or LCx (20%) |
| I, aVL, V5-V6 | Lateral | LCx |

## Antiplatelet Therapy in ACS
| Agent | Loading Dose | Maintenance |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| Aspirin | 162-325 mg | 81 mg daily |
| Ticagrelor | 180 mg | 90 mg BID |
| Clopidogrel | 300-600 mg | 75 mg daily |
| Prasugrel | 60 mg | 10 mg daily |

## Acute Heart Failure/Pulmonary Edema
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Hypertensive (SCAPE) | Nitroglycerin; NIV |
| Normotensive/Hypotensive | Dobutamine or Milrinone; Diuretics |
| Cardiogenic Shock | Inotropes; IABP/Impella |

## Arrhythmias - Atrial Fibrillation
| Clinical Scenario | Management |
|------------------|------------|
| Unstable | Synchronized cardioversion (120-200J) |
| Stable, Onset <48 hrs | Rate or rhythm control |
| Stable, Onset >48 hrs | Rate control; Anticoagulation x 3 weeks |
| WPW with AFib | Avoid AV nodal blockers; Procainamide or Cardioversion |

## Rate Control in Atrial Fibrillation
| Agent | Dose | Target HR |
|-------|------|-----------|
| Metoprolol | 2.5-5 mg IV q5min | <110 bpm |
| Diltiazem | 0.25 mg/kg IV bolus | <110 bpm |
| Digoxin | 0.25-0.5 mg IV | <110 bpm |
| Amiodarone | 150 mg IV over 10 min | Alternative in hypotension/CHF |

## Hypertensive Emergencies
| Condition | Target BP Reduction | Preferred Agents |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|
| Aortic Dissection | SBP 100-120 within 20 min | Esmolol + Nicardipine |
| Hypertensive Encephalopathy | 25% reduction in 2-6 hrs | Nicardipine, Labetalol |
| Acute Ischemic Stroke | <185/110 before tPA | Nicardipine, Labetalol |
| ICH | SBP 140-160 | Nicardipine, Labetalol |

## Aortic Dissection
| Type | Location | Management |
|------|----------|------------|
| Stanford Type A | Ascending aorta | Emergency surgical repair |
| Stanford Type B | Descending aorta | Medical management (BP control) |
