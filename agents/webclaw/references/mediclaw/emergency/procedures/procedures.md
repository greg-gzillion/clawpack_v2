# Emergency Medicine - Procedures

## Airway Procedures
| Procedure | Indications | Key Steps |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Bag-Valve-Mask Ventilation | Respiratory failure, Pre-oxygenation | C-E grip; Jaw thrust; Good seal |
| Oropharyngeal Airway (OPA) | Unconscious patient; Tongue obstruction | Measure from mouth to angle of mandible |
| Nasopharyngeal Airway (NPA) | Conscious patient | Measure from nares to tragus; Lubricate |
| Endotracheal Intubation | Definitive airway | Laryngoscopy; Pass ETT through cords; Confirm ETCO2 |
| Rapid Sequence Intubation | Full stomach, Unstable | Pre-oxygenate; Induction; Paralysis; Intubate |
| Cricothyroidotomy | Cannot intubate, Cannot ventilate | Identify cricothyroid membrane; Vertical incision; Insert tube |
| Needle Cricothyroidotomy | Pediatric; Temporary | 14g angiocath through cricothyroid membrane |

## Breathing Procedures
| Procedure | Indications | Key Steps |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Needle Decompression | Tension pneumothorax | 14g angiocath; 2nd ICS, MCL; Over the rib |
| Tube Thoracostomy | Pneumothorax, Hemothorax | 4th-5th ICS, Anterior axillary line; Dissect to pleura; Insert tube |
| Thoracentesis | Pleural effusion | Ultrasound guidance; Above the rib |

## Vascular Access
| Procedure | Indications | Key Steps |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Peripheral IV | Fluid, Medications | Tourniquet; Vein selection; Insert at 15-30 degrees |
| External Jugular IV | Difficult access | Trendelenburg; Aim toward clavicle |
| Intraosseous (IO) Access | Emergent access | Proximal tibia, Humeral head; Drill or manual |
| Central Venous Line (IJ) | CVP, Long-term access | Ultrasound guidance; Trendelenburg; Seldinger technique |
| Central Venous Line (Subclavian) | Trauma, Long-term access | Infraclavicular approach; Lower infection risk |
| Central Venous Line (Femoral) | Emergent access | Below inguinal ligament; Medial to artery |
| Arterial Line | Continuous BP monitoring | Radial, Femoral; Allen test; Seldinger technique |

## Cardiac Procedures
| Procedure | Indications | Key Steps |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Defibrillation | VF, Pulseless VT | 120-200J biphasic; Clear; Shock |
| Synchronized Cardioversion | Unstable SVT, VT, AFib | Sync on; 50-200J |
| Transcutaneous Pacing | Symptomatic bradycardia | Place pads; Set rate 60-80; Increase mA until capture |
| Pericardiocentesis | Cardiac tamponade | Subxiphoid approach; Ultrasound guidance; Aspirate blood |

## Lumbar Puncture
| Indications | Contraindications |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Meningitis, SAH | Coagulopathy, Mass effect, Infection at site |
| Key Steps | L3-L4 or L4-L5; Measure opening pressure; Collect CSF |

## Abdominal Procedures
| Procedure | Indications | Key Steps |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Paracentesis | Ascites, SBP diagnosis | Ultrasound guidance; Z-track technique; Collect fluid |
| Nasogastric Tube | Decompression, Feeding | Measure from nose to ear to xiphoid; Confirm placement |

## Genitourinary Procedures
| Procedure | Indications | Key Steps |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Foley Catheter | Urinary retention, Output monitoring | Sterile technique; Inflate balloon; Confirm urine return |
| Suprapubic Catheter | Urethral injury, Obstruction | Ultrasound guidance; 2-3 fingerbreadths above pubis |
| Testicular Detorsion | Testicular torsion | "Open the book" (lateral rotation); Confirm with Doppler |

## Musculoskeletal Procedures
| Procedure | Indications | Key Steps |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Shoulder Reduction (Anterior) | Anterior dislocation | Multiple techniques (Stimson, Milch, Cunningham, Scapular manipulation) |
| Hip Reduction | Hip dislocation | Allis technique; Flexion, adduction, internal rotation |
| Knee Reduction | Knee dislocation | Longitudinal traction |
| Elbow Reduction | Elbow dislocation | Flexion with traction |
| Patellar Reduction | Patellar dislocation | Medial pressure with knee extension |
| TMJ Reduction | TMJ dislocation | Downward then backward pressure |
| Fracture Reduction | Displaced fracture | Hematoma block; Traction; Manipulation; Splint |
| Arthrocentesis | Septic arthritis, Gout | Sterile technique; Aspirate fluid |
| Compartment Pressure | Suspected compartment syndrome | Stryker needle; Measure all compartments |

## Wound Care
| Procedure | Indications | Key Steps |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Wound Irrigation | Contaminated wound | High-pressure irrigation; Normal saline |
| Wound Debridement | Devitalized tissue | Remove necrotic tissue |
| Laceration Repair | Clean laceration | Approximate edges; Suture, Staples, or Glue |
| Abscess I&D | Fluctuant abscess | Incision; Break loculations; Pack or drain |
| Foreign Body Removal | Retained foreign body | Explore; Remove; Tetanus prophylaxis |
| Digital Nerve Block | Finger/toe procedure | Dorsal approach; Inject at base |

## Regional Anesthesia Blocks
| Block | Indications | Key Steps |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| Digital Block | Finger/toe procedures | Inject at base; Avoid epinephrine |
| Hematoma Block | Fracture reduction | Inject into hematoma |
| Facial Nerve Block | Facial laceration | Supraorbital, Infraorbital, Mental |
| Intercostal Nerve Block | Rib fracture | Below the rib; Avoid pneumothorax |

## Ultrasound-Guided Procedures
| Procedure | Key Steps |
|-----------|-----------|
| Ultrasound-Guided IV | Identify vein; In-plane or out-of-plane approach |
| Ultrasound-Guided Nerve Block | Identify nerve; Visualize needle; Inject local |
| FAST Exam | RUQ, LUQ, Subxiphoid, Suprapubic |
