# Emergency Medicine - Airway and Breathing Emergencies

## Airway Obstruction
| Cause | Signs | Management |
|-------|-------|------------|
| Foreign Body | Stridor, coughing, cyanosis | Heimlich maneuver; back blows (infants) |
| Angioedema | Lip/tongue swelling, stridor | Epinephrine IM; steroids; antihistamines |
| Epiglottitis | Tripod position, drooling | Do NOT examine throat; secure airway |
| Anaphylaxis | Urticaria, wheezing, hypotension | Epinephrine IM 0.3-0.5 mg; fluids; antihistamines |

## Asthma Exacerbation
| Severity | PEFR | Management |
|----------|------|------------|
| Mild | >70% | SABA nebulizer; oral corticosteroids |
| Moderate | 40-69% | SABA + ipratropium; IV corticosteroids |
| Severe | <40% | Continuous SABA; IV magnesium 2g |
| Life-threatening | <25% | Intubation; ketamine; IV epinephrine |

## COPD Exacerbation
| Intervention | Recommendation |
|--------------|----------------|
| Bronchodilators | Albuterol + Ipratropium nebulized |
| Corticosteroids | Methylprednisolone 125 mg IV |
| Antibiotics | If increased sputum purulence |
| Non-Invasive Ventilation | BiPAP for pH <7.35 |
| Intubation | Refractory hypoxemia, AMS |

## CURB-65 Score for Pneumonia
| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Confusion | 1 |
| Uremia (BUN >19 mg/dL) | 1 |
| Respiratory rate >=30 | 1 |
| Blood pressure (SBP <90 or DBP <=60) | 1 |
| Age >=65 | 1 |

## Pulmonary Embolism (PE)
| Risk Stratification | Management |
|---------------------|------------|
| Low Risk (sPESI 0) | Outpatient anticoagulation |
| Intermediate Risk | Inpatient anticoagulation |
| High Risk (Massive) | Thrombolytics (tPA) or embolectomy |

## Pneumothorax
| Type | Management |
|------|------------|
| Simple (<2 cm) | Observation |
| Simple (>2 cm) | Chest tube |
| Tension | Needle decompression -> Chest tube |

## Non-Invasive Ventilation (NIV) Settings
| Condition | IPAP | EPAP |
|-----------|------|------|
| COPD | 10-15 cm H2O | 5-8 cm H2O |
| CHF/Pulmonary Edema | 10-15 cm H2O | 5-10 cm H2O |

## ARDS Management
| Category | PaO2/FiO2 | Management |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| Mild | 200-300 | Lung-protective ventilation (6 mL/kg) |
| Moderate | 100-200 | Higher PEEP; consider prone positioning |
| Severe | <100 | Neuromuscular blockade; ECMO consideration |
