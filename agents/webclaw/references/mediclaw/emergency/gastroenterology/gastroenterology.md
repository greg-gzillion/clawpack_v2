# Emergency Medicine - Gastrointestinal Emergencies

## Acute Abdominal Pain - Differential by Quadrant
| Location | Differential Diagnosis |
|----------|----------------------|
| RUQ | Cholecystitis, Hepatitis, Pyelonephritis, Pneumonia |
| LUQ | Splenic injury, Gastritis, Pancreatitis |
| RLQ | Appendicitis, Ovarian torsion, Ectopic, Kidney stone |
| LLQ | Diverticulitis, Ovarian torsion, Ectopic |
| Epigastric | Pancreatitis, PUD, MI, Aortic dissection |
| Diffuse | Bowel obstruction, Perforation, Mesenteric ischemia |

## Acute Pancreatitis
| Severity | Management |
|----------|------------|
| Mild | IVF resuscitation; Analgesia; NPO initially |
| Moderate | IVF; ICU consideration; Early enteral nutrition |
| Severe | ICU; Aggressive IVF; Enteral nutrition |
| Antibiotics | Only for infected necrosis |

## Biliary Emergencies
| Condition | Presentation | Management |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Acute Cholecystitis | RUQ pain, Fever, Murphy's sign | Antibiotics; Cholecystectomy |
| Choledocholithiasis | RUQ pain, Jaundice | ERCP with sphincterotomy |
| Ascending Cholangitis | Charcot's Triad | Antibiotics; Emergent ERCP |

## Gastrointestinal Bleeding
| Type | Presentation | Management |
|------|--------------|------------|
| Upper GI Bleed | Hematemesis, Melena | IV PPI; Octreotide if variceal; EGD |
| Lower GI Bleed | Hematochezia | CTA or Tagged RBC scan; Colonoscopy |
| Variceal Bleed | History of cirrhosis | Octreotide; Antibiotics; Emergent EGD |

## Glasgow-Blatchford Score (UGIB)
| Risk Marker | Points |
|-------------|--------|
| BUN | 2-6 points |
| Hemoglobin | 1-6 points |
| SBP <100 | 1-3 points |
| HR >=100 | 1 point |
| Melena | 1 point |
| Syncope | 2 points |
| Hepatic Disease | 2 points |
| Cardiac Failure | 2 points |
| **Score 0-1** | Low risk; Outpatient possible |

## Bowel Obstruction
| Type | Etiology | Management |
|------|----------|------------|
| Small Bowel Obstruction | Adhesions (60%) | NPO, NGT, IVF; Surgery if refractory |
| Large Bowel Obstruction | Malignancy (60%) | Endoscopic decompression; Surgery |
| Closed-Loop Obstruction | Internal hernia | **Surgical emergency** |

## Mesenteric Ischemia
| Type | Management |
|------|------------|
| Acute Mesenteric Ischemia | Anticoagulation; Emergent surgery |
| Colonic Ischemia | Supportive; Antibiotics |
| Key Finding | **Pain out of proportion to exam** |

## Appendicitis
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Classic Presentation | Periumbilical pain migrating to RLQ |
| Imaging | CT abdomen/pelvis (preferred); Ultrasound |
| Management | Appendectomy; Antibiotics alone for select cases |

## Diverticulitis
| Severity | Management |
|----------|------------|
| Uncomplicated | Oral antibiotics; Outpatient |
| Complicated | IV antibiotics; Percutaneous drainage; Surgery |
