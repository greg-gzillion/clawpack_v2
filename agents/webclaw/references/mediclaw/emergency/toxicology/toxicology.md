# Emergency Medicine - Toxicology

## General Approach to the Poisoned Patient
| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1. Stabilize | ABCDE; Treat seizures, hypotension, arrhythmias |
| 2. Decontamination | Activated charcoal if <1-2 hrs and airway protected |
| 3. Enhanced Elimination | Urinary alkalinization; Hemodialysis |
| 4. Antidote | Administer specific antidote if available |
| 5. Supportive Care | ICU admission if needed |

## Common Antidotes
| Toxin | Antidote | Dose |
|-------|----------|------|
| Acetaminophen | N-Acetylcysteine (NAC) | 150 mg/kg over 1 hr; then 50 mg/kg over 4 hrs; then 100 mg/kg over 16 hrs |
| Opioids | Naloxone | 0.4-2 mg IV/IM/IN; repeat q2-3min |
| Benzodiazepines | Flumazenil | 0.2 mg IV; max 3 mg (avoid in chronic use) |
| Organophosphates | Atropine + Pralidoxime | Atropine 2-5 mg IV q5min |
| Beta-Blockers | Glucagon | 3-10 mg IV; then infusion 1-5 mg/hr |
| Calcium Channel Blockers | Calcium + High-dose insulin | Calcium 1-2g IV; Insulin 1 unit/kg bolus + infusion |
| Digoxin | Digoxin Immune Fab | Based on ingested amount or level |
| Ethylene Glycol/Methanol | Fomepizole | 15 mg/kg load; 10 mg/kg q12h |
| Cyanide | Hydroxocobalamin | 5g IV over 15 min |
| Carbon Monoxide | Oxygen 100% | Until COHb <3% |
| Warfarin | Vitamin K + 4-Factor PCC | Vitamin K 10 mg IV |
| Tricyclic Antidepressants | Sodium Bicarbonate | 1-2 mEq/kg bolus for wide QRS |
| Sulfonylureas | Octreotide | 50-100 mcg SC q6-12h |
| Methemoglobinemia | Methylene Blue | 1-2 mg/kg IV over 5 min |

## Acetaminophen Toxicity
| Time Since Ingestion | Management |
|----------------------|------------|
| <4 hours | Activated charcoal 1 g/kg |
| 4-24 hours | Draw APAP level; Plot on Rumack-Matthew nomogram |
| Level above treatment line | NAC (IV or PO) |
| >24 hours or Unknown | NAC if detectable APAP level or elevated AST |

## Salicylate (Aspirin) Toxicity
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Respiratory Alkalosis | Supportive |
| Metabolic Acidosis | Sodium Bicarbonate for urinary alkalinization |
| Altered Mental Status | Hemodialysis indication |
| Hemodialysis Indications | AMS; Pulmonary edema; Renal failure; Level >100 mg/dL |

## Toxic Alcohols
| Finding | Ethylene Glycol | Methanol |
|---------|-----------------|----------|
| Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis | Present | Present |
| End-Organ Toxicity | Renal failure | Visual loss |
| Antidote | Fomepizole or Ethanol | Fomepizole or Ethanol |

## Tricyclic Antidepressant (TCA) Toxicity
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Wide QRS (>100 ms) | Sodium Bicarbonate 1-2 mEq/kg bolus |
| Hypotension | Sodium Bicarbonate; Norepinephrine |
| Seizures | Benzodiazepines |
| Ventricular Arrhythmias | Sodium Bicarbonate; Lidocaine; Avoid Class IA, IC, III |

## Serotonin Syndrome vs NMS
| Feature | Serotonin Syndrome | Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| Onset | Hours to days | Days to weeks |
| Neuromuscular | Hyperreflexia, Clonus | Rigidity (lead-pipe), Bradyreflexia |
| Treatment | Benzodiazepines; Cyproheptadine | Bromocriptine; Dantrolene |

## Common Toxidromes
| Toxidrome | Signs | Common Agents | Antidote |
|-----------|-------|---------------|----------|
| Anticholinergic | Hot, dry, red, mad | Antihistamines, TCAs | Physostigmine |
| Cholinergic | SLUDGE + DUMBELS | Organophosphates | Atropine + Pralidoxime |
| Opioid | CNS depression, Miosis | Heroin, Fentanyl | Naloxone |
| Sympathomimetic | Tachycardia, Mydriasis | Cocaine, Amphetamines | Benzodiazepines |
