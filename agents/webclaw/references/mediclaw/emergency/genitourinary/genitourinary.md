# Emergency Medicine - Genitourinary Emergencies

## Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)
| Type | Etiology | Management |
|------|----------|------------|
| Pre-renal | Hypovolemia, Hypotension | Fluid resuscitation |
| Intrinsic (ATN) | Ischemia, Nephrotoxins | Treat underlying cause |
| Post-renal | Obstruction | Bladder catheter; Relieve obstruction |
| Emergent Dialysis | **AEIOU**: Acidosis, Electrolytes, Ingestion, Overload, Uremia |

## Nephrolithiasis (Kidney Stones)
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Diagnosis | Non-contrast CT (preferred); Ultrasound |
| Pain Control | NSAIDs (Ketorolac) OR Opioids |
| Medical Expulsive Therapy | Tamsulosin 0.4 mg daily for stones 5-10 mm |
| Admission Indications | Intractable pain, Infection, Solitary kidney, AKI |
| Septic Stone | Antibiotics + Emergent decompression |

## Urinary Tract Infections (UTI)
| Type | Management |
|------|------------|
| Uncomplicated Cystitis | Nitrofurantoin 100 mg BID x 5 days |
| Pyelonephritis (Uncomplicated) | Ceftriaxone 1-2g IV daily |
| Pyelonephritis (Complicated) | IV antibiotics; Admit |
| Asymptomatic Bacteriuria | Do NOT treat (except pregnancy) |

## Testicular Emergencies
| Condition | Presentation | Management |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Testicular Torsion | Sudden severe pain, Absent cremasteric | **Surgical emergency** (<6 hrs) |
| Epididymitis | Gradual pain, Prehn's sign positive | Antibiotics; Scrotal support |
| Fournier's Gangrene | Necrotizing fasciitis of perineum | Broad-spectrum antibiotics; Debridement |

## Priapism
| Type | Mechanism | Management |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Ischemic (Low-flow) | Venous obstruction; Painful | **Urologic emergency**; Aspiration |
| Non-ischemic (High-flow) | AV fistula; Painless | Observation; Embolization |

## Acute Urinary Retention
| Etiology | Management |
|----------|------------|
| Obstructive (BPH) | Bladder catheterization; Tamsulosin |
| Neurogenic | Bladder catheterization |
| Clot Retention | Large-bore catheter; Continuous bladder irrigation |

## Rhabdomyolysis
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| CK Elevation | Aggressive hydration (200-300 mL/hr) |
| Urinary Alkalinization | Consider if CK >5,000-10,000 |
| Electrolyte Management | Monitor K+, Ca2+, Phosphate |
| Dialysis Indications | Refractory hyperkalemia, Acidosis, Fluid overload |
