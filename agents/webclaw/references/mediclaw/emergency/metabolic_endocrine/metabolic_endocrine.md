# Emergency Medicine - Metabolic and Endocrine Emergencies

## Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Diagnosis | Glucose >250; pH <7.3; HCO3 <18; Ketones positive |
| Fluids | 0.9% NaCl 15-20 mL/kg/hr first hour |
| Insulin | Regular insulin 0.1 units/kg bolus; then 0.1 units/kg/hr |
| Potassium | If K+ <3.3, hold insulin; If 3.3-5.3, add 20-30 mEq/L |
| Bicarbonate | Only if pH <6.9 |
| Transition | When glucose <200 AND anion gap closed |

## Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Diagnosis | Glucose >600; Osmolality >320; pH >7.3 |
| Fluids | More aggressive than DKA; Often 6-9 L deficit |
| Insulin | Lower doses than DKA (0.05 units/kg/hr) |
| Electrolytes | Monitor K+ closely |

## Hypoglycemia
| Severity | Management |
|----------|------------|
| Conscious | Oral glucose (15-20g); Recheck in 15 min |
| Unconscious | D50 1 amp (25g) IV OR Glucagon 1 mg IM |
| Sulfonylurea-induced | Octreotide 50-100 mcg SC q6-12h |

## Adrenal Crisis
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Presentation | Hypotension, Hyponatremia, Hyperkalemia, Hypoglycemia |
| Treatment | Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV bolus; Then 50 mg q6h |
| Fluids | Aggressive IVF resuscitation |

## Myxedema Coma
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Presentation | Hypothermia, Bradycardia, AMS, Hypotension |
| Treatment | Levothyroxine 200-400 mcg IV load; Then 50-100 mcg daily |
| Steroids | Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV (until adrenal insufficiency ruled out) |

## Thyroid Storm
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Presentation | Hyperthermia, Tachycardia, AMS, CHF |
| Treatment | Propranolol 1 mg IV; PTU 500-1000 mg load; Hydrocortisone 100 mg |
| Supportive | Cooling; IVF; Treat precipitating cause |

## Hyperkalemia
| Severity | Management |
|----------|------------|
| ECG Changes | **Calcium gluconate/chloride 1g IV** (stabilizes membrane) |
| Shift K+ | Insulin 10 units + D50 1 amp; Albuterol nebulized; Bicarbonate |
| Eliminate K+ | Furosemide; Kayexalate; Dialysis |

## Hypokalemia
| Severity | Management |
|----------|------------|
| Mild (3.0-3.4) | Oral replacement |
| Moderate (2.5-2.9) | IV replacement 10-20 mEq/hr |
| Severe (<2.5) or ECG changes | IV replacement 20-40 mEq/hr; Cardiac monitoring |

## Hypernatremia
| Correction Rate | Management |
|-----------------|------------|
| Chronic (>48 hrs) | Correct at <=0.5 mEq/L/hr (max 10-12 per day) |
| Acute (<48 hrs) | Can correct more rapidly (1-2 mEq/L/hr) |
| Formula | Free water deficit = 0.6 x weight x (Na/140 - 1) |

## Hyponatremia
| Severity | Management |
|----------|------------|
| Severe symptoms (seizures, coma) | Hypertonic saline 3% 100 mL bolus over 10 min |
| Asymptomatic | Fluid restriction; Treat underlying cause |
| Correction Limit | <=8 mEq/L per 24 hrs (risk of osmotic demyelination) |
