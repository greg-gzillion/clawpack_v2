## Bradyarrhythmias

|------------|------------|------------|-----|
| Sinus Bradycardia | HR <60 bpm; normal P waves | Treat if symptomatic; atropine; pacemaker | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Sick Sinus Syndrome | Sinus pauses; bradycardia-tachycardia | Pacemaker if symptomatic | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |

## Supraventricular Tachycardias (SVT)
| Type | Mechanism | ECG Features | Acute Management | Chronic Management | URL |
|------|-----------|--------------|------------------|-------------------|-----|
| AVNRT | Dual AV nodal pathways | P waves buried in QRS or pseudo-R' in V1 | Vagal maneuvers; Adenosine | Ablation (Class I) | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| AVRT | Accessory pathway (WPW) | Delta wave (if pre-excited); orthodromic (narrow); antidromic (wide) | Adenosine (if orthodromic); Procainamide | Ablation (Class I) | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Atrial Tachycardia | Ectopic atrial focus | P waves before QRS; different morphology than sinus | Adenosine; Beta-blockers; CCB | Ablation; Beta-blockers | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Atrial Flutter | Macroreentry (typical: CTI-dependent) | Sawtooth flutter waves; rate ~150 bpm (2:1) or ~300 bpm | Rate control; Cardioversion | Ablation (Class I) | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Atrial Fibrillation | Chaotic atrial activity | Irregularly irregular; no P waves | Rate control; Anticoagulation | Rate vs Rhythm control; Anticoagulation | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |

## Atrial Fibrillation
| Aspect | Recommendation | URL |
|--------|----------------|-----|
| Rate Control Target | Resting HR <80 bpm (lenient); <110 bpm acceptable | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Rate Control Agents | Beta-blockers; CCB (Diltiazem, Verapamil); Digoxin | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Rhythm Control Indications | Symptomatic despite rate control; young; first episode; tachycardia-mediated cardiomyopathy | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Antiarrhythmic Drugs | Flecainide; Propafenone (no structural HD); Amiodarone; Dofetilide; Sotalol | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Catheter Ablation | Class I for symptomatic paroxysmal AF refractory/intolerant to drugs; Class I for persistent AF | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |

## Ventricular Arrhythmias
| Arrhythmia | Definition | Acute Management | Chronic Management | URL |
|------------|------------|------------------|-------------------|-----|
| Premature Ventricular Complexes (PVCs) | Early, wide QRS | Usually none | Beta-blockers; Ablation if symptomatic/high burden | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Non-Sustained VT | =3 beats; <30 sec | Treat underlying cause | Beta-blockers; ICD if high-risk | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Sustained Monomorphic VT | =30 sec; uniform morphology | Cardioversion (unstable); Amiodarone/Lidocaine (stable) | ICD; Antiarrhythmics; Ablation | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Polymorphic VT/Torsades | Changing QRS axis; long QT | Magnesium; Isoproterenol; Pacing; Cardioversion | Correct QT prolongation; ICD | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Ventricular Fibrillation | Chaotic electrical activity | Immediate defibrillation | ICD | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
