# Cardiology - Cardiac Emergencies

## Cardiac Arrest - ACLS Algorithm
| Rhythm | Management | URL |
|--------|------------|-----|
| VF/pVT | Immediate defibrillation; CPR; Epinephrine; Amiodarone/Lidocaine | https://www.heart.org/acls |
| Asystole/PEA | CPR; Epinephrine; Treat reversible causes (Hs and Ts) | https://www.heart.org/acls |

## Post-Cardiac Arrest Care
| Component | Target | URL |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| Targeted Temperature Management (TTM) | 32-36°C for =24 hours | https://www.heart.org/acls |
| Hemodynamics | MAP =65 mmHg | https://www.heart.org/acls |
| Oxygenation | SpO2 92-98%; Avoid hyperoxia | https://www.heart.org/acls |
| Ventilation | PaCO2 35-45 mmHg | https://www.heart.org/acls |
| Glucose | 144-180 mg/dL | https://www.heart.org/acls |
| Coronary Angiography | Emergent for STEMI; Consider for all comatose survivors | https://www.heart.org/acls |

## Cardiogenic Shock
| Definition | SBP <90 mmHg for >30 min OR need for vasopressors + CI <2.2 L/min/m2 + PCWP >15 mmHg | URL |
|-----------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Causes | ACS (most common); Acute MR/VSD; Myocarditis; Takotsubo; End-stage HF | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Management | Revascularization (if ACS); Inotropes/Vasopressors; MCS (IABP; Impella; VA-ECMO) | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |

## Hypertensive Emergency
| End-Organ Damage | BP Goal (First Hour) | Agents | URL |
|------------------|---------------------|--------|-----|
| Aortic Dissection | SBP 100-120 mmHg within 20 min | Esmolol; Labetalol; Nicardipine | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Acute Pulmonary Edema | 10-20% MAP reduction | Nitroglycerin; Nicardipine | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| ACS | 10-20% MAP reduction | Nitroglycerin; Beta-blocker | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Acute Ischemic Stroke | No acute reduction unless >220/120 (or thrombolysis candidate >185/110) | Labetalol; Nicardipine | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| ICH | SBP <140 mmHg | Nicardipine; Labetalol | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Encephalopathy | 10-20% MAP reduction | Nicardipine; Labetalol | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |

## Cardiac Tamponade
| Feature | Description | URL |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Beck's Triad | Hypotension; Muffled heart sounds; JVD | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Pulsus Paradoxus | SBP decrease >10 mmHg during inspiration | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Echo | RA/RV diastolic collapse; IVC plethora | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Management | Pericardiocentesis; Pericardial window | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |

## Massive Pulmonary Embolism
| Feature | Description | URL |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Definition | PE with hypotension (SBP <90 mmHg for >15 min) or requiring vasopressors | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Management | Thrombolytics (tPA); Catheter-directed thrombolysis; Embolectomy; VA-ECMO if refractory | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |

## Submassive Pulmonary Embolism
| Definition | PE with RV dysfunction (echo/CT) or elevated biomarkers without hypotension | URL |
|-----------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Management | Anticoagulation; Consider catheter-directed thrombolysis (selected patients) | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
