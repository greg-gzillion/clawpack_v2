# Heart Failure (HF)

## Definition
Inability of the heart to pump sufficient blood to meet metabolic demands

## Classification
- HFrEF (reduced EF): EF ≤40%
- HFimpEF (improved EF): previous EF ≤40%, now >40%
- HFmrEF (mildly reduced): EF 41-49%
- HFpEF (preserved): EF ≥50%

## Causes
- Ischemic heart disease (most common)
- Hypertension
- Valvular disease
- Cardiomyopathy (dilated, hypertrophic, restrictive)
- Myocarditis
- Alcohol/toxic exposure

## Symptoms
- Dyspnea (exertional, paroxysmal nocturnal, orthopnea)
- Fatigue
- Edema (peripheral, pulmonary)
- Jugular venous distension
- Hepatojugular reflux
- S3 gallop

## Diagnosis
- BNP/NT-proBNP: elevated
- Echocardiogram: EF, chamber sizes, valvular function
- Chest X-ray: cardiomegaly, pulmonary edema
- ECG: ischemia, arrhythmias

## Treatment

### HFrEF (GDMT)
- **ARNI** (sacubitril/valsartan) or ACE inhibitor/ARB
- **Beta-blocker** (carvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol)
- **MRA** (spironolactone, eplerenone)
- **SGLT2 inhibitor** (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin)
- Diuretics for volume overload (furosemide, torsemide)

### HFpEF
- SGLT2 inhibitors
- Diuretics for congestion
- BP control
- Treat underlying conditions

### Advanced HF
- ICD for primary prevention (EF ≤35%)
- CRT if LBBB + QRS ≥150ms
- LVAD (bridge to transplant or destination)
- Heart transplantation

## Monitoring
- Daily weights
- Signs of volume overload
- Labs: BMP, Mg, serial EF

## Prognosis
- 1-year mortality: 20-30%
- 5-year mortality: 50-60%
