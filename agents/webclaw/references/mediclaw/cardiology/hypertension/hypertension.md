## Resistant Hypertension
| Definition | BP above goal despite 3 agents (including diuretic) at maximally tolerated doses | URL |
|-----------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Causes | Medication non-adherence; White coat HTN; Secondary HTN; Volume overload; Obstructive sleep apnea | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Management | Optimize diuretic (Chlorthalidone); Add mineralocorticoid antagonist (Spironolactone); Consider secondary causes | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |

## Hypertensive Emergency
| Definition | SBP >180 and/or DBP >120 with acute end-organ damage | URL |
|-----------|---------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| End-Organ Damage | Encephalopathy; ICH; acute ischemic stroke; acute pulmonary edema; ACS; aortic dissection; eclampsia | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Goal | Reduce MAP by 10-20% in first hour; then to 160/100 over 2-6 hours (except aortic dissection: rapid reduction) | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Agents | IV Nicardipine; Labetalol; Esmolol; Clevidipine; Nitroprusside | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |

## Hypertensive Urgency
| Definition | SBP >180 and/or DBP >120 without acute end-organ damage | URL |
|-----------|-----------------------------------------------------------|-----|
| Management | Oral antihypertensives; gradual reduction over 24-48 hours | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Avoid | Rapid reduction; IV agents unless end-organ damage | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |

## Hypertension in Pregnancy
| Category | Definition | Management | URL |
|----------|------------|------------|-----|
| Chronic HTN | HTN before pregnancy or <20 weeks | Labetalol; Nifedipine; Methyldopa | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Gestational HTN | New HTN =20 weeks without proteinuria | Monitor; deliver at 37 weeks | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Preeclampsia | New HTN =20 weeks with proteinuria or end-organ damage | Magnesium for seizure prophylaxis; delivery | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Eclampsia | Preeclampsia with seizures | Magnesium; delivery | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Avoid | ACE-I; ARB; Direct renin inhibitors (teratogenic) | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
