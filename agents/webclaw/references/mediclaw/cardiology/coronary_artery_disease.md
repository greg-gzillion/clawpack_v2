# Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

## Definition
Atherosclerosis of epicardial coronary arteries causing myocardial ischemia

## Risk Factors
- Non-modifiable: age, male sex, family history
- Modifiable: HTN, dyslipidemia, DM, smoking, obesity, sedentary lifestyle

## Clinical Syndromes

### Chronic Coronary Syndrome (Stable Angina)
- Predictable chest discomfort with exertion
- Relieved by rest or nitroglycerin
- No biomarker elevation

### Acute Coronary Syndrome
- Unstable angina: rest symptoms, no biomarkers
- NSTEMI: biomarker elevation, no ST elevation
- STEMI: ST elevation + biomarker elevation

## Diagnosis
- ECG: ST changes, T inversions, Q waves
- Troponin: elevated in NSTEMI/STEMI
- Stress testing: exercise ECG, stress echo, nuclear
- Coronary angiography: gold standard

## Treatment

### Stable CAD
- Aspirin 81mg daily
- High-intensity statin (atorvastatin 40-80mg, rosuvastatin 20-40mg)
- Beta-blocker or CCB for symptoms
- ACE inhibitor/ARB
- Nitroglycerin PRN

### STEMI (Emergency)
- Primary PCI (door-to-balloon <90 min)
- Dual antiplatelet: aspirin + ticagrelor/prasugrel/clopidogrel
- Anticoagulation: heparin
- High-intensity statin
- Beta-blocker within 24 hours

## Secondary Prevention (ABCDE)
- Antiplatelet, ACE inhibitor
- Beta-blocker, BP control
- Cholesterol control, Cigarette cessation
- Diet, Diabetes management
- Education, Exercise
