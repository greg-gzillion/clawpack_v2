# Hypertension

## Definition
Persistently elevated arterial blood pressure ≥130/80 mmHg (ACC/AHA 2017)

## Classification
- Normal: <120/80
- Elevated: 120-129/<80  
- Stage 1: 130-139/80-89
- Stage 2: ≥140/90

## Causes
- Primary (essential): 90-95%, multifactorial
- Secondary: renal artery stenosis, primary aldosteronism, pheochromocytoma, renal parenchymal disease

## Symptoms
- Usually asymptomatic
- Severe: headache, dizziness, blurred vision, epistaxis

## Diagnosis
- Confirm with 2+ readings on 2+ occasions
- Basic labs: BMP, lipids, TSH, UA, ECG

## Treatment

### Lifestyle
- DASH diet
- Sodium <1500-2300 mg/day
- Exercise 150 min/week
- Weight loss (BMI <25)
- Alcohol moderation
- Smoking cessation

### Medications (First-line)
- Thiazide diuretics (chlorthalidone, HCTZ)
- ACE inhibitors (lisinopril, enalapril)
- ARBs (losartan, valsartan)
- CCBs (amlodipine, nifedipine)

### Monitoring
- BP checks at each visit
- Annual labs: BMP, lipids
- Goal BP <130/80

## Emergency (Hypertensive crisis)
- BP >180/120 with end-organ damage
- Requires immediate hospitalization
- IV agents: labetalol, nicardipine, nitroprusside
