# Addiction Medicine - Overview

## What is Addiction Medicine?
Addiction medicine is the medical specialty concerned with the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of substance use disorders and related conditions. It encompasses the biopsychosocial approach to understanding and treating addiction as a chronic, relapsing brain disease.

| Core Principle | Description | URL |
|----------------|-------------|-----|
| **Addiction is a Brain Disease** | **Chronic, relapsing disorder** characterized by **compulsive substance use** despite harmful consequences | https://www.asam.org/quality-care/definition-of-addiction |
| **Biopsychosocial Model** | **Biological, psychological, and social factors** contribute to development and maintenance of addiction | https://www.asam.org/quality-care/definition-of-addiction |
| **Chronic Disease Management** | **Long-term, recovery-oriented approach** similar to other chronic diseases (diabetes, hypertension) | https://www.asam.org/quality-care/definition-of-addiction |
| **Harm Reduction** | **Practical strategies** to reduce negative consequences of substance use | https://www.samhsa.gov/find-help/harm-reduction |

## DSM-5-TR Substance Use Disorder Criteria
| Criterion | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Impaired Control** | **1**. Taking larger amounts/longer than intended; **2**. Persistent desire/unsuccessful efforts to cut down; **3**. Great deal of time spent; **4**. **Craving** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Social Impairment** | **5**. Failure to fulfill obligations; **6**. Continued use despite social problems; **7**. Activities given up | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Risky Use** | **8**. Use in hazardous situations; **9**. Continued use despite physical/psychological problems | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Pharmacologic** | **10**. **Tolerance**; **11**. **Withdrawal** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Severity** | **Mild**: 2-3 criteria; **Moderate**: 4-5 criteria; **Severe**: =6 criteria | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
