## Benzodiazepine Withdrawal
| Stage | Timing (Short-Acting) | Timing (Long-Acting) | Symptoms | URL |
|-------|----------------------|----------------------|----------|-----|
| **Onset** | **6-12h** | **1-3 days** | **Anxiety**, **Insomnia**, **Tremor**, **Diaphoresis** | https://www.benzo.org.uk/manual/ |

| **Severe** | **Variable** | **Variable** | **Seizures**, **Delirium**, **Psychosis** | https://www.benzo.org.uk/manual/ |

## Benzodiazepine Taper
| Principle | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Convert to Long-Acting** | **Diazepam** preferred (long half-life, Available in low doses) | https://www.benzo.org.uk/manual/ |
| **Slow Taper** | **Reduce 5-10% every 1-2 weeks** (or slower) | https://www.benzo.org.uk/manual/ |
| **Duration** | **Weeks to months** (often 6-12 months) | https://www.benzo.org.uk/manual/ |
| **Adjunctive Medications** | **Gabapentin**, **Pregabalin**, **Hydroxyzine**, **Trazodone** | https://www.benzo.org.uk/manual/ |
| **Avoid Flumazenil** | **Precipitates severe withdrawal/seizures** in dependent patients | https://www.benzo.org.uk/manual/ |

## Barbiturate Withdrawal
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|

| **Management** | **Phenobarbital substitution** and **slow taper** (often inpatient) | https://www.nida.nih.gov/research-topics/prescription-drugs |
