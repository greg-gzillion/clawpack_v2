# Toxicology - Toxicology Screening

## Urine Drug Screening (UDS)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Method** | **Immunoassay** (screening) ? **GC-MS or LC-MS/MS** (confirmatory) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Detection Window** | **1-4 days** for most drugs (longer for chronic use, marijuana) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Limitations** | **False positives** (cross-reactivity), **False negatives** (cutoff thresholds, Detection window), **Does NOT measure impairment** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |

## Common UDS False Positives
| Substance Tested | False Positive Caused By | URL |
|------------------|-------------------------|-----|
| **Amphetamines** | **Pseudoephedrine**, **Ephedrine**, **Bupropion**, **Trazodone**, **Ranitidine**, **Selegiline** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Benzodiazepines** | **Oxaprozin**, **Sertraline** (rare) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Opioids** | **Poppy seeds**, **Quinolones**, **Rifampin**, **Dextromethorphan** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **PCP** | **Dextromethorphan**, **Diphenhydramine**, **Ketamine**, **Lamotrigine**, **Venlafaxine** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Cannabis (THC)** | **NSAIDs** (ibuprofen, naproxen), **Pantoprazole**, **Efavirenz**, **Baby soap** (topical) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Tricyclic Antidepressants** | **Cyclobenzaprine**, **Quetiapine**, **Carbamazepine**, **Diphenhydramine** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |

## Serum Drug Levels
| Drug | Therapeutic Range | Toxic Level | Timing | URL |
|------|------------------|-------------|--------|-----|
| **Acetaminophen** | 10-20 mcg/mL | **=150 mcg/mL at 4h** (Rumack-Matthew nomogram) | **=4h post-ingestion** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Salicylate** | 15-30 mg/dL (analgesic), 10-20 mg/dL (antiplatelet) | **>30 mg/dL** (mild), **>60 mg/dL** (moderate), **>100 mg/dL** (severe) | **=2h post-ingestion** (repeat q2-4h until peak) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Lithium** | 0.6-1.2 mEq/L | **>1.5 mEq/L** | **Trough** (=12h post-dose) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Digoxin** | 0.5-0.9 ng/mL (HF), 0.8-2.0 ng/mL (AF) | **>2.0 ng/mL** | **Trough** (=6h post-dose) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Phenytoin** | 10-20 mcg/mL (total) | **>20 mcg/mL** | **Trough** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Valproic Acid** | 50-100 mcg/mL | **>120 mcg/mL** | **Trough** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Theophylline** | 10-20 mcg/mL | **>20 mcg/mL** | **Peak** (1-2h post-dose) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Iron** | 50-150 mcg/dL | **>350 mcg/dL** | **4-6h post-ingestion** (peak) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
