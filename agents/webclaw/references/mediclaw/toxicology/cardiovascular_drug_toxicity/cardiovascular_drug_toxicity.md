## High-Dose Insulin Euglycemia (HIE) Protocol
| Step | Procedure | URL |
|------|-----------|-----|
| **1** | **Insulin** 1 unit/kg IV bolus | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **2** | **Insulin infusion** 0.5-1 unit/kg/h (titrate to hemodynamic response) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **3** | **Dextrose** infusion (D10W or D50W) to maintain euglycemia | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **4** | **Monitor glucose q15-30min** initially | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **5** | **Monitor potassium** (replace if <3.0 mEq/L) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |

## Digoxin
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Mechanism** | **Inhibits Na/K-ATPase** ? **? Intracellular Na** ? **? Intracellular Ca** (? contractility, ? automaticity, ? AV conduction) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/postmarket-drug-safety-information-patients-and-providers/digoxin |
| **Toxic Level** | **>2.0 ng/mL** (therapeutic 0.5-0.9 ng/mL HF, 0.8-2.0 ng/mL AF) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Risk Factors** | **Hypokalemia**, **Hypomagnesemia**, **Hypercalcemia**, **Renal failure**, **Amiodarone/Verapamil interaction** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Acute Toxicity** | **Nausea**, **Vomiting**, **Hyperkalemia** (due to Na/K-ATPase inhibition), **Bradycardia**, **AV block** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Chronic Toxicity** | **Nausea**, **Anorexia**, **Visual changes** (yellow-green halos, Scotomas), **Arrhythmias** (PAT with block, Bidirectional VT, Ventricular tachycardia/fibrillation) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **ECG Findings** | **Scooped ST segments** ("Salvador Dali mustache"), **Shortened QT**, **T-wave flattening/inversion**, **Arrhythmias** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Management** | **Digoxin Immune Fab (Digibind)** (indicated for life-threatening arrhythmias, Hyperkalemia >5.5, Hemodynamic instability), **Atropine** (bradycardia), **Lidocaine/Phenytoin** (ventricular arrhythmias), **Avoid calcium** (may worsen toxicity), **Avoid cardioversion** (unless unstable) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
