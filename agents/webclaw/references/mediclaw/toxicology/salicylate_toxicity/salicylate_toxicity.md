# Toxicology - Salicylate Toxicity

## Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Toxic Dose** | **>150 mg/kg** (acute), **>100 mg/kg/day** (chronic) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Mechanism** | **Uncouples oxidative phosphorylation**, **Stimulates respiratory center** (? RR), **? Anion gap metabolic acidosis**, **Tinnitus**, **Nausea/Vomiting** | https://www.atsdr.cdc.gov/toxprofiles/tp178.pdf |

## Clinical Stages
| Severity | Serum Level (Acute) | Symptoms | URL |
|----------|---------------------|----------|-----|
| **Mild** | **30-50 mg/dL** | **Tinnitus**, **Nausea**, **Vomiting**, **Tachypnea** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Moderate** | **50-100 mg/dL** | **Hyperpnea**, **Fever**, **Lethargy**, **Dehydration** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Severe** | **>100 mg/dL** | **Altered mental status**, **Seizures**, **Pulmonary edema**, **Cerebral edema**, **Cardiovascular collapse** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Chronic Toxicity** | **>30-40 mg/dL** | **May have severe symptoms at lower levels** (due to CNS penetration) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |

## Laboratory Findings
| Finding | Mechanism | URL |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| **? Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis** | **Accumulation of organic acids** (lactate, ketoacids) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Respiratory Alkalosis** | **Direct stimulation of respiratory center** (early) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Mixed Acid-Base Disturbance** | **Primary respiratory alkalosis + Primary metabolic acidosis** (classic) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Hypokalemia** | **Renal losses** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Hyperglycemia** (early) ? **Hypoglycemia** (late) | **Altered glucose metabolism** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |

## Management of Salicylate Toxicity
| Intervention | Indication | URL |
|--------------|------------|-----|
| **Activated Charcoal** | **Recent ingestion** (<2h); **Multidose AC** may be beneficial | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **IV Fluids** | **Aggressive rehydration** (corrects dehydration, Enhances renal excretion) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Urine Alkalinization** | **First-line for moderate-severe toxicity** (target urine pH =7.5) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Potassium Replacement** | **Essential for urine alkalinization** (hypokalemia impairs alkalinization); Target **K >4.0 mEq/L** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Hemodialysis** | **Indications**: **Altered mental status**, **Pulmonary edema**, **Cerebral edema**, **Renal failure**, **Refractory acidosis**, **Level >100 mg/dL** (acute) or **>60 mg/dL** (chronic) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Avoid Intubation if Possible** | **High risk of rapid deterioration** (loss of respiratory alkalosis ? Worsening acidosis) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **If Intubation Required** | **Maintain high minute ventilation** (match patient's hyperventilation) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
