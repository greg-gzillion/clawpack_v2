# Toxicology - Acetaminophen Toxicity

## Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Toxic Dose** | **>150 mg/kg** (or **>7.5-10 g** in adults) within 24h | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Mechanism** | **CYP2E1** metabolizes acetaminophen to **NAPQI** ? **Glutathione depletion** ? **Centrilobular hepatic necrosis** | https://www.atsdr.cdc.gov/toxprofiles/tp162.pdf |
| **Risk Factors** | **Chronic alcohol use**, **Fasting/Malnutrition**, **CYP2E1 inducers** (isoniazid, rifampin, phenytoin, carbamazepine) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |

## Clinical Stages
| Stage | Time | Symptoms | Labs | URL |
|-------|------|----------|------|-----|
| **Stage 1** | **0-24h** | **Nausea**, **Vomiting**, **Malaise**, **Anorexia** | **Normal LFTs** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Stage 2** | **24-72h** | **RUQ pain**, **Tachycardia**, **Hypotension** | **? AST/ALT** (starts 24-36h) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Stage 3** | **72-96h** | **Hepatic failure** (jaundice, Encephalopathy, Coagulopathy, Hypoglycemia, Renal failure) | **Peak AST/ALT** (often >10,000), **? PT/INR**, **? Bilirubin**, **? Cr** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Stage 4** | **4d - 2 weeks** | **Recovery** (or death) | **? LFTs** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |

## Rumack-Matthew Nomogram
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Purpose** | **Predicts hepatotoxicity risk** based on **serum acetaminophen level** and **time since ingestion** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Valid Timing** | **4-24 hours** after **single acute ingestion** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Treatment Threshold** | **=150 mcg/mL at 4 hours** (line from 150 at 4h to 4.7 at 24h) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **NOT Valid For** | **Chronic ingestion**, **Staggered ingestion**, **Unknown time of ingestion**, **>24h post-ingestion**, **Extended-release preparations** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |

## N-Acetylcysteine (NAC) Protocols
| Protocol | Route | Dose | Duration | URL |
|----------|-------|------|----------|-----|
| **IV (21-hour)** | **IV** | **150 mg/kg** over 1h ? **50 mg/kg** over 4h ? **100 mg/kg** over 16h | **21 hours** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/postmarket-drug-safety-information-patients-and-providers/acetylcysteine |
| **Oral (72-hour)** | **PO** | **140 mg/kg** load ? **70 mg/kg** q4h x17 doses | **72 hours** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |

## Indications for NAC
| Scenario | Recommendation | URL |
|----------|----------------|-----|
| **Acute ingestion with known time** | **Level above treatment line** on nomogram | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Acute ingestion >4h, level not yet available** | **Start NAC** (can discontinue if level below line) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Acute ingestion >8h, patient not yet seen** | **Start NAC immediately** (do not wait for level) | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Chronic/staggered ingestion** | **Start NAC if**: **AST/ALT elevated** OR **Acetaminophen level >20 mcg/mL** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Late presentation (>24h)** | **Start NAC if**: **AST/ALT elevated** OR **Acetaminophen detectable** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Hepatic failure** | **Continue NAC** until **clinical improvement** and **LFTs trending down** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
