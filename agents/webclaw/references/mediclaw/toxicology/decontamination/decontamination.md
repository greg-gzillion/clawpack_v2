# Toxicology - Decontamination

## Gastrointestinal Decontamination
| Method | Indications | Contraindications | Dose/Procedure | URL |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|----------------|-----|
| **Activated Charcoal (AC)** | **Recent ingestion** (<1-2h) of **charcoal-adsorbable toxin** | **Altered mental status** (unprotected airway), **Caustics**, **Hydrocarbons**, **Bowel obstruction/perforation** | **1 g/kg** (max 50-100 g) PO/NG | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Multidose Activated Charcoal (MDAC)** | **Carbamazepine**, **Dapsone**, **Phenobarbital**, **Quinine**, **Theophylline** | Same as single-dose AC | **0.5-1 g/kg q4-6h** | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Gastric Lavage** | **Life-threatening ingestion** within **1 hour** | **Caustics**, **Hydrocarbons**, **Unprotected airway**, **Small pills** | **200-300 mL aliquots** via orogastric tube | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
| **Whole Bowel Irrigation (WBI)** | **Sustained-release preparations**, **Drug packets** (body packers), **Metals** (iron, lead) | **Ileus**, **Bowel obstruction/perforation**, **Hemodynamic instability** | **PEG solution 1-2 L/h** (adults) or **25 mL/kg/h** (children) until rectal effluent clear | https://www.acmt.net/Resources/Clinical-Resources.aspx |
