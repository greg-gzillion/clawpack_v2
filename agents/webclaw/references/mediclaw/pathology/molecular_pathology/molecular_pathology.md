# Pathology - Molecular Pathology

## Overview
Molecular pathology involves the analysis of nucleic acids (DNA, RNA) and proteins to diagnose disease, predict prognosis, and guide targeted therapy.

| Technique | Description | Applications | URL |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|-----|
| **PCR** | Amplification of specific DNA sequences | Infectious disease, Mutation detection, Clonality | https://www.amp.org/education/molecular-testing-resources/ |
| **RT-PCR** | Reverse transcription + PCR for RNA | Gene expression, Viral load (HIV, HCV) | https://www.amp.org/education/molecular-testing-resources/ |
| **Sanger Sequencing** | Gold standard for single gene sequencing | BRCA1/2, EGFR, KRAS, BRAF | https://www.genome.gov/genetics-glossary/Sanger-Sequencing |
| **Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)** | Massively parallel sequencing | Comprehensive genomic profiling, Hereditary cancer panels | https://www.genome.gov/about-genomics/fact-sheets/Next-Generation-Sequencing-Fact-Sheet |
| **FISH** | Fluorescent probes hybridize to specific DNA sequences | HER2 amplification, ALK/ROS1 rearrangements, 1p/19q codeletion | https://www.genome.gov/genetics-glossary/Fluorescence-In-Situ-Hybridization |
| **Microarray** | Genome-wide analysis of copy number and SNPs | Chromosomal microarray (CMA), Gene expression profiling | https://www.genome.gov/about-genomics/fact-sheets/Chromosomal-Microarray-Analysis-Fact-Sheet |
