# Pathology - Cytopathology

## Overview
Cytopathology is the study of individual cells to diagnose disease. It includes exfoliative cytology (Pap smears, fluids) and fine-needle aspiration (FNA).

| Specimen Type | Description | URL |
|---------------|-------------|-----|
| **Gynecologic Cytology** | **Pap test** (conventional or liquid-based) | https://www.cancer.gov/types/cervical/pap-hpv-testing-fact-sheet |
| **Non-Gynecologic Cytology** | Urine, CSF, Pleural/Peritoneal fluid, Bronchial washings | https://www.cytopathology.org |
| **Fine-Needle Aspiration (FNA)** | Sampling of palpable or image-guided lesions | https://www.cytopathology.org/fna |

| Category | Risk of CIN3+ | Management | URL |
|----------|---------------|------------|-----|
| **NILM** (Negative for Intraepithelial Lesion or Malignancy) | <0.2% | Routine screening | https://www.asccp.org/guidelines |
| **ASC-US** (Atypical Squamous Cells of Undetermined Significance) | 6-7% | **HPV reflex testing** | https://www.asccp.org/guidelines |
| **LSIL** (Low-Grade Squamous Intraepithelial Lesion) | 13-17% | Colposcopy (except age 21-24) | https://www.asccp.org/guidelines |
| **ASC-H** (Atypical Squamous Cells, Cannot Rule Out HSIL) | 26-68% | **Colposcopy** | https://www.asccp.org/guidelines |
| **HSIL** (High-Grade Squamous Intraepithelial Lesion) | 53-84% | **Colposcopy** or immediate LEEP | https://www.asccp.org/guidelines |
| **AGC** (Atypical Glandular Cells) | 9-54% | **Colposcopy + Endometrial sampling** (if =35) | https://www.asccp.org/guidelines |
| **AIS** (Adenocarcinoma in Situ) | >90% | **Colposcopy + Endometrial sampling + Cone biopsy** | https://www.asccp.org/guidelines |
