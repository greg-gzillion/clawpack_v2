|-----------|-------|-----|
| Annual fall rate in adults =65 | 25-30% | https://www.cdc.gov/falls |
| Falls in adults =80 | 50% | https://www.cdc.gov/falls |

## Orthostatic Hypotension Assessment
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Definition | SBP decrease =20 mmHg or DBP decrease =10 mmHg within 3 minutes of standing | https://www.heart.org |
| Measurement | Supine for 5-10 min ? Measure BP/HR ? Stand ? Measure at 1 and 3 min | https://www.cdc.gov/steadi |
| Causes | Hypovolemia, Medications (antihypertensives, diuretics, alpha-blockers), Autonomic dysfunction (DM, Parkinson), Deconditioning | https://www.nia.nih.gov |
| Symptoms | Dizziness, Lightheadedness, Vision changes, Weakness, Syncope, Falls | |
| Management | Review medications, Hydration, Compression stockings, Slow positional changes, Salt supplementation (if not contraindicated), Midodrine or Fludrocortisone | https://www.acc.org |

## Gait Disorders in Older Adults
| Type | Characteristics | Common Causes | URL |
|------|-----------------|---------------|-----|
| **Antalgic Gait** | Limping, Shortened stance phase on painful side | Osteoarthritis, Trauma, Neuropathy | https://www.aaos.org |
| **Ataxic Gait** | Wide-based, Unsteady, Cannot walk tandem | Cerebellar disorders, Sensory ataxia (B12 deficiency, dorsal column), Vestibular dysfunction | https://www.aan.com |
| **Parkinsonian Gait** | Shuffling, Festination, Freezing, En bloc turning, Reduced arm swing | Parkinson disease, Parkinsonism (vascular, drug-induced) | https://www.parkinson.org |
| **Hemiplegic Gait** | Circumduction of affected leg | Stroke | https://www.stroke.org |
| **Frontal Gait Apraxia** | "Magnetic" gait, Difficulty initiating, Wide-based, Short steps | Normal pressure hydrocephalus, Vascular dementia, Frontal lobe lesions | https://www.alz.org |
| **Waddling Gait** | Trendelenburg sign, Pelvic drop | Proximal muscle weakness (myopathy), Hip osteoarthritis | https://www.rheumatology.org |
| **Steppage Gait** | Foot drop, High stepping | Peroneal neuropathy, L5 radiculopathy, Motor neuron disease | https://www.aan.com |
| **Cautious Gait** | Wide-based, Slow, Arms abducted | Fear of falling, Deconditioning | https://www.americangeriatrics.org |

## Multifactorial Fall Interventions
| Intervention | Evidence | Recommendations | URL |
|--------------|----------|-----------------|-----|
| **Exercise** | Strong | Balance, Strength, Gait training (Tai Chi, Otago Exercise Program) | https://www.cdc.gov/steadi |
| **Medication Review** | Strong | Deprescribe psychotropics, antihypertensives; Optimize vitamin D | https://deprescribing.org |
| **Vision Correction** | Moderate | Annual eye exam; Cataract surgery; Avoid bifocals outdoors | https://www.aoa.org |
| **Home Safety** | Moderate | Remove hazards, Improve lighting, Install grab bars, Non-slip mats | https://www.cdc.gov/falls |
| **Footwear** | Moderate | Low-heeled, Non-slip soles, Proper fit | https://www.apma.org |
| **Vitamin D** | Moderate | 800-2000 IU/day (especially if deficient) | https://ods.od.nih.gov |
| **Cardiac Pacing** | Selective | For carotid sinus hypersensitivity, Cardioinhibitory syncope | https://www.heart.org |
| **Assistive Devices** | Selective | Properly fitted cane or walker; Physical therapy training | https://www.aota.org |
