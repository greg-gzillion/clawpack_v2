## Cognitive Screening Tools
| Tool | Time | Cutoff | Sensitivity/Specificity | URL |
|------|------|--------|------------------------|-----|
| Mini-Cog | 2-3 min | =2/5 (word recall) or abnormal clock | 76%/89% for dementia | https://mini-cog.com |
| MoCA | 10 min | <26/30 | 90%/87% for MCI | https://www.mocatest.org |
| MMSE | 7-10 min | <24/30 | 81%/89% for dementia | https://www.parinc.com |

## Gait and Mobility Assessment
| Test | Description | Predictive Value | URL |
|------|-------------|------------------|-----|
| Gait Speed (4-meter walk) | Time to walk 4 meters at usual pace | <0.8 m/s = Increased fall risk, Mortality | https://www.cdc.gov/steadi |
| Timed Up and Go (TUG) | Rise from chair, walk 3m, return, sit | =12 sec = Increased fall risk | https://www.cdc.gov/steadi |
| Short Physical Performance Battery (SPPB) | Balance, Gait speed, Chair stands | =9/12 = Increased disability risk | https://www.nia.nih.gov |
| 30-Second Chair Stand | Number of stands in 30 seconds | <8 (women), <10 (men) = Weakness | https://www.cdc.gov/steadi |
| Berg Balance Scale | 14-item functional balance assessment | =45/56 = Increased fall risk | https://www.sralab.org |
| Functional Reach | Maximal forward reach | <6 inches = Increased fall risk | https://www.cdc.gov/steadi |

## Nutritional Assessment
| Tool | Components | Interpretation | URL |
|------|------------|----------------|-----|
| Mini Nutritional Assessment (MNA-SF) | 6 items (appetite, weight loss, mobility, stress, neuro, BMI) | 12-14 = Normal; 8-11 = At risk; 0-7 = Malnourished | https://www.mna-elderly.com |
| Malnutrition Screening Tool (MST) | 2 items (weight loss, appetite) | =2 = At risk | https://www.nutritioncare.org |
| DETERMINE Checklist | 10 items | Score 0-2 = Low; 3-5 = Moderate; =6 = High risk | https://acl.gov |

## Frailty Assessment Tools
| Tool | Components | Frailty Criteria | URL |
|------|------------|------------------|-----|
| Fried Frailty Phenotype | Weight loss, Exhaustion, Weakness, Slowness, Low activity | =3 = Frail; 1-2 = Pre-frail | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5390682/ |
| Frailty Index (Rockwood) | Accumulation of deficits (=30 variables) | >0.25 = Frail | https://www.dal.ca |
| FRAIL Scale | Fatigue, Resistance, Ambulation, Illness, Loss of weight | =3 = Frail; 1-2 = Pre-frail | https://www.healthinaging.org |
| Clinical Frailty Scale | 9-point pictorial scale | =5 = Frail | https://www.dal.ca/sites/gmr/our-tools/clinical-frailty-scale.html |
