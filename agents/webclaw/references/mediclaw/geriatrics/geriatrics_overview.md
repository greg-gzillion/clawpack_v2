# Geriatrics - Overview

## What is Geriatrics?
Geriatrics is the medical specialty focused on the health care of older adults, typically defined as age 65 and older. It emphasizes the prevention, diagnosis, and management of diseases and disabilities unique to aging, while promoting quality of life, functional independence, and dignity.

## Core Principles of Geriatric Medicine
| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Patient-Centered Care | Focus on individual goals, preferences, and values |
| Functional Assessment | Emphasis on maintaining independence and quality of life |
| Comprehensive Assessment | Multidimensional evaluation including medical, functional, cognitive, and social domains |
| Interdisciplinary Team | Collaboration among physicians, nurses, pharmacists, therapists, social workers |
| Atypical Presentation | Recognition that older adults often present differently |
| Polypharmacy Management | Careful medication review and deprescribing |
| Transitions of Care | Safe handoffs between care settings |
