# Nutrition - Overview

## What is Clinical Nutrition?
Clinical nutrition is the discipline that deals with the prevention, diagnosis, and management of nutritional and metabolic changes related to acute and chronic diseases. It integrates the science of nutrition with medical care.

## Dietary Reference Intakes (DRIs)
| Term | Definition | URL |
|------|------------|-----|
| **Estimated Average Requirement (EAR)** | Meets needs of **50%** of healthy population | https://ods.od.nih.gov/HealthInformation/Dietary_Reference_Intakes.aspx |
| **Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA)** | Meets needs of **97-98%** of healthy population | https://ods.od.nih.gov/HealthInformation/Dietary_Reference_Intakes.aspx |
| **Adequate Intake (AI)** | Used when **RDA cannot be determined** | https://ods.od.nih.gov/HealthInformation/Dietary_Reference_Intakes.aspx |
| **Tolerable Upper Intake Level (UL)** | **Maximum daily intake** unlikely to cause adverse effects | https://ods.od.nih.gov/HealthInformation/Dietary_Reference_Intakes.aspx |
| **Chronic Disease Risk Reduction (CDRR)** | Intake level to **reduce chronic disease risk** | https://ods.od.nih.gov/HealthInformation/Dietary_Reference_Intakes.aspx |
