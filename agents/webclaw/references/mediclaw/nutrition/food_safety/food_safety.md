# Nutrition - Food Safety

## Foodborne Illness
| Pathogen | Common Sources | Symptoms | Prevention | URL |
|----------|----------------|----------|------------|-----|
| **Salmonella** | Poultry, Eggs, Produce | Diarrhea, Fever, Cramps (6h-6d) | **Cook thoroughly**, Avoid cross-contamination | https://www.cdc.gov/salmonella/index.html |
| **Campylobacter** | Poultry, Unpasteurized milk | Diarrhea (often bloody), Fever, Cramps (2-5d) | Cook poultry thoroughly | https://www.cdc.gov/campylobacter/index.html |
| **E. coli O157:H7** | Undercooked beef, Raw produce | **Bloody diarrhea**, **HUS** (children) | Cook beef to **160°F**, Avoid cross-contamination | https://www.cdc.gov/ecoli/index.html |
| **Listeria monocytogenes** | Deli meats, Soft cheeses, Unpasteurized dairy | Fever, Muscle aches, **Pregnancy complications** | **Avoid high-risk foods in pregnancy**, Heat deli meats | https://www.cdc.gov/listeria/index.html |
| **Norovirus** | Contaminated food/water, Person-to-person | Vomiting, Diarrhea (12-48h) | **Hand hygiene**, **Avoid food prep when sick** | https://www.cdc.gov/norovirus/index.html |
| **Clostridium perfringens** | Meat/poultry held at unsafe temps | Diarrhea, Cramps (6-24h) | **Keep hot foods hot (>140°F)** , **Cool rapidly** | https://www.cdc.gov/foodsafety/diseases/clostridium-perfringens.html |
| **Staphylococcus aureus** | Foods handled by infected person | Vomiting, Diarrhea (30min-6h) | **Hand hygiene**, **Avoid food prep with skin infections** | https://www.cdc.gov/foodsafety/diseases/staphylococcal.html |
| **Bacillus cereus** | Rice, Pasta (emetic); Meats, Stews (diarrheal) | Vomiting (1-6h) or Diarrhea (6-15h) | **Refrigerate cooked rice promptly** | https://www.cdc.gov/foodsafety/diseases/bacillus-cereus.html |

## Core Four Food Safety Practices (CDC)
| Practice | Description | URL |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| **Clean** | **Wash hands** (20 sec with soap), **Wash surfaces/utensils**, **Rinse produce** | https://www.cdc.gov/foodsafety/keep-food-safe.html |
| **Separate** | **Separate raw meat/poultry/seafood/eggs** from ready-to-eat foods; **Use separate cutting boards** | https://www.cdc.gov/foodsafety/keep-food-safe.html |
| **Cook** | **Cook to safe internal temperature**; Use **food thermometer** | https://www.cdc.gov/foodsafety/keep-food-safe.html |
| **Chill** | **Refrigerate within 2 hours** (1 hour if >90°F); **Keep refrigerator =40°F**, **Freezer =0°F** | https://www.cdc.gov/foodsafety/keep-food-safe.html |

## Safe Minimum Internal Temperatures
| Food | Temperature | URL |
|------|-------------|-----|
| **Poultry (whole, parts, ground)** | **165°F** | https://www.foodsafety.gov/food-safety-charts/safe-minimum-internal-temperatures |
| **Ground Meat (beef, pork, lamb)** | **160°F** | https://www.foodsafety.gov/food-safety-charts/safe-minimum-internal-temperatures |
| **Beef, Pork, Lamb (steaks, chops, roasts)** | **145°F** + **3 min rest** | https://www.foodsafety.gov/food-safety-charts/safe-minimum-internal-temperatures |
| **Fish** | **145°F** (flesh opaque and flakes) | https://www.foodsafety.gov/food-safety-charts/safe-minimum-internal-temperatures |
| **Eggs** | Cook until **yolk and white are firm** | https://www.foodsafety.gov/food-safety-charts/safe-minimum-internal-temperatures |
| **Leftovers/Casseroles** | **165°F** | https://www.foodsafety.gov/food-safety-charts/safe-minimum-internal-temperatures |

## Food Safety for High-Risk Populations
| Population | Special Considerations | URL |
|-----------|----------------------|-----|
| **Pregnant Women** | Avoid: **Raw/undercooked meat/seafood/eggs**, **Unpasteurized dairy**, **Deli meats/hot dogs** (unless heated), **High-mercury fish**, **Raw sprouts** | https://www.fda.gov/food/people-risk-foodborne-illness/food-safety-pregnant-women |
| **Infants/Young Children** | Avoid: **Honey** (<1 year - botulism), **Choking hazards**, **Unpasteurized dairy/juice** | https://www.fda.gov/food/people-risk-foodborne-illness/food-safety-infants-toddlers |
| **Older Adults** | **? Risk of severe illness**; Follow Core Four practices strictly | https://www.fda.gov/food/people-risk-foodborne-illness/food-safety-older-adults |
| **Immunocompromised** | **? Risk of severe illness**; Avoid high-risk foods (raw/undercooked, Unpasteurized) | https://www.fda.gov/food/people-risk-foodborne-illness/food-safety-people-with-weakened-immune-systems |
