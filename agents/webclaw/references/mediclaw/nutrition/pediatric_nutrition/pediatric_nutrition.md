# Nutrition - Pediatric Nutrition

## Infant Feeding
| Age | Recommendation | URL |
|-----|----------------|-----|
| **0-6 months** | **Exclusive breastfeeding** (preferred) or **Iron-fortified infant formula** | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/breastfeeding/ |
| **6 months** | **Introduce complementary foods** while continuing breastfeeding/formula | https://www.cdc.gov/nutrition/infantandtoddlernutrition/index.html |
| **6-12 months** | **Iron-rich foods** (pureed meats, Iron-fortified cereals), **Introduce allergenic foods early** (peanut, egg) | https://www.cdc.gov/nutrition/infantandtoddlernutrition/index.html |
| **12 months** | **Whole cow's milk** (if no breastfeeding); **No honey** (botulism risk) | https://www.cdc.gov/nutrition/infantandtoddlernutrition/index.html |

## Introduction of Allergenic Foods (AAP/AAAAI)
| Food | Recommendation | URL |
|------|----------------|-----|
| **Peanut** | **Introduce at 4-6 months** (especially high-risk infants with severe eczema/egg allergy); **Screen first** if high-risk | https://www.niaid.nih.gov/diseases-conditions/guidelines-peanut-allergy-prevention |
| **Egg** | Introduce early (cooked egg) | https://www.aaaai.org/tools-for-the-public/conditions-library/allergies/prevention-of-allergies |
| **Cow's Milk** | Yogurt/cheese at 6 months; Whole milk at 12 months | https://www.aaaai.org/tools-for-the-public/conditions-library/allergies/prevention-of-allergies |
| **Wheat, Soy, Fish, Shellfish, Tree Nuts** | No need to delay | https://www.aaaai.org/tools-for-the-public/conditions-library/allergies/prevention-of-allergies |

## Childhood Nutrition
| Age Group | Key Considerations | URL |
|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| **Toddlers (1-3 years)** | **Transition to family diet**, **Small frequent meals**, **Limit juice** (<4 oz/day), **Choking prevention** | https://www.cdc.gov/nutrition/infantandtoddlernutrition/index.html |
| **Preschool (3-5 years)** | **Role modeling**, **Division of responsibility** (parent provides, child decides), **Limit added sugars** | https://www.myplate.gov/life-stages/preschoolers |
| **School-Age (5-12 years)** | **Regular meals/snacks**, **Involve in food preparation**, **Limit screen time** | https://www.myplate.gov/life-stages/kids |
| **Adolescents (12-18 years)** | **? Calorie needs**, **? Calcium** (1,300 mg), **? Iron** (menstruating females), **Screen for disordered eating** | https://www.myplate.gov/life-stages/teens |

## Pediatric Obesity
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **USPSTF Grade** | **B** (Screen children =6 years, Offer/refer comprehensive behavioral interventions) | https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/obesity-in-children-and-adolescents-screening |
| **Intervention** | **=26 contact hours** of intensive behavioral intervention | https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/obesity-in-children-and-adolescents-screening |
| **Approach** | **Family-based**, **Focus on health behaviors** (not weight), **Avoid weight stigma** | https://www.cdc.gov/obesity/childhood/index.html |

## Failure to Thrive (FTT)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Weight <2nd percentile** OR **Weight crossing =2 major percentile lines** | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2016/0915/p499.html |
| **Etiology** | **Inadequate intake** (most common), **Malabsorption**, **Increased metabolic demand** | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2016/0915/p499.html |
| **Management** | **Increase caloric density**, **Address underlying cause**, **Multidisciplinary approach** | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2016/0915/p499.html |
