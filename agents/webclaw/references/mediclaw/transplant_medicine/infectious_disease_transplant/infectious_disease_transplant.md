# Transplant Medicine - Infectious Disease in Transplantation

## Timeline of Infections Post-Transplant
| Period | Common Infections | URL |
|--------|-------------------|-----|
| **<1 month** | **Nosocomial** (SSI, UTI, Pneumonia, C. diff), **Donor-derived** | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |
| **1-6 months** | **Opportunistic** (CMV, EBV, BKV, PJP, Listeria, Nocardia, TB, Aspergillus) | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |
| **>6 months** | **Community-acquired**, **Late viral** (CMV, EBV, BKV, VZV, HCV), **Fungal** (Cryptococcus, Endemic mycoses) | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |

## Donor-Derived Infections
| Pathogen | Management | URL |
|----------|------------|-----|
| **Bacterial** | **Antibiotics** based on donor cultures | https://optn.transplant.hrsa.gov/resources/clinical/donor-derived-infection/ |
| **Viral** (HIV, HBV, HCV) | **Prophylaxis/Treatment** (see below) | https://optn.transplant.hrsa.gov/resources/clinical/donor-derived-infection/ |
| **Fungal** (Coccidioides, Histoplasma) | **Prophylaxis** (if donor from endemic area) | https://optn.transplant.hrsa.gov/resources/clinical/donor-derived-infection/ |
| **Parasitic** (Toxoplasma, Trypanosoma) | **Prophylaxis/Treatment** | https://optn.transplant.hrsa.gov/resources/clinical/donor-derived-infection/ |

## CMV Prevention and Management
| Strategy | Description | URL |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| **Universal Prophylaxis** | **Valganciclovir** x3-6 months (D+/R-: 6 months) | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |
| **Preemptive Therapy** | **Monitor CMV PCR weekly**, **Treat if rising** | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |
| **Treatment** | **Valganciclovir** (mild-moderate) or **Ganciclovir** (severe) | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |
| **Resistance** | **Foscarnet**, **Cidofovir**, **Maribavir**, **Letermovir** | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |

## EBV and PTLD
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Risk** | **Highest in EBV D+/R-** | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |
| **Monitoring** | **EBV PCR** (especially first year) | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |
| **Management** | **Reduce immunosuppression** (first-line), **Rituximab**, **Chemotherapy** (high-grade) | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |

## Prophylaxis Guidelines (AST IDCOP)
| Infection | Prophylaxis | Duration | URL |
|-----------|-------------|----------|-----|
| **CMV** | **Valganciclovir** | **3-6 months** (6 months for D+/R-) | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |
| **PJP** | **TMP-SMX** (or dapsone, atovaquone, pentamidine) | **6-12 months** (lifelong for lung, intestinal) | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |
| **Fungal** | **Fluconazole** or **Echinocandin** (liver, pancreas, intestinal) | **Variable** | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |
| **HSV/VZV** | **Acyclovir** (if not on valganciclovir) | **=1 month** | https://www.myast.org/education/infectious-disease |
