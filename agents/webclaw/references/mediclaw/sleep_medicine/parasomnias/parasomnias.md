# Sleep Medicine - Parasomnias

## Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Undesirable physical events or experiences** that occur during **sleep entry**, **within sleep**, or during **arousal from sleep** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Classification** | **NREM Parasomnias** (disorders of arousal), **REM Parasomnias**, **Other Parasomnias** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |

## NREM Parasomnias (Disorders of Arousal)
| Type | Key Features | URL |
|------|--------------|-----|
| **Confusional Arousals** | **Incomplete awakening** with **confusion**, **Disorientation**, **Amnesia**; Occurs in **N3** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Sleepwalking (Somnambulism)** | **Complex motor behaviors** during **N3**; **Amnesia**; Risk of **injury** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Sleep Terrors** | **Abrupt arousal** with **intense fear**, **Screaming**, **Autonomic activation**; **Amnesia**; Occurs in **N3** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Sleep-Related Eating Disorder** | **Recurrent episodes of involuntary eating/drinking** during arousals | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |

## NREM vs REM Parasomnias
| Feature | NREM Parasomnias | REM Parasomnias | URL |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Sleep Stage** | **N3** (deep sleep) | **REM** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Timing** | **First third of night** | **Last half of night** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Recall** | **Amnesia** | **Vivid dream recall** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Triggers** | **Sleep deprivation**, **Stress**, **Fever**, **OSA** | **Narcolepsy**, **Antidepressants**, **Alcohol withdrawal** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Treatment** | **Reassurance**, **Safety measures**, **Treat triggers**, **Benzodiazepines** (clonazepam), **Antidepressants** | **Treat underlying cause**, **Clonazepam**, **Melatonin** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |

## REM Sleep Behavior Disorder (RBD)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Loss of normal REM atonia** with **dream enactment behavior** (vocalizations, Complex movements, Injury) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Pathophysiology** | **Dysfunction of brainstem nuclei** (subcoeruleus) regulating REM atonia | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/rem-sleep-behavior-disorder |
| **Association** | **Synucleinopathies**: **Parkinson disease** (>50% develop within 10 years), **Dementia with Lewy bodies**, **Multiple system atrophy** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/rem-sleep-behavior-disorder |
| **Diagnosis** | **PSG** showing **REM without atonia** (RSWA) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Treatment** | **Environmental safety** (first-line), **Melatonin** (3-12 mg), **Clonazepam** (0.25-2 mg) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |

## Other Parasomnias
| Type | Key Features | URL |
|------|--------------|-----|
| **Nightmare Disorder** | **Recurrent frightening dreams** with **vivid recall**; Occurs in **REM** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Sleep Enuresis** | **Involuntary urination** during sleep **=2x/week** in person **=5 years** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Exploding Head Syndrome** | **Sudden loud noise** or **explosion sensation** during sleep-wake transitions | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Sleep-Related Hallucinations** | **Hallucinations** at sleep onset (hypnagogic) or awakening (hypnopompic) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
