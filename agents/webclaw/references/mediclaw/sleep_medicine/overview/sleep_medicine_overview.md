# Sleep Medicine - Overview

## What is Sleep Medicine?
Sleep medicine is the medical specialty devoted to the diagnosis and therapy of sleep disturbances and disorders. It encompasses the evaluation of sleep-wake function, the identification of sleep disorders, and the implementation of appropriate therapeutic interventions.

| Core Concept | Description | URL |
|--------------|-------------|-----|
| **Normal Sleep Architecture** | **NREM** (N1, N2, N3) + **REM** sleep cycling every 90-110 minutes | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/public-education/brain-basics/brain-basics-understanding-sleep |
| **Sleep-Wake Regulation** | **Homeostatic drive** (Process S) + **Circadian rhythm** (Process C) | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/sleep/why-sleep-important |
| **Recommended Sleep Duration** | **Adults**: 7-9 hours; **Older adults**: 7-8 hours; **Teens**: 8-10 hours; **Children**: 9-12 hours | https://www.cdc.gov/sleep/about_sleep/how_much_sleep.html |
| **Insufficient Sleep** | **>35% of US adults** report <7 hours sleep/night | https://www.cdc.gov/sleep/data-and-statistics/adults.html |
