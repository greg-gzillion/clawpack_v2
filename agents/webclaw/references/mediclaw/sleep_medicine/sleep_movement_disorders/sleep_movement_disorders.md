# Sleep Medicine - Sleep-Related Movement Disorders

## Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **URGE to move legs**, usually accompanied by **uncomfortable sensations**, **worse at rest/in evening**, **relieved by movement** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **URGE Criteria** | **U**rge to move, **R**est worsens, **G**ets better with activity, **E**vening/night predominance | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/restless-legs-syndrome |
| **Prevalence** | **5-10%** of adults; **Women > Men (2:1)** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/restless-legs-syndrome |
| **Associations** | **Iron deficiency** (low ferritin), **Pregnancy**, **CKD**, **Peripheral neuropathy**, **Medications** (antidepressants, Antihistamines, Dopamine antagonists) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/restless-legs-syndrome |
| **Workup** | **Ferritin** (target >50-75 ng/mL), **Iron studies**, **CBC**, **BMP**, **B12**, **Folate**, **TSH** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |

## Treatment of RLS (AASM Guidelines)
| Intervention | Recommendation | URL |
|--------------|----------------|-----|
| **Iron Replacement** | **If ferritin =75 ng/mL**: **Ferrous sulfate 325 mg + Vitamin C** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Gabapentinoids** | **First-line pharmacotherapy**: **Gabapentin enacarbil** (FDA-approved), **Pregabalin**, **Gabapentin** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Dopamine Agonists** | **Effective but risk of augmentation**: **Pramipexole**, **Ropinirole**, **Rotigotine** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Opioids** | **Refractory RLS**: **Tramadol**, **Oxycodone**, **Methadone** (low-dose) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Benzodiazepines** | **Not recommended** (limited efficacy, Dependence) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |

## Augmentation
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Worsening of RLS symptoms** with **earlier onset**, **Increased intensity**, **Spread to other body parts** after initiation of dopaminergic therapy | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Risk Factors** | **Dopamine agonists** (especially pramipexole, ropinirole), **Higher doses**, **Longer duration**, **Low ferritin** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Management** | **Reduce/stop dopamine agonist**, **Treat iron deficiency**, **Switch to gabapentinoid** or **opioid** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |

## Periodic Limb Movement Disorder (PLMD)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Periodic limb movements during sleep (PLMS)** associated with **sleep disturbance** or **daytime impairment** (not explained by another disorder) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **PLMS Criteria** | **=15 periodic limb movements/hour** (adults) or **=5/hour** (children) on PSG | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **PLMD vs RLS** | **PLMD**: Movements during sleep, May be asymptomatic; **RLS**: Urge to move while awake | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Treatment** | **Treat underlying cause** (RLS, OSA, Medications), **Gabapentinoids**, **Dopamine agonists** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |

## Sleep-Related Bruxism
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Repetitive jaw muscle activity** characterized by **clenching or grinding** of teeth during sleep | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Consequences** | **Tooth wear**, **Dental damage**, **TMJ pain**, **Headache** | https://www.sleepfoundation.org/bruxism |
| **Treatment** | **Occlusal splint** (night guard), **Stress management**, **Avoid caffeine/alcohol**, **Clonidine** (limited evidence) | https://www.sleepfoundation.org/bruxism |

## Sleep-Related Leg Cramps
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Sudden painful muscle contractions** in calf or foot during sleep | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Treatment** | **Stretching**, **Hydration**, **Magnesium** (limited evidence), **Quinine** (not recommended due to toxicity) | https://www.sleepfoundation.org/leg-cramps |
