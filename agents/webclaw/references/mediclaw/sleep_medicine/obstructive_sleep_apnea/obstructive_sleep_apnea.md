# Sleep Medicine - Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA)

## Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Recurrent episodes of complete (apnea) or partial (hypopnea) upper airway obstruction** during sleep, leading to **intermittent hypoxemia**, **hypercapnia**, and **sleep fragmentation** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Prevalence** | **~25-30%** of adults (moderate-severe OSA **~10-15%**); **Men > Women (2-3:1)** | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/sleep-apnea |
| **Risk Factors** | **Obesity** (strongest), **Male sex**, **Age >50**, **Craniofacial abnormalities** (retrognathia, Tonsillar hypertrophy), **Family history** | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/sleep-apnea/causes |

## Clinical Features
| Feature | Description | URL |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| **Nocturnal Symptoms** | **Loud snoring**, **Witnessed apneas**, **Gasping/choking arousals**, **Nocturia**, **Insomnia** | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/sleep-apnea/symptoms |
| **Daytime Symptoms** | **Excessive daytime sleepiness** (EDS), **Morning headache**, **Dry mouth**, **Cognitive impairment**, **Mood disturbance** | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/sleep-apnea/symptoms |
| **Physical Exam** | **Obesity** (BMI =30), **Large neck circumference** (>43 cm men, >38 cm women), **Mallampati class III-IV**, **Tonsillar hypertrophy**, **Retrognathia** | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/sleep-apnea/diagnosis |

## Screening Tools
| Tool | Description | Scoring | URL |
|------|-------------|---------|-----|
| **STOP-BANG** | **S**noring, **T**ired, **O**bserved apnea, **P**ressure (HTN), **B**MI >35, **A**ge >50, **N**eck >40 cm (M) or >38 cm (F), **G**ender (male) | **=3**: Intermediate risk; **=5**: High risk | https://www.mdcalc.com/calc/3992/stop-bang-score-obstructive-sleep-apnea |
| **Epworth Sleepiness Scale (ESS)** | 8 questions assessing likelihood of dozing | **=11**: Excessive daytime sleepiness | https://www.mdcalc.com/calc/10444/epworth-sleepiness-scale-ess |
| **Berlin Questionnaire** | 3 categories: Snoring, Daytime sleepiness, HTN/BMI | **=2 positive categories**: High risk | https://www.sleepapnea.org/diagnosis/berlin-questionnaire/ |

## Diagnostic Criteria (ICSD-3 / AASM)
| Criteria | Requirement | URL |
|----------|------------|-----|
| **A** | **Signs/symptoms** (sleepiness, Non-restorative sleep, Fatigue, Insomnia, Snoring, Witnessed apneas) OR **Comorbidities** (HTN, Mood disorder, Cognitive dysfunction, CAD, CVA, CHF, Afib, DM2) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **B** | **PSG or HSAT** showing **=5 obstructive respiratory events/hour** (AHI or RDI) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Diagnosis** | **A + B** = OSA | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |

## Severity Classification (AHI)
| Severity | AHI (events/hour) | URL |
|----------|-------------------|-----|
| **Mild** | **5 to <15** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Moderate** | **15 to <30** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Severe** | **=30** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |

## Treatment of OSA
| Treatment | Indications | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Positive Airway Pressure (PAP)** | **First-line** for all OSA (especially moderate-severe) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Weight Loss** | **All overweight/obese patients** (=10% weight loss significantly reduces AHI) | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/sleep-apnea/treatment |
| **Positional Therapy** | **Positional OSA** (supine AHI =2x non-supine) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Oral Appliance Therapy** | **Mild-moderate OSA**, **CPAP intolerant** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Upper Airway Surgery** | **Refractory OSA** with **specific anatomic obstruction** (e.g., Tonsillectomy, UPPP, MMA) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Hypoglossal Nerve Stimulation** | **Moderate-severe OSA**, **CPAP intolerant**, **BMI <32**, **No concentric collapse on DISE** | https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/recently-approved-devices/inspire-upper-airway-stimulation-p130008s039 |

## Consequences of Untreated OSA
| System | Consequences | URL |
|--------|--------------|-----|
| **Cardiovascular** | **Hypertension** (resistant), **Atrial fibrillation**, **Heart failure**, **Stroke**, **Pulmonary hypertension** | https://www.heart.org/en/health-topics/sleep-disorders/sleep-apnea-and-heart-disease-stroke |
| **Metabolic** | **Insulin resistance**, **Type 2 diabetes**, **Metabolic syndrome** | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/sleep-apnea |
| **Neurocognitive** | **Excessive daytime sleepiness**, **Cognitive impairment**, **Depression**, **Motor vehicle accidents** | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/sleep-apnea |
| **Perioperative** | **? Postoperative complications** (hypoxia, Arrhythmias, Delirium) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
