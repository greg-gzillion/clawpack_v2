# Sleep Medicine - Central Sleep Apnea (CSA)

## Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Cessation of airflow without respiratory effort** due to **loss of ventilatory drive** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Pathophysiology** | **Unstable ventilatory control** (high loop gain) ? **Overshoot/undershoot of PaCO2** ? **Apnea/Hyperpnea cycling** | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/central-sleep-apnea |
| **Prevalence** | **<1%** of general population; **Higher** in heart failure, Opioid use, High altitude | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/central-sleep-apnea |

## Types of Central Sleep Apnea
| Type | Key Features | Underlying Conditions | URL |
|------|--------------|----------------------|-----|
| **CSA with Cheyne-Stokes Respiration (CSR)** | **Crescendo-decrescendo** pattern, **Prolonged circulation time**, **Central apneas/hypopneas alternating with hyperpnea** | **Heart failure** (LVEF <40-45%), **Stroke**, **Renal failure** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **CSA Due to High-Altitude Periodic Breathing** | Occurs at **altitude >2,500m** | Hypobaric hypoxia ? **Hypocapnia** ? Central apnea | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **CSA Due to Medication or Substance** | **Opioids** (especially methadone, long-acting) | **Ataxic/Biot's breathing**, **Mixed apneas** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Primary CSA (Idiopathic)** | **No identifiable cause** | **Hypocapnic** (PaCO2 <40 mmHg during wake) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **Treatment-Emergent CSA** | **Emerges during PAP therapy for OSA** (formerly "complex sleep apnea") | **OSA** unmasking underlying CSA | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |

## Diagnostic Criteria (ICSD-3)
| Criteria | Requirement | URL |
|----------|------------|-----|
| **A** | **=1 of**: Sleepiness, Difficulty initiating/maintaining sleep, Frequent awakenings, Non-restorative sleep, Snoring, Witnessed apneas | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **B** | **PSG** showing **=5 central apneas and/or central hypopneas/hour** AND **Central events =50% of total events** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |
| **C** | **No evidence of daytime or nocturnal hypoventilation** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/international-classification-sleep-disorders/ |

## Treatment of CSA
| Type | Treatment | URL |
|------|-----------|-----|
| **CSA with CSR (Heart Failure)** | **Optimize HF therapy** (GDMT), **ASV** (if LVEF >45%), **Oxygen**, **CPAP** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **CSA Due to Opioids** | **Reduce/eliminate opioids**, **ASV**, **Bilevel PAP with backup rate** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Primary CSA** | **CPAP**, **Bilevel PAP with backup rate**, **ASV**, **Acetazolamide**, **Zolpidem** (controversial) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Treatment-Emergent CSA** | **Reassure** (often resolves within 8 weeks), **Lower CPAP**, **ASV**, **Bilevel PAP with backup rate** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
