## Common Sleep Disorders in Older Adults
| Disorder | Key Features | Treatment | URL |
|----------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Insomnia** | **Sleep-maintenance predominant**, **Early awakening** | **CBT-I** (first-line), **Avoid benzodiazepines**, **Consider low-dose doxepin**, **Ramelteon** | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **OSA** | **Prevalence increases with age**; Often **less symptomatic** (less sleepiness) | **CPAP** (tolerated similarly to younger adults) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **RLS/PLMD** | **? Prevalence with age** | **Check ferritin**, **Gabapentinoids** (caution with renal function, Falls) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **REM Sleep Behavior Disorder** | **? Prevalence in older men**; **Strong association with synucleinopathies** | **Environmental safety**, **Melatonin**, **Clonazepam** (caution with falls) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
| **Advanced Sleep-Wake Phase Disorder** | **Early bedtime**, **Early awakening** | **Bright light therapy** (evening) | https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/ |
