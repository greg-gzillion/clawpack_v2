## Dry Eye - Treatment
| Severity | Treatment | URL |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| **Mild** | **Artificial tears** (preservative-free preferred), **Warm compresses**, **Lid hygiene**, **Omega-3 supplements** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/dry-eye-treatment |
| **Moderate** | **Punctal plugs**, **Topical cyclosporine** (Restasis, Cequa), **Lifitegrast** (Xiidra), **Varenicline nasal spray** (Tyrvaya) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/dry-eye-treatment |
| **Severe** | **Autologous serum tears**, **Scleral contact lenses**, **Intense pulsed light (IPL)** , **Thermal pulsation** (LipiFlow), **Tarsorrhaphy** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/dry-eye-treatment |

## Dacryocystitis
| Type | Description | Treatment | URL |
|------|-------------|-----------|-----|
| **Acute Dacryocystitis** | **Painful**, Erythematous swelling over lacrimal sac; Often with purulent discharge | **Oral/IV antibiotics** (cover S. aureus, Streptococci), **Warm compresses**, **Incision and drainage** if abscess, **Dacryocystorhinostomy (DCR)** after resolution | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/dacryocystitis |
| **Chronic Dacryocystitis** | Recurrent tearing, Mucopurulent discharge with pressure over sac | **Dacryocystorhinostomy (DCR)** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/dacryocystitis |
| **Congenital Dacryocystitis** | **Nasolacrimal duct obstruction** in infants; Tearing, Matting of lashes | **Lacrimal sac massage**, **Topical antibiotics**, **Probing** if persistent (>12 months) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/nasolacrimal-duct-obstruction-children |
