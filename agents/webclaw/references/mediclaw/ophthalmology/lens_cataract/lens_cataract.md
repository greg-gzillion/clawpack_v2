# Ophthalmology - Lens and Cataract

## Cataract - Types
| Type | Location | Risk Factors | Clinical Features | URL |
|------|----------|--------------|-------------------|-----|
| **Nuclear Sclerotic** | **Nucleus** | **Aging** (most common), Smoking | **Gradual yellowing/browning**, **Myopic shift** ("second sight"), Decreased contrast | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/cataracts |
| **Cortical** | **Cortex** | Aging, Diabetes, UV exposure | **Spoke-like opacities**, **Glare**, **Monocular diplopia** | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/cataracts |
| **Posterior Subcapsular** | **Posterior capsule** | **Steroids**, Diabetes, Inflammation, Radiation | **Rapid progression**, **Significant glare** (worse with bright lights, Night driving) | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/cataracts |
| **Congenital** | Variable | **TORCH infections**, **Galactosemia**, **Lowe syndrome**, **Rubella** | **Leukocoria**, Nystagmus, Amblyopia | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/cataracts |

## Cataract Surgery
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Indications** | **Visual symptoms affecting daily activities**, **Anisometropia**, **Phacomorphic glaucoma**, **Phacolytic uveitis**, **Inability to visualize fundus** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/cataract-surgery |
| **Preoperative Evaluation** | **Biometry** (axial length, Keratometry), **IOL power calculation**, **Endothelial cell count** (if risk factors), **Macular OCT** (if risk factors) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/cataract-surgery |
| **Procedure** | **Phacoemulsification** + **IOL implantation** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/cataract-surgery |
| **IOL Types** | **Monofocal** (distance), **Multifocal**, **Extended depth of focus (EDOF)** , **Toric** (astigmatism), **Light-adjustable** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/cataract-surgery |
| **Postoperative Care** | **Topical antibiotic + steroid + NSAID**, **Eye shield**, **Avoid heavy lifting/straining** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/cataract-surgery |

## Cataract Surgery - Complications
| Timing | Complication | Management | URL |
|--------|--------------|------------|-----|
| **Intraoperative** | **Posterior capsule rupture**, **Vitreous loss**, **Dropped nucleus**, **Suprachoroidal hemorrhage** | **Anterior vitrectomy**, **Retained lens fragment retrieval** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/cataract-surgery-complications |
| **Early Postoperative** | **Endophthalmitis** (1:1000-5000), **Elevated IOP**, **Corneal edema**, **Hyphema** | **Endophthalmitis**: **Emergent vitreous tap/inject** (vancomycin + ceftazidime) or **vitrectomy** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/cataract-surgery-complications |
| **Late Postoperative** | **Posterior capsule opacification (PCO)** , **Cystoid macular edema (CME)** , **Retinal detachment**, **IOL dislocation** | **PCO**: **YAG capsulotomy**; **CME**: **Topical NSAIDs/steroids** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/cataract-surgery-complications |

## Congenital Cataract
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Etiology** | **Idiopathic** (most), **Hereditary** (AD most common), **TORCH** (rubella, CMV, Toxo, HSV), **Metabolic** (galactosemia, Lowe, Wilson), **Chromosomal** (Down, Turner) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/congenital-cataract |
| **Evaluation** | **Red reflex** (newborn), **Dilated fundus exam**, **B-scan** if unable to visualize fundus, **TORCH titers**, **Urine reducing substances** (galactosemia), **Genetic testing** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/congenital-cataract |
| **Management** | **Surgery** within first 6-8 weeks (unilateral) or 6-8 weeks (bilateral) to prevent **amblyopia**; **Aphakic contact lenses** or **IOL** (controversial in infants); **Patching** for amblyopia | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/congenital-cataract |

## Ectopia Lentis (Lens Dislocation)
| Direction | Associations | URL |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| **Superotemporal** | **Marfan syndrome** (bilateral), **Simple ectopia lentis** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/ectopia-lentis |
| **Inferonasal** | **Homocystinuria** (bilateral) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/ectopia-lentis |
| **Any Direction** | **Trauma**, **Weill-Marchesani syndrome**, **Sulfite oxidase deficiency** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/ectopia-lentis |
| **Management** | **Glasses/contact lenses** (if mild), **Lensectomy + vitrectomy + IOL** (if significant visual symptoms, Glaucoma, Uveitis) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/ectopia-lentis |
