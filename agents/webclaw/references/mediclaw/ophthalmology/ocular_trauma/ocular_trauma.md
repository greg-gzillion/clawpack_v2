# Ophthalmology - Ocular Trauma

## Open Globe Injury
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Full-thickness wound** of cornea and/or sclera | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/open-globe-injury |
| **Signs** | **Severe vision loss**, **Peaked pupil**, **Shallow anterior chamber**, **Hyphema**, **Subconjunctival hemorrhage** with chemosis, **Positive Seidel test** (fluorescein streaming) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/open-globe-injury |
| **Initial Management** | **Eye shield** (NOT patch), **AVOID any pressure on globe**, **NPO**, **IV antibiotics** (cefazolin or vancomycin + ceftazidime or gentamicin), **Tetanus prophylaxis**, **Antiemetics** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/open-globe-injury |
| **Definitive Management** | **Emergent surgical repair** within 24 hours | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/open-globe-injury |

## Hyphema
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Blood in anterior chamber** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/hyphema |
| **Grading** | **Grade 1**: <1/3 AC; **Grade 2**: 1/3-1/2; **Grade 3**: >1/2; **Grade 4**: 100% (8-ball) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/hyphema |
| **Management** | **Elevate head of bed** (30-45ｰ), **Eye shield**, **Cycloplegics** (atropine), **Topical steroids**, **Avoid antiplatelets/anticoagulants**, **Monitor IOP** (daily) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/hyphema |
| **Complications** | **Rebleeding** (days 3-5), **Elevated IOP** ? **Glaucoma**, **Corneal blood staining**, **Optic atrophy** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/hyphema |
| **Surgical Indications** | **IOP >50 mmHg x 5 days** or **>35 mmHg x 7 days**, **Corneal blood staining**, **8-ball hyphema** with elevated IOP | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/hyphema |

## Chemical Ocular Burns
| Type | Common Agents | Pathophysiology | Management | URL |
|------|---------------|-----------------|------------|-----|
| **Alkali** | **Lye**, **Ammonia**, **Lime**, **Drain cleaners**, **Cement** | **Liquefactive necrosis** ? **Deep penetration**, **Worse prognosis** | **Immediate copious irrigation** (minimum 1-2 L, Continue until pH 7.0-7.4), **Cycloplegics**, **Topical steroids**, **Antibiotics**, **Ascorbate/Citrate** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/chemical-burns |
| **Acid** | **Battery acid**, **Sulfuric acid**, **Hydrochloric acid** | **Coagulative necrosis** ? **Superficial injury**, **Better prognosis** | Same irrigation protocol | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/chemical-burns |

## Corneal Abrasion
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Clinical Features** | **Pain**, **Photophobia**, **Foreign body sensation**, **Fluorescein uptake** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/corneal-abrasion |
| **Management** | **Topical antibiotic** (erythromycin, Fluoroquinolone), **Cycloplegic** (cyclopentolate), **Avoid topical anesthetics**, **Avoid contact lens wear**, **Patching NOT recommended** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/corneal-abrasion |
| **Follow-Up** | Recheck in 24-48 hours; **Refer if non-healing**, **Infiltrate**, **Contact lens wearer** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/corneal-abrasion |

## Orbital Blowout Fracture
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Clinical Features** | **Enophthalmos**, **Infraorbital anesthesia** (CN V2), **Diplopia** (especially vertical), **Limitation of extraocular movements** (entrapment), **Periorbital ecchymosis/edema** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/orbital-blowout-fracture |
| **Diagnosis** | **CT orbits** (coronal and axial views) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/orbital-blowout-fracture |
| **Management** | **Antibiotics** (cover sinus flora), **Avoid nose blowing**, **Ice packs**, **Surgical repair** if: **Persistent diplopia**, **Enophthalmos >2 mm**, **Large fracture (>50% floor)** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/orbital-blowout-fracture |

## Traumatic Optic Neuropathy
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Clinical Features** | **Decreased vision** after trauma, **Afferent pupillary defect**, **Normal fundus** initially ? **Optic atrophy** (weeks later) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/traumatic-optic-neuropathy |
