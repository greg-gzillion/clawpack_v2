# Ophthalmology - Retina and Vitreous

## Retinal Detachment
| Type | Mechanism | Risk Factors | Clinical Features | Treatment | URL |
|------|-----------|--------------|-------------------|-----------|-----|
| **Rhegmatogenous** | **Full-thickness retinal break** ? **Vitreous liquefaction** ? **Fluid seeps under retina** | **High myopia**, **Lattice degeneration**, **Trauma**, **Prior cataract surgery**, **Family history** | **Flashes**, **Floaters**, **Shadow/curtain** in vision, **Reduced visual acuity** (if macula involved) | **Pneumatic retinopexy**, **Scleral buckle**, **Pars plana vitrectomy** with gas or silicone oil | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/retinal-detachment |
| **Tractional** | **Fibrovascular membranes** pull retina off RPE | **Proliferative diabetic retinopathy**, **ROP**, **Sickle cell**, **Trauma** | Gradual vision loss, **No flashes/floaters**, **Traction lines** | **Pars plana vitrectomy** with membrane peeling | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/retinal-detachment |
| **Exudative (Serous)** | **Fluid accumulation** under retina without break | **Central serous chorioretinopathy**, **VKH**, **Posterior scleritis**, **Choroidal tumor**, **Hypertension** | **No retinal break**, **Shifting fluid** on exam | **Treat underlying cause** | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/retinal-detachment |

## Retinal Tears and Lattice Degeneration
| Condition | Description | Risk of RD | Management | URL |
|-----------|-------------|------------|------------|-----|
| **Retinal Tear** | **Full-thickness break** with persistent vitreoretinal traction | **High** (30-50% if untreated) | **Laser photocoagulation** or **Cryotherapy** around tear | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/retinal-tear |
| **Lattice Degeneration** | **Thinned**, **Atrophic** retina with vitreous adhesion | **Low** (<1% overall), Higher if symptomatic or high-risk features | **Observation** (most); **Consider prophylactic laser** if high-risk (aphakic/pseudophakic RD in fellow eye) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/lattice-degeneration |

## Posterior Vitreous Detachment (PVD)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Separation of posterior vitreous cortex from internal limiting membrane** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/posterior-vitreous-detachment |
| **Symptoms** | **Sudden onset flashes and floaters** (Weiss ring) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/posterior-vitreous-detachment |
| **Complications** | **Retinal tear** (10-15%), **Retinal detachment**, **Vitreous hemorrhage** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/posterior-vitreous-detachment |
| **Management** | **Dilated fundus exam** with scleral depression; **Laser** if retinal tear present; **Reassurance** if no tear | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/posterior-vitreous-detachment |

## Vitreous Hemorrhage
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Causes** | **Proliferative diabetic retinopathy** (most common), **Retinal tear/detachment**, **Posterior vitreous detachment**, **Trauma**, **Terson syndrome**, **Valsalva** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/vitreous-hemorrhage |
| **Clinical Features** | **Sudden painless vision loss**, **"Currant jelly"** or **"Coffee grounds"** appearance, **Absent red reflex** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/vitreous-hemorrhage |
| **Diagnosis** | **B-scan ultrasound** (if no view to fundus) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/vitreous-hemorrhage |
| **Management** | **Head elevation** (allow blood to settle), **Treat underlying cause**, **Pars plana vitrectomy** if non-clearing (4-8 weeks) or RD present | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/vitreous-hemorrhage |

## Epiretinal Membrane (ERM) and Macular Hole
| Condition | Pathophysiology | OCT Findings | Treatment | URL |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Epiretinal Membrane** | **Fibrocellular proliferation** on ILM surface | **Hyperreflective membrane**, **Retinal thickening**, **Loss of foveal contour** | **Observation** (mild); **Pars plana vitrectomy + membrane peel** (symptomatic) | https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/epiretinal-membrane |
