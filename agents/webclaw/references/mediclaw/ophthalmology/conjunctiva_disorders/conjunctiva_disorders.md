# Ophthalmology - Conjunctiva Disorders

## Conjunctivitis - Differential Diagnosis
| Type | Etiology | Discharge | Lymphadenopathy | Treatment | URL |
|------|----------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|-----|
| **Viral** | **Adenovirus** (most common), Enterovirus, HSV | **Watery** | **Preauricular** | **Supportive** (cold compresses, Artificial tears), **Hygiene** (highly contagious) | https://www.cdc.gov/conjunctivitis/index.html |
| **Bacterial** | S. aureus, S. pneumoniae, H. influenzae (adults); H. influenzae, S. pneumoniae, M. catarrhalis (children) | **Purulent** | Uncommon | **Topical antibiotics** (erythromycin, Fluoroquinolone, TMP-polymyxin B) | https://www.cdc.gov/conjunctivitis/index.html |
| **Allergic** | Seasonal (pollen) or Perennial (dust mites, Dander) | **Watery/Ropy** | None | **Topical antihistamine/mast cell stabilizer** (olopatadine, Ketotifen), **Oral antihistamines** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/allergic-conjunctivitis |
| **Gonococcal** | N. gonorrhoeae | **Hyperacute purulent** | **Preauricular** | **Ceftriaxone 1g IM + Azithromycin 1g PO** (chlamydia coverage) | https://www.cdc.gov/std/gonorrhea/treatment.htm |
| **Chlamydial** | C. trachomatis (D-K) | Mucopurulent | Preauricular | **Doxycycline 100 mg BID x 7 days** or **Azithromycin 1g x 1** | https://www.cdc.gov/std/chlamydia/treatment.htm |

## Subconjunctival Hemorrhage
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Etiology** | **Valsalva** (coughing, Straining, Vomiting), **Trauma**, **Hypertension**, **Anticoagulation**, **Idiopathic** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/subconjunctival-hemorrhage |
| **Clinical Features** | **Painless**, **Well-demarcated** area of blood under conjunctiva; **Does NOT cross limbus** | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/subconjunctival-hemorrhage |
| **Management** | **Reassurance**, **Artificial tears**, Resolves in 1-2 weeks; **Check BP**, **Coagulation studies** if recurrent | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/subconjunctival-hemorrhage |

## Pterygium and Pinguecula
| Lesion | Description | Location | Management | URL |
|--------|-------------|----------|------------|-----|
| **Pinguecula** | **Yellowish**, Raised conjunctival nodule; **Does NOT cross limbus** | Nasal or temporal interpalpebral | **UV protection**, **Artificial tears**, **Topical NSAIDs** (if inflamed) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/pinguecula-pterygium |
| **Pterygium** | **Fibrovascular**, Wing-shaped growth; **Crosses limbus** onto cornea | Nasal > temporal interpalpebral | **UV protection**, **Artificial tears**, **Topical steroids** (if inflamed); **Surgical excision** if vision-threatening or symptomatic | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/pinguecula-pterygium |

## Conjunctival Tumors
| Tumor | Characteristics | Malignant Potential | Treatment | URL |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|-----------|-----|
| **Conjunctival Nevus** | Pigmented, Mobile, Cysts common | **Benign** | **Observation** (photo documentation) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/conjunctival-nevus |
| **Primary Acquired Melanosis (PAM)** | Unilateral, Patchy brown pigmentation; **PAM with atypia** = Precursor to melanoma | **PAM without atypia**: Low; **PAM with atypia**: High | **Excisional biopsy** (if suspicious), **Cryotherapy**, **Topical mitomycin C** | https://www.cancer.gov/types/eye/hp/intraocular-melanoma-treatment-pdq |
| **Conjunctival Melanoma** | Pigmented or amelanotic nodule; Feeder vessels | **High** | **Wide local excision** with cryotherapy, **Topical mitomycin C**, **Sentinel lymph node biopsy** | https://www.cancer.gov/types/eye/hp/intraocular-melanoma-treatment-pdq |
| **Squamous Cell Carcinoma** | **Gelatinous**, Leukoplakic, or Papilliform | **Low metastatic potential** | **Wide local excision** with cryotherapy, **Topical mitomycin C/5-FU** | https://www.cancer.gov/types/eye/hp/intraocular-melanoma-treatment-pdq |
| **Lymphoma** | **Salmon-patch** appearance | **Associated with systemic lymphoma** | **Biopsy**, **External beam radiation**, **Systemic chemotherapy** | https://www.cancer.gov/types/lymphoma/hp/ocular-adnexal-lymphoma-treatment-pdq |
