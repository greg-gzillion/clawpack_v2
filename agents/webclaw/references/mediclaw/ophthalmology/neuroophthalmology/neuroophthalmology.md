# Ophthalmology - Neuroophthalmology

## Cranial Nerve Palsies (III, IV, VI)
| CN | Clinical Features | Common Causes | URL |
|----|-------------------|---------------|-----|
| **III (Oculomotor)** | **Ptosis**, **"Down and out" eye**, **Pupil involvement** (compressive - PCOM aneurysm) or **sparing** (ischemic - diabetes, HTN) | **PCOM aneurysm** (pupil-involved), **Microvascular** (pupil-spared), Trauma, Tumor | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/third-nerve-palsy |
| **IV (Trochlear)** | **Vertical diplopia**, **Head tilt opposite side** (Bielschowsky sign), Difficulty reading/down stairs | **Congenital** (most common), **Trauma**, Microvascular | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/fourth-nerve-palsy |
| **VI (Abducens)** | **Horizontal diplopia** (worse at distance), **Esotropia** | **Microvascular** (diabetes, HTN), **Trauma**, **Elevated ICP** (false localizing), Tumor | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/sixth-nerve-palsy |

## Horner Syndrome
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Triad** | **Ptosis** (mild), **Miosis**, **Anhidrosis** (central/first-order) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/horner-syndrome |
| **Localization** | **First-order** (hypothalamus to C8-T2): Stroke, Tumor; **Second-order** (C8-T2 to superior cervical ganglion): **Pancoast tumor**, Neck trauma; **Third-order** (superior cervical ganglion to eye): **Carotid dissection**, Cluster headache | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/horner-syndrome |
| **Pharmacologic Testing** | **Apraclonidine** (reversal of anisocoria), **Cocaine** (confirms Horner - no dilation), **Hydroxyamphetamine** (localizes pre- vs post-ganglionic) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/horner-syndrome |

## Anisocoria - Differential
| Condition | Pupil Size | Light Response | Accommodation | URL |
|-----------|------------|----------------|---------------|-----|
| **Physiologic** | Difference <1 mm | Normal | Normal | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/anisocoria |
| **Horner Syndrome** | **Smaller in affected eye** | Normal | Normal | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/horner-syndrome |
| **Adie Tonic Pupil** | **Larger in affected eye** | **Poor** (tonic), **Vermiform movements** | **Tonic** (better than light) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/adie-pupil |
| **CN III Palsy** | **Larger in affected eye** | **Poor** | Poor | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/third-nerve-palsy |
| **Argyll Robertson Pupil** | Small, Irregular | **Poor** | **Intact** (light-near dissociation) | https://www.aao.org/eye-health/diseases/argyll-robertson-pupil |
