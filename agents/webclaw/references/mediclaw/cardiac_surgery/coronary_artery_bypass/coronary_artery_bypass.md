# Cardiac Surgery - Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting

## CABG Indications
| Indication | Class of Recommendation | Evidence Level | URL |
|------------|------------------------|----------------|-----|
| Left Main Disease (=50% stenosis) | I | A | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Three-Vessel Disease | I | A | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Two-Vessel Disease with Proximal LAD | I | A | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Single-Vessel Proximal LAD | IIa | B | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Left Ventricular Dysfunction (EF <35%) | I | B | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Diabetes with Multivessel Disease | I | A | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |

## CABG vs. PCI Decision Making
| Factor | Favors CABG | Favors PCI | URL |
|--------|-------------|------------|-----|
| Coronary Anatomy | Left main; 3-vessel; proximal LAD | 1-2 vessel (non-proximal LAD) | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| SYNTAX Score | >33 (high); 23-32 (intermediate) | <22 (low) | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Diabetes | Multivessel disease | Non-multivessel | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| LV Dysfunction | EF <35% | Preserved EF | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Age | Younger (durability benefit) | Elderly/frail | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Surgical Risk | Low-moderate | High/prohibitive | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |

## Conduit Selection
| Conduit | Patency (10-year) | Advantages | Disadvantages | URL |
|---------|-------------------|------------|---------------|-----|
| Left Internal Mammary Artery (LIMA) to LAD | >90% | Superior patency; survival benefit | Limited length; sternal blood supply | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |
| Right Internal Mammary Artery (RIMA) | 80-85% | Arterial conduit | Sternal wound infection (especially in diabetics) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |
| Radial Artery | 80-85% | Arterial conduit; easily harvested | Vasospasm; contraindicated with positive Allen test | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |
| Saphenous Vein Graft (SVG) | 50-60% | Plentiful; easily harvested | Intimal hyperplasia; atherosclerosis | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |
| Right Gastroepiploic Artery | 70-80% | Arterial conduit | Abdominal complications | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |

## SYNTAX Score
| Score Range | Classification | Recommended Revascularization | URL |
|-------------|----------------|------------------------------|-----|
| =22 | Low | PCI or CABG | https://www.syntaxscore.org |
| 23-32 | Intermediate | CABG preferred (especially with diabetes) | https://www.syntaxscore.org |
| =33 | High | CABG | https://www.syntaxscore.org |

## Off-Pump vs. On-Pump CABG
| Parameter | Off-Pump (OPCAB) | On-Pump (CABG) | URL |
|-----------|------------------|----------------|-----|
| Stroke Risk | Lower in high-risk patients | Similar overall | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |
| Graft Patency | Slightly lower | Slightly higher | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |
| Completeness of Revascularization | May be less complete | More complete | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |
| Renal Outcomes | Lower AKI risk | Similar | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |
| Transfusion | Less transfusion | More transfusion | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |
| Long-Term Survival | Similar | Similar | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |

## Arterial Revascularization Strategies
| Strategy | Description | Evidence | URL |
|----------|-------------|----------|-----|
| Single IMA (SIMA) | LIMA to LAD; SVG to other targets | Standard | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |
| Bilateral IMA (BIMA) | LIMA to LAD; RIMA to other targets | Survival benefit; increased sternal infection | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |
| Total Arterial Revascularization (TAR) | All arterial conduits | Potential survival benefit; technically demanding | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |
| Radial Artery | Second arterial conduit | Improved patency vs SVG | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470357/ |

## Post-CABG Medical Therapy
| Medication | Indication | Timing | URL |
|-----------|------------|--------|-----|
| Aspirin | All patients | Within 6 hours post-op; continue indefinitely | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Dual Antiplatelet Therapy (DAPT) | Consider for SVG; post-MI | 1 year (individualized) | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Beta-Blockers | All patients | Pre-op; continue post-op | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| Statins | All patients | High-intensity; continue indefinitely | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
| ACE-I/ARB | LV dysfunction; hypertension; diabetes | Continue post-op | https://www.acc.org/guidelines |
