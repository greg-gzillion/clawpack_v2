## CKD - Leading Causes
| Cause | Percentage | Key Features | URL |
|-------|------------|--------------|-----|
| **Diabetes Mellitus** | 40-50% | Leading cause of ESKD; Nodular glomerulosclerosis (Kimmelstiel-Wilson) | https://www.cdc.gov/kidneydisease/publications-resources/annual-report.html |
| **Hypertension** | 25-30% | Hypertensive nephrosclerosis; Arteriolar hyalinosis | https://www.cdc.gov/kidneydisease/publications-resources/annual-report.html |
| **Glomerulonephritis** | 10-15% | IgA nephropathy, FSGS, Membranous nephropathy | https://www.cdc.gov/kidneydisease/publications-resources/annual-report.html |
| **Polycystic Kidney Disease** | 5% | Autosomal dominant; Multiple bilateral cysts | https://www.cdc.gov/kidneydisease/publications-resources/annual-report.html |
| **Other** | 10-15% | Obstructive nephropathy, Interstitial nephritis, Unknown | https://www.cdc.gov/kidneydisease/publications-resources/annual-report.html |

## CKD - Complications and Management
| Complication | Pathophysiology | Management | URL |
|--------------|-----------------|------------|-----|
| **Anemia** | Decreased EPO production | Iron supplementation, ESAs, Transfusion | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/anemia-in-ckd/ |
| **Mineral Bone Disorder (CKD-MBD)** | Hyperphosphatemia, Hypocalcemia, Decreased 1,25(OH)2D, Secondary hyperparathyroidism | Phosphate binders, Vitamin D analogs, Calcimimetics | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/ckd-mbd/ |
| **Hyperkalemia** | Decreased renal K+ excretion | Low-K+ diet, Diuretics, K+ binders (patiromer, Sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/hyperkalemia-high-potassium |
| **Metabolic Acidosis** | Decreased acid excretion | Sodium bicarbonate (goal HCO3 =22) | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/ckd-evaluation-and-management/ |
| **Volume Overload** | Decreased Na+ excretion | Sodium restriction, Diuretics | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/ckd-evaluation-and-management/ |
| **Cardiovascular Disease** | Traditional and non-traditional risk factors | Statins, BP control, Smoking cessation, Aspirin | https://www.heart.org/en/health-topics/chronic-kidney-disease |

## CKD - RAAS Blockade
| Drug Class | Examples | Benefits | Monitoring | URL |
|------------|----------|----------|------------|-----|
| **ACE Inhibitors** | Lisinopril, Ramipril, Enalapril | **Slows CKD progression**, Reduces proteinuria, CV protection | Cr and K+ (expect 30% Cr rise, K+ <5.5) | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/ckd-evaluation-and-management/ |
| **ARBs** | Losartan, Valsartan, Irbesartan | Same as ACE inhibitors | Same | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/ckd-evaluation-and-management/ |
| **SGLT2 Inhibitors** | Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin, Canagliflozin | **Slows CKD progression**, Reduces CV events, Reduces proteinuria | Monitor for euglycemic DKA, UTI, Volume depletion | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **Non-Steroidal MRAs** | Finerenone | **Slows CKD progression**, Reduces CV events in diabetic CKD | Monitor K+ (risk of hyperkalemia) | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
