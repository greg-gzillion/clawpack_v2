# Nephrology - Pregnancy and Kidney

## Physiologic Renal Changes in Pregnancy
| Change | Mechanism | Clinical Implication | URL |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|-----|
| **Increased GFR** | ? Renal plasma flow, ? Oncotic pressure | **Serum Cr decreases** (0.4-0.8 mg/dL); "Normal" Cr may indicate AKI | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/pregnancy |
| **Increased renal size** | ? Blood flow, Interstitial volume | **Mild hydronephrosis** (right > left) - physiologic | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/pregnancy |
| **Glucosuria** | ? GFR exceeds tubular reabsorption | Normal finding | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/pregnancy |
| **Proteinuria** | ? GFR | **Up to 300 mg/day normal**; >300 mg/day abnormal | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/pregnancy |
| **Decreased serum osmolality** | Reset osmostat | **Na 3-5 mEq/L lower** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/pregnancy |
| **Respiratory alkalosis** | Progesterone-driven hyperventilation | **PaCO2 28-32 mmHg**, Compensated metabolic acidosis | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/pregnancy |

## Hypertensive Disorders of Pregnancy
| Disorder | Definition | Management | URL |
|----------|------------|------------|-----|
| **Gestational Hypertension** | BP =140/90 after 20 weeks, No proteinuria | Monitor, Antihypertensives if severe | https://www.acog.org/womens-health/faqs/preeclampsia-and-high-blood-pressure-during-pregnancy |
| **Preeclampsia** | BP =140/90 + Proteinuria =300 mg/24h or end-organ damage | **Delivery** (definitive), **Magnesium** (seizure prophylaxis), Antihypertensives | https://www.acog.org/womens-health/faqs/preeclampsia-and-high-blood-pressure-during-pregnancy |
| **Severe Preeclampsia** | BP =160/110, Thrombocytopenia, Renal failure, Pulmonary edema | **Magnesium**, Antihypertensives, **Delivery** | https://www.acog.org/womens-health/faqs/preeclampsia-and-high-blood-pressure-during-pregnancy |
| **Eclampsia** | Seizures in setting of preeclampsia | **Magnesium**, Airway management, **Delivery** | https://www.acog.org/womens-health/faqs/preeclampsia-and-high-blood-pressure-during-pregnancy |
| **HELLP Syndrome** | Hemolysis, Elevated LFTs, Low Platelets | **Delivery**, Corticosteroids if <34 weeks | https://www.acog.org/womens-health/faqs/preeclampsia-and-high-blood-pressure-during-pregnancy |

## Preeclampsia - Pathophysiology and Management
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Pathophysiology** | **Abnormal placentation** ? **Impaired spiral artery remodeling** ? **Placental ischemia** ? **Release of anti-angiogenic factors** (sFlt-1, sEng) ? **Endothelial dysfunction** | https://www.nichd.nih.gov/health/topics/preeclampsia |
| **Risk Factors** | **Primiparity**, **Prior preeclampsia**, **Chronic HTN**, **CKD**, **Diabetes**, **Multifetal gestation**, **Obesity**, **Age >35**, **Thrombophilia**, **SLE** | https://www.nichd.nih.gov/health/topics/preeclampsia |
| **Prevention** | **Low-dose aspirin** (81-162 mg daily) starting 12-28 weeks for high-risk women | https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/low-dose-aspirin-use-for-the-prevention-of-morbidity-and-mortality-from-preeclampsia-preventive-medication |
| **Antihypertensives** | **Labetalol**, **Hydralazine**, **Nifedipine**; **AVOID ACE-I/ARB** (teratogenic) | https://www.acog.org/womens-health/faqs/preeclampsia-and-high-blood-pressure-during-pregnancy |
| **Magnesium** | **Loading**: 4-6 g IV over 20-30 min; **Maintenance**: 1-2 g/hr; **Monitor** reflexes, Respiratory rate, Urine output | https://www.acog.org/womens-health/faqs/preeclampsia-and-high-blood-pressure-during-pregnancy |

## CKD in Pregnancy
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Preconception Counseling** | **Optimize BP**, **Stop teratogenic medications** (ACE-I/ARB, Mycophenolate, Cyclophosphamide), **Assess risk** | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/pregnancy-and-kidney-disease |
| **CKD Stage 1-2** | **Generally good outcomes**; Monitor for preeclampsia, Preterm birth | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/pregnancy-and-kidney-disease |
| **CKD Stage 3-4** | **Higher risk** of preeclampsia, Preterm birth, IUGR, Worsening renal function | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/pregnancy-and-kidney-disease |
| **CKD Stage 5 (Dialysis)** | **Intensive hemodialysis** (=36 hours/week) improves outcomes; **High-risk pregnancy** | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/pregnancy-and-kidney-disease |
| **Kidney Transplant** | **Wait =1 year post-transplant**, Stable graft function, Safe immunosuppression | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/pregnancy-and-kidney-disease |

## Acute Kidney Injury in Pregnancy
| Cause | Timing | Management | URL |
|-------|--------|------------|-----|

| **Septic Abortion** | First trimester | Broad-spectrum antibiotics, D&C | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/pregnancy |
| **Acute Fatty Liver of Pregnancy (AFLP)** | Third trimester | **Delivery** (urgent), Supportive care | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/pregnancy |
| **HELLP Syndrome** | Third trimester | **Delivery**, Corticosteroids | https://www.acog.org/womens-health/faqs/preeclampsia-and-high-blood-pressure-during-pregnancy |
| **Thrombotic Microangiopathy** | Any | Differentiate TTP, HUS, aHUS, HELLP; **Delivery if pregnancy-related** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/pregnancy |
| **Postpartum AKI** | Postpartum | Hemorrhage, Sepsis, TMA | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/pregnancy |
