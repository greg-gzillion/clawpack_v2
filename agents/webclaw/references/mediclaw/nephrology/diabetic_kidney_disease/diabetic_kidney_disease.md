# Nephrology - Diabetic Kidney Disease (DKD)

## DKD - Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | CKD attributed to diabetes mellitus; **Leading cause of ESKD worldwide** (40-50%) | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/diabetes/overview/preventing-problems/diabetic-kidney-disease |
| **Natural History** | **Glomerular hyperfiltration** ? **Microalbuminuria** (30-300 mg/g) ? **Macroalbuminuria** (>300 mg/g) ? **Declining GFR** ? **ESKD** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/diabetes/overview/preventing-problems/diabetic-kidney-disease |
| **Pathology** | **Nodular glomerulosclerosis** (Kimmelstiel-Wilson), **Thickened GBM**, **Mesangial expansion**, **Arteriolar hyalinosis** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK534200/ |
| **Screening** | **Annual urine albumin:creatinine ratio (UACR)** + **eGFR** (Type 1: 5 years after diagnosis; Type 2: at diagnosis and annually) | https://www.diabetes.org/diabetes/complications/kidney-disease-nephropathy |

## DKD - Risk Factors
| Risk Factor | Description | URL |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| **Hyperglycemia** | **Primary driver**; Intensive glycemic control reduces DKD progression | https://www.diabetes.org/diabetes/complications/kidney-disease-nephropathy |
| **Hypertension** | Accelerates progression; **Target BP <130/80 mmHg** | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **Dyslipidemia** | Contributes to glomerulosclerosis | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **Smoking** | Independent risk factor for progression | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **Family History** | Genetic susceptibility | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **African American, Hispanic, Native American** | Higher risk of progression to ESKD | https://www.cdc.gov/kidneydisease/publications-resources/annual-report.html |

## DKD - Glycemic Control
| Population | HbA1c Target | URL |
|------------|--------------|-----|
| **Most patients with DKD** | **<7.0%** (individualize) | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **Older adults, Multiple comorbidities, Hypoglycemia unawareness** | <7.5-8.0% | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **Limited life expectancy** | Avoid symptomatic hyperglycemia | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |

## DKD - Antihyperglycemic Agents in CKD
| Drug Class | eGFR Cutoff | Benefits in DKD | Considerations | URL |
|------------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|-----|
| **Metformin** | eGFR =30 (reduce dose if 30-44) | CV benefits, Inexpensive | **Risk of lactic acidosis** (avoid if eGFR <30) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |
| **SGLT2 Inhibitors** | Empagliflozin: eGFR =20; Dapagliflozin: eGFR =25; Canagliflozin: eGFR =30 | **Slows CKD progression**, **Reduces CV events**, **Reduces proteinuria** | **Euglycemic DKA**, **UTI**, **Volume depletion** | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **GLP-1 Agonists** | eGFR =15 | **Reduces CV events**, **Weight loss**, **Reduces proteinuria** | **GI side effects**, **Expensive** | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **DPP-4 Inhibitors** | All (dose adjust) | **Weight neutral**, **Well-tolerated** | Modest efficacy | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **Sulfonylureas** | Avoid in CKD G4-G5 | Inexpensive | **Hypoglycemia** (prolonged in CKD) | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **Insulin** | All (dose adjust) | Effective | **Hypoglycemia**, Weight gain | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |

## DKD - RAAS Blockade
| Drug Class | Examples | Benefits | Monitoring | URL |
|------------|----------|----------|------------|-----|
| **ACE Inhibitors** | Lisinopril, Ramipril | **Slows DKD progression**, **Reduces proteinuria**, **CV protection** | **Cr** (expect 30% rise), **K+** (goal <5.5) | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **ARBs** | Losartan, Irbesartan | Same as ACE inhibitors | Same | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **Non-Steroidal MRAs** | **Finerenone** | **Slows DKD progression**, **Reduces CV events** | **K+** (risk of hyperkalemia) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |

## DKD - SGLT2 Inhibitors (Cornerstone of Therapy)
| Drug | eGFR Initiation | eGFR Discontinuation | Key Evidence | URL |
|------|-----------------|----------------------|--------------|-----|
| **Empagliflozin** | =20 | <20 | **EMPA-KIDNEY**: 28% reduction in kidney disease progression | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **Dapagliflozin** | =25 | <25 | **DAPA-CKD**: 39% reduction in kidney disease progression | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |
| **Canagliflozin** | =30 | <30 | **CREDENCE**: 30% reduction in kidney disease progression | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/diabetes-ckd/ |

## DKD - Blood Pressure Management
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Target BP** | **<130/80 mmHg** (ACC/AHA); **<120/80 mmHg** (KDIGO 2021) | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/blood-pressure-in-ckd/ |
| **First-Line Agents** | **ACE-I or ARB** (if albuminuria =300 mg/g) | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/blood-pressure-in-ckd/ |
| **Second-Line Agents** | **Thiazide diuretics** (if eGFR =30), **Loop diuretics** (if eGFR <30), **CCBs** | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/blood-pressure-in-ckd/ |
| **Sodium Restriction** | **<2 g/day** (optimal) | https://kdigo.org/guidelines/blood-pressure-in-ckd/ |

## DKD - Indications for Nephrology Referral
| Indication | URL |
|------------|-----|
