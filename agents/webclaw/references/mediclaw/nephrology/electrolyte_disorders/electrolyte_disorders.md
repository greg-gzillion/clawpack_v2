## Hyponatremia - Management by Severity
| Severity | Symptoms | Management | URL |
|----------|----------|------------|-----|
| **Severe (Na <120 or symptomatic)** | Seizures, Coma, Obtundation | **Hypertonic saline (3% NaCl)** 100 mL bolus over 10 min; Repeat until symptoms improve; **Correction goal: 4-6 mEq/L in first 6 hours** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470386/ |
| **Moderate** | Headache, Nausea, Confusion | Treat underlying cause; Fluid restriction; **Correction limit: =8 mEq/L per 24 hours** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470386/ |
| **Asymptomatic** | None | Treat underlying cause; Fluid restriction | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470386/ |
| **Osmotic Demyelination Risk** | **Overly rapid correction** | **If overcorrected (>10 mEq/L in 24h): Re-lower with D5W + Desmopressin** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK551690/ |

## Hypernatremia (Na >145 mEq/L)
| Type | Mechanism | Causes | Treatment | URL |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|-----|
| **Hypovolemic** | Water loss > Na loss | **GI losses** (diarrhea), **Skin losses** (burns, Sweating), **Osmotic diuresis** (glucose, Urea, Mannitol) | **Correct volume deficit with isotonic fluids**, Then free water | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK441960/ |
| **Euvolemic** | Water loss only | **Diabetes insipidus** (central or nephrogenic), **Hypodipsia**, **Insensible losses** (fever, Hyperventilation) | **Free water replacement**; **DDAVP** (central DI); **Thiazides, Low-solute diet** (nephrogenic DI) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK441960/ |
| **Hypervolemic** | Na gain > water gain | **Hypertonic saline infusion**, **Hyperaldosteronism**, **Cushing syndrome** | **Diuretics** + **Free water**, Treat underlying cause | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK441960/ |

## Hypernatremia - Management
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Free Water Deficit** | TBW x (Na/140 - 1); TBW = 0.6 x weight (men), 0.5 x weight (women), 0.5 x weight (elderly) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK441960/ |
| **Correction Rate** | **Chronic (>48h): =0.5 mEq/L/h (=10-12 mEq/L/day)**; **Acute (<48h): 1-2 mEq/L/h** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK441960/ |
| **Route** | **Oral/NGT** (preferred); **IV D5W** or **ｽ NS** (if unable to take PO) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK441960/ |
| **Monitoring** | **Serum Na q2-4h** during active correction | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK441960/ |

## Hypokalemia (K <3.5 mEq/L)
| Severity | K+ Level | ECG Changes | Management | URL |
|----------|----------|-------------|------------|-----|
| **Mild** | 3.0-3.4 | None or flattened T waves | **Oral KCl** 40-80 mEq/day | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK482465/ |
| **Moderate** | 2.5-2.9 | **U waves**, **ST depression**, **Flattened T waves** | **Oral KCl** 40-120 mEq/day | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK482465/ |
| **Severe** | <2.5 or symptomatic | **U waves**, **ST depression**, **Ventricular arrhythmias** | **IV KCl** 10-20 mEq/h (central line: 20-40 mEq/h), **Cardiac monitoring** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK482465/ |

## Hypokalemia - Causes
| Mechanism | Causes | URL |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| **Decreased Intake** | Anorexia, NPO, IV fluids without K+ | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK482465/ |
| **GI Losses** | **Vomiting** (metabolic alkalosis ? Renal K+ wasting), **Diarrhea**, **Laxative abuse**, **NG suction** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK482465/ |
| **Renal Losses** | **Diuretics** (loop, Thiazides), **Mineralocorticoid excess** (Primary hyperaldosteronism, Cushing, Licorice), **Renal tubular acidosis** (Type 1, 2), **Hypomagnesemia** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK482465/ |
| **Transcellular Shift** | **Insulin**, **Beta-agonists** (albuterol), **Alkalosis**, **Hypokalemic periodic paralysis** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK482465/ |

## Hyperkalemia (K >5.0 mEq/L)
| Severity | K+ Level | ECG Changes | Management | URL |
|----------|----------|-------------|------------|-----|
| **Mild** | 5.1-5.9 | **Peaked T waves** | **Dietary restriction**, **Stop offending drugs**, **Diuretics**, **K+ binders** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |
| **Moderate** | 6.0-6.4 | **Peaked T waves**, **PR prolongation** | **IV Calcium** (if ECG changes), **Insulin/glucose**, **Beta-agonists**, **K+ binders** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |
| **Severe** | =6.5 or ECG changes | **Widened QRS**, **Sine wave**, **Ventricular fibrillation** | **IV Calcium gluconate** (immediate membrane stabilization), **Insulin/glucose**, **Beta-agonists**, **Dialysis** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |

## Hyperkalemia - Causes
| Mechanism | Causes | URL |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| **Decreased Excretion** | **CKD** (most common), **AKI**, **Hypoaldosteronism** (Type IV RTA), **Medications** (ACE-I, ARB, MRA, NSAIDs, Trimethoprim, Calcineurin inhibitors) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |
| **Increased Intake** | K+ supplements, Salt substitutes, Dietary, PRBC transfusion | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |
| **Transcellular Shift** | **Acidosis**, **Insulin deficiency**, **Beta-blockers**, **Digoxin toxicity**, **Hyperkalemic periodic paralysis**, **Tumor lysis syndrome**, **Rhabdomyolysis** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |
| **Pseudohyperkalemia** | **Hemolysis** during blood draw, **Thrombocytosis**, **Leukocytosis**, **Prolonged tourniquet** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |

## Hyperkalemia - Treatment
| Intervention | Onset | Duration | Mechanism | URL |
|--------------|-------|----------|-----------|-----|
| **Calcium Gluconate/Chloride** | 1-3 min | 30-60 min | **Membrane stabilization** (no effect on K+) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |
| **Insulin (10 units) + D50 (1 amp)** | 15-30 min | 2-4 hours | **Intracellular shift** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |
| **Albuterol (nebulized)** | 30-60 min | 2-4 hours | **Intracellular shift** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |
| **Sodium Bicarbonate** | Variable | Variable | **Intracellular shift** (only if metabolic acidosis) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |
| **Loop Diuretics** | 30-60 min | Hours | **Renal excretion** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |
| **K+ Binders** (Patiromer, SZC) | 1-4 hours | Hours-days | **GI binding** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |
| **Hemodialysis** | Immediate | Duration of dialysis | **Removal** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK470284/ |

## Hypomagnesemia (Mg <1.7 mg/dL)
| Severity | Mg Level | Symptoms | Management | URL |
|----------|----------|----------|------------|-----|
| **Mild** | 1.0-1.6 | Asymptomatic or mild weakness | **Oral Mg oxide/chloride** 300-600 mg daily | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK500003/ |
| **Moderate** | 0.5-0.9 | Weakness, Tremor, Tetany | **IV MgSO4** 1-2 g over 1-2 hours | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK500003/ |
| **Severe** | <0.5 or symptomatic | **Seizures**, **Arrhythmias** (Torsades), **Tetany** | **IV MgSO4** 2-4 g over 4-8 hours, **Cardiac monitoring** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK500003/ |

## Hypomagnesemia - Causes
| Mechanism | Causes | URL |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| **GI Losses** | **Diarrhea**, **Malabsorption**, **PPI use** (chronic), **Alcoholism** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK500003/ |
| **Renal Losses** | **Loop diuretics**, **Thiazides**, **Aminoglycosides**, **Amphotericin B**, **Cisplatin**, **Calcineurin inhibitors**, **Hypercalcemia**, **Alcohol** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK500003/ |
| **Other** | **Refeeding syndrome**, **Hungry bone syndrome**, **Diabetes mellitus** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK500003/ |
