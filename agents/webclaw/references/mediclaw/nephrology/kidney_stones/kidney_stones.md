# Nephrology - Kidney Stones (Nephrolithiasis)

## Kidney Stones - Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Epidemiology** | Lifetime prevalence 10-15%; Male > Female; Peak incidence 20-50 years; **50% recurrence at 10 years** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/kidney-stones |
| **Stone Types** | **Calcium oxalate** (70-80%), **Calcium phosphate** (10-15%), **Uric acid** (5-10%), **Struvite** (5-10%), **Cystine** (1%) | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/kidney-stones |
| **Risk Factors** | **Dehydration**, **Diet** (high Na+, High oxalate, High animal protein), **Obesity**, **Family history**, **Metabolic syndrome**, **Hypercalciuria**, **Hyperuricosuria**, **Hypocitraturia** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/kidney-stones |

## Kidney Stones - Clinical Presentation and Diagnosis
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Acute Presentation** | **Severe, Colicky flank pain** radiating to groin, **Nausea/Vomiting**, **Gross or microscopic hematuria**, **Urinary urgency/frequency** (stone in distal ureter) | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/kidney-stones |
| **Imaging** | **Non-contrast CT** (gold standard), **Ultrasound** (pregnancy, Pediatrics), **KUB X-ray** (radiopaque stones) | https://www.acr.org/Clinical-Resources/ACR-Appropriateness-Criteria |
| **Laboratory** | **Urinalysis** (hematuria, pH, Crystals), **Serum** (Cr, Ca, Uric acid), **Stone analysis** (if passed) | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/kidney-stones |
| **Indications for Admission** | **Intractable pain**, **Nausea/Vomiting**, **Infection** (septic stone), **AKI**, **Solitary kidney**, **Bilateral obstruction**, **Stone >10 mm** | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |

## Kidney Stones - Acute Management
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Pain Control** | **NSAIDs** (ketorolac, Ibuprofen) first-line; **Opioids** if refractory | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
| **Medical Expulsive Therapy (MET)** | **Tamsulosin 0.4 mg daily** for **distal ureteral stones 5-10 mm** | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
| **Antiemetics** | Ondansetron, Metoclopramide | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
| **Septic Stone** | **Emergent urologic consultation** + **Broad-spectrum antibiotics** + **Urgent decompression** (ureteral stent or nephrostomy) | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |

## Kidney Stones - Surgical Management
| Procedure | Indications | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|-----|
| **Shock Wave Lithotripsy (SWL)** | **Proximal ureteral stones <10 mm**, **Renal stones <20 mm** | Non-invasive; **Avoid in pregnancy**, **Bleeding diathesis**, **Distal obstruction** | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
| **Ureteroscopy with Laser Lithotripsy** | **Distal ureteral stones**, **SWL failures**, **Cystine stones** | Minimally invasive; **Higher stone-free rate** than SWL | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
| **Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy (PCNL)** | **Renal stones >20 mm**, **Staghorn calculi**, **SWL/URS failures** | Invasive; **Highest stone-free rate** | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |

## Stone-Specific Management and Prevention
| Stone Type | Urine Findings | Dietary/Lifestyle | Medications | URL |
|------------|----------------|-------------------|-------------|-----|
| **Calcium Oxalate** | **Hypercalciuria**, **Hyperoxaluria**, **Hypocitraturia** | **High fluid intake** (>2.5 L/day), **Low sodium** (<2 g/day), **Normal calcium** (1000-1200 mg/day), **Low oxalate** (avoid spinach, Nuts, Rhubarb), **Low animal protein** | **Thiazide diuretics** (hypercalciuria), **Potassium citrate** (hypocitraturia), **Allopurinol** (hyperuricosuria) | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/kidney-stones |
| **Calcium Phosphate** | **Alkaline urine** (pH >6.5), **Hypercalciuria**, **Hypocitraturia** | High fluid, Low sodium, Normal calcium, **Low animal protein** | **Thiazide diuretics**, **Potassium citrate** (if hypocitraturia), **Avoid urinary alkalinization** | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/kidney-stones |
| **Uric Acid** | **Acidic urine** (pH <5.5), **Hyperuricosuria**, **Low urine volume** | High fluid, Low purine (red meat, Organ meats, Shellfish), **Urinary alkalinization** (goal pH 6.5-7.0) | **Potassium citrate**, **Allopurinol** (if hyperuricosuria or gout) | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/kidney-stones |
| **Struvite (Infection)** | **Alkaline urine**, **Urease-producing bacteria** (Proteus, Klebsiella, Pseudomonas) | High fluid | **Complete surgical removal**, **Antibiotics** for UTI, **Acetohydroxamic acid** (urease inhibitor - limited use) | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/kidney-stones |
| **Cystine** | **Cystinuria** (autosomal recessive), **Hexagonal crystals** | **Very high fluid intake** (>3-4 L/day), **Urinary alkalinization** (goal pH >7.5), Low sodium, Low animal protein | **Potassium citrate**, **Tiopronin**, **D-Penicillamine**, **Captopril** | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/kidney-stones |

## Metabolic Evaluation for Recurrent Stone Formers
| Test | Timing | Purpose | URL |
|------|--------|---------|-----|
| **Serum** | Random | **Calcium**, **Phosphate**, **Uric acid**, **Creatinine**, **Bicarbonate**, **PTH** | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
| **24-Hour Urine** | **At least 6 weeks after stone passage/surgery**, On usual diet | **Volume**, **Calcium**, **Oxalate**, **Citrate**, **Uric acid**, **Sodium**, **Creatinine**, **pH**, **Sulfate** (animal protein), **Cystine** (if suspected) | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
| **Stone Analysis** | After passage or removal | **Definitive stone composition** | https://www.auanet.org/guidelines/kidney-stones |
