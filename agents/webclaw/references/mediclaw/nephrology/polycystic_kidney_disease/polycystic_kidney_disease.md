# Nephrology - Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD)

## Autosomal Dominant Polycystic Kidney Disease (ADPKD)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Genetics** | **PKD1** (85%, chromosome 16) - more severe, ESKD age ~54; **PKD2** (15%, chromosome 4) - milder, ESKD age ~74 | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/polycystic-kidney-disease |
| **Pathophysiology** | "Second hit" somatic mutation ? **Cyst formation**, **Epithelial proliferation**, **Fluid secretion** ? **Progressive cyst enlargement** ? **Compression of normal parenchyma** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532306/ |
| **Renal Manifestations** | **Bilateral kidney enlargement**, **Flank pain**, **Hematuria**, **UTI**, **Nephrolithiasis**, **Progressive CKD** ? **ESKD** (50% by age 60) | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/polycystic-kidney-disease |
| **Extrarenal Manifestations** | **Hepatic cysts** (80%, more common in women), **Cerebral aneurysms** (5-10%), **Mitral valve prolapse**, **Colonic diverticula**, **Pancreatic cysts**, **Seminal vesicle cysts** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532306/ |

## ADPKD - Diagnosis
| Criteria | Age | Findings | URL |
|----------|-----|----------|-----|
| **Ultrasound (Family History Positive)** | 15-39 years | =3 cysts (unilateral or bilateral) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532306/ |
| **Ultrasound (Family History Positive)** | 40-59 years | =2 cysts in each kidney | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532306/ |
| **Ultrasound (Family History Positive)** | =60 years | =4 cysts in each kidney | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532306/ |
| **Genetic Testing** | Indeterminate imaging, Early diagnosis, Family planning | **PKD1/PKD2 sequencing** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532306/ |
| **MRI/CT** | **Total Kidney Volume (TKV)** | Prognostic marker (Mayo Classification) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532306/ |

## ADPKD - Management
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Blood Pressure Control** | **Target <110/75 mmHg** (HALT-PKD trial) in young, Otherwise <130/80 mmHg | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/polycystic-kidney-disease |
| **First-Line Antihypertensive** | **ACE-I or ARB** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/polycystic-kidney-disease |
| **Vasopressin V2 Receptor Antagonist** | **Tolvaptan** (slows TKV growth and eGFR decline) for rapidly progressive ADPKD (Mayo 1C-1E) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |
| **Hydration** | **>3 L/day** (suppresses ADH) | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/polycystic-kidney-disease |
| **Sodium Restriction** | <2 g/day | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/polycystic-kidney-disease |
| **Avoid Caffeine** | Theoretical benefit (increases cAMP) | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/polycystic-kidney-disease |
| **Cerebral Aneurysm Screening** | **MRA brain** if: **Family history of aneurysm**, **Personal history of SAH**, **High-risk occupation** (pilot), **Pre-transplant** | https://www.stroke.org |

## ADPKD - Complications and Management
| Complication | Management | URL |
|--------------|------------|-----|
| **Cyst Hemorrhage** | **Conservative** (bed rest, Analgesia, Hydration); **Avoid anticoagulation/antiplatelets** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/polycystic-kidney-disease |
| **Cyst Infection** | **Lipophilic antibiotics** (fluoroquinolones, TMP-SMX) for 4-6 weeks; **Drainage if refractory** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/polycystic-kidney-disease |
| **Nephrolithiasis** | **Uric acid stones** more common; **Hydration**, **Potassium citrate** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/polycystic-kidney-disease |
| **Flank Pain** | **Acute**: Analgesia, Rule out complication; **Chronic**: Avoid NSAIDs, Consider **cyst aspiration/sclerotherapy** or **laparoscopic cyst decortication** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/polycystic-kidney-disease |
| **ESKD** | **Hemodialysis**, **Peritoneal dialysis** (feasible despite enlarged kidneys), **Kidney transplantation** | https://www.kidney.org/kidney-topics/kidney-failure |

## Autosomal Recessive Polycystic Kidney Disease (ARPKD)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Genetics** | **PKHD1** mutation (encodes fibrocystin/polyductin) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK1326/ |
| **Clinical Features** | **Potter sequence** (oligohydramnios, Pulmonary hypoplasia), **Bilateral flank masses**, **Hypertension**, **Congenital hepatic fibrosis** (portal hypertension, Varices) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK1326/ |
| **Diagnosis** | **Prenatal ultrasound** (enlarged echogenic kidneys), **Genetic testing** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK1326/ |
| **Prognosis** | **30-50% neonatal mortality** (pulmonary hypoplasia); Survivors progress to ESKD and require **combined liver-kidney transplant** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK1326/ |

| **Nephronophthisis** | **NPHP genes** | **Autosomal recessive**, **Most common genetic cause of ESKD in children**, **Tubulointerstitial fibrosis**, **Extrarenal** (retinitis pigmentosa, Liver fibrosis, Skeletal) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK1326/ |
