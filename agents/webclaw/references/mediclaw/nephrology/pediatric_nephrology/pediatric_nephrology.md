## Congenital Anomalies of Kidney and Urinary Tract (CAKUT)
| Anomaly | Description | Clinical Significance | URL |
|---------|-------------|----------------------|-----|
| **Renal Agenesis** | Unilateral (1:1000) or Bilateral (Potter sequence, Lethal) | **Compensatory hypertrophy** of solitary kidney; **Monitor for proteinuria, HTN** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/children |
| **Renal Hypoplasia** | Small kidney with normal architecture | **Reduced nephron mass** ? **CKD risk** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/children |
| **Renal Dysplasia** | Abnormal parenchymal development (cysts, Cartilage) | **Non-functional**; **Contralateral abnormalities common** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/children |
| **Multicystic Dysplastic Kidney (MCDK)** | Multiple non-communicating cysts, No function | **Usually involutes**; **Monitor contralateral VUR** (30%) | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/children |
| **Horseshoe Kidney** | Fusion of lower poles | **Associated with Turner syndrome**, **Increased risk of stones, UTI, Wilms tumor** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/children |
| **Ectopic Kidney** | Pelvic or thoracic location | **Usually asymptomatic**; **Increased risk of obstruction, Stones** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/children |

## Vesicoureteral Reflux (VUR)
| Grade | Description | Management | URL |
|-------|-------------|------------|-----|
| **Grade I** | Reflux into non-dilated ureter | **Observation**, **Antibiotic prophylaxis** (if recurrent UTI) | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/vesicoureteral-reflux |
| **Grade II** | Reflux into non-dilated renal pelvis | Observation, Prophylaxis | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/vesicoureteral-reflux |
| **Grade III** | Mild-moderate dilation of ureter/pelvis | Prophylaxis, **Consider surgery if persistent** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/vesicoureteral-reflux |
| **Grade IV** | Moderate dilation, Blunting of calyces | **Surgical correction** (ureteral reimplantation or endoscopic injection) | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/vesicoureteral-reflux |
| **Grade V** | Severe dilation, Tortuous ureter | **Surgical correction** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/urologic-diseases/vesicoureteral-reflux |

## Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome (HUS)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Typical HUS (STEC-HUS)** | **Shiga toxin-producing E. coli** (O157:H7), Bloody diarrhea ? AKI, Thrombocytopenia, MAHA | https://www.cdc.gov/ecoli/index.html |
| **Atypical HUS (aHUS)** | **Complement dysregulation** (genetic or acquired), No diarrhea, Recurrent | https://www.cdc.gov/ecoli/index.html |
| **Diagnosis** | **Schistocytes**, **Thrombocytopenia**, **AKI**, **Negative ADAMTS13** | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK482327/ |
| **Treatment (STEC-HUS)** | **Supportive**; **AVOID antibiotics** (increase toxin release) | https://www.cdc.gov/ecoli/index.html |
| **Treatment (aHUS)** | **Eculizumab** (anti-C5 monoclonal antibody) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |
