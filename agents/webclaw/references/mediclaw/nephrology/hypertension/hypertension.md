## Resistant Hypertension
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | BP above goal despite **=3 antihypertensives** (including diuretic) at maximally tolerated doses OR **controlled on =4 medications** | https://www.acc.org/guidelines/hypertension |
| **Pseudoresistance** | **White coat HTN**, **Medication non-adherence**, **Inaccurate BP measurement**, **Poor lifestyle adherence** | https://www.acc.org/guidelines/hypertension |
| **Secondary Causes** | **Primary aldosteronism** (5-10%), **Renal artery stenosis**, **OSA**, **CKD**, **Thyroid disease**, **Pheochromocytoma**, **Coarctation** | https://www.acc.org/guidelines/hypertension |
| **Management** | **Optimize diuretic** (chlorthalidone), **Add MRA**, **Add beta-blocker/vasodilator**, **Refer to hypertension specialist** | https://www.acc.org/guidelines/hypertension |

## Hypertensive Emergencies
| Condition | Target BP Reduction | Preferred Agents | URL |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|-----|
| **Hypertensive Encephalopathy** | 25% reduction in 2-6 hours | **Nicardipine**, **Labetalol**, **Clevidipine** | https://www.acc.org/guidelines/hypertension |
| **Aortic Dissection** | **SBP 100-120 within 20 min** | **Esmolol** + **Nicardipine/Clevidipine** | https://www.acc.org/guidelines/hypertension |
| **Acute Ischemic Stroke (thrombolytics planned)** | **<185/110 before tPA** | **Nicardipine**, **Labetalol** | https://www.stroke.org |
| **Acute Ischemic Stroke (no thrombolytics)** | **Permissive to 220/120** | No acute treatment unless >220/120 | https://www.stroke.org |
| **ICH** | **SBP 140-160** | **Nicardipine**, **Labetalol** | https://www.stroke.org |
| **Acute Coronary Syndrome** | Gradual reduction | **Nitroglycerin**, **Esmolol**, **Metoprolol** | https://www.heart.org |
| **Preeclampsia/Eclampsia** | **SBP <160, DBP <105** | **Labetalol**, **Hydralazine**, **Nifedipine**, **Magnesium** (seizure prophylaxis) | https://www.acog.org |

## Secondary Hypertension - Causes
| Cause | Clinical Clues | Diagnostic Test | URL |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Primary Aldosteronism** | **Hypokalemia** (may be normokalemic), Resistant HTN | **Aldosterone:Renin Ratio (ARR)** >20-30, **Confirmatory testing** (saline, Fludrocortisone) | https://www.endocrine.org/guidelines/primary-aldosteronism |
| **Renal Artery Stenosis** | **Abdominal bruit**, **Flash pulmonary edema**, **AKI with ACE-I/ARB**, **Asymmetric kidney size** | **Duplex ultrasound**, **CT/MR angiography** | https://www.acc.org/guidelines/hypertension |
| **Obstructive Sleep Apnea** | **Snoring**, **Daytime somnolence**, **Obesity** | **Polysomnography** | https://www.sleepfoundation.org |
| **Pheochromocytoma** | **Paroxysmal HTN**, **Headache**, **Palpitations**, **Diaphoresis** | **Plasma metanephrines**, **24h urine metanephrines** | https://www.endocrine.org/guidelines/pheochromocytoma |
| **Thyroid Disease** | Hyperthyroidism (systolic HTN), Hypothyroidism (diastolic HTN) | **TSH** | https://www.thyroid.org |
| **Coarctation of Aorta** | **HTN in arms, Low BP in legs**, **Radio-femoral delay**, **Rib notching on CXR** | **Echocardiography**, **CT/MR angiography** | https://www.heart.org |
| **Cushing Syndrome** | **Central obesity**, **Moon facies**, **Buffalo hump**, **Purple striae** | **24h urine cortisol**, **Dexamethasone suppression**, **Late-night salivary cortisol** | https://www.endocrine.org/guidelines/cushing-syndrome |
