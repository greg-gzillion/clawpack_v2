# Mental Health - Depressive Disorders

## Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **DSM-5-TR Criteria** | **=5 symptoms for =2 weeks**, including **depressed mood** OR **anhedonia** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **SIGECAPS** | **S**leep, **I**nterest, **G**uilt, **E**nergy, **C**oncentration, **A**ppetite, **P**sychomotor, **S**uicidality | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics/depression |
| **Prevalence** | Lifetime: 17% (women 21%, men 13%) | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/statistics/major-depression |
| **Age of Onset** | Peak: 20s-30s | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/statistics/major-depression |

## MDD Severity Specifiers (DSM-5-TR)
| Severity | Definition | URL |
|----------|------------|-----|
| **Mild** | Few symptoms beyond minimum, Mild functional impairment | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Moderate** | Symptoms and impairment between mild and severe | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Severe** | Most symptoms present, Marked functional impairment | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **With Psychotic Features** | **Delusions** (guilt, nihilistic, persecutory) or **Hallucinations** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **With Melancholic Features** | **Anhedonia**, **Early morning awakening**, **Psychomotor change**, **Worse in morning** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **With Atypical Features** | **Mood reactivity**, **Hypersomnia**, **Hyperphagia**, **Leaden paralysis**, **Rejection sensitivity** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **With Catatonia** | Stupor, Mutism, Posturing, Negativism, Echophenomena | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **With Peripartum Onset** | Onset during pregnancy or within 4 weeks postpartum | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **With Seasonal Pattern** | Recurrent episodes with seasonal relationship | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |

## Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **DSM-5-TR Criteria** | **Depressed mood for =2 years** (1 year in children/adolescents) + =2 additional symptoms | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Symptoms** | Poor appetite/overeating, Insomnia/hypersomnia, Low energy, Low self-esteem, Poor concentration, Hopelessness | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics/persistent-depressive-disorder-dysthymic-disorder |

## Treatment of MDD
| Phase | Duration | Goal | URL |
|-------|----------|------|-----|
| **Acute** | 8-12 weeks | **Achieve remission** | https://www.apa.org/depression-guideline |
| **Continuation** | 4-9 months | **Prevent relapse** | https://www.apa.org/depression-guideline |
| **Maintenance** | =1 year (often indefinite for recurrent) | **Prevent recurrence** | https://www.apa.org/depression-guideline |

## Treatment-Resistant Depression (TRD)
| Definition | Management Options | URL |
|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| **Failure of =2 adequate antidepressant trials** | **Augmentation**: Atypical antipsychotics (aripiprazole, brexpiprazole, quetiapine XR), Lithium, T3 | https://www.apa.org/depression-guideline |
| | **Switching**: Different class antidepressant | https://www.apa.org/depression-guideline |
| | **Combination**: Two antidepressants with different mechanisms | https://www.apa.org/depression-guideline |
| | **Neuromodulation**: **ECT** (gold standard), **TMS**, **Esketamine** (Spravato) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/postmarket-drug-safety-information-patients-and-providers/esketamine |

## Neuromodulation for Depression
| Treatment | Mechanism | Indication | Efficacy | URL |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|----------|-----|
| **Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT)** | Induced seizure | **Severe MDD**, **Catatonia**, **Treatment-resistant**, **Urgent response needed** | **70-90% response** (highest of all treatments) | https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/ect |
| **Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS)** | Magnetic pulses to DLPFC | **Treatment-resistant MDD** | **30-50% response**, **20-30% remission** | https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/recently-approved-devices/neurostar-tms-therapy-system-p790019s013 |
| **Esketamine (Spravato)** | NMDA antagonist | **Treatment-resistant MDD** (with oral antidepressant) | **~50% response** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/postmarket-drug-safety-information-patients-and-providers/esketamine |
| **Vagus Nerve Stimulation (VNS)** | Intermittent vagus nerve stimulation | **Chronic, Severe treatment-resistant MDD** | **~30% response** (long-term) | https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/recently-approved-devices/vns-therapy-system-p970003s050 |
