# Mental Health - Emergency Psychiatry

## Psychiatric Emergencies
| Emergency | Key Features | Management | URL |
|-----------|--------------|------------|-----|
| **Suicidality** | Ideation, Plan, Intent, Means | **Safety planning**, **Hospitalization** if high risk, **Means restriction** | https://www.samhsa.gov/suicide |
| **Homicidality/Aggression** | Threat to others | **De-escalation**, **Verbal intervention**, **Medication** (antipsychotic ± benzodiazepine), **Seclusion/Restraint** (last resort) | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/clinical-practice-guidelines |
| **Agitation** | Psychomotor excitement, Threatening behavior | **Verbal de-escalation**, **Offer medication**, **Involuntary medication** if dangerous | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/clinical-practice-guidelines |
| **Catatonia** | Stupor, Mutism, Posturing, Negativism | **Lorazepam challenge** (1-2 mg IV/IM), **ECT** if refractory | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/clinical-practice-guidelines |
| **Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome** | Fever, Rigidity, Autonomic instability | **Stop antipsychotic**, **Supportive care**, **Bromocriptine**, **Dantrolene** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/neuroleptic-malignant-syndrome |
| **Serotonin Syndrome** | Clonus, Hyperreflexia, Agitation | **Stop serotonergic agents**, **Benzodiazepines**, **Cyproheptadine** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/postmarket-drug-safety-information-patients-and-providers/serotonin-syndrome |
| **Acute Dystonia** | Muscle spasms (neck, eyes, tongue) | **Benztropine** 1-2 mg IM/IV or **Diphenhydramine** 25-50 mg IM/IV | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/clinical-practice-guidelines |
| **Akathisia** | Inner restlessness, Pacing | **Propranolol**, **Benzodiazepine**, **Lower antipsychotic dose** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/clinical-practice-guidelines |

## Medications for Acute Agitation
| Medication | Dose | Onset | Key Considerations | URL |
|------------|------|-------|-------------------|-----|
| **Olanzapine IM** | 5-10 mg | 15-30 min | **Avoid with benzodiazepines** (respiratory depression) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/postmarket-drug-safety-information-patients-and-providers/olanzapine |
| **Haloperidol IM** | 2.5-5 mg | 30-60 min | **QTc prolongation**, **EPS**; Often combined with lorazepam or promethazine | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/postmarket-drug-safety-information-patients-and-providers/haloperidol |
| **Lorazepam IM/IV** | 1-2 mg | 15-30 min | **Respiratory depression**, **Sedation** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/postmarket-drug-safety-information-patients-and-providers/lorazepam |
| **Midazolam IM** | 2.5-5 mg | 5-15 min | **Rapid onset**, **Respiratory depression** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/postmarket-drug-safety-information-patients-and-providers/midazolam |
| **Ziprasidone IM** | 10-20 mg | 15-30 min | **QTc prolongation** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/postmarket-drug-safety-information-patients-and-providers/ziprasidone |

## Involuntary Hospitalization Criteria
| Criterion | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Danger to Self** | Suicidal ideation with intent/plan, Self-harm behavior | https://www.samhsa.gov |
| **Danger to Others** | Homicidal ideation, Threats, Assaultive behavior | https://www.samhsa.gov |
| **Grave Disability** | Inability to provide for basic needs (food, shelter, clothing) due to mental illness | https://www.samhsa.gov |
| **Duration** | Varies by state (typically 72 hours for emergency hold) | https://www.treatmentadvocacycenter.org |
