# Mental Health - Feeding and Eating Disorders

## Anorexia Nervosa
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **DSM-5-TR Criteria** | **A. Restriction of energy intake** leading to **significantly low body weight**; **B. Intense fear of gaining weight** or **persistent behavior that interferes with weight gain**; **C. Disturbance in self-perceived weight/shape** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Subtypes** | **Restricting**: No binge/purge in last 3 months; **Binge-eating/Purging**: Recurrent binge/purge in last 3 months | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Severity (BMI)** | **Mild**: =17; **Moderate**: 16-16.99; **Severe**: 15-15.99; **Extreme**: <15 | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Prevalence** | Lifetime: 1-2% (women), 0.1-0.3% (men) | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/statistics/eating-disorders |
| **Mortality** | **Highest of any psychiatric disorder** (SMR ~5-6) | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/statistics/eating-disorders |

## Bulimia Nervosa
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **DSM-5-TR Criteria** | **A. Recurrent binge eating**; **B. Recurrent inappropriate compensatory behaviors** (vomiting, laxatives, diuretics, fasting, excessive exercise); **C. =1x/week for =3 months**; **D. Self-evaluation unduly influenced by weight/shape** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Prevalence** | Lifetime: 2-3% (women), 0.3-0.5% (men) | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/statistics/eating-disorders |

## Binge-Eating Disorder
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **DSM-5-TR Criteria** | **A. Recurrent binge eating**; **B. =3 of**: Eating rapidly, Eating until uncomfortably full, Eating large amounts when not hungry, Eating alone due to embarrassment, Feeling disgusted/depressed/guilty after; **C. =1x/week for =3 months**; **D. NO compensatory behaviors** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Prevalence** | Lifetime: 2-3% (women and men more equal than other eating disorders) | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/statistics/eating-disorders |

## Treatment of Eating Disorders
| Disorder | Psychotherapy | Pharmacotherapy | URL |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Anorexia Nervosa** | **Family-Based Treatment (FBT)** for adolescents; **CBT-E**, **MANTRA**, **SSCM** for adults | **Limited efficacy**; Olanzapine (weight gain), Fluoxetine (relapse prevention) | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/clinical-practice-guidelines |
| **Bulimia Nervosa** | **CBT-E** (first-line), **IPT** | **Fluoxetine 60 mg** (FDA-approved), **Other SSRIs** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/clinical-practice-guidelines |
| **Binge-Eating Disorder** | **CBT-E**, **IPT**, **DBT** | **Lisdexamfetamine** (FDA-approved), **SSRI**, **Topiramate** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/clinical-practice-guidelines |

## Medical Complications of Eating Disorders
| Complication | Anorexia | Bulimia | URL |
|--------------|----------|---------|-----|
| **Cardiovascular** | **Bradycardia**, **Hypotension**, **QTc prolongation**, **Mitral valve prolapse** | **Arrhythmias** (electrolyte disturbance), **Ipecac cardiomyopathy** | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics/eating-disorders |
| **Gastrointestinal** | **Delayed gastric emptying**, **Constipation**, **SMA syndrome** | **Esophagitis**, **Mallory-Weiss tears**, **Dental erosion**, **Parotitis** | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics/eating-disorders |
| **Endocrine** | **Amenorrhea**, **Osteoporosis**, **Euthyroid sick syndrome** | **Menstrual irregularities** | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics/eating-disorders |
| **Electrolytes** | Variable | **Hypokalemia**, **Hypochloremia**, **Metabolic alkalosis** (vomiting); **Metabolic acidosis** (laxatives) | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics/eating-disorders |
| **Dermatologic** | **Lanugo**, **Acrocyanosis**, **Alopecia** | **Russell sign** (calluses on knuckles) | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics/eating-disorders |

## Avoidant/Restrictive Food Intake Disorder (ARFID)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **DSM-5-TR Criteria** | **Eating/feeding disturbance** (lack of interest, Sensory aversion, Fear of aversive consequences) leading to **weight loss/failure to gain**, **Nutritional deficiency**, **Dependence on supplements**, **Psychosocial impairment**; **NO body image disturbance** | https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm |
| **Treatment** | **CBT-AR**, **Family-based treatment**, **Address nutritional deficiencies** | https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics/eating-disorders |

## Refeeding Syndrome
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Metabolic disturbances** occurring when **nutrition is reintroduced** after period of starvation | https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng69 |
| **Key Features** | **Hypophosphatemia**, **Hypokalemia**, **Hypomagnesemia**, **Thiamine deficiency**, **Fluid overload** | https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng69 |
| **At-Risk Patients** | **BMI <16**, **>15% weight loss in 3-6 months**, **>10 days minimal intake**, **Low K/PO4/Mg** | https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng69 |
| **Management** | **Start low, go slow** (10-20 kcal/kg/day), **Monitor electrolytes**, **Thiamine supplementation** | https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng69 |
