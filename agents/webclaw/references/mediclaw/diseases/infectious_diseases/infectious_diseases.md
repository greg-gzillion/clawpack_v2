# Diseases - Infectious Diseases

## Bacterial Infections
| Disease | Pathogen | Transmission | Clinical Features | Diagnosis | Treatment | URL |
|---------|----------|--------------|-------------------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Streptococcal Pharyngitis** | **S. pyogenes** (GAS) | Respiratory droplets | Sore throat, Fever, Exudate, **Tender anterior cervical LAD** | **Rapid strep test**, Throat culture | **Penicillin**, Amoxicillin | https://www.cdc.gov/groupastrep/diseases-hcp/strep-throat.html |
| **Pneumonia** | **S. pneumoniae** (most common) | Respiratory droplets | Fever, Cough, Dyspnea, Pleuritic chest pain | **CXR**, Sputum culture, Urinary antigen | Amoxicillin, Respiratory FQ, Macrolide | https://www.cdc.gov/pneumonia |
| **Urinary Tract Infection** | **E. coli** (75-95%) | Ascending | Dysuria, Frequency, Urgency, Suprapubic pain | **Urinalysis** (pyuria, nitrites), Urine culture | **Nitrofurantoin**, TMP-SMX, Fosfomycin | https://www.cdc.gov/uti/about/index.html |
| **Cellulitis** | **S. aureus**, **S. pyogenes** | Skin breaks | Erythema, Warmth, Edema, Pain | Clinical | Cephalexin, Clindamycin, TMP-SMX (if MRSA suspected) | https://www.cdc.gov/groupastrep/diseases-hcp/cellulitis.html |
| **Meningitis** | **S. pneumoniae**, **N. meningitidis**, **L. monocytogenes** | Respiratory droplets | Fever, **Headache**, **Neck stiffness**, **Altered mental status** | **LP** (CSF studies), Blood cultures | **Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin** (± Ampicillin) | https://www.cdc.gov/meningitis/index.html |
| **Infective Endocarditis** | **S. aureus**, Viridans strep, Enterococcus | Hematogenous | Fever, **New murmur**, **Osler nodes**, **Janeway lesions**, **Roth spots** | **Modified Duke Criteria**, **Blood cultures**, **Echocardiography** | IV antibiotics x4-6 weeks, Surgery if indicated | https://www.heart.org/en/health-topics/infective-endocarditis |
| **Clostridioides difficile** | **C. difficile** (toxin A/B) | Fecal-oral | Watery diarrhea, Fever, Leukocytosis | **PCR/ELISA for toxin**, EIA GDH | **Oral vancomycin**, **Fidaxomicin** (preferred), FMT for recurrent | https://www.cdc.gov/cdiff/index.html |
| **Tuberculosis** | **M. tuberculosis** | Airborne | Cough >2 weeks, Hemoptysis, Night sweats, Weight loss | **IGRA/TST**, **AFB smear/culture**, **CXR** (upper lobe cavitary lesions) | **RIPE**: Rifampin, Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol | https://www.cdc.gov/tb/default.htm |
| **Lyme Disease** | **B. burgdorferi** | **Ixodes tick** | **Erythema migrans** (bullseye), Early: Flu-like; Late: Arthritis, Carditis, Neuro | Clinical + Serology (two-tier) | **Doxycycline** (all stages) | https://www.cdc.gov/lyme/index.html |
| **Syphilis** | **T. pallidum** | Sexual, Vertical | Primary: **Chancre**; Secondary: **Rash**, Condyloma lata; Tertiary: Gummas, Neurosyphilis | **RPR/VDRL** + Confirmatory **TPPA/FTA-ABS** | **Penicillin G benzathine** (primary/secondary), IV Penicillin (neurosyphilis) | https://www.cdc.gov/std/syphilis/default.htm |

## Viral Infections
| Disease | Pathogen | Transmission | Clinical Features | Diagnosis | Treatment | URL |
|---------|----------|--------------|-------------------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Influenza** | Influenza A/B | Respiratory droplets | Fever, Cough, Myalgias, Sore throat | **Rapid antigen**, PCR | **Oseltamivir** (within 48h), Supportive | https://www.cdc.gov/flu/index.htm |
| **COVID-19** | SARS-CoV-2 | Respiratory droplets, Airborne | Fever, Cough, Dyspnea, Anosmia, Ageusia | **NAAT/PCR**, Antigen | **Paxlovid**, Remdesivir, Corticosteroids (severe) | https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/index.html |
| **HIV/AIDS** | HIV-1, HIV-2 | Sexual, Blood, Perinatal | Acute: Flu-like; Chronic: Immunodeficiency, Opportunistic infections | **4th gen Ag/Ab**, RNA PCR | **ART** (integrase inhibitor + 2 NRTIs) | https://www.cdc.gov/hiv/default.html |
| **Herpes Simplex** | HSV-1, HSV-2 | Direct contact | **Vesicular lesions** (oral/genital), Recurrent | Clinical, PCR, Culture | **Acyclovir**, Valacyclovir, Famciclovir | https://www.cdc.gov/std/herpes/default.htm |
| **Varicella-Zoster** | VZV | Respiratory, Direct contact | Primary: **Chickenpox**; Reactivation: **Shingles** (dermatomal) | Clinical, PCR | Acyclovir, Valacyclovir, **Vaccine** (Shingrix) | https://www.cdc.gov/chickenpox/index.html |
| **Epstein-Barr Virus** | EBV | Saliva | **Infectious mononucleosis**: Fever, Pharyngitis, **LAD**, **Splenomegaly** | **Monospot**, EBV serologies | Supportive; **Avoid contact sports** (splenic rupture) | https://www.cdc.gov/epstein-barr/index.html |
| **Hepatitis Viruses** | HAV, HBV, HCV, HDV, HEV | Fecal-oral, Blood, Sexual | Jaundice, RUQ pain, Nausea, Elevated LFTs | Serologies, PCR | See Hepatobiliary section | https://www.cdc.gov/hepatitis/index.htm |

## Fungal Infections
| Disease | Pathogen | Risk Factors | Clinical Features | Diagnosis | Treatment | URL |
|---------|----------|--------------|-------------------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Candidiasis** | Candida spp. | Immunocompromise, Antibiotics, Diabetes | Oropharyngeal (thrush), Esophageal, Vulvovaginal, Invasive | KOH prep, Culture, **Beta-D-glucan** | Fluconazole, Echinocandins (invasive) | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/diseases/candidiasis/index.html |
| **Aspergillosis** | Aspergillus spp. | Neutropenia, Steroids, COPD | Allergic (ABPA), **Pulmonary aspergilloma** (cavitary), Invasive | **Galactomannan**, Culture, CT (halo sign) | **Voriconazole**, Isavuconazole, Echinocandins | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/diseases/aspergillosis/index.html |
| **Cryptococcosis** | C. neoformans | **HIV/AIDS**, Immunocompromise | **Meningoencephalitis**, Pneumonia | **CSF cryptococcal antigen**, India ink | **Amphotericin B + Flucytosine** ? Fluconazole | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/diseases/cryptococcosis-neoformans/index.html |
| **Histoplasmosis** | H. capsulatum | Ohio/Mississippi River valleys | Pneumonia, Disseminated in immunocompromised | **Urine/serum antigen**, Culture | Itraconazole, Amphotericin B (severe) | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/diseases/histoplasmosis/index.html |
| **Coccidioidomycosis** | Coccidioides spp. | **Southwest US** | **Valley fever**: Pneumonia, Erythema nodosum | Serology (IgM/IgG), Culture | Fluconazole, Itraconazole, Amphotericin B | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/diseases/coccidioidomycosis/index.html |
| **Pneumocystis Pneumonia** | P. jirovecii | **HIV (CD4 <200)** , Immunocompromise | Fever, Dry cough, Hypoxia, **? LDH** | **PCR/DIF** on BAL/induced sputum | **TMP-SMX** (prophylaxis and treatment) | https://www.cdc.gov/fungal/diseases/pneumocystis-pneumonia/index.html |

## Parasitic Infections
| Disease | Pathogen | Transmission | Clinical Features | Diagnosis | Treatment | URL |
|---------|----------|--------------|-------------------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Malaria** | Plasmodium spp. | **Anopheles mosquito** | Cyclic fevers, Chills, Splenomegaly, Anemia | **Thick/thin smear**, Rapid antigen | Artemisinin-based combination therapy (ACT) | https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/index.html |
| **Giardiasis** | G. lamblia | Fecal-oral (waterborne) | Foul-smelling diarrhea, Bloating, Steatorrhea | **Stool O&P**, Antigen | **Metronidazole**, Tinidazole, Nitazoxanide | https://www.cdc.gov/parasites/giardia/index.html |
| **Toxoplasmosis** | T. gondii | Undercooked meat, Cat feces | Immunocompetent: Asymptomatic/flu-like; HIV: **Ring-enhancing brain lesions** | Serology, PCR | Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin | https://www.cdc.gov/parasites/toxoplasmosis/index.html |
| **Strongyloidiasis** | S. stercoralis | Skin penetration | **Larva currens**, GI symptoms, **Hyperinfection** in immunocompromised | Stool O&P, Serology | **Ivermectin** | https://www.cdc.gov/parasites/strongyloides/index.html |

## Sepsis and Septic Shock
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Sepsis** | Life-threatening organ dysfunction due to dysregulated host response to infection | https://www.cdc.gov/sepsis/index.html |
| **Septic Shock** | Sepsis + **Vasopressor requirement** + **Lactate >2 mmol/L** despite adequate fluids | https://www.sccm.org/SurvivingSepsisCampaign/Guidelines |
| **qSOFA** | **RR =22**, **SBP =100**, **Altered mental status** (=2 = high risk) | https://www.mdcalc.com/calc/2654/qsofa-quick-sofa-score-sepsis |
| **SOFA Score** | PaO2/FiO2, Platelets, Bilirubin, MAP/Vasopressors, GCS, Creatinine/UOP | https://www.mdcalc.com/calc/691/sequential-organ-failure-assessment-sofa-score |
| **Management** | **Early antibiotics** (within 1h), **IV fluids** (30 mL/kg), **Vasopressors** (norepinephrine first-line), Source control | https://www.sccm.org/SurvivingSepsisCampaign/Guidelines |
