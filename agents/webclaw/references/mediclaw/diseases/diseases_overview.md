# Diseases - Overview

## What is Disease?
A disease is a particular abnormal condition that negatively affects the structure or function of all or part of an organism and is not immediately due to any external injury.

## Disease Classification
| Category | Description | Examples | URL |
|----------|-------------|----------|-----|
| **Infectious** | Caused by pathogenic microorganisms | Pneumonia, Tuberculosis, HIV/AIDS | https://www.cdc.gov/diseasesconditions |
| **Genetic** | Caused by abnormalities in genes or chromosomes | Cystic fibrosis, Sickle cell disease | https://www.genome.gov/For-Patients-and-Families/Genetic-Disorders |
| **Autoimmune** | Immune system attacks self-tissues | Rheumatoid arthritis, Lupus, MS | https://www.niaid.nih.gov/diseases-conditions/autoimmune-diseases |
| **Metabolic** | Disruption of normal metabolism | Diabetes, Thyroid disorders | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information |
| **Neoplastic** | Abnormal cell growth | Cancer, Benign tumors | https://www.cancer.gov |
| **Degenerative** | Progressive deterioration of tissues | Osteoarthritis, Alzheimer disease | https://www.nia.nih.gov/health |
| **Nutritional** | Deficiency or excess of nutrients | Scurvy, Obesity, Iron deficiency | https://ods.od.nih.gov |
| **Environmental** | Exposure to environmental factors | Lead poisoning, Asbestosis | https://www.niehs.nih.gov |

## Disease Epidemiology Terms
| Term | Definition | URL |
|------|------------|-----|
| **Incidence** | Number of new cases in a population over a specific time period | https://www.cdc.gov/csels/dsepd/ss1978/lesson3/section2.html |
| **Prevalence** | Proportion of a population with a disease at a specific time | https://www.cdc.gov/csels/dsepd/ss1978/lesson3/section2.html |
| **Morbidity** | State of being diseased or the rate of disease in a population | https://www.cdc.gov/csels/dsepd/ss1978/lesson3/section2.html |
| **Mortality** | Death rate in a population | https://www.cdc.gov/csels/dsepd/ss1978/lesson3/section2.html |
| **Case Fatality Rate** | Proportion of cases that result in death | https://www.cdc.gov/csels/dsepd/ss1978/lesson3/section2.html |
| **Attack Rate** | Proportion of exposed persons who become ill | https://www.cdc.gov/csels/dsepd/ss1978/lesson3/section2.html |
