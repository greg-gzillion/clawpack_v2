# Diseases - Hepatobiliary Diseases

## Viral Hepatitis
| Type | Transmission | Chronicity | Prevention | Treatment | URL |
|------|--------------|------------|------------|-----------|-----|
| **Hepatitis A** | Fecal-oral | **Never chronic** | **Vaccine**, Hygiene | Supportive | https://www.cdc.gov/hepatitis/hav/index.htm |
| **Hepatitis B** | Blood, Sexual, Perinatal | 5-10% adults, 90% infants | **Vaccine** | Antivirals (tenofovir, entecavir) | https://www.cdc.gov/hepatitis/hbv/index.htm |
| **Hepatitis C** | **Blood** (IVDU, Transfusion) | **75-85%** | No vaccine, Harm reduction | **DAAs** (>95% cure) | https://www.cdc.gov/hepatitis/hcv/index.htm |
| **Hepatitis D** | Blood (requires HBV) | Accelerates HBV progression | HBV vaccine | Peginterferon | https://www.cdc.gov/hepatitis/hdv/index.htm |
| **Hepatitis E** | Fecal-oral (waterborne) | Rare (immunocompromised) | Hygiene | Supportive; Ribavirin (chronic) | https://www.cdc.gov/hepatitis/hev/index.htm |

|-----------|-------------|-----|

## Nonalcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | Hepatic steatosis without significant alcohol consumption | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/liver-disease/nafld-nash |
| **Spectrum** | NAFL (steatosis only) ? NASH (steatosis + inflammation) ? Cirrhosis ? HCC | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/liver-disease/nafld-nash |
| **Risk Factors** | **Obesity**, **Type 2 DM**, **Metabolic syndrome** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/liver-disease/nafld-nash |
| **Diagnosis** | Imaging (US, CT, MRI), **Liver biopsy** (gold standard for NASH) | https://www.aasld.org/practice-guidelines/nafld |
| **Treatment** | **Weight loss** (7-10%), Exercise, Treat metabolic syndrome | https://www.aasld.org/practice-guidelines/nafld |

## Cholelithiasis and Cholecystitis
| Condition | Pathophysiology | Symptoms | Diagnosis | Treatment | URL |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Cholelithiasis** | Gallstones (cholesterol 80%, pigment 20%) | Often asymptomatic; Biliary colic if obstructing | **Ultrasound** | Elective cholecystectomy if symptomatic | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/digestive-diseases/gallstones |
| **Acute Cholecystitis** | Gallstone impaction ? Inflammation | **RUQ pain**, Fever, **Murphy sign** | **Ultrasound** (gallstones + wall thickening + pericholecystic fluid) | **Cholecystectomy** (early, within 72h) | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/digestive-diseases/gallstones |
| **Choledocholithiasis** | Stone in common bile duct | **Jaundice**, RUQ pain, Cholangitis | **MRCP/EUS**, **ERCP** (therapeutic) | **ERCP + sphincterotomy** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/digestive-diseases/gallstones |
| **Ascending Cholangitis** | Bile duct obstruction + Infection | **Charcot triad**: Fever, Jaundice, RUQ pain; **Reynolds pentad**: + Hypotension, AMS | Clinical + Imaging | **ERCP + Antibiotics** | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/digestive-diseases/gallstones |

## Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Epidemiology** | Most common primary liver cancer | https://www.cancer.gov/types/liver |
| **Risk Factors** | **Cirrhosis** (any cause), Chronic HBV, Chronic HCV, NAFLD | https://www.cancer.gov/types/liver |
| **Screening** | **Ultrasound ± AFP q6 months** in high-risk patients | https://www.aasld.org/practice-guidelines/hcc |
| **Diagnosis** | **LI-RADS** on multiphase CT/MRI (arterial enhancement + washout) | https://www.acr.org/Clinical-Resources/Reporting-and-Data-Systems/LI-RADS |
| **Treatment** | Resection, Transplant, Ablation, TACE, TARE, Systemic therapy | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/hcc |
