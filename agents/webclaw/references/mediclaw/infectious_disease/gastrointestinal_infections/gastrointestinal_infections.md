# Infectious Disease - Gastrointestinal Infections

## Acute Gastroenteritis - Overview
| Pathogen Type | Common Pathogens | Incubation | Clinical Features | Diagnosis | Treatment | URL |
|---------------|-----------------|------------|-------------------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Viral** | Norovirus, Rotavirus, Adenovirus | 12-48 hours | Vomiting predominant, Watery diarrhea | PCR, Antigen | Supportive | https://www.cdc.gov/norovirus/index.html |
| **Bacterial (Toxin)** | B. cereus, S. aureus, C. perfringens | 1-6 hours | Vomiting, Watery diarrhea | Clinical | Supportive | https://www.cdc.gov/foodsafety/index.html |
| **Bacterial (Inflammatory)** | Campylobacter, Salmonella, Shigella, STEC | 1-7 days | Fever, Bloody diarrhea, Tenesmus | Stool culture, PCR | Supportive; Antibiotics if severe | https://www.cdc.gov/foodsafety/index.html |

## Clostridioides difficile Infection (CDI)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Pathogenesis** | Antibiotic exposure ? Disruption of gut microbiota ? C. difficile germination ? Toxins A and B | https://www.cdc.gov/cdiff/index.html |
| **Risk Factors** | Antibiotics, Age >65, Hospitalization, PPI use, Immunosuppression | https://www.cdc.gov/cdiff/index.html |
| **Diagnosis** | PCR (toxin B gene), EIA (toxin A/B), GDH + Toxin algorithm | https://www.cdc.gov/cdiff/index.html |
| **Treatment (Non-severe)** | Oral vancomycin 125 mg QID x 10 days or Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID x 10 days | https://www.cdc.gov/cdiff/index.html |
| **Treatment (Fulminant)** | Oral vancomycin 500 mg QID + IV Metronidazole; Surgical consultation | https://www.cdc.gov/cdiff/index.html |
| **Recurrent CDI** | Fidaxomicin, Vancomycin taper/pulse, Bezlotoxumab, FMT | https://www.cdc.gov/cdiff/index.html |

## Foodborne Illness - Key Pathogens
| Pathogen | Common Sources | Key Features | Treatment | URL |
|----------|----------------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Staphylococcus aureus** | Cream-filled pastries, Mayonnaise | Preformed toxin; Vomiting (1-6 hours) | Supportive | https://www.cdc.gov/foodsafety/diseases/staphylococcal.html |
| **Bacillus cereus** | Fried rice | Emetic toxin (vomiting) OR Diarrheal toxin | Supportive | https://www.cdc.gov/foodsafety/diseases/bacillus-cereus.html |
| **Clostridium botulinum** | Home-canned foods, Honey | Descending flaccid paralysis | Botulism antitoxin | https://www.cdc.gov/botulism/index.html |
| **Salmonella (non-typhoidal)** | Poultry, Eggs, Reptiles | Fever, Diarrhea (6-72h) | Supportive; Antibiotics if severe | https://www.cdc.gov/salmonella/index.html |
| **Campylobacter jejuni** | Poultry, Unpasteurized milk | Bloody diarrhea, Guillain-Barrť | Azithromycin | https://www.cdc.gov/campylobacter/index.html |
| **Shigella** | Fecal-oral, Daycare | Dysentery, Seizures (children) | Ciprofloxacin, Azithromycin | https://www.cdc.gov/shigella/index.html |
| **STEC** | Undercooked ground beef | Bloody diarrhea, HUS | Supportive; AVOID antibiotics | https://www.cdc.gov/ecoli/index.html |
| **Listeria monocytogenes** | Deli meats, Soft cheeses | Meningitis, Pregnancy (fetal loss) | Ampicillin + Gentamicin | https://www.cdc.gov/listeria/index.html |
| **Vibrio vulnificus** | Raw oysters | Rapidly progressive cellulitis/sepsis | Ceftriaxone + Doxycycline | https://www.cdc.gov/vibrio/index.html |
| **Hepatitis A** | Shellfish, Contaminated produce | Fever, Jaundice | Supportive | https://www.cdc.gov/hepatitis/hav/index.html |

## Traveler's Diarrhea
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Pathogens** | ETEC, Campylobacter, Shigella, Salmonella, Norovirus | https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/yellowbook/2024/preparing/travelers-diarrhea |
