# Infectious Disease - Atypical Bacterial Infections

## Mycoplasma and Ureaplasma

|---------|------------------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Mycoplasma pneumoniae** | Walking pneumonia, Tracheobronchitis, Bullous myringitis, Erythema multiforme, Cold agglutinin hemolytic anemia, Guillain-Barré | PCR (preferred), Cold agglutinins (50-70%), Culture (slow) | Azithromycin, Doxycycline, Levofloxacin | https://www.cdc.gov/mycoplasma/about/index.html |

## Chlamydia and Chlamydophila
| Species | Clinical Syndrome | Diagnosis | Treatment | URL |
|---------|------------------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Chlamydia trachomatis (D-K)** | Urethritis, Cervicitis, PID, Proctitis, Reactive arthritis (Reiter syndrome) | NAAT (urine, Swab) | Doxycycline 100 mg BID x 7 days (preferred); Azithromycin 1g x 1 (alternative) | https://www.cdc.gov/std/treatment-guidelines/chlamydia.htm |
| **C. trachomatis (L1-L3)** | Lymphogranuloma venereum (LGV) | NAAT (genotype) | Doxycycline 100 mg BID x 21 days | https://www.cdc.gov/std/treatment-guidelines/lgv.htm |
| **C. trachomatis (A-C)** | Trachoma (leading infectious cause of blindness) | Clinical | Azithromycin (mass drug administration) | https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/trachoma |
| **Chlamydophila pneumoniae** | Atypical pneumonia, Pharyngitis, Bronchitis | PCR, Serology | Doxycycline, Azithromycin, Levofloxacin | https://www.cdc.gov/cpneumoniae/about/index.html |
| **Chlamydophila psittaci** | Psittacosis (atypical pneumonia after bird exposure) | PCR, Serology | Doxycycline | https://www.cdc.gov/psittacosis/about/index.html |

## Rickettsial Infections
| Species | Disease | Vector | Clinical Features | Diagnosis | Treatment | URL |
|---------|---------|--------|-------------------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Rickettsia rickettsii** | Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever (RMSF) | Tick (Dermacentor) | Fever, Headache, Rash (starts wrists/ankles ? trunk, Palms/soles), Petechiae | Serology (convalescent), PCR (blood/skin), Immunohistochemistry | Doxycycline (all ages, Even pregnancy) | https://www.cdc.gov/rmsf/index.html |
| **Rickettsia typhi** | Murine Typhus | Flea | Fever, Headache, Rash (less severe than RMSF) | Serology | Doxycycline | https://www.cdc.gov/typhus/murine/index.html |
| **Rickettsia prowazekii** | Epidemic Typhus, Brill-Zinsser disease (recrudescent) | Louse | Fever, Headache, Rash, Delirium | Serology | Doxycycline | https://www.cdc.gov/typhus/epidemic/index.html |
| **Orientia tsutsugamushi** | Scrub Typhus | Mite (chigger) | Fever, Headache, Eschar, Lymphadenopathy | Serology, PCR | Doxycycline, Azithromycin | https://www.cdc.gov/scrubtyphus/index.html |
| **Ehrlichia chaffeensis** | Human Monocytic Ehrlichiosis (HME) | Tick (Amblyomma) | Fever, Headache, Leukopenia, Thrombocytopenia, Elevated LFTs, Morulae in monocytes | PCR (blood), Serology | Doxycycline | https://www.cdc.gov/ehrlichiosis/index.html |
| **Anaplasma phagocytophilum** | Human Granulocytic Anaplasmosis (HGA) | Tick (Ixodes) | Fever, Headache, Leukopenia, Thrombocytopenia, Elevated LFTs, Morulae in granulocytes | PCR (blood), Serology | Doxycycline | https://www.cdc.gov/anaplasmosis/index.html |

## Coxiella burnetii (Q Fever)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Transmission | Inhalation of aerosols from infected animals (cattle, Sheep, Goats); Parturient fluids highly infectious | https://www.cdc.gov/qfever/index.html |
| Acute Q Fever | Flu-like illness, Atypical pneumonia, Hepatitis, Headache | https://www.cdc.gov/qfever/symptoms/index.html |
| Chronic Q Fever | Culture-negative endocarditis (most common), Vascular infection, Osteomyelitis; More common in immunocompromised, Valvular disease | https://www.cdc.gov/qfever/chronic/index.html |
| Diagnosis | Serology (Phase I and II antibodies); PCR (tissue) | https://www.cdc.gov/qfever/diagnosis/index.html |
| Treatment (Acute) | Doxycycline x 14 days | https://www.cdc.gov/qfever/treatment/index.html |
| Treatment (Chronic) | Doxycycline + Hydroxychloroquine x 18-24 months | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK556086/ |

## Tropheryma whipplei (Whipple Disease)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Clinical Features | Arthralgias (years before diagnosis), Diarrhea, Weight loss, Abdominal pain, Neurologic (oculomasticatory myorhythmia, Dementia), Cardiac (culture-negative endocarditis) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/whipple-disease |
| Diagnosis | PAS-positive macrophages in duodenal biopsy, PCR (tissue, CSF, Synovial fluid) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK441937/ |
| Treatment | Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily x 2 weeks ? TMP-SMX DS BID x 1 year | https://www.merckmanuals.com/professional/gastrointestinal-disorders/malabsorption-syndromes/whipple-disease |

## Nocardia
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Risk Factors | Immunocompromised (steroids, Transplant, HIV), CGD, Alveolar proteinosis | https://www.cdc.gov/nocardiosis/about/index.html |
| Clinical Syndromes | Pulmonary (most common - cavitary nodules), CNS (brain abscess), Cutaneous (lymphocutaneous), Disseminated | https://www.cdc.gov/nocardiosis/symptoms/index.html |
| Diagnosis | Gram stain (beaded, Branching, Gram-positive, Partially acid-fast), Culture (slow growth), PCR | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK526075/ |
| Treatment | TMP-SMX (first-line); Alternatives: Imipenem, Ceftriaxone, Linezolid, Amikacin; Prolonged therapy (6-12 months) | https://www.cdc.gov/nocardiosis/treatment/index.html |

## Actinomyces
| Component | Description | URL |
