# Infectious Disease - Gram-Negative Bacterial Infections

|---------|----------------------|----------------|-----------|-----|
| **Escherichia coli** | UTI, Intra-abdominal, Bacteremia, Neonatal meningitis | ESBL, AmpC | Ceftriaxone, Ciprofloxacin, TMP-SMX | https://www.cdc.gov/ecoli/index.html |
| **Klebsiella pneumoniae** | Pneumonia, Liver abscess, UTI, Bacteremia | ESBL, KPC carbapenemase | Carbapenems, Ceftazidime-avibactam | https://www.cdc.gov/klebsiella/about/index.html |
| **Proteus mirabilis** | UTI, Struvite stones (urease producer) | Usually susceptible | Ampicillin, Ceftriaxone | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK482327/ |
| **Enterobacter cloacae** | Healthcare-associated infections | Chromosomal AmpC | Cefepime, Carbapenems | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK557829/ |
| **Serratia marcescens** | Healthcare-associated infections, Bacteremia | AmpC | Same as Enterobacter | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK482442/ |
| **Citrobacter freundii** | Healthcare-associated infections | AmpC | Same as Enterobacter | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK482439/ |
| **Salmonella (non-typhoidal)** | Gastroenteritis, Bacteremia | Usually susceptible | Supportive; Ciprofloxacin, Ceftriaxone | https://www.cdc.gov/salmonella/index.html |
| **Salmonella Typhi/Paratyphi** | Enteric fever (Typhoid/Paratyphoid) | Increasing FQ resistance | Ceftriaxone, Azithromycin | https://www.cdc.gov/typhoid-fever/index.html |
| **Shigella** | Dysentery (bloody diarrhea, Tenesmus, Fever) | Increasing MDR | Ciprofloxacin, Azithromycin, Ceftriaxone | https://www.cdc.gov/shigella/index.html |
| **Yersinia enterocolitica** | Gastroenteritis, Pseudoappendicitis, Reactive arthritis | Usually susceptible | Supportive; TMP-SMX, Ciprofloxacin, Doxycycline | https://www.cdc.gov/yersinia/index.html |
| **Yersinia pestis** | Plague (Bubonic, Septicemic, Pneumonic) | Usually susceptible | Gentamicin, Streptomycin, Doxycycline | https://www.cdc.gov/plague/index.html |
