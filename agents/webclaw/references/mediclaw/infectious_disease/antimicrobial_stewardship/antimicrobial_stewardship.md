# Infectious Disease - Antimicrobial Stewardship

## Antimicrobial Stewardship Principles
| Principle | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Right Drug** | Select appropriate antimicrobial based on likely pathogens, Local antibiogram, Patient factors | https://www.cdc.gov/antimicrobial-resistance/prevention/index.html |
| **Right Dose** | Optimize dose for site of infection, Renal/hepatic function, Pharmacodynamics | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK448069/ |
| **Right Duration** | Shortest effective duration; Avoid unnecessarily prolonged courses | https://www.cdc.gov/antibiotic-use/core-elements/index.html |
| **Right Route** | IV-to-PO conversion when clinically stable, Tolerating PO, Functioning GI tract | https://www.ahrq.gov/antibiotic-use/index.html |
| **De-escalation** | Narrow spectrum based on culture results | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6354181/ |
| **Time-Out** | Reassess need for antibiotics at 48-72 hours | https://www.jointcommission.org/standards/standard-faqs/hospital-and-hospital-clinics/national-patient-safety-goals-npsg/000002213/ |

## Pharmacokinetic/Pharmacodynamic (PK/PD) Principles
| Pattern | Description | Examples | Optimization Strategy | URL |
|---------|-------------|----------|----------------------|-----|
| **Time-Dependent** | Efficacy depends on time above MIC (T>MIC) | Beta-lactams, Vancomycin, Carbapenems | Extended/continuous infusion, Frequent dosing | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5813898/ |
| **Concentration-Dependent** | Efficacy depends on peak concentration/MIC (Cmax/MIC) | Aminoglycosides, Fluoroquinolones, Daptomycin, Metronidazole | High dose once daily (aminoglycosides) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5813898/ |
| **AUC-Dependent** | Efficacy depends on AUC/MIC ratio | Vancomycin, Fluoroquinolones, Linezolid, Tetracyclines | Therapeutic drug monitoring (vancomycin AUC) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK459263/ |

## Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM)
| Drug | Target | Monitoring | URL |
|------|--------|------------|-----|
| **Vancomycin** | AUC/MIC 400-600 (serious MRSA) | AUC-guided dosing (preferred) or Trough 10-20 mcg/mL | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vancomycin/ |
| **Aminoglycosides** | Peak: Gentamicin 8-12, Tobramycin 8-12, Amikacin 25-35; Trough: <1-2 | Serum concentrations | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK541105/ |
| **Voriconazole** | Trough 1-5.5 mcg/mL | CYP2C19 polymorphisms, Drug interactions | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK537281/ |

## IV-to-PO Conversion Criteria
| Criterion | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Clinical Stability** | Afebrile, Hemodynamically stable, Improving clinically | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5373083/ |
| **Functional GI Tract** | Tolerating PO intake, No malabsorption, No ileus/obstruction | https://www.ahrq.gov/antibiotic-use/acute-care/improve/iv-to-po.html |
| **Bioavailable PO Option** | High oral bioavailability (fluoroquinolones, Linezolid, Metronidazole, TMP-SMX, Doxycycline, Fluconazole) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5373083/ |
| **No Contraindication** | No severe infection requiring prolonged IV (endocarditis, Meningitis, CNS infection, S. aureus bacteremia) | https://www.idsociety.org/globalassets/idsa/practice-guidelines/implementing-an-antibiotic-stewardship-program.pdf |

## Duration of Therapy - Common Infections
| Infection | Recommended Duration | URL |
|-----------|---------------------|-----|
| **Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP)** | 5 days (if clinically stable) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/cap/ |
| **Hospital-Acquired/Ventilator-Associated Pneumonia (HAP/VAP)** | 7 days | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/hap-vap/ |
| **Uncomplicated Cystitis** | Nitrofurantoin 5 days, TMP-SMX 3 days, Fosfomycin 1 dose | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/uti/ |
| **Pyelonephritis** | Fluoroquinolone 7 days, Beta-lactam 10-14 days | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/uti/ |
| **Intra-abdominal Infection** | 4 days after source control | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/intra-abdominal-infection/ |
| **Cellulitis** | 5-7 days | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/ssti/ |
| **S. aureus Bacteremia** | 4-6 weeks (complicated), 2 weeks (uncomplicated) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/mrsa/ |
| **Gram-Negative Bacteremia** | 7 days (uncomplicated) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/gram-negative-bacteremia/ |
| **Vertebral Osteomyelitis** | 6 weeks | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/vertebral-osteomyelitis/ |
| **Prosthetic Joint Infection** | 6 weeks (plus chronic suppression) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/pji/ |

## Common PK/PD Adjustments
| Condition | Adjustment | URL |
|-----------|------------|-----|
| **Renal Impairment** | Adjust doses for renally cleared drugs (beta-lactams, Vancomycin, Aminoglycosides, Fluoroquinolones, TMP-SMX, Daptomycin) | https://www.kidney.org/professionals/kdoqi/gfr_calculator |
| **Hepatic Impairment** | Adjust for hepatically metabolized drugs (macrolides, Clindamycin, Metronidazole, Rifampin, Voriconazole) | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK557717/ |
| **Obesity** | Use adjusted body weight for aminoglycosides, Vancomycin; Actual body weight for beta-lactams, Fluoroquinolones | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5860130/ |
| **Cystic Fibrosis** | Increased volume of distribution and clearance ? Higher doses of beta-lactams, Aminoglycosides | https://www.cff.org/managing-cf/antibiotics |
