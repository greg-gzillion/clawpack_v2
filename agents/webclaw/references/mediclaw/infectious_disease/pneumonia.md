# Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP)

## Definition
Acute infection of lung parenchyma acquired outside healthcare setting

## Etiology
- Typical bacteria: S. pneumoniae (most common), H. influenzae, M. catarrhalis
- Atypical: M. pneumoniae, C. pneumoniae, L. pneumophila
- Viral: influenza, RSV, SARS-CoV-2
- Anaerobes (aspiration)

## Risk Factors
- Age >65 years
- Smoking, COPD
- Immunosuppression
- Diabetes
- Alcoholism
- Aspiration risk (stroke, dementia)

## Clinical Presentation
- Fever, chills
- Cough (productive or dry)
- Dyspnea, tachypnea
- Pleuritic chest pain
- Sputum production (purulent, rust-colored)
- Altered mental status (elderly)

## Physical Exam
- Crackles (rales)
- Egophony, bronchophony
- Tactile fremitus increased
- Dullness to percussion (effusion)

## Diagnosis
- Chest X-ray (infiltrate, lobar consolidation)
- Labs: CBC, BMP, lactate, procalcitonin
- Blood cultures (if severe or hospitalized)
- Sputum Gram stain/culture
- Urinary antigen: pneumococcus, Legionella
- PCR: COVID-19, influenza, M. pneumoniae

## Severity Scoring

### CURB-65 (1 point each)
- Confusion
- Uremia (BUN >19 mg/dL)
- Respiratory rate ≥30
- BP <90/60
- Age ≥65

Score 0-1: outpatient
Score 2: brief hospitalization
Score 3+: ICU admission

### PSI/PORT Score
- More comprehensive, used for risk stratification

## Treatment

### Outpatient (CURB-65 0-1)
- **Standard:** amoxicillin 1g TID x 5-7 days
- **Alternative (comorbidities):** doxycycline 100mg BID, or macrolide (if low resistance)
- **Atypicals coverage:** doxycycline or macrolide

### Inpatient (non-ICU, CURB-65 2)
- **Preferred:** beta-lactam (ceftriaxone, cefpodoxime) + macrolide
- **Alternative:** respiratory fluoroquinolone (levofloxacin, moxifloxacin)

### ICU (CURB-65 ≥3)
- **Preferred:** beta-lactam (ceftriaxone, cefotaxime) + macrolide
- **Alternative:** beta-lactam + respiratory fluoroquinolone

### Duration
- 5 days (if afebrile 48-72 hours, clinically stable)
- Longer if slow response, cavitation, or S. aureus

## Complications
- Parapneumonic effusion
- Empyema (drainage required)
- Lung abscess
- Sepsis, septic shock
- ARDS
- Respiratory failure

## Prevention
- Pneumococcal vaccines (PCV15/20, PPSV23)
- Influenza vaccine annually
- COVID-19 vaccination
- Smoking cessation
