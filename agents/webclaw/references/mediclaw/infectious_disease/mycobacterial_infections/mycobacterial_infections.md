## Latent TB Infection (LTBI)
| Screening | Interpretation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **TST (PPD)** | =5 mm: HIV, Immunocompromised; =10 mm: High-risk; =15 mm: Low-risk | https://www.cdc.gov/tb/topic/testing/default.htm |
| **IGRA** | Positive = TB infection; No cross-reactivity with BCG | https://www.cdc.gov/tb/topic/testing/default.htm |
| LTBI Treatment | Isoniazid x 9 months; Rifampin x 4 months; 3HP (weekly x 12 weeks) | https://www.cdc.gov/tb/topic/treatment/ltbi.htm |

## Active TB Treatment (RIPE Therapy)
| Phase | Drugs | Duration | URL |
|-------|-------|----------|-----|
| **Intensive Phase** | Rifampin + Isoniazid + Pyrazinamide + Ethambutol | 2 months | https://www.cdc.gov/tb/topic/treatment/tbdisease.htm |
| **Continuation Phase** | Rifampin + Isoniazid | 4 months | https://www.cdc.gov/tb/topic/treatment/tbdisease.htm |

## Antituberculous Drugs - Adverse Effects
| Drug | Key Adverse Effects | URL |
|------|---------------------|-----|
| **Isoniazid (INH)** | Hepatotoxicity, Peripheral neuropathy (give pyridoxine) | https://www.cdc.gov/tb/topic/treatment/adverseevents.htm |
| **Rifampin** | Orange body fluids, Hepatotoxicity, Potent CYP3A4 inducer | https://www.cdc.gov/tb/topic/treatment/adverseevents.htm |
| **Pyrazinamide (PZA)** | Hepatotoxicity, Hyperuricemia | https://www.cdc.gov/tb/topic/treatment/adverseevents.htm |
| **Ethambutol (EMB)** | Optic neuritis | https://www.cdc.gov/tb/topic/treatment/adverseevents.htm |

## Drug-Resistant TB
| Type | Definition | Treatment | URL |
|------|------------|-----------|-----|
| **MDR-TB** | Resistant to INH + Rifampin | Bedaquiline, Pretomanid, Linezolid, Moxifloxacin | https://www.cdc.gov/tb/topic/drtb/default.htm |
| **XDR-TB** | MDR-TB + Resistant to fluoroquinolone + one injectable | Individualized | https://www.cdc.gov/tb/topic/drtb/default.htm |

## Nontuberculous Mycobacteria (NTM)
| Species | Clinical Syndrome | Treatment | URL |
|---------|------------------|-----------|-----|
| **M. avium Complex (MAC)** | Pulmonary, Disseminated (HIV) | Azithromycin + Rifampin + Ethambutol | https://www.cdc.gov/nontuberculous-mycobacteria/index.html |
| **M. kansasii** | Pulmonary disease | Isoniazid + Rifampin + Ethambutol | https://www.cdc.gov/nontuberculous-mycobacteria/index.html |
| **M. abscessus** | Pulmonary, Skin/soft tissue | Azithromycin + Amikacin + Cefoxitin/Imipenem | https://www.cdc.gov/nontuberculous-mycobacteria/index.html |
| **M. marinum** | Fish tank granuloma | Clarithromycin + Ethambutol | https://www.cdc.gov/nontuberculous-mycobacteria/index.html |
