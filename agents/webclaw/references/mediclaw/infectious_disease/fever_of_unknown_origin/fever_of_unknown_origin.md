# Infectious Disease - Fever of Unknown Origin (FUO)

## FUO - Definitions
| Category | Definition | URL |
|----------|------------|-----|
| **Classic FUO** | Fever =38.3°C, Duration =3 weeks, No diagnosis after 3 outpatient visits or 3 days inpatient | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **Nosocomial FUO** | Fever in hospitalized patient, =3 days evaluation | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **Neutropenic FUO** | Fever + ANC <500/µL, =3 days evaluation | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **HIV-Associated FUO** | Fever in HIV-infected patient, =4 weeks outpatient or =3 days inpatient | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |

## Classic FUO - Etiologies
| Category | Frequency | Examples | URL |
|----------|-----------|----------|-----|
| **Infections** | 20-30% | TB, Intra-abdominal abscess, Endocarditis, Osteomyelitis, Brucellosis, Q fever | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **Malignancies** | 10-20% | Lymphoma, Renal cell carcinoma, Hepatocellular carcinoma, Leukemia | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **Non-Infectious Inflammatory Diseases** | 20-30% | Adult-onset Still disease, Giant cell arteritis, SLE, Sarcoidosis | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **Miscellaneous** | 10-15% | Drug fever, Factitious fever, Thyroiditis, Hematoma, PE | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **Undiagnosed** | 20-30% | Despite extensive evaluation | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |

## FUO - Diagnostic Approach
| Step | Evaluation | URL |
|------|------------|-----|
| **1. Confirm Fever** | Document fever; Rule out factitious fever | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **2. Comprehensive History** | Travel, Animal/insect exposure, Occupational, Dietary, Sexual, IVDU, Medications | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **3. Physical Exam** | Lymphadenopathy, Hepatosplenomegaly, Heart murmur, Skin lesions, Joints, Temporal arteries | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **4. Initial Labs** | CBC, CMP, ESR/CRP, Ferritin, LDH, Blood cultures, HIV, EBV/CMV, CXR, UA, TST/IGRA | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **5. Further Testing** | CT chest/abdomen/pelvis, Echocardiography, PET-CT | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **6. Invasive Testing** | Lymph node biopsy, Bone marrow biopsy, Liver biopsy, Temporal artery biopsy | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |

## Key Diagnostic Clues in FUO
| Finding | Suspected Etiology | URL |
|---------|---------------------|-----|
| **Relative bradycardia** | Typhoid, Brucellosis, Leptospirosis, Drug fever | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **Episodic fever** (Pel-Ebstein) | Hodgkin lymphoma | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **Elevated ferritin >10,000** | Adult-onset Still disease, HLH | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
| **Eosinophilia** | Drug reaction, Parasitic infection, Adrenal insufficiency | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK532256/ |
