# Infectious Disease - Bioterrorism Agents

## CDC Category A Agents (Highest Priority)
| Agent | Disease | Transmission | Incubation | Clinical Features | Diagnosis | Treatment | Prophylaxis | URL |
|-------|---------|--------------|------------|-------------------|-----------|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Bacillus anthracis** | Anthrax | Inhalation, Cutaneous, GI | 1-7 days (up to 60) | Inhalational: Widened mediastinum, Hemorrhagic mediastinitis; Cutaneous: Painless eschar | Culture, PCR, Gram stain | Ciprofloxacin or Doxycycline + Clindamycin + Antitoxin | Ciprofloxacin or Doxycycline x 60 days + Vaccine | https://www.cdc.gov/anthrax/index.html |
| **Yersinia pestis** | Plague | Flea bite, Inhalation | 1-7 days | Bubonic: Painful lymphadenopathy; Pneumonic: Fever, Cough, Hemoptysis | Gram stain (bipolar), Culture, PCR | Gentamicin, Doxycycline, Ciprofloxacin | Doxycycline or Ciprofloxacin x 7 days | https://www.cdc.gov/plague/index.html |
| **Variola major** | Smallpox | Inhalation, Contact | 7-17 days | Fever, Synchronous centrifugal vesiculopustular rash | PCR (BSL-4 lab) | Tecovirimat, Brincidofovir | Vaccine (within 3-4 days) | https://www.cdc.gov/smallpox/index.html |
| **Francisella tularensis** | Tularemia | Inhalation, Tick bite, Contact | 3-5 days | Ulceroglandular, Typhoidal, Pneumonic | Serology, Culture (BSL-3) | Streptomycin, Gentamicin, Doxycycline | Doxycycline or Ciprofloxacin x 14 days | https://www.cdc.gov/tularemia/index.html |
| **Filoviruses (Ebola, Marburg)** | Viral Hemorrhagic Fever | Direct contact with body fluids | 2-21 days | Fever, Vomiting, Diarrhea, Hemorrhage, Multi-organ failure | RT-PCR | Inmazeb, Ebanga (Ebola); Supportive | Vaccine (Ebola) for high-risk contacts | https://www.cdc.gov/vhf/ebola/index.html |
| **Arenaviruses (Lassa, Machupo)** | Viral Hemorrhagic Fever | Rodent contact, Person-to-person | 5-16 days | Fever, Pharyngitis, Hemorrhage, Sensorineural hearing loss | RT-PCR, Serology | Ribavirin (Lassa) | None | https://www.cdc.gov/vhf/lassa/index.html |
