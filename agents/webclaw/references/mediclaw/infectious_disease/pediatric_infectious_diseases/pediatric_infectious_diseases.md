## Common Pediatric Exanthems
| Disease | Pathogen | Clinical Features | Treatment | URL |
|---------|----------|-------------------|-----------|-----|
| **Measles (Rubeola)** | Measles virus | 3 C's, Koplik spots, Rash | Vitamin A; Supportive | https://www.cdc.gov/measles/index.html |
| **Rubella** | Rubella virus | Fever, Lymphadenopathy, Rash | Supportive | https://www.cdc.gov/rubella/index.html |
| **Erythema Infectiosum (Fifth Disease)** | Parvovirus B19 | "Slapped cheeks" ? Lacy rash | Supportive | https://www.cdc.gov/parvovirusb19/index.html |
| **Roseola Infantum** | HHV-6, HHV-7 | High fever ? Rash as fever defervesces | Supportive | https://www.cdc.gov/hhv-6/index.html |
| **Hand-Foot-Mouth Disease** | Coxsackievirus A16 | Vesicles on hands, Feet, Mouth | Supportive | https://www.cdc.gov/hand-foot-mouth/index.html |
| **Scarlet Fever** | S. pyogenes | Sandpaper rash, Strawberry tongue | Penicillin or Amoxicillin | https://www.cdc.gov/groupastrep/index.html |
| **Varicella (Chickenpox)** | VZV | Pruritic vesicular rash | Supportive; Acyclovir if high-risk | https://www.cdc.gov/chickenpox/index.html |
