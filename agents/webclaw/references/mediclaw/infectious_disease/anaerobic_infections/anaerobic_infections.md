## Clostridial Infections
| Species | Syndrome | Pathogenesis | Treatment | URL |
|---------|----------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **C. difficile** | Antibiotic-associated diarrhea ? Pseudomembranous colitis ? Toxic megacolon | **Toxins A (enterotoxin) and B (cytotoxin)** | **Oral vancomycin** (first-line) or **Fidaxomicin**; **Fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT)** for recurrent | https://www.cdc.gov/cdiff/about/index.html |
| **C. perfringens** | **Gas gangrene (myonecrosis)**, **Food poisoning**, **Necrotizing enteritis** | **Alpha toxin** (lecithinase) ? Tissue destruction | **Emergent surgical debridement** + **Penicillin G + Clindamycin** | https://www.merckmanuals.com/professional/infectious-diseases/anaerobic-bacteria/clostridial-gas-gangrene |
| **C. tetani** | **Tetanus** (trismus/lockjaw, Risus sardonicus, Opisthotonos, Autonomic instability) | **Tetanospasmin** ? Inhibits GABA/glycine release | **Tetanus immune globulin (TIG)** + **Metronidazole** + **Benzodiazepines** + **Supportive care** | https://www.cdc.gov/tetanus/hcp/clinical-overview/index.html |
| **C. botulinum** | **Botulism** (descending flaccid paralysis, Diplopia, Dysphagia, Respiratory failure) | **Botulinum toxin** ? Inhibits ACh release | **Botulism antitoxin** + **Supportive care**; **Avoid aminoglycosides** | https://www.cdc.gov/botulism/hcp/clinical-overview/index.html |

## Non-Sporulating Anaerobes - Above Diaphragm
| Syndrome | Pathogens | Clinical Features | Treatment | URL |
|----------|-----------|------------------|-----------|-----|
| **Dental Abscess** | Peptostreptococcus, Prevotella, Fusobacterium | Painful tooth, Swelling | **Amoxicillin-clavulanate**, **Clindamycin**, **Penicillin + Metronidazole** + **Drainage** | https://www.msdmanuals.com/professional/dental-disorders/dental-emergencies/dental-abscess |
| **Lemierre's Syndrome** | **Fusobacterium necrophorum** | **Pharyngitis ? Septic thrombophlebitis of internal jugular vein ? Septic pulmonary emboli** | **Ampicillin-sulbactam** or **Piperacillin-tazobactam** or **Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole** x 4-6 weeks | https://rarediseases.org/rare-diseases/lemierre-syndrome/ |
| **Aspiration Pneumonia/Lung Abscess** | Oral anaerobes (Peptostreptococcus, Prevotella, Fusobacterium) | Indolent, Foul-smelling sputum, Necrotizing pneumonia | **Ampicillin-sulbactam** or **Clindamycin**; **Drainage** if large abscess | https://www.lung.org/lung-health-diseases/lung-disease-lookup/lung-abscess |

## Non-Sporulating Anaerobes - Below Diaphragm
| Syndrome | Pathogens | Clinical Features | Treatment | URL |
|----------|-----------|------------------|-----------|-----|
| **Intra-abdominal Abscess** | **B. fragilis**, E. coli, Enterococci | Fever, Abdominal pain, Leukocytosis | **Source control** (drainage) + **Piperacillin-tazobactam** or **Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole** or **Ertapenem** | https://www.merckmanuals.com/professional/gastrointestinal-disorders/acute-abdomen-and-surgical-gastroenterology/intra-abdominal-abscesses |
| **Appendicitis** | Same | RLQ pain, Anorexia, Nausea | **Surgery** + **Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole** or **Ertapenem** | https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/health/conditions-and-diseases/appendicitis |
| **Diverticulitis** | Same | LLQ pain, Fever, Leukocytosis | **Amoxicillin-clavulanate** (outpatient) or **Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole** (inpatient) | https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/diverticulitis/symptoms-causes/syc-20371758 |
| **Bacterial Vaginosis** | Gardnerella, Prevotella, Mobiluncus, Atopobium | Thin gray discharge, Fishy odor, pH >4.5, Clue cells | **Metronidazole** (oral or vaginal) or **Clindamycin** | https://www.cdc.gov/bacterial-vaginosis/about/index.html |

## Actinomycosis
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Pathogen | **Actinomyces israelii** (Gram-positive branching filamentous rod) | https://www.cdc.gov/actinomycosis/about/ (NOTE: CDC redirects to /actinomycosis/) |
| Clinical Forms | **Cervicofacial** (most common - "lumpy jaw"), Thoracic, Abdominal, Pelvic (associated with IUD) | https://www.msdmanuals.com/professional/infectious-diseases/anaerobic-bacteria/actinomycosis |
| Key Features | **Indolent**, **Crosses tissue planes**, **Sulfur granules** (yellow aggregates), **Sinus tract formation** | https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/17898-actinomycosis |
| Diagnosis | **Culture** (anaerobic, Slow growth), **Histopathology** (sulfur granules, Filamentous bacteria) | https://www.mountsinai.org/health-library/diseases-conditions/actinomycosis |
| Treatment | **Penicillin G** or **Amoxicillin** x **6-12 months**; **Surgical debridement** for extensive disease | https://www.pennmedicine.org/for-patients-and-visitors/patient-information/conditions-treated-a-to-z/actinomycosis |
