# Clinical Pharmacology - Oncology Pharmacology

## Chemotherapy Principles
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Cell Cycle Specificity** | Some agents target specific phases (S-phase: antimetabolites; M-phase: vinca alkaloids, taxanes) | https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/types/chemotherapy |
| **Log-Kill Hypothesis** | Each cycle kills constant fraction (not number) of cells | https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/types/chemotherapy |
| **Dose Intensity** | Maintaining dose and schedule is critical for cure | https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/types/chemotherapy |

## Chemotherapy Classes
| Class | Examples | Mechanism | Key Toxicities | URL |
|-------|----------|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Alkylating Agents** | Cyclophosphamide, Ifosfamide, Cisplatin, Carboplatin | DNA crosslinking | **Myelosuppression**, **Hemorrhagic cystitis** (cyclo/ifos), **Nephrotoxicity/Ototoxicity** (cisplatin) | https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/drugs |
| **Antimetabolites** | Methotrexate, 5-FU, Gemcitabine, Cytarabine | Inhibit nucleotide synthesis | **Myelosuppression**, **Mucositis**, **Hand-foot syndrome** (5-FU/capecitabine) | https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/drugs |
| **Antitumor Antibiotics** | Doxorubicin, Daunorubicin, Bleomycin | DNA intercalation, Topo II inhibition | **Cardiomyopathy** (anthracyclines), **Pulmonary fibrosis** (bleomycin) | https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/drugs |
| **Topoisomerase Inhibitors** | Etoposide, Irinotecan, Topotecan | Inhibit Topo I or II | Myelosuppression, **Diarrhea** (irinotecan) | https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/drugs |
| **Mitotic Inhibitors** | Paclitaxel, Docetaxel, Vincristine, Vinblastine | Inhibit microtubule dynamics | **Neuropathy** (taxanes, vinca), **Myelosuppression** | https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/drugs |

| **CAR-T Therapy** | Tisagenlecleucel, Axicabtagene ciloleucel | CD19 | **CRS**, **Neurotoxicity** (ICANS) | https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/research/car-t-cells |

## Chemotherapy-Induced Nausea and Vomiting (CINV) Prophylaxis
| Emetogenic Risk | Examples | Prophylaxis | URL |
|-----------------|----------|-------------|-----|
| **High (>90%)** | Cisplatin, AC (doxorubicin + cyclophosphamide) | **NK1 antagonist + 5-HT3 antagonist + Dexamethasone + Olanzapine** | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/antiemesis |
| **Moderate (30-90%)** | Carboplatin, Oxaliplatin, Irinotecan | 5-HT3 antagonist + Dexamethasone ± NK1 antagonist | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/antiemesis |
| **Low (10-30%)** | Paclitaxel, Docetaxel, 5-FU | Dexamethasone or 5-HT3 antagonist | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/antiemesis |
| **Minimal (<10%)** | Vincristine, Bleomycin | No routine prophylaxis | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/antiemesis |

## Tumor Lysis Syndrome Prophylaxis
| Risk | Management | URL |
|------|------------|-----|
| **Low** | Hydration, Allopurinol | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines |
| **Intermediate** | Hydration, Allopurinol, Monitor labs | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines |
| **High** | Hydration, **Rasburicase** (or allopurinol if contraindicated), Monitor labs q4-6h | https://www.nccn.org/guidelines |
