# Clinical Pharmacology - Overview

## What is Clinical Pharmacology?
Clinical pharmacology is the scientific discipline concerned with the rational, safe, and effective use of medications in humans, bridging the gap between basic pharmacology and clinical therapeutics.

|---------|------------|----------------|-----|

## Key Pharmacokinetic Parameters
| Parameter | Symbol | Definition | Clinical Significance | URL |
|-----------|--------|------------|----------------------|-----|
| **Bioavailability** | F | Fraction of dose reaching systemic circulation | Determines oral vs IV dosing | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-approvals-and-databases/pharmacokinetics-drug-development |
| **Volume of Distribution** | Vd | Theoretical volume drug occupies | Determines loading dose | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-approvals-and-databases/pharmacokinetics-drug-development |
| **Clearance** | CL | Volume cleared of drug per unit time | Determines maintenance dose | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-approvals-and-databases/pharmacokinetics-drug-development |
| **Half-Life** | t˝ | Time for drug concentration to decrease by 50% | Determines dosing interval, Time to steady state | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-approvals-and-databases/pharmacokinetics-drug-development |
| **Steady State** | Css | Rate of drug administration = Rate of elimination | **Achieved after 4-5 half-lives** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-approvals-and-databases/pharmacokinetics-drug-development |
