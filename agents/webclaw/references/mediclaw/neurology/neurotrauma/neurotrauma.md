# Neurology - Neurotrauma

## Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) - Classification
| Severity | GCS | Loss of Consciousness | Post-Traumatic Amnesia | URL |
|----------|-----|----------------------|------------------------|-----|
| **Mild (Concussion)** | 13-15 | <30 min | <24 hours | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/traumatic-brain-injury |
| **Moderate** | 9-12 | 30 min - 24 hours | 1-7 days | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/traumatic-brain-injury |
| **Severe** | 3-8 | >24 hours | >7 days | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/traumatic-brain-injury |

## TBI - Management Principles
| Component | Recommendation | URL |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Initial Assessment** | **ABCDE**, **GCS**, **Pupils**, **Motor exam** | https://www.braintrauma.org/guidelines |
| **Neuroimaging** | **Non-contrast CT head** (indicated if GCS <15, Focal deficit, Vomiting, Age =65, Dangerous mechanism, Anticoagulation) | https://www.braintrauma.org/guidelines |
| **ICP Monitoring** | Indicated if **GCS =8** with abnormal CT or high-risk features | https://www.braintrauma.org/guidelines |
| **ICP Management** | **Head elevation 30ｰ**, **Sedation**, **Hyperosmolar therapy** (mannitol, Hypertonic saline), **CSF drainage** (EVD), **Hyperventilation** (temporary, PaCO2 30-35), **Barbiturate coma**, **Decompressive craniectomy** | https://www.braintrauma.org/guidelines |
| **CPP Target** | **60-70 mmHg** | https://www.braintrauma.org/guidelines |
| **Seizure Prophylaxis** | **Levetiracetam** or **Phenytoin** x 7 days (severe TBI); **Not routinely** for mild TBI | https://www.braintrauma.org/guidelines |

## Concussion (Mild TBI)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | Traumatically induced transient disturbance of brain function; **Normal structural imaging** | https://www.cdc.gov/traumaticbraininjury/index.html |
| **Symptoms** | **Headache**, **Dizziness**, **Nausea/Vomiting**, **Photophobia/Phonophobia**, **Cognitive fog**, **Emotional lability**, **Sleep disturbance** | https://www.cdc.gov/traumaticbraininjury/index.html |
| **Evaluation** | **SCAT5** (Sport Concussion Assessment Tool), **Neurologic exam**, **Vestibular/ocular motor screening** | https://www.cdc.gov/traumaticbraininjury/index.html |
| **Management** | **Relative rest** (24-48 hours) ? **Gradual return to activity**, **Avoid contact sports until cleared**, **Treat symptoms** (headache, Sleep) | https://www.cdc.gov/traumaticbraininjury/index.html |
| **Return to Play** | **Stepwise progression**: No activity ? Light aerobic ? Sport-specific ? Non-contact drills ? Full contact ? Game play; **Must be asymptomatic at each step** | https://www.cdc.gov/traumaticbraininjury/index.html |

## Spinal Cord Injury (SCI)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Classification** | **ASIA Impairment Scale (AIS)** : A (Complete), B (Sensory incomplete), C (Motor incomplete - <3/5), D (Motor incomplete - =3/5), E (Normal) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-cord-injury |
| **Neurogenic Shock** | **Hypotension + Bradycardia** (loss of sympathetic tone); **Treat with fluids + vasopressors** (norepinephrine, Phenylephrine) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-cord-injury |
| **Spinal Shock** | **Temporary loss of reflexes** below level of injury; **Return of bulbocavernosus reflex** marks end | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-cord-injury |
| **Imaging** | **CT spine** (initial), **MRI spine** (cord compression, Ligament injury, Hemorrhage) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-cord-injury |
| **Management** | **Immobilization**, **MAP goal 85-90 mmHg** (first 7 days), **Early surgical decompression** (<24 hours), **Methylprednisolone NOT recommended** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-cord-injury |

## Spinal Cord Syndromes
| Syndrome | Mechanism | Clinical Features | URL |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| **Central Cord Syndrome** | **Hyperextension** (elderly with cervical stenosis) | **Upper extremity > lower extremity weakness**, **Sensory loss**, **Bladder dysfunction** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-cord-injury |
| **Anterior Cord Syndrome** | **Flexion/compression** (anterior spinal artery) | **Bilateral motor paralysis**, **Pain/temp loss** (spinothalamic), **Preserved proprioception/vibration** (dorsal columns) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-cord-injury |
| **Brown-S駲uard Syndrome** | **Hemisection** (penetrating trauma) | **Ipsilateral weakness** (corticospinal), **Ipsilateral proprioception/vibration loss** (dorsal columns), **Contralateral pain/temp loss** (spinothalamic) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-cord-injury |
| **Posterior Cord Syndrome** | Rare (tabes dorsalis, B12 deficiency) | **Loss of proprioception/vibration**, **Sensory ataxia**, **Romberg positive** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-cord-injury |
| **Cauda Equina Syndrome** | Compression below L1-L2 | **Saddle anesthesia**, **Urinary retention**, **Fecal incontinence**, **Bilateral leg weakness**, **Hyporeflexia** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-cord-injury |
