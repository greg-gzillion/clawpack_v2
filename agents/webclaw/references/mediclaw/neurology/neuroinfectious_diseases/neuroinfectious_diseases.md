# Neurology - Neuroinfectious Diseases

## Acute Bacterial Meningitis
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Common Pathogens** | **S. pneumoniae** (most common adults), **N. meningitidis** (young adults, Outbreaks), **Listeria monocytogenes** (age >50, Immunocompromised), **GBS**, **E. coli** (neonates) | https://www.cdc.gov/meningitis/index.html |
| **Clinical Features** | **Fever**, **Headache**, **Nuchal rigidity**, **Photophobia**, **Altered mental status**, **Kernig/Brudzinski signs** | https://www.cdc.gov/meningitis/index.html |
| **Diagnosis** | **LP**: Opening pressure (elevated), WBC >1000 (PMN predominance), Glucose <40, Protein >100, **Gram stain**, **Culture**, **PCR** | https://www.cdc.gov/meningitis/index.html |
| **Empiric Treatment** | **Ceftriaxone 2g IV q12h + Vancomycin + Dexamethasone 10 mg IV q6h** (start before or with antibiotics); **Add Ampicillin** if Listeria risk | https://www.cdc.gov/meningitis/index.html |
| **Dexamethasone** | **Adults with suspected pneumococcal meningitis**: 10 mg IV q6h x 4 days; **Reduces mortality and hearing loss** | https://www.cdc.gov/meningitis/index.html |

## Viral Meningitis / Aseptic Meningitis
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Common Pathogens** | **Enteroviruses** (most common), **HSV-2** (Mollaret meningitis), **VZV**, **Arboviruses** (West Nile), **HIV** | https://www.cdc.gov/meningitis/viral.html |
| **Clinical Features** | Similar to bacterial meningitis but **less severe**; Normal mental status, No focal deficits | https://www.cdc.gov/meningitis/viral.html |
| **CSF Findings** | WBC 10-500 (lymphocyte predominant), Normal glucose, Normal or mildly elevated protein | https://www.cdc.gov/meningitis/viral.html |
| **Diagnosis** | **CSF PCR** (multiplex panel) | https://www.cdc.gov/meningitis/viral.html |
| **Treatment** | **Supportive**; **Acyclovir** for HSV/VZV | https://www.cdc.gov/meningitis/viral.html |

## Herpes Simplex Encephalitis (HSE)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Pathogen** | **HSV-1** (95% in adults), **HSV-2** (neonates) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/encephalitis |
| **Clinical Features** | **Fever**, **Headache**, **Altered mental status**, **Seizures**, **Personality changes**, **Aphasia**, **Focal neurologic deficits** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/encephalitis |
| **Diagnosis** | **MRI brain**: T2/FLAIR hyperintensity in **temporal lobe(s)**, **CSF HSV PCR** (gold standard), **EEG**: Temporal lobe epileptiform discharges | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/encephalitis |
| **Treatment** | **Acyclovir 10 mg/kg IV q8h x 14-21 days** (start empirically if suspected) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/encephalitis |
| **Prognosis** | **70% mortality untreated**; **20-30% mortality treated**; High rate of neurologic sequelae | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/encephalitis |

## West Nile Virus (WNV) Neuroinvasive Disease
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Epidemiology** | **Mosquito-borne** (Culex); Peak summer-fall; <1% develop neuroinvasive disease | https://www.cdc.gov/westnile/index.html |
| **Clinical Syndromes** | **Meningitis**, **Encephalitis**, **Acute flaccid paralysis** (anterior horn cell - polio-like) | https://www.cdc.gov/westnile/index.html |
| **Risk Factors** | **Age >50**, **Immunocompromised**, **Hypertension**, **Diabetes** | https://www.cdc.gov/westnile/index.html |
| **Diagnosis** | **CSF**: Lymphocytic pleocytosis, Normal glucose, Elevated protein; **WNV IgM** in CSF (diagnostic) or serum | https://www.cdc.gov/westnile/index.html |
| **Treatment** | **Supportive**; No proven antiviral therapy | https://www.cdc.gov/westnile/index.html |

## Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy (PML)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Pathogen** | **JC virus** (reactivation in immunosuppressed) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/progressive-multifocal-leukoencephalopathy |
| **Risk Factors** | **HIV** (CD4 <200), **Natalizumab** (MS), **Rituximab**, **Fingolimod**, **Dimethyl fumarate**, **Organ transplant** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/progressive-multifocal-leukoencephalopathy |
| **Clinical Features** | **Subacute progressive focal neurologic deficits** (hemiparesis, Visual field cut, Aphasia, Ataxia), **Cognitive decline**, **No fever or headache** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/progressive-multifocal-leukoencephalopathy |
| **MRI** | **Multifocal**, **Asymmetric**, **Non-enhancing white matter lesions** (subcortical U-fibers) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/progressive-multifocal-leukoencephalopathy |
| **Diagnosis** | **CSF JC virus PCR** (sensitivity 70-90%), **Brain biopsy** (gold standard) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/progressive-multifocal-leukoencephalopathy |
| **Treatment** | **Restore immune function** (ART for HIV, Stop offending drug), **Consider pembrolizumab** (anti-PD-1) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/progressive-multifocal-leukoencephalopathy |

## Neurosyphilis
| Syndrome | Timing | Clinical Features | URL |
|----------|--------|-------------------|-----|
| **Asymptomatic Neurosyphilis** | Any | **CSF abnormalities only** (pleocytosis, Elevated protein, Positive VDRL) | https://www.cdc.gov/std/syphilis/index.htm |
| **Meningitis** | <1-2 years | Headache, Nuchal rigidity, Cranial neuropathies | https://www.cdc.gov/std/syphilis/index.htm |
| **Meningovascular** | 5-10 years | **Stroke** (medium vessel vasculitis), Prodromal headache/personality changes | https://www.cdc.gov/std/syphilis/index.htm |
| **General Paresis** | 15-20 years | **Dementia**, **Personality changes**, **Delusions of grandeur**, **Argyll Robertson pupils** | https://www.cdc.gov/std/syphilis/index.htm |
| **Tabes Dorsalis** | 15-20 years | **Sensory ataxia**, **Lancinating pains**, **Argyll Robertson pupils**, **Charcot joints**, **Urinary incontinence** | https://www.cdc.gov/std/syphilis/index.htm |

## Neurosyphilis - Diagnosis and Treatment
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Indications for LP** | **Neurologic signs/symptoms**, **Ocular syphilis**, **Otosyphilis**, **Tertiary syphilis**, **Treatment failure**, **HIV + CD4 <350 or RPR =1:32** | https://www.cdc.gov/std/syphilis/index.htm |
| **CSF Findings** | **Pleocytosis** (>5 WBC), **Elevated protein** (>45), **Positive CSF VDRL** (specific but insensitive) | https://www.cdc.gov/std/syphilis/index.htm |
| **Treatment** | **Aqueous crystalline penicillin G 18-24 million units/day IV** (continuous or q4h) x 10-14 days | https://www.cdc.gov/std/syphilis/index.htm |
| **Alternative** | **Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily x 10-14 days** (limited data) | https://www.cdc.gov/std/syphilis/index.htm |
| **Follow-Up** | **Repeat LP every 6 months** until CSF WBC normal and CSF VDRL nonreactive | https://www.cdc.gov/std/syphilis/index.htm |
