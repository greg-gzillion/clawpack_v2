# Ischemic Stroke

## Definition
Acute focal neurological deficit from cerebral infarction due to thrombotic or embolic occlusion

## Types
- Thrombotic (large artery atherosclerosis)
- Embolic (cardioembolism: AF, valvular disease)
- Lacunar (small vessel disease)
- Cryptogenic (no identified cause)

## Risk Factors
- Hypertension
- Atrial fibrillation
- Diabetes
- Hyperlipidemia
- Smoking
- Prior TIA/stroke

## Clinical Presentation (BE FAST)
- Balance loss
- Eye vision changes
- Face drooping
- Arm weakness
- Speech difficulty
- Time to call emergency

## Diagnosis
- Non-contrast CT head (excludes hemorrhage)
- CTA head/neck (large vessel occlusion)
- MRI brain (ischemic core)
- ECG, telemetry (AF detection)
- Echocardiogram (cardioembolic source)

## Treatment (Acute)

### Ischemic Stroke (within 4.5 hours)
- IV alteplase (tPA) 0.9 mg/kg
- Mechanical thrombectomy (large vessel occlusion, up to 24 hours)
- BP management (allow up to 185/110 before tPA)
- Aspirin 325mg within 48 hours

### Secondary Prevention
- Antiplatelet: aspirin, clopidogrel, or aspirin/dipyridamole
- Statin (high-intensity)
- BP control (<130/80)
- Anticoagulation if AF (DOAC: apixaban, rivaroxaban)
- Carotid endarterectomy/stenting (if >70% stenosis)

## Rehabilitation
- Early mobilization
- Speech therapy (aphasia, dysarthria)
- Physical therapy (motor deficits)
- Occupational therapy (ADLs)

## Prognosis
- 30-day mortality: 10-15%
- Disability: 30-50% have permanent deficits
- Recurrence: 5-10% per year without secondary prevention
