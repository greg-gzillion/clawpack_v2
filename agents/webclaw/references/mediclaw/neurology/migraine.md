# Migraine

## Definition
Recurrent headache disorder with moderate to severe throbbing pain, often unilateral with associated symptoms

## Types
- Migraine without aura (80%)
- Migraine with aura (20%)
- Chronic migraine (≥15 days/month)

## Phases
1. Prodrome (hours to days): fatigue, mood changes, food cravings
2. Aura (5-60 min): visual (scotoma, fortification spectra), sensory, speech
3. Headache (4-72 hours): unilateral, throbbing, moderate-severe
4. Postdrome: fatigue, cognitive impairment

## Associated Symptoms
- Nausea/vomiting
- Photophobia
- Phonophobia
- Osmophobia
- Cutaneous allodynia

## Triggers
- Stress
- Hormonal changes (menstruation)
- Sleep disruption
- Certain foods (aged cheese, wine, chocolate)
- Weather changes
- Caffeine withdrawal

## Diagnosis (clinical)
- ≥5 attacks meeting criteria
- ICHD-3 diagnostic criteria

## Acute Treatment

### Mild-moderate
- NSAIDs (ibuprofen 400-600mg, naproxen 500mg)
- Acetaminophen 1000mg
- Combination: aspirin/acetaminophen/caffeine (Excedrin)

### Moderate-severe
- Triptans (sumatriptan, rizatriptan, eletriptan)
- Gepants (ubrogepant, rimegepant)
- Ditans (lasmiditan)
- Dihydroergotamine (nasal/injectable)

### Rescue (ED setting)
- IV metoclopramide + diphenhydramine
- IV prochlorperazine
- IV valproate
- IV magnesium

## Prevention (if ≥4 attacks/month)

### First-line
- Beta-blockers (propranolol, metoprolol)
- Antidepressants (amitriptyline, venlafaxine)
- Anticonvulsants (topiramate, valproate)
- CGRP mAbs (erenumab, galcanezumab, fremanezumab)

### Monitoring
- Headache diary
- Track frequency, severity, disability (MIDAS, HIT-6)
