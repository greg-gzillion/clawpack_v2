# Neurology - Movement Disorders

## Parkinson Disease (PD)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Pathophysiology** | **Loss of dopaminergic neurons** in substantia nigra pars compacta; **Lewy bodies** (alpha-synuclein aggregates) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/parkinsons-disease |
| **Cardinal Motor Features** | **TRAP**: **T**remor (resting, pill-rolling), **R**igidity (cogwheel), **A**kinesia/Bradykinesia, **P**ostural instability (late) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/parkinsons-disease |
| **Non-Motor Features** | **Hyposmia**, **REM sleep behavior disorder**, **Constipation**, **Depression**, **Anxiety**, **Cognitive impairment**, **Autonomic dysfunction** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/parkinsons-disease |
| **Diagnosis** | **Clinical** (UK Parkinson's Disease Society Brain Bank Criteria); **DaTscan** (abnormal in PD, Normal in essential tremor/drug-induced) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/parkinsons-disease |

## Parkinson Disease - Treatment
| Drug Class | Examples | Mechanism | Key Adverse Effects | URL |
|------------|----------|-----------|---------------------|-----|
| **Levodopa/Carbidopa** | Sinemet, Rytary, Duopa | **Dopamine precursor** (most effective) | **Motor fluctuations** (wearing-off, Dyskinesia), Nausea, Orthostasis, Hallucinations | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/parkinsons-disease |
| **Dopamine Agonists** | Pramipexole, Ropinirole, Rotigotine | Stimulate dopamine receptors | **Impulse control disorders** (gambling, Hypersexuality), Nausea, Orthostasis, Edema | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/parkinsons-disease |
| **MAO-B Inhibitors** | Rasagiline, Selegiline, Safinamide | Inhibit dopamine breakdown | Insomnia, Orthostasis, **Serotonin syndrome** (with SSRIs) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/parkinsons-disease |
| **COMT Inhibitors** | Entacapone, Opicapone, Tolcapone | Inhibit levodopa breakdown | **Diarrhea**, **Orange urine**, **Hepatotoxicity** (tolcapone) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/parkinsons-disease |
| **Anticholinergics** | Trihexyphenidyl, Benztropine | Reduce acetylcholine activity | **Cognitive impairment**, **Constipation**, **Urinary retention**, **Blurred vision** (avoid in elderly) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/parkinsons-disease |
| **Amantadine** | Symmetrel, Gocovri | NMDA antagonist, Dopamine release | **Livedo reticularis**, **Edema**, **Confusion**, **Hallucinations** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/parkinsons-disease |

## Parkinson Disease - Advanced Therapies
| Therapy | Indications | Description | URL |
|---------|-------------|-------------|-----|
| **Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS)** | **Motor fluctuations**, **Dyskinesia**, **Tremor refractory to medication** | Electrodes in **STN** or **GPi** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/deep-brain-stimulation |
| **Duopa (Levodopa-Carbidopa Intestinal Gel)** | **Severe motor fluctuations** | Continuous jejunal infusion via PEG-J tube | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |
| **Focused Ultrasound** | **Tremor-dominant PD** | Unilateral thalamotomy | https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices |

## Parkinsonism - Differential Diagnosis
| Disorder | Key Features | DaTscan | URL |
|----------|--------------|---------|-----|
| **Drug-Induced Parkinsonism** | **Symmetric**, Rapid onset, **Dopamine-blocking drugs** (antipsychotics, Antiemetics) | Normal | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/parkinsons-disease |
| **Vascular Parkinsonism** | **Lower body predominant**, Gait disturbance, Postural instability, MRI: White matter disease | Normal | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/parkinsons-disease |
| **Dementia with Lewy Bodies (DLB)** | **Dementia BEFORE or within 1 year of parkinsonism**, Fluctuating cognition, Visual hallucinations, REM sleep behavior disorder | Abnormal | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/lewy-body-dementia |
| **Progressive Supranuclear Palsy (PSP)** | **Early falls**, **Vertical gaze palsy**, Axial rigidity, Poor levodopa response | Abnormal | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/progressive-supranuclear-palsy |
| **Multiple System Atrophy (MSA)** | **Autonomic failure** (orthostasis, Incontinence), Cerebellar signs (MSA-C) or Parkinsonism (MSA-P), Poor levodopa response | Abnormal | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/multiple-system-atrophy |
| **Corticobasal Degeneration (CBD)** | **Asymmetric apraxia**, **Alien limb phenomenon**, Cortical sensory loss, Rigidity | Abnormal | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/corticobasal-degeneration |

## Essential Tremor (ET)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Epidemiology** | **Most common movement disorder**; 5% of adults =65 | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/essential-tremor |
| **Clinical Features** | **Bilateral action/postural tremor** (hands, Head, Voice); **Improves with alcohol**; No other neurologic signs | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/essential-tremor |
| **Diagnosis** | Clinical; Distinguish from PD (resting tremor, Bradykinesia, Rigidity) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/essential-tremor |
| **Treatment** | **Propranolol**, **Primidone** (first-line); Gabapentin, Topiramate, Benzodiazepines; **DBS** or **Focused ultrasound** for refractory | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/essential-tremor |

## Huntington Disease (HD)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Genetics** | **Autosomal dominant**, **HTT gene** (CAG trinucleotide repeat), **Anticipation** (paternal transmission) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/huntingtons-disease |
| **Clinical Features** | **Chorea**, **Cognitive decline** (executive dysfunction), **Psychiatric symptoms** (depression, Irritability, Apathy), **Weight loss** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/huntingtons-disease |
| **Diagnosis** | **Genetic testing** (CAG repeat =40 = full penetrance; 36-39 = reduced penetrance) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/huntingtons-disease |
| **Treatment** | **Tetrabenazine**, **Deutetrabenazine**, **Valbenazine** (VMAT2 inhibitors for chorea); **SSRIs** (depression/anxiety); **Supportive care** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/huntingtons-disease |

## Tourette Syndrome
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Diagnostic Criteria** | **Multiple motor tics + =1 vocal tic** for >1 year; Onset <18 years | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/tourette-syndrome |
| **Clinical Features** | **Simple tics** (blinking, Throat clearing), **Complex tics** (touching, Coprolalia, Echolalia), **Premonitory urge** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/tourette-syndrome |
| **Comorbidities** | **ADHD** (50-60%), **OCD** (30-50%), Anxiety, Depression | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/tourette-syndrome |
