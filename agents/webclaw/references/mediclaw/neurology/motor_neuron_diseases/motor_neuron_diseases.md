# Neurology - Motor Neuron Diseases

## Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Epidemiology** | Incidence 1-2 per 100,000; Peak onset 55-75 years; Male > Female | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/amyotrophic-lateral-sclerosis-als |
| **Pathophysiology** | **Degeneration of upper motor neurons** (motor cortex) and **lower motor neurons** (anterior horn cells) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/amyotrophic-lateral-sclerosis-als |
| **Genetics** | **C9orf72** (most common - 40% familial, 7% sporadic), **SOD1**, **TARDBP**, **FUS** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/amyotrophic-lateral-sclerosis-als |
| **Clinical Features** | **UMN signs**: Spasticity, Hyperreflexia, Babinski, Pseudobulbar affect; **LMN signs**: Weakness, Atrophy, Fasciculations, Cramps; **Bulbar**: Dysarthria, Dysphagia; **Respiratory**: Dyspnea, Orthopnea | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/amyotrophic-lateral-sclerosis-als |
| **Diagnosis** | **El Escorial Criteria**: UMN + LMN signs in =1 region, Progressive spread, Exclusion of mimics | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/amyotrophic-lateral-sclerosis-als |
| **Differential** | **Multifocal motor neuropathy**, **Kennedy disease**, **Cervical spondylotic myelopathy**, **Inclusion body myositis** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/amyotrophic-lateral-sclerosis-als |

## ALS - Management
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Disease-Modifying** | **Riluzole** (glutamate antagonist, Prolongs survival by 2-3 months), **Edaravone** (antioxidant, Slows progression in select patients), **Tofersen** (SOD1-ALS), **AMX0035** (sodium phenylbutyrate/taurursodiol) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |
| **Symptom Management** | **Spasticity**: Baclofen, Tizanidine; **Sialorrhea**: Atropine, Glycopyrrolate, Botulinum toxin; **Pseudobulbar affect**: Dextromethorphan/quinidine; **Cramps**: Mexiletine, Quinine | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/amyotrophic-lateral-sclerosis-als |
| **Respiratory Support** | **Non-invasive ventilation (NIV)** (improves survival and quality of life), **Mechanical ventilation via tracheostomy** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/amyotrophic-lateral-sclerosis-als |
| **Nutrition** | **Percutaneous endoscopic gastrostomy (PEG)** when FVC <50% or weight loss >10% | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/amyotrophic-lateral-sclerosis-als |
| **Multidisciplinary Care** | **ALS clinics** (neurologist, Pulmonologist, PT/OT/SLP, Nutritionist, Social worker, Palliative care) | https://www.als.org |

## Primary Lateral Sclerosis (PLS)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Isolated UMN degeneration** (no LMN signs) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/primary-lateral-sclerosis |
| **Clinical Features** | **Progressive spasticity**, **Hyperreflexia**, **Babinski**, **Pseudobulbar affect**, **No atrophy or fasciculations** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/primary-lateral-sclerosis |
| **Diagnosis** | **Exclusion of other causes** (MRI spine/brain, EMG/NCS to rule out LMN involvement) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/primary-lateral-sclerosis |
| **Prognosis** | **Slower progression than ALS**; Median survival 20+ years | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/primary-lateral-sclerosis |

## Progressive Muscular Atrophy (PMA)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Isolated LMN degeneration** (no UMN signs) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/progressive-muscular-atrophy |
| **Clinical Features** | **Progressive weakness**, **Atrophy**, **Fasciculations**, **Hyporeflexia**, **No spasticity or Babinski** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/progressive-muscular-atrophy |
| **Diagnosis** | **EMG/NCS** (LMN involvement), **Exclusion of mimics** (multifocal motor neuropathy, Kennedy disease) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/progressive-muscular-atrophy |
| **Prognosis** | **Variable**; Some progress to ALS (UMN signs emerge) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/progressive-muscular-atrophy |

## Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA)
| Type | Age of Onset | Maximum Milestone | Life Expectancy | URL |
|------|--------------|-------------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Type 0** | Prenatal | None | <6 months | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-muscular-atrophy |
| **Type 1 (Werdnig-Hoffmann)** | <6 months | Never sit | <2 years | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-muscular-atrophy |
| **Type 2** | 6-18 months | Sit, Never stand | 70% alive at 25 years | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-muscular-atrophy |
| **Type 3 (Kugelberg-Welander)** | >18 months | Stand and walk | Normal | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-muscular-atrophy |
| **Type 4** | Adulthood | Walk | Normal | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/spinal-muscular-atrophy |
