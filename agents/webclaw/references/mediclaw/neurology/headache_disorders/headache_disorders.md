# Neurology - Headache Disorders

## Primary Headache Disorders
| Disorder | Key Features | Duration | Treatment | URL |
|----------|--------------|----------|-----------|-----|
| **Migraine (Without Aura)** | **Unilateral**, **Throbbing**, **Moderate-severe**, **Nausea/Vomiting**, **Photophobia/Phonophobia** | 4-72 hours | **Abortive**: NSAIDs, Triptans, Gepants, Ditans; **Preventive**: Beta-blockers, Anticonvulsants, Antidepressants, CGRP antagonists | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/migraine |
| **Migraine (With Aura)** | **Reversible focal neurologic symptoms** (visual, Sensory, Speech, Motor) preceding or accompanying headache | 5-60 minutes (aura) | Same as above; **Avoid triptans in hemiplegic migraine or brainstem aura** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/migraine |
| **Tension-Type Headache** | **Bilateral**, **Pressing/Tightening**, **Mild-moderate**, **No nausea**, **No aggravation by activity** | 30 min - 7 days | **NSAIDs**, Acetaminophen, Caffeine combinations; **Avoid medication overuse** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/tension-headache |
| **Cluster Headache** | **Unilateral orbital/supraorbital/temporal**, **Severe**, **Autonomic features** (lacrimation, Rhinorrhea, Ptosis, Miosis), **Restlessness** | 15-180 min | **Acute**: High-flow O2, Sumatriptan SC/IN; **Preventive**: Verapamil, Lithium, Topiramate, Galcanezumab | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/cluster-headache |
| **Other Trigeminal Autonomic Cephalalgias (TACs)** | Paroxysmal hemicrania, SUNCT/SUNA, Hemicrania continua | Variable | **Indomethacin** (paroxysmal hemicrania, Hemicrania continua) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/trigeminal-autonomic-cephalalgias |

## Migraine - Acute (Abortive) Treatment
| Drug Class | Examples | Indications | URL |
|------------|----------|-------------|-----|
| **NSAIDs** | Ibuprofen, Naproxen, Diclofenac, Aspirin | Mild-moderate migraine | https://americanheadachesociety.org |
| **Triptans** | Sumatriptan, Rizatriptan, Eletriptan, Naratriptan | Moderate-severe migraine; **Contraindicated in cardiovascular disease**, **Hemiplegic migraine**, **Brainstem aura** | https://americanheadachesociety.org |
| **Gepants** | Ubrogepant, Rimegepant | Acute migraine; **Safe in cardiovascular disease** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |
| **Ditans** | Lasmiditan | Acute migraine; **No vasoconstriction**; **Driving restriction** (8 hours) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |
| **Antiemetics** | Metoclopramide, Prochlorperazine, Ondansetron | Nausea/vomiting; Migraine abortive | https://americanheadachesociety.org |

| **Beta-Blockers** | Propranolol, Metoprolol, Timolol | =4 headache days/month; **Avoid in asthma, Bradycardia** | https://americanheadachesociety.org |
| **Anticonvulsants** | Topiramate, Valproate | =4 headache days/month | https://americanheadachesociety.org |
| **Antidepressants** | Amitriptyline, Venlafaxine | =4 headache days/month | https://americanheadachesociety.org |
| **CGRP Antagonists** | Erenumab, Fremanezumab, Galcanezumab, Eptinezumab (mAbs); Atogepant, Rimegepant (oral) | =4 headache days/month; **Monthly or quarterly injections** | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |
| **OnabotulinumtoxinA** | Botox | **Chronic migraine** (=15 headache days/month, =8 migraine days) | https://www.fda.gov/drugs |

## Secondary Headache - Red Flags (SNNOOP10)
| Red Flag | Concern | URL |
|----------|---------|-----|
| **S**ystemic symptoms | Fever, Weight loss, Immunosuppression, Malignancy | https://americanheadachesociety.org |
| **N**eurologic deficits | Focal deficits, Altered mental status, Papilledema | https://americanheadachesociety.org |
| **O**nset sudden | **Thunderclap headache** (SAH, RCVS, Dissection) | https://americanheadachesociety.org |
| **O**lder age | >50 years (Giant cell arteritis, Mass) | https://americanheadachesociety.org |
| **P**ositional | Worse with standing (low pressure), Worse supine (high pressure) | https://americanheadachesociety.org |
| **P**attern change | Change in frequency, Severity, or character | https://americanheadachesociety.org |
| **P**regnancy | Preeclampsia, CVT, Pituitary apoplexy | https://americanheadachesociety.org |
| **P**rogressive | Worsening over weeks-months | https://americanheadachesociety.org |

## Thunderclap Headache - Differential Diagnosis
| Cause | Key Features | Diagnosis | URL |
|-------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Subarachnoid Hemorrhage** | **Worst headache of life**, Nuchal rigidity, Focal deficits | **Non-contrast CT**, **LP** (if CT negative) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/cerebral-aneurysms |
| **Reversible Cerebral Vasoconstriction Syndrome (RCVS)** | **Recurrent thunderclap headaches**, Normal CT/LP | **CTA/MRA/DSA** (multifocal segmental vasoconstriction) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/rcvs |
| **Cervical Artery Dissection** | Neck pain, **Horner syndrome**, Focal deficits | **CTA/MRA neck** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/cervical-artery-dissection |
| **Cerebral Venous Thrombosis** | Headache, Papilledema, Seizures, Focal deficits | **MRV** or **CTV** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/cerebral-venous-thrombosis |
| **Pituitary Apoplexy** | Sudden headache, Visual loss, Oculomotor palsy | **MRI pituitary** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/pituitary-tumors |

## Idiopathic Intracranial Hypertension (IIH)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Epidemiology** | **Obese women of childbearing age** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/pseudotumor-cerebri |
| **Clinical Features** | **Headache** (worse supine), **Pulsatile tinnitus**, **Transient visual obscurations**, **Diplopia** (CN VI palsy), **Papilledema** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/pseudotumor-cerebri |
| **Diagnosis** | **MRI/MRV** (rule out CVT, Mass), **LP** (elevated opening pressure >25 cm H2O), Normal CSF composition | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/pseudotumor-cerebri |
| **Treatment** | **Weight loss** (primary), **Acetazolamide**, **Topiramate**, **Optic nerve sheath fenestration**, **CSF shunting** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/pseudotumor-cerebri |

## Giant Cell Arteritis (Temporal Arteritis)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Epidemiology** | **Age >50**, Female > Male | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/giant-cell-arteritis |
| **Clinical Features** | **New headache** (temporal), **Scalp tenderness**, **Jaw claudication**, **Visual symptoms** (amaurosis fugax, Diplopia, Vision loss), **PMR symptoms** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/giant-cell-arteritis |
| **Diagnosis** | **Elevated ESR/CRP**, **Temporal artery biopsy** (gold standard), **Temporal artery ultrasound** (halo sign) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/giant-cell-arteritis |
| **Treatment** | **High-dose corticosteroids** (prednisone 40-60 mg daily); **Do NOT delay steroids for biopsy**; **Tocilizumab** (steroid-sparing) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/giant-cell-arteritis |
