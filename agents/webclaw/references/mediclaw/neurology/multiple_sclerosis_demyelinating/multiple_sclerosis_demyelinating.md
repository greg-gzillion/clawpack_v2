# Neurology - Multiple Sclerosis and Demyelinating Diseases

## Multiple Sclerosis (MS) - Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Epidemiology** | **Most common autoimmune demyelinating disease**; Female > Male (3:1); Onset 20-40 years | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/what-is-ms |
| **Pathophysiology** | **T cell-mediated autoimmune attack** on CNS myelin ? **Demyelination**, **Axonal loss**, **Gliosis** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/multiple-sclerosis |
| **Risk Factors** | **HLA-DRB1*15:01**, **Epstein-Barr virus** (EBV), **Vitamin D deficiency**, **Smoking**, **Obesity** (adolescent) | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/what-is-ms/what-causes-ms |
| **Clinical Courses** | **Relapsing-Remitting (RRMS)** - 85%, **Secondary Progressive (SPMS)**, **Primary Progressive (PPMS)** - 10-15% | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/what-is-ms/types-of-ms |

## MS - Clinical Features
| Symptom | Description | URL |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| **Optic Neuritis** | **Monocular vision loss**, **Pain with eye movement**, **Afferent pupillary defect**, **Desaturation of red** | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/symptoms-diagnosis/ms-symptoms/vision-problems |
| **Myelitis** | **Sensory level**, **Weakness**, **Bowel/bladder dysfunction**, **Lhermitte sign** (electric shock with neck flexion) | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/symptoms-diagnosis/ms-symptoms |
| **Brainstem/Cerebellar** | **Internuclear ophthalmoplegia (INO)** , **Ataxia**, **Dysarthria**, **Vertigo**, **Trigeminal neuralgia** | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/symptoms-diagnosis/ms-symptoms |
| **Cerebral** | **Fatigue** (most common), **Cognitive impairment**, **Depression**, **Uhthoff phenomenon** (worsening with heat) | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/symptoms-diagnosis/ms-symptoms |

## MS - Diagnostic Criteria (McDonald 2017)
| Criterion | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Dissemination in Space (DIS)** | =1 T2 lesion in =2 of 4 CNS areas: **Periventricular**, **Cortical/Juxtacortical**, **Infratentorial**, **Spinal cord** | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/symptoms-diagnosis/diagnosing-ms |
| **Dissemination in Time (DIT)** | **Simultaneous enhancing and non-enhancing lesions** OR **New T2/enhancing lesion on follow-up MRI** OR **OCB-positive CSF** | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/symptoms-diagnosis/diagnosing-ms |
| **CSF Analysis** | **Oligoclonal bands (OCB)** present in CSF but not serum (positive in >90% MS) | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/symptoms-diagnosis/diagnosing-ms |

## MS - Disease-Modifying Therapies (DMTs)
| Efficacy | DMTs | Mechanism | Monitoring | URL |
|----------|------|-----------|------------|-----|
| **High Efficacy** | **Ocrelizumab**, **Ofatumumab**, **Rituximab** (off-label) | **Anti-CD20** (B cell depletion) | CBC, LFTs, **HBV screening**, **JCV antibody** | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/treating-ms/medications |
| **High Efficacy** | **Natalizumab** | **Anti-VLA-4** (blocks lymphocyte migration) | **JCV antibody q6 months** (PML risk) | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/treating-ms/medications |
| **High Efficacy** | **Alemtuzumab** | **Anti-CD52** (lymphocyte depletion) | Monthly labs x 4 years (autoimmunity), **Thyroid**, **ITP** | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/treating-ms/medications |
| **High Efficacy** | **Cladribine** | Purine analog (lymphocyte depletion) | CBC, LFTs, **Malignancy screening** | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/treating-ms/medications |
| **Moderate Efficacy** | **Fingolimod**, **Siponimod**, **Ozanimod**, **Ponesimod** | **S1P receptor modulators** | **Baseline ECG** (bradycardia), **Ophthalmology** (macular edema), **LFTs**, **VZV IgG** | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/treating-ms/medications |
| **Moderate Efficacy** | **Dimethyl fumarate**, **Diroximel fumarate**, **Monomethyl fumarate** | **Nrf2 activation** | CBC (lymphopenia), **JCV antibody** (PML risk with prolonged lymphopenia) | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/treating-ms/medications |
| **Moderate Efficacy** | **Teriflunomide** | **Pyrimidine synthesis inhibitor** | **LFTs**, **CBC**, **Pregnancy test** (teratogenic) | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/treating-ms/medications |
| **Injection Therapies** | **Interferon beta**, **Glatiramer acetate** | Immunomodulation | LFTs, CBC (interferon) | https://www.nationalmssociety.org/treating-ms/medications |

## Neuromyelitis Optica Spectrum Disorder (NMOSD)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Pathophysiology** | **Anti-AQP4 antibodies** (70-80%) ? Complement-mediated astrocyte injury | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/neuromyelitis-optica |
| **Clinical Features** | **Severe optic neuritis** (often bilateral), **Longitudinally extensive transverse myelitis** (=3 vertebral segments), **Area postrema syndrome** (intractable hiccups, Nausea/Vomiting) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/neuromyelitis-optica |
| **Diagnosis** | **Anti-AQP4 antibody**, **MRI** (long spinal cord lesions, Optic nerve), **Distinguish from MS** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/neuromyelitis-optica |
| **Treatment (Acute)** | **IV Methylprednisolone**, **Plasmapheresis** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/neuromyelitis-optica |
| **Treatment (Maintenance)** | **Eculizumab** (anti-C5), **Inebilizumab** (anti-CD19), **Satralizumab** (anti-IL-6R), **Rituximab**, **Mycophenolate**, **Azathioprine** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/neuromyelitis-optica |

## Acute Disseminated Encephalomyelitis (ADEM)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Epidemiology** | **Children** > Adults; Post-infectious (viral) or post-vaccination | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/acute-disseminated-encephalomyelitis |
| **Clinical Features** | **Encephalopathy** (required for diagnosis), **Multifocal neurologic deficits**, **Seizures** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/acute-disseminated-encephalomyelitis |
| **MRI** | **Large, Multifocal, Bilateral, Asymmetric white matter lesions**, Often with **thalamic/basal ganglia involvement** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/acute-disseminated-encephalomyelitis |
| **Treatment** | **IV Methylprednisolone**, **Plasmapheresis**, **IVIG** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/acute-disseminated-encephalomyelitis |
| **Prognosis** | **Monophasic** (80-90%); Recurrent or multiphasic forms exist | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/acute-disseminated-encephalomyelitis |
