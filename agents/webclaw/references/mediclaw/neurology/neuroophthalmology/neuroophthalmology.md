# Neurology - Neuroophthalmology

## Optic Neuritis
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Inflammation of optic nerve** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/optic-neuritis |
| **Clinical Features** | **Monocular vision loss** (hours-days), **Pain with eye movement**, **Afferent pupillary defect (APD)** , **Desaturation of red**, **Central/cecocentral scotoma** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/optic-neuritis |
| **Associations** | **Multiple sclerosis** (most common), **NMOSD** (anti-AQP4), **MOGAD** (anti-MOG), Idiopathic, Post-infectious | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/optic-neuritis |
| **Diagnosis** | **MRI orbits with contrast** (optic nerve enhancement), **MRI brain** (white matter lesions ? MS risk) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/optic-neuritis |
| **Treatment** | **IV Methylprednisolone 1000 mg x 3-5 days** (accelerates recovery, Does not affect final outcome); **Avoid oral prednisone alone** (increased recurrence) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/optic-neuritis |
| **Prognosis** | **Spontaneous improvement** in 80-90% within weeks | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/optic-neuritis |

## Papilledema
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Bilateral optic disc edema** due to **elevated intracranial pressure (ICP)** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/pseudotumor-cerebri |
| **Causes** | **Idiopathic intracranial hypertension (IIH)** , **Mass lesion**, **Cerebral venous thrombosis**, **Meningitis**, **Hydrocephalus** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/pseudotumor-cerebri |
| **Clinical Features** | **Headache** (worse supine), **Pulsatile tinnitus**, **Transient visual obscurations**, **Diplopia** (CN VI palsy), **Enlarged blind spot** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/pseudotumor-cerebri |
| **Diagnosis** | **Fundoscopy** (bilateral disc edema), **MRI/MRV brain**, **LP** (elevated opening pressure >25 cm H2O) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/pseudotumor-cerebri |
| **Treatment** | **Treat underlying cause**; **Weight loss**, **Acetazolamide** (IIH); **Optic nerve sheath fenestration** or **CSF shunting** for vision-threatening IIH | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/pseudotumor-cerebri |

|------|-------|------------|---------|-----------|-----|

## Cranial Nerve Palsies (III, IV, VI)
| CN | Features | Common Causes | URL |
|----|----------|---------------|-----|
| **III (Oculomotor)** | **Ptosis**, **"Down and out" eye**, **Pupil involvement** (compressive - PCOM aneurysm) or **sparing** (ischemic - diabetes, HTN) | **PCOM aneurysm** (pupil-involved), **Microvascular** (pupil-spared), Trauma, Tumor | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/eye-movement-disorders |
| **IV (Trochlear)** | **Vertical diplopia**, **Head tilt opposite side** (Bielschowsky sign), Difficulty reading/down stairs | **Congenital** (most common), **Trauma**, Microvascular | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/eye-movement-disorders |
| **VI (Abducens)** | **Horizontal diplopia** (worse at distance), **Esotropia** | **Microvascular** (diabetes, HTN), **Trauma**, **Elevated ICP** (false localizing), Tumor | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/eye-movement-disorders |

## Internuclear Ophthalmoplegia (INO)
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|

## Horner Syndrome
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Triad** | **Ptosis** (mild), **Miosis**, **Anhidrosis** (central/first-order lesions) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/horner-syndrome |
| **Localization** | **First-order** (hypothalamus to C8-T2): Stroke, Tumor, Syrinx; **Second-order** (C8-T2 to superior cervical ganglion): **Pancoast tumor**, Neck trauma; **Third-order** (superior cervical ganglion to eye): **Carotid dissection**, Cluster headache | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/horner-syndrome |
| **Pharmacologic Testing** | **Cocaine** (confirms Horner - no dilation), **Apraclonidine** (reversal of anisocoria), **Hydroxyamphetamine** (localizes pre- vs post-ganglionic) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/horner-syndrome |
