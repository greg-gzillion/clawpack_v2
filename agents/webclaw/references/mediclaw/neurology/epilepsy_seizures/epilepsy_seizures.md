## Epilepsy - Definition and Diagnosis
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Epilepsy Definition** | =2 unprovoked seizures >24 hours apart; OR 1 unprovoked seizure + high recurrence risk (>60%); OR Epilepsy syndrome diagnosis | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/epilepsy |
| **Provoked Seizures** | Due to acute insult (metabolic, Toxic, Infectious, Structural) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/epilepsy |
| **Diagnostic Workup** | **EEG** (routine, Sleep-deprived, Ambulatory, Video-EEG), **Brain MRI** (epilepsy protocol), **Labs** (CBC, CMP, Toxicology) | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/epilepsy |
| **Differential** | **Syncope**, **Psychogenic non-epileptic seizures (PNES)**, **Migraine**, **TIA**, **Movement disorders**, **Sleep disorders** | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/epilepsy |

## Antiseizure Medications - Key Adverse Effects
| ASM | Key Adverse Effects | Monitoring | URL |
|-----|---------------------|------------|-----|
| **Phenytoin** | **Gingival hyperplasia**, **Osteoporosis**, **Cerebellar atrophy**, **Rash (SJS/TEN)** | Levels, LFTs, CBC | https://www.epilepsy.com/treatment/medicines |
| **Valproate** | **Weight gain**, **Tremor**, **Thrombocytopenia**, **Hepatotoxicity**, **Pancreatitis**, **Teratogenicity** | LFTs, CBC, Ammonia, Levels | https://www.epilepsy.com/treatment/medicines |
| **Carbamazepine** | **Hyponatremia**, **Rash (SJS/TEN in HLA-B*1502)** , **Dizziness**, **Diplopia** | HLA-B*1502 (Asian), LFTs, CBC, Na | https://www.epilepsy.com/treatment/medicines |
| **Lamotrigine** | **Rash (SJS/TEN)**, **Insomnia**, **Headache** | Slow titration, Monitor for rash | https://www.epilepsy.com/treatment/medicines |
| **Levetiracetam** | **Irritability**, **Aggression**, **Depression**, **Somnolence** | Monitor mood | https://www.epilepsy.com/treatment/medicines |
| **Topiramate** | **Cognitive slowing**, **Word-finding difficulty**, **Weight loss**, **Metabolic acidosis**, **Kidney stones** | Bicarbonate, Weight | https://www.epilepsy.com/treatment/medicines |

## Status Epilepticus
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | Seizure =5 minutes OR =2 seizures without return to baseline | https://www.epilepsy.com/complications-risks/emergencies/status-epilepticus |
| **Refractory Status** | Failure of first-line and second-line agents | https://www.epilepsy.com/complications-risks/emergencies/status-epilepticus |
| **Super-Refractory** | Failure of anesthetic agents for =24 hours | https://www.epilepsy.com/complications-risks/emergencies/status-epilepticus |

## Status Epilepticus - Management
| Timeframe | Intervention | URL |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| **0-5 min** | **Benzodiazepines**: **Lorazepam 4 mg IV** (preferred) or **Midazolam 10 mg IM** | https://www.epilepsy.com/complications-risks/emergencies/status-epilepticus |
| **5-20 min** | **Second-line ASM**: **Fosphenytoin 20 mg PE/kg**, **Valproate 40 mg/kg**, **Levetiracetam 60 mg/kg** | https://www.epilepsy.com/complications-risks/emergencies/status-epilepticus |
| **20-40 min** | **Repeat second-line ASM** (different agent) or **Third-line ASM** (Lacosamide, Phenobarbital) | https://www.epilepsy.com/complications-risks/emergencies/status-epilepticus |
| **>40 min** | **Refractory**: **Continuous IV anesthetic** (Midazolam, Propofol, Pentobarbital) + **Continuous EEG** | https://www.epilepsy.com/complications-risks/emergencies/status-epilepticus |

## Epilepsy Syndromes
| Syndrome | Age of Onset | Key Features | Treatment | URL |
|----------|--------------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Childhood Absence Epilepsy** | 4-10 years | **Frequent brief absence seizures**, **3 Hz spike-wave on EEG** | **Ethosuximide**, Valproate, Lamotrigine | https://www.epilepsy.com/what-is-epilepsy/syndromes/childhood-absence-epilepsy |
| **Juvenile Myoclonic Epilepsy (JME)** | 12-18 years | **Myoclonic jerks** (morning), **GTCs**, **Absence**, **4-6 Hz polyspike-wave** | **Valproate**, Levetiracetam, Lamotrigine | https://www.epilepsy.com/what-is-epilepsy/syndromes/juvenile-myoclonic-epilepsy |
| **Lennox-Gastaut Syndrome** | 1-8 years | **Multiple seizure types** (tonic, Atonic, Atypical absence), **Slow spike-wave**, **Intellectual disability** | Valproate, Lamotrigine, Topiramate, Clobazam, Rufinamide, Cannabidiol | https://www.epilepsy.com/what-is-epilepsy/syndromes/lennox-gastaut-syndrome |
| **Dravet Syndrome** | <1 year | **Prolonged febrile seizures** ? **Multiple seizure types**, **SCN1A mutation** | Valproate, Clobazam, Stiripentol, Cannabidiol, **AVOID sodium channel blockers** | https://www.epilepsy.com/what-is-epilepsy/syndromes/dravet-syndrome |
| **West Syndrome** | 3-12 months | **Infantile spasms**, **Hypsarrhythmia on EEG**, **Developmental regression** | **ACTH**, Vigabatrin, Corticosteroids | https://www.ninds.nih.gov/health-information/disorders/infantile-spasms |
