# Integrative Medicine - Research Methods

## Challenges in CAM Research
| Challenge | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Blinding** | Difficult to blind for hands-on therapies (acupuncture, massage) | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/research/research-results/research-challenges |
| **Placebo Control** | Appropriate sham controls (e.g., sham acupuncture) | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/research/research-results/research-challenges |
| **Individualization** | CAM often individualized (e.g., TCM formula, Ayurvedic constitution) | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/research/research-results/research-challenges |
| **Standardization** | Natural products vary in composition | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/research/research-results/research-challenges |
| **Whole Systems** | Complex interventions difficult to reduce to single component | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/research/research-results/research-challenges |

## Levels of Evidence in Integrative Medicine
| Level | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Strong** | Multiple high-quality RCTs, Meta-analyses | Acupuncture for chronic low back pain |
| **Moderate** | Some RCTs, Consistent observational studies | Yoga for anxiety |
| **Limited** | Small studies, Inconsistent results | Reiki for pain |
| **No Evidence/Placebo** | Negative RCTs, No plausible mechanism | Homeopathy |
