# Integrative Medicine - Botanical Medicine

## Overview
Botanical medicine involves the use of plants or plant parts for therapeutic purposes. Many conventional pharmaceuticals originated from plant compounds.

| Botanical | Common Uses | Evidence Grade | Safety Concerns | URL |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Turmeric (Curcumin)** | Inflammation, OA, Dyspepsia | **Moderate** | Generally safe; High doses may cause GI upset | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/turmeric |
| **Ginger** | Nausea (pregnancy, post-op), Dyspepsia | **Strong** (nausea); **Moderate** (other) | Generally safe; May ? bleeding risk | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/ginger |
| **Milk Thistle (Silymarin)** | Liver disease (hepatoprotective) | **Limited** | Generally safe; Mild GI effects | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/milk-thistle |
| **Echinacea** | Common cold prevention/treatment | **Limited** | Generally safe; Allergic reactions possible | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/echinacea |
| **Garlic** | CVD (lipid-lowering), Antimicrobial | **Moderate** (lipids) | ? Bleeding risk; GI upset | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/garlic |
| **Ginkgo biloba** | Cognitive function, Claudication | **Limited** (dementia); **Moderate** (claudication) | ? Bleeding risk; Seizures (rare) | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/ginkgo |
| **Saw Palmetto** | BPH symptoms | **Limited (no better than placebo)** | Generally safe; Mild GI effects | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/saw-palmetto |
| **Feverfew** | Migraine prevention | **Limited** | Mouth ulcers; Withdrawal rebound headache | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/feverfew |
| **Butterbur** | Migraine prevention, Allergic rhinitis | **Moderate** | **Hepatotoxicity** (PA-containing products); Use PA-free only | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/butterbur |
| **Black Cohosh** | Menopausal symptoms | **Limited** | **Hepatotoxicity** (rare); Monitor LFTs | https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/black-cohosh |
