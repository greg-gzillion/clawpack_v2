# Family Medicine - Musculoskeletal in Primary Care

## Common Musculoskeletal Conditions
| Condition | Key Features | Diagnosis | Treatment | URL |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Osteoarthritis** | **Use-related pain**, Morning stiffness <30 min, Bony enlargement | **Clinical** (X-ray if atypical) | Weight loss, Exercise, NSAIDs, Intraarticular CS | https://www.rheumatology.org/I-Am-A/Patient-Caregiver/Diseases-Conditions/Osteoarthritis |
| **Rotator Cuff Tendinopathy** | **Pain with overhead activity**, Night pain, Weakness | **Positive impingement signs** (Neer, Hawkins) | **Physical therapy**, NSAIDs, Subacromial CS injection | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/rotator-cuff-tears/ |
| **Lateral Epicondylitis** | **Pain with gripping**, Tenderness at lateral epicondyle | **Cozen test** (pain with resisted wrist extension) | **Counterforce brace**, PT, CS injection | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/tennis-elbow-lateral-epicondylitis/ |
| **De Quervain Tenosynovitis** | **Pain over radial styloid**, Thumb movement | **Positive Finkelstein test** | Thumb spica splint, CS injection | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/de-quervains-tendinosis/ |
| **Carpal Tunnel Syndrome** | **Nocturnal paresthesias** (thumb, index, middle), Thenar atrophy | **Phalen**, **Tinel**, Nerve conduction studies | **Night splint**, CS injection, Surgery | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/carpal-tunnel-syndrome/ |
| **Plantar Fasciitis** | **First-step pain** in heel, Improves with activity | **Tenderness at medial calcaneal tubercle** | **Stretching**, Night splint, CS injection | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/plantar-fasciitis-and-bone-spurs/ |
| **Greater Trochanteric Pain Syndrome** | **Lateral hip pain**, Worse lying on affected side | Tenderness over greater trochanter | PT, CS injection | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/greater-trochanteric-pain-syndrome/ |

## Joint Injection Techniques
| Joint | Landmarks | Volume | Corticosteroid Example | URL |
|-------|-----------|--------|----------------------|-----|
| **Knee** | Superolateral or anteromedial approach | 4-8 mL | **Triamcinolone 40 mg** + Lidocaine | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2002/1015/p1497.html |
| **Shoulder (Subacromial)** | Posterior or lateral approach | 2-4 mL | Triamcinolone 40 mg + Lidocaine | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2021/0201/p164.html |
| **Greater Trochanteric Bursa** | Point of maximal tenderness | 2-4 mL | Triamcinolone 40 mg + Lidocaine | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2002/1015/p1497.html |
| **Lateral Epicondyle** | Peppering technique | 1-2 mL | Triamcinolone 10-20 mg + Lidocaine | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2021/0201/p164.html |
| **Trigger Finger** | At A1 pulley | 0.5-1 mL | Triamcinolone 10 mg + Lidocaine | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2021/0201/p164.html |

## Gout Management
| Phase | Treatment | URL |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| **Acute Flare** | **NSAIDs** (first-line), **Colchicine**, **Corticosteroids** | https://www.rheumatology.org/Practice-Quality/Clinical-Support/Clinical-Practice-Guidelines |
| **Urate-Lowering Therapy (ULT)** | **Allopurinol** (start 100 mg daily, titrate) | https://www.rheumatology.org/Practice-Quality/Clinical-Support/Clinical-Practice-Guidelines |
| **ULT Target** | **Uric acid <6 mg/dL** (<5 mg/dL if tophi) | https://www.rheumatology.org/Practice-Quality/Clinical-Support/Clinical-Practice-Guidelines |
| **Flare Prophylaxis** | **Colchicine** or **NSAIDs** for 3-6 months after starting ULT | https://www.rheumatology.org/Practice-Quality/Clinical-Support/Clinical-Practice-Guidelines |

## Osteoporosis Management
| Intervention | Recommendation | URL |
|--------------|----------------|-----|
| **Calcium** | 1200 mg/day (preferably dietary) | https://www.bonehealthandosteoporosis.org |
| **Vitamin D** | 800-1000 IU/day | https://www.bonehealthandosteoporosis.org |
| **Weight-Bearing Exercise** | Walking, Jogging, Strength training | https://www.bonehealthandosteoporosis.org |
| **Bisphosphonates** | **Alendronate 70 mg weekly** (first-line) | https://www.endocrine.org/clinical-practice-guidelines/osteoporosis |
| **Denosumab** | 60 mg SC q6 months | https://www.endocrine.org/clinical-practice-guidelines/osteoporosis |
| **Drug Holiday** | Consider after 5 years (oral) or 3 years (IV) if low fracture risk | https://www.endocrine.org/clinical-practice-guidelines/osteoporosis |
