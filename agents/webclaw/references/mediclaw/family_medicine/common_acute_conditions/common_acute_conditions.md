# Family Medicine - Common Acute Conditions

## Upper Respiratory Infections (URI)
| Condition | Key Features | Treatment | URL |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Common Cold** | Rhinorrhea, Nasal congestion, Sore throat, Cough | **Supportive** (saline, honey, acetaminophen) | https://www.cdc.gov/antibiotic-use/colds.html |
| **Acute Sinusitis** | **>10 days**, **Double sickening**, Purulent discharge, Facial pain | **Amoxicillin-clavulanate** if bacterial | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/acute-bacterial-rhinosinusitis/ |
| **Acute Pharyngitis** | Sore throat, Fever, Exudate, **Tender anterior cervical LAD**, **No cough** | **Penicillin** if GAS positive | https://www.cdc.gov/groupastrep/diseases-hcp/strep-throat.html |
| **Acute Bronchitis** | **Cough** (productive or dry), ± Wheeze, **Clear CXR** | **Supportive** (NO antibiotics) | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2016/1001/p560.html |
| **Influenza** | Fever, Myalgias, Cough, Sore throat (seasonal) | **Oseltamivir** if <48h and high-risk | https://www.cdc.gov/flu/professionals/index.htm |

## Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)
| Type | Features | Treatment | URL |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| **Uncomplicated Cystitis** | Dysuria, Frequency, Urgency, No fever | **Nitrofurantoin** 100 mg BID x5d, **TMP-SMX** DS BID x3d, **Fosfomycin** 3g x1 | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/uti/ |
| **Complicated UTI** | Men, Pregnancy, Catheter, Structural abnormality | **Ciprofloxacin** 500 mg BID x7d or **Levofloxacin** 750 mg daily x5d | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/uti/ |
| **Pyelonephritis** | **Fever**, **Flank pain**, **CVA tenderness**, Nausea | **Ciprofloxacin** 500 mg BID x7d or **Levofloxacin** 750 mg daily x5-7d (outpatient) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/uti/ |
| **Asymptomatic Bacteriuria** | NO symptoms, +Urine culture | **NO treatment** (except pregnancy or urologic procedure) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/uti/ |

## Acute Gastroenteritis
| Pathogen | Features | Treatment | URL |
|----------|----------|-----------|-----|
| **Viral** | Watery diarrhea, N/V, Low-grade fever | **Oral rehydration**, Symptomatic | https://www.cdc.gov/norovirus/index.html |
| **Bacterial** | Bloody diarrhea, Fever, Severe abdominal pain | **Supportive** (most); Antibiotics if severe or high-risk | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/infectious-diarrhea/ |
| **C. difficile** | Watery diarrhea, Recent antibiotics, Leukocytosis | **Oral vancomycin** or **Fidaxomicin** | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/c-difficile/ |

## Acute Low Back Pain
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Red Flags** | **Trauma**, **Fever**, **Unexplained weight loss**, **Neurologic deficits** (saddle anesthesia, bowel/bladder dysfunction), **History of cancer**, **IVDU**, **Steroid use** | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2018/0501/p586.html |
| **Imaging** | **NOT routinely indicated** <6 weeks without red flags | https://www.choosingwisely.org/clinician-lists/ |
| **Treatment** | **NSAIDs**, **Muscle relaxants** (short-term), **Stay active**, Heat, Physical therapy | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2018/0501/p586.html |
| **Prognosis** | **90% improve within 6 weeks** | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2018/0501/p586.html |

## Acute Otitis Media (AOM)
| Criteria | Management | URL |
|----------|------------|-----|
| **Observation Criteria** | Age =6 months, Unilateral, Mild symptoms, No otorrhea, Reliable follow-up | Observe 48-72h | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/acute-otitis-media/ |
| **Treatment Criteria** | **Age <6 months**, **Bilateral** (age <2 years), **Severe symptoms** (moderate-severe otalgia, Fever =39°C), **Otorrhea** | **Amoxicillin** 80-90 mg/kg/day | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/acute-otitis-media/ |
| **Treatment Failure** | No improvement in 48-72h | **Amoxicillin-clavulanate** | https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/acute-otitis-media/ |

## Allergic Rhinitis
| Symptom | First-Line Treatment | Alternative | URL |
|---------|---------------------|-------------|-----|
| **Nasal congestion** | **Intranasal corticosteroids** (fluticasone, mometasone) | Oral antihistamines, Decongestants | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/rhinitis |
| **Sneezing, Rhinorrhea, Itching** | **Oral antihistamines** (cetirizine, loratadine, fexofenadine) | Intranasal antihistamines (azelastine) | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/rhinitis |
| **Ocular symptoms** | **Ophthalmic antihistamines** (olopatadine, ketotifen) | Oral antihistamines | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/rhinitis |
| **Moderate-Severe Persistent** | Intranasal corticosteroid + Consider **immunotherapy** | Add LTRA (montelukast) | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/rhinitis |

## Minor Skin Infections
| Condition | Pathogen | Treatment | URL |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-----|
| **Impetigo** | S. aureus, S. pyogenes | **Topical mupirocin** (limited), **Oral cephalexin** (extensive) | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2014/0801/p229.html |
| **Cellulitis** | S. aureus, S. pyogenes | **Cephalexin**, Clindamycin, TMP-SMX (if MRSA risk) | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/skin-soft-tissue-infections/ |
| **Abscess** | S. aureus (often MRSA) | **Incision and drainage** ± Antibiotics | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/skin-soft-tissue-infections/ |
| **Erysipelas** | **S. pyogenes** | **Penicillin**, Cephalexin | https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/skin-soft-tissue-infections/ |
