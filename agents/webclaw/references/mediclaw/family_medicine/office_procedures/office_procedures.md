# Family Medicine - Office Procedures

## Common Office Procedures
| Procedure | Indication | Key Steps | URL |
|-----------|------------|-----------|-----|
| **Skin Biopsy** | Suspicious skin lesions | **Shave**: Tangential; **Punch**: 3-4 mm, Suture | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2011/1101/p1017.html |
| **Incision and Drainage** | Abscess | #11 blade, Break loculations, Pack or leave open | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2020/0801/p177.html |
| **Cryotherapy** | Actinic keratosis, Warts | Liquid nitrogen 5-10 sec freeze, 1-2 cm halo | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2012/0701/p38.html |
| **Joint Injection** | OA, Bursitis, Tendinitis | Aseptic technique, Corticosteroid + Lidocaine | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2002/1015/p1497.html |
| **Trigger Point Injection** | Myofascial pain | 1-2 mL lidocaine (with or without CS) | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2002/0215/p653.html |
| **IUD Placement** | Contraception, Menorrhagia | Sound uterus, Insert IUD, Trim strings | https://www.acog.org/womens-health/faqs/long-acting-reversible-contraception |
| **Nexplanon Insertion** | Contraception | Insert subdermally on medial upper arm | https://www.nexplanon.com |
| **Toenail Removal** | Onychocryptosis (ingrown nail) | Digital block, Partial or complete avulsion | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2019/0801/p158.html |
| **Cerumen Removal** | Cerumen impaction | Irrigation or curettage | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2018/1015/p525.html |
| **Punch Biopsy** | Inflammatory or deep lesions | 3-4 mm punch, Suture closure | https://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2011/1101/p1017.html |
