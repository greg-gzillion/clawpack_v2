# Family Medicine - Women's Health

## Cervical Cancer Screening
| Age | Recommendation | URL |
|-----|----------------|-----|
| **<21 years** | **No screening** | https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/cervical-cancer-screening |
| **21-29 years** | **Pap smear every 3 years** | https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/cervical-cancer-screening |
| **30-65 years** | **Pap + HPV co-testing every 5 years** (preferred) OR Pap alone every 3 years OR HPV alone every 5 years | https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/cervical-cancer-screening |
| **>65 years** | Stop if adequate prior screening and no CIN2+ in last 25 years | https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/cervical-cancer-screening |
| **Post-Hysterectomy** | Stop if cervix removed and no history of CIN2+ | https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/cervical-cancer-screening |

|--------|------------------------|------------|---------------|-----|

| **LNG-IUD** | 0.2% | 3-8 years, ? Bleeding, Endometrial protection | Irregular spotting initially | https://www.cdc.gov/reproductivehealth/contraception/index.htm |

## Menopausal Hormone Therapy (MHT)
| Indication | Recommendation | URL |
|------------|----------------|-----|
| **Vasomotor Symptoms** | **Effective**; Lowest dose for shortest duration | https://www.menopause.org/docs/default-source/professional/nams-2022-hormone-therapy-position-statement.pdf |
| **Genitourinary Syndrome** | **Low-dose vaginal estrogen** (safe for most women) | https://www.menopause.org/docs/default-source/professional/nams-2022-hormone-therapy-position-statement.pdf |
| **Osteoporosis Prevention** | Effective but **not first-line**; Consider if other indications | https://www.menopause.org/docs/default-source/professional/nams-2022-hormone-therapy-position-statement.pdf |
| **Contraindications** | Breast cancer, Endometrial cancer, VTE, Active liver disease, Undiagnosed vaginal bleeding | https://www.menopause.org/docs/default-source/professional/nams-2022-hormone-therapy-position-statement.pdf |
