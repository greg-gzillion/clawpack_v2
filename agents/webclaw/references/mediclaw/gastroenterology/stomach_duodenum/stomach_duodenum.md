# Gastroenterology - Stomach and Duodenal Disorders

## Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Etiology | **H. pylori** (70-90%), **NSAIDs** (10-30%), Other (Zollinger-Ellison, Malignancy) |
| Gastric Ulcer | Epigastric pain with meals (food aggravates), Weight loss |
| Duodenal Ulcer | Epigastric pain 2-3 hours after meals (food relieves), Nighttime awakening |
| Diagnosis | **EGD** (gold standard); H. pylori testing (biopsy, stool antigen, urea breath test) |
| Complications | Bleeding, Perforation, Gastric outlet obstruction |

## H. pylori Infection
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Epidemiology | Affects 30-40% of US population; 70-90% of PUD |
| Diagnostic Tests | Stool antigen (active infection), Urea breath test, EGD biopsy (histology, CLO test, culture) |
| Serology | Detects antibodies (past or present); NOT for test of cure |
| Indications for Treatment | All positive tests (PUD, MALToma, ITP, Family history of gastric cancer, Dyspepsia) |
| Test of Cure | Stool antigen or Urea breath test >=4 weeks after therapy |

## H. pylori Treatment Regimens
| Regimen | Components | Duration | Notes |
|---------|------------|----------|-------|
| Bismuth Quadruple | PPI + Bismuth + Metronidazole + Tetracycline | 14 days | Preferred first-line |
| Clarithromycin Triple | PPI + Clarithromycin + Amoxicillin | 14 days | Only if clarithromycin resistance <15% |
| Concomitant | PPI + Clarithromycin + Amoxicillin + Metronidazole | 14 days | Alternative |
| Levofloxacin Triple | PPI + Levofloxacin + Amoxicillin | 14 days | Salvage therapy |
| Rifabutin Triple | PPI + Rifabutin + Amoxicillin | 14 days | Salvage therapy |

## NSAID-Induced Gastropathy
| Risk Factors | Prevention |
|--------------|------------|
| Age >65 | Use lowest effective NSAID dose |
| Prior PUD or GI bleeding | **PPI** or **Misoprostol** |
| High-dose NSAIDs | Consider COX-2 selective (Celecoxib) + PPI |
| Concurrent steroids, Anticoagulants | Avoid NSAIDs if possible |
| H. pylori infection | Test and treat before starting NSAIDs |
| Cardiovascular disease | Avoid COX-2 inhibitors; Use Naproxen + PPI |

## Gastroparesis
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Etiology | **Idiopathic** (50%), **Diabetic** (30%), Post-surgical, Neurologic, Medications |
| Symptoms | Nausea, Vomiting, Early satiety, Bloating, Abdominal pain |
| Diagnosis | Gastric emptying scintigraphy (GES) at 4 hours (>10% retention at 4h = delayed) |
| Dietary | Small, frequent meals; Low fat; Low fiber; Liquid/pureed foods |
| Medical | **Metoclopramide** (prokinetic, 5-10 mg TID AC/HS); **Domperidone** (limited access); Antiemetics |
| Refractory | **Gastric electrical stimulator (GES)**; Pyloromyotomy (G-POEM); Feeding jejunostomy |

## Functional Dyspepsia
| Subtype | Symptoms | Treatment |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Epigastric Pain Syndrome (EPS) | Epigastric pain/burning | PPI; H. pylori eradication; TCA |
| Postprandial Distress Syndrome (PDS) | Early satiety, Postprandial fullness | Prokinetics; Dietary modification; TCA |
| Rome IV Criteria | Symptoms for >=3 months, onset >=6 months ago | Exclude organic disease with EGD if age >60 or alarm features |

## Gastric Cancer
| Type | Risk Factors | Location | Prognosis |
|------|--------------|----------|-----------|
| Intestinal Adenocarcinoma | H. pylori, Atrophic gastritis, Diet, Smoking | Distal stomach | Better |
| Diffuse Adenocarcinoma | Genetic (CDH1 mutation), Younger age | Proximal stomach | Worse |
| GIST | KIT/PDGFRA mutations | Anywhere | Imatinib responsive |
| MALT Lymphoma | **H. pylori** | Antrum | Excellent (often cured with H. pylori eradication) |
| Neuroendocrine Tumor | Chronic atrophic gastritis, MEN1 | Gastric body | Variable |

## H. pylori and Gastric Cancer
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Carcinogenesis Pathway | Chronic gastritis -> Atrophic gastritis -> Intestinal metaplasia -> Dysplasia -> Cancer |
| Risk Reduction | **H. pylori eradication reduces gastric cancer risk by 35-50%** |
| Screening | Consider in high-risk populations (East Asia, Family history) |
| Surveillance | EGD with mapping biopsies for extensive intestinal metaplasia |

## Zollinger-Ellison Syndrome (Gastrinoma)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Pathophysiology | Gastrin-secreting neuroendocrine tumor (usually in duodenum or pancreas) |
| Presentation | Severe PUD, Diarrhea, Weight loss, MEN1 association |
| Diagnosis | Fasting gastrin >1000 pg/mL + pH <2; Secretin stimulation test |
| Localization | CT, MRI, EUS, Somatostatin receptor scintigraphy (Octreotide scan) |
| Treatment | **High-dose PPI** (Pantoprazole 40-80 mg BID); Surgical resection if localized |

## Gastric Volvulus
| Type | Presentation | Management |
|------|--------------|------------|
| Organoaxial | Rotation along long axis; Intermittent symptoms | Endoscopic reduction; Gastropexy |
| Mesenteroaxial | Rotation along short axis; **Acute obstruction, Ischemia** | **Emergent surgery** |
| Borchardt's Triad | Severe epigastric pain, Retching without vomiting, Inability to pass NGT |
