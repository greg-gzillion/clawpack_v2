## Colorectal Cancer
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Epidemiology | 3rd most common cancer; 2nd leading cause of cancer death |
| Risk Factors | Age >50, IBD, Family history, Lynch syndrome, FAP, Diet (red/processed meat), Obesity, Smoking, Alcohol |
| Protective Factors | Aspirin/NSAIDs, High-fiber diet, Physical activity |
| Presentation | Hematochezia, Iron deficiency anemia, Change in bowel habits, Weight loss, Obstruction |
| Diagnosis | **Colonoscopy with biopsy** |
| Staging | **CT chest/abdomen/pelvis**; **CEA** (tumor marker, not diagnostic); MRI rectum (for rectal cancer) |
| Treatment (Colon) | **Surgical resection** + Adjuvant chemotherapy (Stage III and high-risk Stage II) |
| Treatment (Rectal) | **Neoadjuvant chemoradiation** -> **Total mesorectal excision (TME)** -> Adjuvant chemotherapy |
| Metastatic Disease | Chemotherapy (FOLFOX, FOLFIRI); **RAS/BRAF testing**; Anti-EGFR (Cetuximab, Panitumumab) for RAS wild-type; Anti-VEGF (Bevacizumab); **Immunotherapy** for MSI-H/dMMR |

## Colorectal Cancer - Staging and Survival
| Stage | Description | 5-Year Survival |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| I | T1-2, N0, M0 | >90% |
| II | T3-4, N0, M0 | 70-85% |
| III | Any T, N1-2, M0 | 40-70% |
| IV | Any T, Any N, M1 | 10-15% |

## Lynch Syndrome (Hereditary Nonpolyposis Colorectal Cancer - HNPCC)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Genetics | Autosomal dominant; Mutations in **MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2, EPCAM** |
| Mechanism | Defective **DNA mismatch repair (dMMR)** -> **Microsatellite instability (MSI-H)** |
| Lifetime CRC Risk | 30-70% (depending on gene) |
| Extracolonic Cancers | **Endometrial** (40-60%), Ovarian, Gastric, Small bowel, Urothelial, Pancreatic, Biliary, Brain (Turcot) |
| Amsterdam II Criteria | Clinical criteria for Lynch syndrome |
| Bethesda Guidelines | Indications for MSI testing |
| Universal Screening | **All CRCs should be tested for MSI or IHC for MMR proteins** |
| Surveillance | **Colonoscopy q1-2 years starting age 20-25**; EGD q2-3 years; Endometrial biopsy/transvaginal US q1-2 years |
| Aspirin Chemoprevention | **Aspirin 600 mg/day** reduces CRC risk in Lynch syndrome |

## Familial Adenomatous Polyposis (FAP)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Genetics | Autosomal dominant; Mutation in **APC gene** (5q21) |
| Classic FAP | **>100 colorectal adenomas**; 100% CRC risk by age 40-50 if untreated |
| Attenuated FAP (AFAP) | 10-100 adenomas; Later onset; Lower CRC risk |
| Extracolonic Manifestations | **Duodenal adenomas** (periampullary), Gastric fundic gland polyps, Desmoid tumors, CHRPE, Osteomas, Supernumerary teeth |
| Gardner Syndrome | FAP + Osteomas + Soft tissue tumors + CHRPE |
| Turcot Syndrome | FAP + Medulloblastoma |
| Surveillance | **Annual flexible sigmoidoscopy starting age 10-12**; EGD q1-3 years starting age 20-25 |
| Surgical Management | **Prophylactic colectomy** (usually late teens/early 20s) |
| Chemoprevention | **Sulindac** or **Celecoxib** (COX-2 inhibitors) reduce adenoma burden |

## MUTYH-Associated Polyposis (MAP)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Genetics | Autosomal recessive; Biallelic mutations in **MUTYH gene** |
| Phenotype | Similar to AFAP (10-100 adenomas) or classic FAP (>100 adenomas) |
| CRC Risk | 80% by age 70 |
| Surveillance | Colonoscopy q1-2 years starting age 20-25 |
| Surgical Management | Colectomy if polyp burden unmanageable or CRC |

## Serrated Polyposis Syndrome (SPS)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| WHO Criteria (2019) | (1) >=5 serrated polyps proximal to rectum, all >=5 mm, >=2 >=10 mm; OR (2) >=20 serrated polyps anywhere, >=5 proximal to rectum |
| CRC Risk | Elevated (15-30% lifetime) |
| Surveillance | **Colonoscopy q1-2 years** |
| Family Screening | First-degree relatives: Colonoscopy starting age 40 or 10 years before youngest affected |

## Peutz-Jeghers Syndrome
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Genetics | Autosomal dominant; Mutation in **STK11 gene** |
| Features | **Mucocutaneous pigmentation** (lips, buccal mucosa, fingers), **Hamartomatous polyps** (small bowel > colon) |
| Cancer Risks | **Breast** (50%), **Colorectal** (40%), **Pancreatic** (35%), **Gastric** (30%), **Small bowel** (15%), **Ovarian** (sex cord tumors), **Cervical** (adenoma malignum), **Testicular** (Sertoli cell) |
| Surveillance | EGD + Colonoscopy q2-3 years starting age 8; **Video capsule endoscopy** or **MR enterography** q2-3 years; Breast MRI/mammography q1 year starting age 25; Pancreatic imaging q1-2 years starting age 30 |

## Juvenile Polyposis Syndrome (JPS)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Genetics | Autosomal dominant; Mutations in **SMAD4** or **BMPR1A** |
| Features | **>=5 juvenile polyps** in colorectum OR multiple juvenile polyps throughout GI tract |
| SMAD4 Mutation | **Hereditary Hemorrhagic Telangiectasia (HHT)** overlap syndrome |
| Cancer Risks | **Colorectal** (40-50%), **Gastric** (20%) |
| Surveillance | Colonoscopy + EGD q1-3 years starting age 12-15 |
| Management | Polypectomy; Colectomy or Gastrectomy if polyp burden unmanageable |

## Cowden Syndrome (PTEN Hamartoma Tumor Syndrome)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Genetics | Autosomal dominant; Mutation in **PTEN gene** |
| Features | **Trichilemmomas**, Acral keratoses, Papillomatous papules, Macrocephaly, **Hamartomatous polyps** (colon, stomach, small bowel) |
| Cancer Risks | **Breast** (85%), **Thyroid** (35%), **Endometrial** (30%), **Renal cell** (35%), Colorectal (9-15%), Melanoma |
| Surveillance | Colonoscopy starting age 35, then q5 years; Thyroid US annually starting age 18; Breast MRI/mammography starting age 30; Endometrial biopsy annually starting age 30; Renal imaging q1-2 years starting age 40 |

## Anal Cancer
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Types | Squamous cell carcinoma (80%), Adenocarcinoma, Melanoma |
| Risk Factors | **HPV infection** (HPV-16, HPV-18), Immunosuppression (HIV), Receptive anal intercourse, Smoking |
| Presentation | Anal pain, Bleeding, Mass, Pruritus |
| Diagnosis | Anoscopy with biopsy |
| Staging | MRI pelvis, CT chest/abdomen/pelvis, PET-CT |
| Treatment | **Chemoradiation** (Nigro protocol: 5-FU + Mitomycin C + Radiation) |
| Surgery | Abdominoperineal resection (APR) for salvage or T1N0 well-differentiated |
| Screening | Anal Pap smear in high-risk populations (HIV+, MSM) |
