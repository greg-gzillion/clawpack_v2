# Gastroenterology - Inflammatory Bowel Disease

## Crohn's Disease vs Ulcerative Colitis
| Feature | Crohn's Disease | Ulcerative Colitis |
|---------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Location | Anywhere (mouth to anus); Skip lesions | Colon only; Continuous from rectum |
| Rectal Involvement | Often spared | **Always involved** |
| Depth of Inflammation | **Transmural** | Mucosal/submucosal |
| Endoscopic Findings | Aphthous ulcers, Cobblestoning, Strictures, Fistulas | Erythema, Granularity, Friability, Ulcers |
| Histology | Non-caseating granulomas (30-50%), Lymphoid aggregates | Crypt abscesses, Crypt distortion, Basal plasmacytosis |
| Complications | **Strictures, Fistulas, Abscesses** | **Toxic megacolon, Dysplasia/Cancer** |
| Smoking | **Worsens disease** | **Protective** (or milder disease) |
| Surgery | Not curative; Risk of recurrence | **Curative** (total proctocolectomy) |

## Crohn's Disease - Montreal Classification
| Category | Subtypes |
|----------|----------|
| Age at Diagnosis | A1: <16 years; A2: 17-40 years; A3: >40 years |
| Location | L1: Ileal; L2: Colonic; L3: Ileocolonic; L4: Upper GI |
| Behavior | B1: Inflammatory (non-stricturing, non-penetrating); B2: Stricturing; B3: Penetrating |
| Perianal Disease | p: Perianal disease modifier |

## Ulcerative Colitis - Montreal Classification
| Extent | Definition |
|--------|------------|
| E1: Proctitis | Limited to rectum (distal to rectosigmoid junction) |
| E2: Left-sided | Distal to splenic flexure |
| E3: Extensive (Pancolitis) | Proximal to splenic flexure |

## IBD - Medical Therapy
| Drug Class | Examples | Indications | Monitoring |
|------------|----------|-------------|------------|
| 5-ASA (Mesalamine) | Mesalamine, Sulfasalazine, Balsalazide | **UC (first-line)**; Mild Crohn's | Renal function |
| Corticosteroids | Prednisone, Budesonide | **Induction of remission** (not maintenance) | Glucose, BP, Bone density |
| Thiopurines | Azathioprine, 6-Mercaptopurine | Maintenance (steroid-sparing) | **TPMT** before starting; CBC, LFTs |
| Methotrexate | Methotrexate | Crohn's maintenance | CBC, LFTs, Renal function |
| Anti-TNF | Infliximab, Adalimumab, Certolizumab, Golimumab | Moderate-severe disease | TB screening, HBV screening |
| Anti-Integrin | Vedolizumab (gut-selective), Natalizumab (PML risk) | Moderate-severe disease | PML risk (Natalizumab) |
| Anti-IL12/23 | Ustekinumab | Moderate-severe Crohn's | TB screening |
| JAK Inhibitor | Tofacitinib, Upadacitinib | Moderate-severe UC | **VTE risk**, Shingles, Lipids |

## IBD - Treatment Targets (STRIDE-II)
| Target | Goal |
|--------|------|
| Clinical Response | Symptom resolution (PRO-2: stool frequency, rectal bleeding) |
| Biochemical Remission | Normalization of **CRP** and **Fecal calprotectin** |
| Endoscopic Healing | **Mayo score 0-1** (UC); **SES-CD <3** or resolution of ulceration (Crohn's) |
| Histologic Healing | Emerging target (UC) |

## Crohn's Disease - Management by Severity
| Severity | Management |
|----------|------------|
| Mild-Moderate | Budesonide (ileal/right colon); 5-ASA (limited role) |
| Moderate-Severe | **Anti-TNF** (Infliximab, Adalimumab) or **Ustekinumab** or **Vedolizumab** |
| Fistulizing | **Anti-TNF** (Infliximab preferred); Seton placement; Antibiotics (Metronidazole, Ciprofloxacin) |
| Post-Surgical Prophylaxis | **Anti-TNF** (high risk); 6-MP/AZA (moderate risk); Mesalamine (low risk); No therapy (lowest risk) |

## Ulcerative Colitis - Management by Severity
| Severity | Management |
|----------|------------|
| Mild-Moderate (Proctitis) | **Mesalamine suppositories** (first-line) |
| Mild-Moderate (Left-sided) | **Mesalamine enemas** or Oral mesalamine + Enemas |
| Mild-Moderate (Extensive) | **Oral mesalamine** (4.8g/day) |
| Moderate-Severe | **Anti-TNF** (Infliximab, Adalimumab, Golimumab) or **Vedolizumab** or **Tofacitinib/Upadacitinib** |
| Acute Severe UC | **IV steroids**; If refractory: **Infliximab** or **Cyclosporine**; **Colectomy** if no response in 3-5 days |

## Acute Severe Ulcerative Colitis (ASUC)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Definition (Truelove & Witts) | >=6 bloody stools/day + One of: HR >90, Temp >37.8C, Hb <10.5, ESR >30, CRP elevated |
| Initial Management | **IV Methylprednisolone** 60 mg/day or Hydrocortisone 100 mg q6h; VTE prophylaxis; NPO if toxic megacolon |
| Response Assessment | **Day 3**: Stool frequency <3 + No visible blood + CRP decline = Responder |
| Day 3 Non-Responder | **Infliximab** 5-10 mg/kg OR **Cyclosporine** 2-4 mg/kg/day (monitor levels) |
| Day 5-7 Non-Responder | **Colectomy** |
| Toxic Megacolon | Colonic dilation >6 cm + Systemic toxicity; **Emergent surgical consultation** |

## IBD - Cancer Surveillance
| Risk Factor | Surveillance Interval |
|-------------|----------------------|
| UC/Crohn's colitis (pancolitis) | Begin 8-10 years after diagnosis; q1-3 years |
| UC/Crohn's colitis (left-sided) | Begin 12-15 years after diagnosis; q1-3 years |
| PSC with IBD | **Annual colonoscopy** from diagnosis |
| Family history of CRC | More frequent surveillance |
| Strictures | Biopsy to rule out malignancy |

## IBD - Health Maintenance
| Component | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Vaccinations | Influenza (annual), Pneumococcal (PCV20 or PCV15 + PPSV23), **Shingrix** (recombinant), HPV, Hepatitis B |
| Live Vaccines | **Contraindicated** if on immunosuppression |
| Bone Health | DXA scan; Calcium/Vitamin D; Bisphosphonates if steroid exposure >3 months |
| Skin Cancer | Annual skin exam (increased risk with thiopurines, anti-TNF) |
| Cervical Cancer | Annual Pap smear |
| Depression/Anxiety | Screen and treat |
