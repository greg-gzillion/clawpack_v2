# Gastroenterology - Gastrointestinal Pathology

## Esophageal Pathology
| Diagnosis | Histologic Findings | Clinical Correlation |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Reflux Esophagitis | Basal cell hyperplasia, Elongated papillae, Intraepithelial eosinophils (<15/HPF) | GERD |
| Eosinophilic Esophagitis | **>15 eosinophils per HPF**, Eosinophil microabscesses, Superficial layering | Dysphagia, Food impaction |
| Barrett's Esophagus | **Intestinal metaplasia with goblet cells** (Alcian blue positive) | GERD, Adenocarcinoma risk |
| Esophageal Adenocarcinoma | Invasive glands, Mucin production | Barrett's associated |
| Squamous Cell Carcinoma | Keratin pearls, Intercellular bridges | Smoking, Alcohol |
| Candida Esophagitis | Yeast and pseudohyphae (PAS/GMS positive) | Immunosuppression |
| HSV Esophagitis | Multinucleated giant cells, **Cowdry type A inclusions** | Immunosuppression |
| CMV Esophagitis | **Owl's eye inclusions** | Immunosuppression |

## Gastric Pathology
| Diagnosis | Histologic Findings | Clinical Correlation |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| H. pylori Gastritis | Chronic active gastritis, **Curved rods** (Giemsa, Diff-Quik), Lymphoid aggregates | PUD, MALToma |
| Autoimmune Gastritis | Atrophy of body/fundus, Enterochromaffin-like (ECL) cell hyperplasia, **Spares antrum** | Pernicious anemia, Carcinoid risk |
| Intestinal Metaplasia | Goblet cells in stomach (Alcian blue positive) | Gastric cancer risk |
| Gastric Adenocarcinoma (Intestinal) | Gland formation, Desmoplasia | H. pylori, Atrophic gastritis |
| Gastric Adenocarcinoma (Diffuse) | **Signet ring cells**, Linitis plastica | CDH1 mutation |
| MALT Lymphoma | Dense lymphocytic infiltrate, **Lymphoepithelial lesions** | H. pylori associated |
| GIST | Spindle or epithelioid cells, **CD117 (c-KIT) positive**, DOG1 positive | KIT/PDGFRA mutations |
| Neuroendocrine Tumor | Nests of uniform cells, **Synaptophysin and Chromogranin positive** | Type I (autoimmune gastritis) |

## Small Bowel Pathology
| Diagnosis | Histologic Findings | Clinical Correlation |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Celiac Disease | **Villous atrophy**, Crypt hyperplasia, **Increased intraepithelial lymphocytes (IELs)** | Marsh classification |
| Crohn's Disease | **Non-caseating granulomas** (30-50%), Transmural inflammation, Fissuring ulcers | Ileal involvement |
| Whipple's Disease | **PAS-positive macrophages** in lamina propria, Foamy macrophages | Tropheryma whipplei |
| Giardiasis | Pear-shaped trophozoites on villous surface | Diarrhea, Malabsorption |
| Adenocarcinoma | Gland formation, Desmoplasia | Duodenum/jejunum |
| Carcinoid (NET) | Nests of uniform cells, Salt-and-pepper chromatin | Ileum most common |

## Colorectal Pathology
| Diagnosis | Histologic Findings | Clinical Correlation |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Tubular Adenoma | **Low-grade dysplasia**, Tubular architecture | Low malignant potential |
| Villous Adenoma | Villous architecture (>75%) | Higher malignant potential |
| Sessile Serrated Lesion (SSL) | **Serrated architecture throughout crypt**, Crypt dilation, Horizontal growth | BRAF mutation, CIMP pathway |
| Traditional Serrated Adenoma (TSA) | Serrated architecture, Eosinophilic cytoplasm, Ectopic crypt foci | KRAS/BRAF mutations |
| Ulcerative Colitis | **Crypt abscesses**, Crypt distortion, Basal plasmacytosis, **Continuous inflammation** | Chronic UC |
| Crohn's Disease | **Non-caseating granulomas**, Transmural inflammation, Skip lesions | Ileocolonic |
| Microscopic Colitis | Lymphocytic: >20 IELs/100 epithelial cells; Collagenous: >10 µm collagen band | Chronic watery diarrhea |
| Adenocarcinoma | Invasive glands, **Desmoplastic reaction**, Necrosis | CRC |
| Mucinous Adenocarcinoma | >50% extracellular mucin | Right colon, MSI-H |
| Signet Ring Adenocarcinoma | >50% signet ring cells | Poor prognosis |

## Liver Pathology
| Diagnosis | Histologic Findings | Clinical Correlation |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Steatosis | **Macrovesicular fat** in hepatocytes | NAFLD, Alcohol |
| Steatohepatitis (NASH/ASH) | Steatosis + **Ballooning degeneration** + Lobular inflammation ± **Mallory-Denk bodies**, Fibrosis | NASH, Alcoholic hepatitis |
| Alcoholic Hepatitis | Ballooning, Mallory-Denk bodies, Neutrophilic infiltrate, **Cholestasis** | Severe alcoholic hepatitis |
| Autoimmune Hepatitis | **Interface hepatitis** (piecemeal necrosis), **Plasma cell infiltrate**, Rosettes | AIH |
| Primary Biliary Cholangitis | **Florid duct lesion** (lymphocytic destruction of bile ducts), Granulomas | AMA positive |
| Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis | **Onion-skin fibrosis** around bile ducts, Ductopenia | PSC, UC association |
| Hemochromatosis | **Iron deposition** (hepatocytes > Kupffer cells), Prussian blue positive | HFE gene |
| Wilson's Disease | **Copper deposition** (Rhodanine positive), Steatosis, Glycogenated nuclei | ATP7B mutation |
| Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency | **PAS-positive, diastase-resistant globules** in periportal hepatocytes | SERPINA1 mutation |
| Cirrhosis | **Regenerative nodules** surrounded by **fibrous septa** | End-stage liver disease |
| Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC) | Thickened trabeculae (>3 cells), **Unpaired arteries**, Reticulin loss | Chronic liver disease |

## Pancreatic Pathology
| Diagnosis | Histologic Findings | Clinical Correlation |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Acute Pancreatitis | Fat necrosis, Hemorrhage, Inflammation | Gallstones, Alcohol |
| Chronic Pancreatitis | Fibrosis, Acinar atrophy, Ductal dilation, **Calcifications** | Alcohol, Genetic |
| Autoimmune Pancreatitis (Type 1) | **Lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate**, **Storiform fibrosis**, **Obliterative phlebitis**, **IgG4-positive plasma cells** | IgG4-related disease |
| Autoimmune Pancreatitis (Type 2) | **Granulocytic epithelial lesions (GELs)** | IBD associated |
| Pancreatic Ductal Adenocarcinoma (PDAC) | Infiltrating glands, **Perineural invasion**, Desmoplasia | KRAS, p53, SMAD4 mutations |
| Pancreatic Neuroendocrine Tumor (PNET) | Nests of uniform cells, Salt-and-pepper chromatin, **Synaptophysin/Chromogranin positive** | WHO Grade 1-3 |
| Solid Pseudopapillary Neoplasm | Solid and pseudopapillary patterns, **Beta-catenin nuclear positivity** | Young women |
| Mucinous Cystic Neoplasm (MCN) | **Ovarian-type stroma** (ER/PR positive), Mucinous epithelium | Body/tail of pancreas |
