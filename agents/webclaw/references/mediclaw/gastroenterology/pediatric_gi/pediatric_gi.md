# Gastroenterology - Pediatric Gastroenterology

## Infantile Hypertrophic Pyloric Stenosis
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | **Non-bilious projectile vomiting** at 3-6 weeks of age, **Palpable olive mass**, Visible peristalsis |
| Risk Factors | Firstborn male, Family history, Macrolide exposure |
| Labs | **Hypochloremic, hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis** |
| Diagnosis | **Ultrasound** (pyloric muscle thickness >3 mm, length >15 mm) |
| Treatment | **Fluid resuscitation**, Correct electrolytes, **Pyloromyotomy** |

## Intussusception
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | **Intermittent severe abdominal pain**, **Currant jelly stools**, Lethargy, Vomiting |
| Peak Age | 3 months - 3 years |
| Pathologic Lead Point | **Meckel's diverticulum**, Polyp, Lymphoma (older children) |
| Diagnosis | **Ultrasound** (target sign, pseudokidney sign) |
| Treatment | **Air or contrast enema reduction** (if stable); **Surgery** if failed reduction or unstable |
| Recurrence | 5-10%; Usually within 24-48 hours |

## Meckel's Diverticulum
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Embryology | Remnant of **omphalomesenteric (vitelline) duct** |
| Rule of 2s | **2%** of population, **2 feet** from ileocecal valve, **2 inches** long, **2 years** of age (symptomatic), **2:1** male predominance |
| Presentation | **Painless rectal bleeding** (most common), Intussusception, Diverticulitis, Obstruction |
| Ectopic Tissue | **Gastric mucosa** (50-60%), Pancreatic tissue (5%) |
| Diagnosis | **Meckel's scan** (Tc-99m pertechnetate) - detects gastric mucosa |
| Treatment | **Surgical resection** |

## Hirschsprung Disease
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Pathophysiology | Failure of neural crest cell migration -> **Aganglionosis** of distal colon |
| Presentation | **Delayed passage of meconium** (>48 hours), Abdominal distention, Vomiting, **Enterocolitis** |
| Diagnosis | **Contrast enema** (transition zone), **Rectal suction biopsy** (absence of ganglion cells, hypertrophied nerve fibers) |
| Treatment | **Surgical resection** (pull-through procedure) |

## Biliary Atresia
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | **Persistent jaundice >2 weeks**, Acholic stools, Dark urine, Hepatomegaly |
| Diagnosis | **Hepatobiliary scintigraphy (HIDA)** (no excretion into bowel), Liver biopsy (bile duct proliferation, fibrosis) |
| Treatment | **Kasai portoenterostomy** (before 8 weeks of age for best outcome) |
| Prognosis | **Liver transplant** often needed by adolescence |

## Necrotizing Enterocolitis (NEC)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Risk Factors | **Prematurity**, Formula feeding, Intestinal ischemia, Bacterial colonization |
| Presentation | Feeding intolerance, Abdominal distention, Bloody stools, Lethargy, Apnea, Shock |
| Diagnosis | **Abdominal X-ray** (pneumatosis intestinalis, portal venous gas, free air) |
| Bell Staging | Stage I (Suspected), II (Definite - pneumatosis), III (Advanced - perforation) |
| Treatment | **NPO**, **NGT decompression**, **IV fluids**, **Broad-spectrum antibiotics**, **Surgery** if perforation or deterioration |

## Celiac Disease in Children
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Diarrhea, Failure to thrive, Abdominal distention, Irritability, Delayed puberty |
| Atypical Presentations | Short stature, Iron deficiency anemia, Dental enamel defects, Arthritis |
| Diagnosis | **tTG-IgA** + **Total IgA**; **EGD with duodenal biopsies** (Marsh classification) |
| Treatment | **Strict gluten-free diet** for life |
| Monitoring | Growth, tTG-IgA, Nutritional deficiencies |

## Functional Constipation in Children
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Rome IV Criteria | <2 years: >=1 month of symptoms; >2 years: >=1 month, at least 2 criteria |
| Red Flags | Delayed meconium passage, Ribbon stools, Failure to thrive, Abdominal distention, Neurologic abnormalities |
| Initial Management | **Disimpaction** (PEG 1-1.5 g/kg/day x 3-6 days) |
| Maintenance | **PEG** 0.4-0.8 g/kg/day; Behavioral modification (toilet sitting after meals) |
| Refractory | Rule out organic causes (Hirschsprung, Hypothyroidism, Celiac, Spinal cord abnormalities) |

## Cow's Milk Protein Allergy (CMPA)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| IgE-Mediated | Urticaria, Angioedema, Anaphylaxis (minutes to hours) |
| Non-IgE Mediated | **Allergic proctocolitis** (blood-streaked stools, well infant), **Food protein-induced enterocolitis syndrome (FPIES)** (profuse vomiting, lethargy, dehydration) |
| Diagnosis | Clinical; Elimination diet; Oral food challenge |
| Treatment | **Maternal elimination diet** (if breastfeeding); **Extensively hydrolyzed formula** or **Amino acid-based formula** |
| Prognosis | Most outgrow by 1-3 years |

## Eosinophilic Esophagitis (EoE) in Children
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Feeding difficulties, Vomiting, Abdominal pain, Food refusal, Failure to thrive (younger); Dysphagia, Food impaction (older) |
| Diagnosis | **EGD with biopsies** (>15 eosinophils per HPF) |
| Treatment | **PPI** (first-line); **Elimination diet** (6-food, 4-food, or 2-food); **Swallowed topical steroids** (Fluticasone, Budesonide slurry) |
| Monitoring | Repeat EGD to assess response |
