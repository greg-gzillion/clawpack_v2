# Gastroenterology - Colon and Rectal Disorders

## Colorectal Cancer Screening
| Modality | Interval | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Colonoscopy | q10 years | **Gold standard**; Diagnostic and therapeutic |
| FIT (Fecal Immunochemical Test) | Annual | Positive requires colonoscopy |
| gFOBT (Guaiac FOBT) | Annual | Dietary restrictions; Lower sensitivity |
| FIT-DNA (Cologuard) | q3 years | Higher sensitivity; Higher false positives |
| Flexible Sigmoidoscopy | q5-10 years | Only examines left colon |
| CT Colonography | q5 years | Requires bowel prep; Positive requires colonoscopy |
| Colon Capsule | q5 years | Requires bowel prep; Not widely available |

## Colorectal Cancer Screening - Start and Stop
| Population | Start Age | Stop Age |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| Average Risk | 45 years | 75 years (individualized 75-85) |
| Family History (1 FDR <60) | 40 years or 10 years before youngest affected | Same as average risk |
| Family History (>=2 FDR or FDR <50) | 40 years or 10 years before youngest | Same as average risk |
| IBD (Pancolitis) | 8-10 years after diagnosis | Per guidelines |
| Lynch Syndrome | 20-25 years | Per guidelines |
| FAP | 10-12 years | Per guidelines |

## Colonic Polyps
| Type | Malignant Potential | Surveillance Interval |
|------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Hyperplastic (distal) | None | 10 years |
| Hyperplastic (proximal, >=10 mm) | Serrated pathway risk | 5-10 years |
| Sessile Serrated Lesion (SSL) | Yes | 3-5 years |
| Traditional Serrated Adenoma (TSA) | Yes | 3 years |
| Tubular Adenoma | Low | 5-10 years (depending on size, number) |
| Tubulovillous Adenoma | Intermediate | 3 years |
| Villous Adenoma | High | 3 years |
| Adenoma with HGD | High | 3 years |

## Post-Polypectomy Surveillance
| Findings | Next Colonoscopy |
|----------|------------------|
| 1-2 tubular adenomas <10 mm | 7-10 years |
| 3-4 tubular adenomas <10 mm | 3-5 years |
| 5-10 tubular adenomas | 3 years |
| >10 adenomas | 1 year (consider genetic syndrome) |
| Adenoma >=10 mm | 3 years |
| Adenoma with HGD | 3 years |
| Villous or tubulovillous histology | 3 years |
| Sessile serrated lesion >=10 mm | 3 years |
| Sessile serrated lesion with dysplasia | 1-3 years |
| Piecemeal resection of polyp >=20 mm | 6 months |

## Diverticular Disease
| Condition | Presentation | Management |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Diverticulosis | Asymptomatic | High-fiber diet; No need to avoid nuts/seeds |
| Symptomatic Uncomplicated Diverticular Disease (SUDD) | LLQ pain, No fever, Normal labs | Fiber; Mesalamine; Rifaximin |
| Acute Diverticulitis (Uncomplicated) | LLQ pain, Fever, Leukocytosis, No abscess/perforation | **Antibiotics not routinely needed** (selective use); Outpatient management |
| Acute Diverticulitis (Complicated) | Abscess, Perforation, Obstruction, Fistula | IV antibiotics; Percutaneous drainage; Surgery |
| Diverticular Bleeding | Painless hematochezia | Resuscitation; Colonoscopy (after prep); Angiography if massive |

## Diverticulitis - Hinchey Classification
| Stage | Description | Management |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Ia | Pericolic inflammation/phlegmon | Medical management |
| Ib | Pericolic/mesocolic abscess | Medical management ｱ antibiotics |
| II | Pelvic, intra-abdominal, or retroperitoneal abscess | Percutaneous drainage |
| III | Purulent peritonitis | Surgery (laparoscopic lavage vs resection) |
| IV | Feculent peritonitis | **Emergent surgery** (Hartmann's procedure) |

## Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) - see Functional GI Disorders section

## Colonic Volvulus
| Type | Frequency | Management |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Sigmoid Volvulus | 60-75% | **Endoscopic detorsion**; Sigmoidopexy or resection |
| Cecal Volvulus | 25-40% | **Surgery** (endoscopic detorsion less effective) |
| Transverse Volvulus | Rare | Surgery |
| Presentation | Obstruction, Abdominal distention, Constipation, Nausea/Vomiting |
| Imaging | **Coffee bean sign** (sigmoid); **Kidney bean sign** (cecal) |

## Ogilvie's Syndrome (Acute Colonic Pseudo-obstruction)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Risk Factors | Surgery, Trauma, Critical illness, Electrolyte abnormalities, Medications (opioids, anticholinergics) |
| Presentation | Massive colonic distention (often cecum), Obstipation |
| Diagnosis | CT abdomen (rule out mechanical obstruction) |
| Management | NPO, NGT, Correct electrolytes, Stop offending drugs, Mobilization |
| Medical | **Neostigmine** 2 mg IV (with atropine available) |
| Endoscopic | Colonoscopic decompression |
| Surgical | Cecostomy or resection if refractory or perforation |

## Constipation
| Type | Features | Treatment |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Slow Transit Constipation | Infrequent bowel movements, Bloating | Fiber, Osmotic laxatives (PEG), Stimulants, Prucalopride |
| Pelvic Floor Dyssynergia | Straining, Incomplete evacuation, Digital maneuvers | **Biofeedback therapy** |
| IBS-C | Abdominal pain associated with constipation | Fiber, PEG, Lubiprostone, Linaclotide, Plecanatide, Tenapanor |
| Opioid-Induced Constipation | Constipation with opioid use | **PAMORAs** (Methylnaltrexone, Naldemedine), Naloxegol, Lubiprostone |
