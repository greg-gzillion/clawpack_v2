# Gastroenterology - Functional GI Disorders

## Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) - Rome IV Criteria
| Criteria | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Recurrent abdominal pain | At least 1 day/week in last 3 months |
| Associated with >=2 of: | (1) Related to defecation; (2) Change in stool frequency; (3) Change in stool form |
| Onset | >=6 months before diagnosis |
| Subtypes | **IBS-C** (Constipation predominant), **IBS-D** (Diarrhea predominant), **IBS-M** (Mixed), **IBS-U** (Unspecified) |

## IBS - Management by Subtype
| Subtype | First-Line | Second-Line | Third-Line |
|---------|------------|-------------|------------|
| IBS-C | Fiber (Psyllium), PEG | **Lubiprostone**, **Linaclotide**, **Plecanatide** | **Tenapanor**, **Tegaserod** (restricted) |
| IBS-D | Loperamide | **Rifaximin** (if SIBO suspected), **Eluxadoline** | **Alosetron** (women only, restricted), **Ondansetron** |
| IBS-M | Fiber, Antispasmodics | **TCAs** (Desipramine, Nortriptyline), **SSRIs** | Rifaximin |
| All Subtypes | **Low FODMAP diet**, Cognitive behavioral therapy, Gut-directed hypnotherapy | | |

## Low FODMAP Diet
| Phase | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Elimination | 2-6 weeks | Strict avoidance of high FODMAP foods |
| Reintroduction | 6-8 weeks | Systematic reintroduction of individual FODMAP groups |
| Personalization | Long-term | Individualized diet based on tolerance |
| **F**ermentable **O**ligosaccharides, **D**isaccharides, **M**onosaccharides, **A**nd **P**olyols |

## Functional Dyspepsia
| Subtype | Symptoms | Treatment |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Epigastric Pain Syndrome (EPS) | Epigastric pain/burning | PPI; H. pylori eradication; TCA |
| Postprandial Distress Syndrome (PDS) | Early satiety, Postprandial fullness | Prokinetics (Metoclopramide, Domperidone); Dietary (small meals); TCA |
| Overlap | Both EPS and PDS | Combination therapy |

## Functional Constipation
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Rome IV Criteria | <3 BM/week, Straining, Lumpy/hard stools, Sensation of incomplete evacuation, Sensation of anorectal obstruction, Manual maneuvers |
| Step 1 | Increase fiber (Psyllium), Fluids, Exercise |
| Step 2 | **Osmotic laxatives** (PEG, Lactulose, Magnesium) |
| Step 3 | **Stimulant laxatives** (Bisacodyl, Senna) - safe for long-term use |
| Step 4 | **Secretagogues** (Lubiprostone, Linaclotide, Plecanatide) |
| Step 5 | **5-HT4 agonist** (Prucalopride) |
| Refractory | **Anorectal manometry**, Balloon expulsion test -> **Biofeedback** for dyssynergic defecation |

## Functional Diarrhea
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Rome IV Criteria | Loose/watery stools >=25% of BMs, No abdominal pain, Onset >=6 months |
| Differential | IBS-D, Microscopic colitis, Bile acid diarrhea, Celiac disease, SIBO, Carbohydrate malabsorption |
| Diagnostic Workup | Stool studies, Celiac serologies, Colonoscopy with biopsies, **SeHCAT** or trial of bile acid sequestrants |
| Treatment | **Loperamide**, **Cholestyramine** (if bile acid diarrhea), **Ondansetron** |

## Cyclic Vomiting Syndrome (CVS)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Stereotypical episodes of intense vomiting lasting hours to days, Asymptomatic between episodes |
| Triggers | Stress, Infection, Menstruation, Sleep deprivation, Certain foods |
| Diagnosis | Rome IV criteria; Exclude other causes (EGD, CT, Gastric emptying) |
| Prophylaxis | **TCAs** (Amitriptyline), **Topiramate**, **CoQ10**, **L-carnitine** |
| Abortive Therapy | **Sumatriptan** (nasal spray or SC) at onset of prodrome |
| Acute Episode | Dark quiet room, IV fluids, Antiemetics (Ondansetron), Benzodiazepines |

## Cannabinoid Hyperemesis Syndrome (CHS)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Chronic cannabis use (>1 year), Cyclic vomiting, **Compulsive hot bathing** (pathognomonic) |
| Diagnosis | Clinical; Rome IV criteria |
| Acute Episode | **IV fluids**, **Capsaicin cream** (0.1%) applied to abdomen, **Haloperidol** or **Droperidol** |
| Long-Term | **Cannabis cessation** (only definitive treatment) |
| Relapse | Common with resumption of cannabis use |
