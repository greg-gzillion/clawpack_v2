# Gastroenterology - Esophageal Disorders

## Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Typical Symptoms | Heartburn, Regurgitation, Dysphagia |
| Atypical Symptoms | Chronic cough, Laryngitis, Asthma, Dental erosions, Chest pain |
| Diagnosis | Clinical (empiric PPI trial); EGD if alarm symptoms or refractory; pH testing if uncertain |
| Alarm Symptoms | Dysphagia, Weight loss, GI bleeding, Anemia, Age >60 with new symptoms |
| Lifestyle Modifications | Weight loss, Head of bed elevation, Avoid meals 2-3 hrs before bed, Avoid trigger foods |
| Medical Treatment | PPI (Omeprazole, Pantoprazole, Esomeprazole) daily for 8 weeks; H2RA alternative |
| Surgical Treatment | Fundoplication for refractory GERD or desire to stop medications |

## Complications of GERD
| Complication | Findings | Management |
|--------------|----------|------------|
| Erosive Esophagitis | LA Grade A-D | PPI; Repeat EGD to confirm healing |
| Peptic Stricture | Dysphagia, Narrowing on EGD | **Endoscopic dilation** + PPI |
| Barrett's Esophagus | Salmon-colored mucosa, Intestinal metaplasia | Surveillance EGD; PPI |
| Esophageal Adenocarcinoma | Mass, Nodularity | Staging; Esophagectomy, Chemotherapy |

## Barrett's Esophagus
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Definition | Intestinal metaplasia (goblet cells) in distal esophagus |
| Risk Factors | Chronic GERD, Male, Caucasian, Obesity, Age >50, Hiatal hernia |
| Diagnosis | EGD with biopsy (Seattle protocol - 4 quadrant biopsies every 1-2 cm) |
| Dysplasia | None, Indefinite, Low-grade, High-grade, Intramucosal carcinoma |
| Surveillance | No dysplasia: EGD q3-5 years; LGD: EGD q6-12 months or ablation |
| Treatment | **Radiofrequency ablation (RFA)** for LGD/HGD; EMR for nodules; Esophagectomy for invasive cancer |

## Eosinophilic Esophagitis (EoE)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Dysphagia, Food impaction, Chest pain (often young adults, atopic history) |
| Diagnosis | EGD with biopsies: **>15 eosinophils per HPF** |
| Endoscopic Findings | Rings (trachealization), Furrows, White plaques, Strictures, Crepe-paper mucosa |
| Treatment Options | **PPI** (first-line); **Swallowed topical steroids** (Fluticasone, Budesonide slurry); Elimination diet; Dilation for strictures |
| Maintenance | Long-term therapy needed; Risk of recurrence after stopping |

## Esophageal Motility Disorders
| Disorder | Manometry Findings | Treatment |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| Achalasia | Impaired LES relaxation, Absent peristalsis, Elevated IRP | Pneumatic dilation, Heller myotomy, POEM |
| EGJ Outflow Obstruction | Elevated IRP with preserved peristalsis | Similar to achalasia |
| Diffuse Esophageal Spasm | >=20% premature contractions | CCB, Nitrates, Antidepressants |
| Jackhammer Esophagus | >=20% hypercontractile swallows (DCI >8000) | CCB, Nitrates, Antidepressants |
| Ineffective Esophageal Motility | >=50% ineffective swallows | Treat underlying GERD |
| Absent Contractility | 100% failed peristalsis | Evaluate for systemic sclerosis |

## Achalasia
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Types | Type I: No pressurization; Type II: Panesophageal pressurization; Type III: Spastic |
| Diagnosis | Manometry (gold standard); Barium swallow (bird-beak appearance); EGD (rule out pseudoachalasia) |
| Treatment | **Pneumatic dilation**; **Laparoscopic Heller myotomy** with partial fundoplication; **POEM** (Peroral Endoscopic Myotomy) |
| Surveillance | Increased risk of esophageal SCC; Consider EGD surveillance |

## Esophageal Strictures
| Type | Causes | Management |
|------|--------|------------|
| Peptic | Chronic GERD | **Savary or balloon dilation** + PPI |
| Eosinophilic | EoE | Dilation + Medical therapy |
| Caustic | Lye ingestion | Complex strictures; Repeated dilations |
| Radiation | Radiation therapy | Dilation; Risk of perforation |
| Anastomotic | Post-esophagectomy | Dilation |
| Malignant | Esophageal cancer | Stenting, Palliation |

## Infectious Esophagitis
| Pathogen | Risk Factors | Endoscopic Findings | Treatment |
|----------|--------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Candida | Immunosuppression, Diabetes, Inhaled steroids | White plaques | Fluconazole 200-400 mg daily x 14-21 days |
| HSV | Immunosuppression | Vesicles, "Volcano ulcers" | Acyclovir 400 mg 5x daily x 7-14 days |
| CMV | HIV (CD4 <50), Transplant | Large, deep ulcers | Ganciclovir or Valganciclovir |

## Esophageal Cancer
| Type | Risk Factors | Location | Treatment |
|------|--------------|----------|-----------|
| Adenocarcinoma | Barrett's esophagus, GERD, Obesity | Distal esophagus/GEJ | Esophagectomy, Chemotherapy, Radiation |
| Squamous Cell Carcinoma | Smoking, Alcohol, Achalasia, Lye ingestion | Mid-upper esophagus | Chemoradiation, Esophagectomy |
