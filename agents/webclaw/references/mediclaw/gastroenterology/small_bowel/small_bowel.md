# Gastroenterology - Small Bowel Disorders

## Celiac Disease
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Pathophysiology | Immune-mediated enteropathy triggered by gluten (wheat, barley, rye) in genetically susceptible individuals (HLA-DQ2/DQ8) |
| Presentation | Diarrhea, Steatorrhea, Weight loss, Bloating, Anemia (iron, folate, B12), Osteoporosis, Dermatitis herpetiformis |
| Diagnosis | **tTG-IgA** (screening); **EMA-IgA** (confirmatory); **EGD with duodenal biopsies** (Marsh classification) |
| Total IgA | Check to exclude IgA deficiency (false negative tTG-IgA) |
| Treatment | **Strict gluten-free diet** for life |
| Monitoring | tTG-IgA at 6-12 months, then annually; Bone density; Vitamin levels |
| Complications | Refractory celiac disease, Enteropathy-associated T-cell lymphoma (EATL), Ulcerative jejunoileitis |

## Celiac Disease - Marsh Classification
| Stage | Histology |
|-------|-----------|
| Marsh 0 | Normal |
| Marsh 1 | Increased intraepithelial lymphocytes (IELs) >25/100 enterocytes |
| Marsh 2 | IELs + Crypt hyperplasia |
| Marsh 3a | Partial villous atrophy |
| Marsh 3b | Subtotal villous atrophy |
| Marsh 3c | Total villous atrophy |
| Marsh 4 | Hypoplastic (rare) |

## Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth (SIBO)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Risk Factors | Motility disorders (scleroderma, diabetes), Anatomic abnormalities (diverticula, strictures), Prior surgery, PPI use |
| Symptoms | Bloating, Diarrhea, Steatorrhea, Weight loss, B12 deficiency |
| Diagnosis | **Glucose or Lactulose breath test** (rise in H2 >20 ppm within 90 min); Duodenal aspirate (>10^3 CFU/mL) |
| Treatment | **Rifaximin** 550 mg TID x 14 days; Treat underlying cause; Prokinetics |
| Recurrence | Common; May require cyclic antibiotics |

## Short Bowel Syndrome
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Definition | Malabsorption due to extensive small bowel resection (<200 cm remaining) |
| Causes | Crohn's disease, Mesenteric ischemia, Trauma, Malignancy |
| Management | **Teduglutide** (GLP-2 analog) stimulates adaptation; Antidiarrheals; PPI; Vitamin/mineral supplementation; Parenteral nutrition |
| Complications | PN-associated liver disease, Catheter infections, Metabolic bone disease |

## Mesenteric Ischemia
| Type | Etiology | Management |
|------|----------|------------|
| Acute Mesenteric Ischemia | Embolus (SMA, 50%), Thrombosis (25%), NOMI (20%) | **Emergent revascularization** (embolectomy, bypass); Anticoagulation |
| Chronic Mesenteric Ischemia | Atherosclerosis of mesenteric vessels | Elective revascularization (stenting or bypass) |
| Colonic Ischemia | Low-flow states, Hypotension | Supportive; Antibiotics; Surgery if necrosis |
| Key Finding | **Pain out of proportion to exam** (acute); Postprandial pain, Weight loss, Food fear (chronic) |

## Small Bowel Tumors
| Tumor | Frequency | Features | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Adenocarcinoma | Most common malignant | Duodenum (periampullary), Jejunum | Surgical resection |
| Carcinoid (NET) | Second most common | Ileum; May cause carcinoid syndrome if liver metastases | Surgery; Somatostatin analogs |
| GIST | KIT/PDGFRA mutations | Submucosal mass; Bleeding, Obstruction | Surgery; Imatinib |
| Lymphoma | MALT, DLBCL | Celiac-associated (EATL) | Chemotherapy |
| Adenoma | Premalignant | Duodenal adenomas (FAP) | Endoscopic resection |

## Microscopic Colitis
| Type | Histology | Treatment |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Lymphocytic Colitis | >20 IELs per 100 epithelial cells | **Budesonide** 9 mg daily x 8 weeks |
| Collagenous Colitis | >10 µm subepithelial collagen band | **Budesonide** 9 mg daily x 8 weeks |
| Presentation | Chronic watery diarrhea, Normal colonoscopy | Stop NSAIDs, PPI; Budesonide; Cholestyramine |
| Incomplete Response | Consider Bismuth subsalicylate, Loperamide, Immunomodulators |

## Whipple's Disease
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Pathogen | **Tropheryma whipplei** (Gram-positive actinomycete) |
| Presentation | Arthralgias, Diarrhea, Weight loss, Abdominal pain, Neurologic symptoms (oculomasticatory myorhythmia) |
| Diagnosis | **PAS-positive macrophages** in duodenal biopsies; PCR |
| Treatment | **Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily x 2 weeks**, then **TMP-SMX DS BID x 1 year** |
| Prognosis | Excellent if treated; Fatal if untreated |
