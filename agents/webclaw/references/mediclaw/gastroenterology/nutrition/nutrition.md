# Gastroenterology - Nutrition

## Nutritional Assessment
| Component | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| Anthropometric | Weight, BMI, Weight loss history, Body composition |
| Biochemical | Albumin (long-term), Prealbumin (short-term), CRP, Micronutrient levels |
| Clinical | Physical exam (muscle wasting, edema, glossitis, skin changes) |
| Dietary | 24-hour recall, Food frequency questionnaire, Calorie count |
| Functional | Handgrip strength, Gait speed |

## Malnutrition - GLIM Criteria
| Criterion | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Phenotypic (1 required) | (1) Weight loss >5% in 6 months or >10% beyond 6 months; (2) Low BMI (<20 if <70 years, <22 if >=70 years); (3) Reduced muscle mass |
| Etiologic (1 required) | (1) Reduced food intake/assimilation; (2) Inflammation or disease burden |
| Diagnosis | At least 1 phenotypic + 1 etiologic criterion |

## Refeeding Syndrome
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Definition | Metabolic disturbances after reintroduction of nutrition in severely malnourished patients |
| Risk Factors | **BMI <16**, **Weight loss >15% in 3-6 months**, **Little/no intake >10 days**, Low K+/Mg2+/PO4 before feeding |
| Pathophysiology | Hypophosphatemia, Hypokalemia, Hypomagnesemia, Thiamine deficiency, Fluid overload |
| Prevention | **Start low, go slow**: 10-15 kcal/kg/day initially; Increase gradually over 4-7 days |
| Monitoring | Daily electrolytes (PO4, K+, Mg2+), Fluid balance, Cardiac monitoring |
| Supplementation | **Thiamine** 100-300 mg IV before feeding; Electrolyte repletion |

## Enteral Nutrition
| Access Route | Indications | Considerations |
|--------------|-------------|----------------|
| Nasogastric (NG) | Short-term (<4-6 weeks), Normal gastric emptying | Easiest; Risk of aspiration |
| Nasoduodenal/Nasojejunal | Gastroparesis, High aspiration risk | Requires endoscopic or fluoroscopic placement |
| PEG (Percutaneous Endoscopic Gastrostomy) | Long-term (>4-6 weeks), Intact GI tract | Avoid if ascites, coagulopathy |
| PEJ (Percutaneous Endoscopic Jejunostomy) | Gastroparesis, High aspiration risk | Technical challenges |
| Surgical Gastrostomy/Jejunostomy | When endoscopic not possible | More invasive |

## Enteral Nutrition Formulas
| Type | Features | Indications |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Standard (Polymeric) | Intact proteins, Complex carbohydrates | Most patients |
| High-Protein | Increased protein (20-25% of calories) | Wound healing, Protein malnutrition |
| Fiber-Containing | Added fiber | Diarrhea, Constipation |
| Semi-Elemental | Hydrolyzed proteins, MCT oil | Malabsorption, Pancreatic insufficiency |
| Elemental | Amino acids, Minimal fat | Severe malabsorption, Crohn's disease |
| Renal | Low protein, Low K+/PO4 | CKD not on dialysis |
| Hepatic | Branched-chain amino acids | Hepatic encephalopathy |

## Parenteral Nutrition (PN)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Indications | Non-functional GI tract, Short bowel syndrome, High-output fistula, Severe ileus, Mesenteric ischemia |
| Central PN | Hypertonic (>900 mOsm/L); Requires central venous access |
| Peripheral PN | Lower osmolarity (<900 mOsm/L); Short-term use |
| Components | Dextrose, Amino acids, Lipid emulsion (20%), Electrolytes, Vitamins, Trace elements |
| Complications | **Catheter-related bloodstream infection (CRBSI)**, **PN-associated liver disease (PNALD)**, Metabolic (hyperglycemia, refeeding), Mechanical (pneumothorax, thrombosis) |
| Monitoring | Daily weight, Electrolytes (daily initially), LFTs weekly, Triglycerides weekly |
| Cyclic PN | Overnight infusion; Improves quality of life, Reduces liver injury |

## Vitamin Deficiencies
| Vitamin | Deficiency | At-Risk Populations | Diagnosis | Treatment |
|---------|------------|---------------------|-----------|-----------|
| Vitamin A | Night blindness, Xerophthalmia, Follicular hyperkeratosis | Fat malabsorption (CF, PBC, short bowel) | Serum retinol | Vitamin A 50,000-200,000 IU |
| Vitamin D | Osteomalacia, Rickets | Limited sun exposure, Fat malabsorption, Obesity | 25(OH)D <20 ng/mL | Cholecalciferol 50,000 IU weekly x 8 weeks |
| Vitamin E | Hemolytic anemia, Neuropathy, Ataxia | Fat malabsorption, Abetalipoproteinemia | Serum alpha-tocopherol | Vitamin E 400-800 IU daily |
| Vitamin K | Coagulopathy (elevated PT/INR) | Fat malabsorption, Broad-spectrum antibiotics | PT/INR | Vitamin K 10 mg IV/PO |
| Vitamin B1 (Thiamine) | **Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome**, Beriberi | Alcoholism, Bariatric surgery, Hyperemesis | Clinical; RBC transketolase | **Thiamine 100-500 mg IV** (before glucose) |
| Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin) | Cheilosis, Glossitis, Seborrheic dermatitis | Malnutrition | Clinical | Riboflavin 5-10 mg daily |
| Vitamin B3 (Niacin) | **Pellagra** (Dermatitis, Diarrhea, Dementia) | Corn-based diet, Alcoholism, Carcinoid | Clinical; Urinary N-methylnicotinamide | Niacinamide 100-500 mg daily |
| Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine) | Seborrheic dermatitis, Glossitis, Neuropathy | Alcoholism, Isoniazid use | Plasma PLP | Pyridoxine 50-100 mg daily |
| Vitamin B9 (Folate) | Macrocytic anemia, Neural tube defects | Alcoholism, Pregnancy, Methotrexate use | Serum/RBC folate | Folic acid 1 mg daily |
| Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin) | **Macrocytic anemia**, **Subacute combined degeneration** | Pernicious anemia, Gastrectomy, Ileal resection, Vegan diet | Serum B12, MMA, Homocysteine | **IM B12** 1000 mcg monthly or high-dose oral |
| Vitamin C | **Scurvy** (Bleeding gums, Perifollicular hemorrhage, Corkscrew hairs) | Elderly, Alcoholism, Restricted diet | Clinical; Leukocyte ascorbic acid | Vitamin C 500-1000 mg daily |

## Trace Element Deficiencies
| Element | Deficiency | At-Risk Populations | Treatment |
|---------|------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Iron | Microcytic anemia, Pica, Koilonychia | Menstruating women, GI bleeding, Celiac disease | Oral iron; IV iron if malabsorption |
| Zinc | Acrodermatitis enteropathica, Delayed wound healing, Dysgeusia | Chronic diarrhea, PN without zinc, Alcoholism | Zinc sulfate 220 mg BID |
| Copper | Anemia, Neutropenia, Myeloneuropathy | Gastric bypass, Zinc toxicity, PN without copper | Copper gluconate 2-4 mg daily |
| Selenium | Cardiomyopathy (Keshan disease), Myopathy | PN without selenium, Severe malnutrition | Selenium 100-200 mcg daily |
| Iodine | Goiter, Hypothyroidism | Iodine-deficient regions | Iodized salt, Levothyroxine |

## Obesity and Bariatric Surgery - Nutritional Considerations
| Procedure | Nutritional Deficiencies | Monitoring |
|-----------|-------------------------|------------|
| Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass (RYGB) | **B12, Iron, Calcium, Vitamin D, Folate, Thiamine, Copper, Zinc** | CBC, Iron panel, B12, Folate, 25(OH)D, PTH, Thiamine, Copper, Zinc q3-6 months x 1 year, then annually |
| Sleeve Gastrectomy (SG) | Vitamin D, B12, Iron, Folate | Same as RYGB (less severe) |
| Biliopancreatic Diversion with Duodenal Switch (BPD-DS) | **Severe deficiencies**: Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K), Calcium, Iron, Zinc, Copper | More frequent monitoring; Lifelong supplementation |
| Supplementation | **Multivitamin** (2 daily for RYGB/BPD-DS), **Calcium citrate** 1200-1500 mg/day, **Vitamin D** 3000 IU/day, **B12** 1000 mcg IM monthly or 1000 mcg PO daily, **Iron** 45-60 mg/day |
