## Bowel Obstruction
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Small Bowel Obstruction (SBO) | NPO, NGT decompression, IVF, Serial abdominal exams, **Water-soluble contrast challenge** |
| Large Bowel Obstruction (LBO) | NPO, IVF, Endoscopic decompression (sigmoid volvulus, colonic stent), Surgery |
| Closed-Loop Obstruction | **Surgical emergency** (risk of strangulation) |
| Signs of Strangulation | Fever, Tachycardia, Peritoneal signs, Acidosis, Lactate elevation |
| Indications for Surgery | Closed-loop obstruction, Strangulation, Failed non-operative management, Perforation |

## Acute Mesenteric Ischemia
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | **Pain out of proportion to exam**, Nausea, Vomiting, Diarrhea (later) |
| Types | Embolic (SMA, 50%), Thrombotic (25%), Non-occlusive (NOMI, 20%), Venous (5%) |
| Diagnosis | **CTA abdomen/pelvis** (gold standard); Elevated lactate (late finding) |
| Management | **Fluid resuscitation**, Broad-spectrum antibiotics, Anticoagulation |
| Embolic/Thrombotic | **Emergent revascularization** (embolectomy, mesenteric bypass, stenting) |
| NOMI | Treat underlying cause, **Papaverine** infusion |
| Venous | Anticoagulation; Surgery if bowel necrosis |

## Toxic Megacolon
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Definition | Colonic dilation >6 cm + Systemic toxicity |
| Causes | **Severe colitis** (IBD, Infectious - C. diff, CMV) |
| Presentation | Abdominal distention, Pain, Fever, Tachycardia, Decreased bowel sounds |
| Diagnosis | Abdominal X-ray or CT (colonic dilation, mucosal islands) |
| Management | **NPO**, **NGT decompression**, **IV fluids**, **IV steroids** (if IBD), **Antibiotics** (if C. diff) |
| Contraindicated | **Anticholinergics, Opioids, Colonoscopy** (risk of perforation) |
| Surgery | **Colectomy** if no improvement in 48-72 hours or signs of perforation |

## Esophageal Perforation (Boerhaave Syndrome)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | **Mackler's triad**: Vomiting, Chest pain, Subcutaneous emphysema |
| Diagnosis | **CT chest with oral contrast**; Esophagram (Gastrografin then barium) |
| Management | **NPO**, **Broad-spectrum antibiotics**, **Antifungals**, **PPI**, IVF |
| Contained Perforation | Non-operative management (NPO, Antibiotics, Drainage) |
| Free Perforation | **Emergent surgical repair** or **Endoscopic stent/clip** |
| Prognosis | Mortality increases with delayed diagnosis (>24 hours) |

## Gastric Volvulus
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Types | Organoaxial (rotation along long axis), Mesenteroaxial (rotation along short axis) |
| Presentation | **Borchardt's triad**: Severe epigastric pain, Retching without vomiting, Inability to pass NGT |
| Diagnosis | CT abdomen or Upper GI series |
| Acute Volvulus | **Emergent EGD for detorsion**; **Surgical gastropexy** |
| Chronic Volvulus | Elective surgical gastropexy |

## Caustic Ingestion
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Agents | **Alkali** (lye, drain cleaners): Liquefactive necrosis, Deep injury; **Acid** (battery acid, toilet cleaners): Coagulative necrosis, Superficial injury |
| Initial Management | **DO NOT induce vomiting**, **DO NOT give neutralizing agents**, **DO NOT place NGT blindly** |
| Airway | Assess for stridor, hoarseness (impending airway compromise) |
| Diagnosis | **EGD within 12-24 hours** (not immediately due to edema) |
| EGD Findings | Grade 1: Erythema/edema; Grade 2a: Superficial ulcers; Grade 2b: Deep ulcers; Grade 3a: Focal necrosis; Grade 3b: Extensive necrosis |
| Management | Grade 1-2a: Supportive; Grade 2b-3b: **ICU**, Steroids controversial, **Antibiotics** if perforation suspected |
| Complications | Stricture formation, Perforation, Fistula, Cancer (long-term) |

## Foreign Body Ingestion
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| High-Risk Objects | **Button batteries** (emergency), **Magnets** (>1 magnet or magnet + metal), Sharp objects, Objects >6 cm (proximal to duodenum), Objects >2.5 cm in children |
| Button Battery | **Emergent removal** (within 2 hours); Damage in 2 hours; Honey or Sucralfate if delay |
| Magnets | **Emergent removal** (risk of pressure necrosis, perforation, fistula) |
| Food Impaction | **EGD within 24 hours** (emergent if unable to handle secretions) |
| Asymptomatic Blunt Objects | Observation; Serial X-rays |
| Endoscopic Removal | High-risk objects, Symptomatic, Failed passage after 3-4 weeks |

## Acute Liver Failure
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Definition | Acute liver injury + Coagulopathy (INR >1.5) + Encephalopathy within 26 weeks; No prior liver disease |
| Causes | **Acetaminophen** (most common in US), Viral hepatitis, AIH, Wilson's disease, Mushroom poisoning, Ischemia, Budd-Chiari |
| Initial Management | **ABCs**, IVF, **N-acetylcysteine** (even if not acetaminophen), Treat underlying cause |
| ICP Management | Head elevation, Mannitol/Hypertonic saline, Hyperventilation (bridge to transplant) |
| Complications | Cerebral edema, Infection, Bleeding, Renal failure |
| Prognosis | **King's College Criteria** (predicts poor prognosis) |
| Treatment | **Liver transplant** for eligible patients |
