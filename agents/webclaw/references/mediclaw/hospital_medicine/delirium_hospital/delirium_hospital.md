## Types of Delirium
| Type | Description | URL |
|------|-------------|-----|
| **Hyperactive** | **Agitation**, **Restlessness**, **Hypervigilance** (least common, most recognized) | https://www.hospitalmedicine.org/clinical-topics/delirium/ |
| **Hypoactive** | **Lethargy**, **Decreased responsiveness**, **Withdrawn** (most common, often missed) | https://www.hospitalmedicine.org/clinical-topics/delirium/ |
| **Mixed** | **Fluctuating** between hyperactive and hypoactive | https://www.hospitalmedicine.org/clinical-topics/delirium/ |

## Delirium Management
| Intervention | Recommendation | URL |
|--------------|----------------|-----|
| **Non-Pharmacologic** | **First-line**: **Reorient**, **Mobilize**, **Hearing/visual aids**, **Sleep hygiene**, **Family presence** | https://www.hospitalmedicine.org/clinical-topics/delirium/ |
| **Pharmacologic** | **Antipsychotics** (haloperidol, quetiapine) for **severe agitation threatening safety** ONLY; **Avoid benzodiazepines** (except withdrawal) | https://www.hospitalmedicine.org/clinical-topics/delirium/ |
