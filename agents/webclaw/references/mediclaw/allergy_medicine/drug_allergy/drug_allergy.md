## Classification of Adverse Drug Reactions
| Type | Mechanism | Timing | Examples | URL |
|------|-----------|--------|----------|-----|
| **Type I (IgE)** | Mast cell degranulation | Minutes-1 hour | Anaphylaxis, Urticaria | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK560631/ |
| **Type II (Cytotoxic)** | IgG/IgM + complement | 5-10 days | Hemolytic anemia, Thrombocytopenia | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK560631/ |
| **Type III (Immune Complex)** | IgG/IgM complexes | 7-14 days | Serum sickness, Vasculitis | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK560631/ |
| **Type IV (Delayed)** | T-cell mediated | 48h-weeks | MPE, DRESS, SJS/TEN, AGEP | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK560631/ |

## Penicillin Allergy
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **False Labeling** | **95% of reported penicillin allergies** are NOT allergic | https://www.cdc.gov/antibiotic-use/clinicians/Penicillin-Allergy.html |
| **Cross-Reactivity** | **<2%** with cephalosporins (mostly side-chain specific) | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/drug-allergy |
| **Evaluation** | Penicillin skin testing (Pre-Pen + Penicillin G + Amoxicillin) | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/drug-allergy |
| **Graded Challenge** | For low-risk patients with negative skin test | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/drug-allergy |
| **Desensitization** | For IgE-mediated allergy when drug essential | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/drug-allergy |

## Severe Cutaneous Adverse Reactions (SCAR)
| Syndrome | Features | Mortality | Culprit Drugs | URL |
|----------|----------|-----------|---------------|-----|
| **DRESS** | Fever, Eosinophilia, Organ involvement | 10% | Anticonvulsants, Allopurinol, Sulfonamides | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK430878/ |
| **SJS/TEN** | Skin detachment (SJS <10%, TEN >30%) | SJS 5%, TEN 30% | Anticonvulsants, Allopurinol, Sulfonamides, NSAIDs | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK430878/ |
| **AGEP** | Pustular eruption, Fever | <5% | Antibiotics (aminopenicillins), Diltiazem | https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK430878/ |
