# Allergy Medicine - Anaphylaxis

## Overview
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | Serious, life-threatening systemic hypersensitivity reaction | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **Incidence** | 50-200 episodes per 100,000 person-years | https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/products/databriefs/db455.htm |
| **Mortality** | 0.5-1% of episodes; 1-2 per 1,000,000 population | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |

## Diagnostic Criteria (WAO/AAAAI)
| Criterion 1 | Criterion 2 | Criterion 3 | URL |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|-----|
| **Acute onset** (minutes-hours) of **skin/mucosal** symptoms AND at least **one** of: **Respiratory** compromise OR **Reduced BP** | **Two or more** after exposure to **likely allergen**: Skin/mucosal, Respiratory, Reduced BP, **Persistent GI** symptoms | **Reduced BP** after exposure to **known allergen** | https://www.worldallergy.org/education-and-programs/education/allergic-disease-resource-center/professionals/anaphylaxis |

## Common Triggers
| Trigger | Examples | URL |
|---------|----------|-----|
| **Food** | Peanut, Tree nuts, Shellfish, Milk, Egg | https://www.foodallergy.org/resources/anaphylaxis |
| **Medications** | Antibiotics (penicillins), NSAIDs, Chemotherapy | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **Insect Stings** | Hymenoptera (bee, wasp, hornet, yellow jacket, fire ant) | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/stinging-insect-allergy |
| **Latex** | Gloves, Catheters, Balloons | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/latex-allergy |
| **Idiopathic** | No identifiable cause (up to 20%) | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |

## Clinical Manifestations
| System | Symptoms | URL |
|--------|----------|-----|
| **Skin/Mucosal (80-90%)** | Urticaria, Angioedema, Flushing, Pruritus | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **Respiratory (70%)** | Dyspnea, Wheeze, Stridor, Hypoxia | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **Cardiovascular (45%)** | Hypotension, Tachycardia, Syncope, Shock | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **GI (45%)** | Nausea, Vomiting, Diarrhea, Abdominal pain | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |

## Emergency Management
| Step | Intervention | Dose | URL |
|------|--------------|------|-----|
| **1. Epinephrine** | **IM vastus lateralis** | 0.01 mg/kg (max 0.5 mg adult, 0.3 mg child) | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **2. Position** | Supine with legs elevated (or semi-recumbent if respiratory distress) | - | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **3. Oxygen** | High-flow O2 | 6-8 L/min | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **4. IV Fluids** | Normal saline bolus | 1-2 L (adult), 10-20 mL/kg (child) | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **5. Adjuncts** | Antihistamines (H1/H2), Corticosteroids, Bronchodilators | See below | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |

## Biphasic Anaphylaxis
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | Recurrence of symptoms without re-exposure to trigger | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **Incidence** | 1-20% of anaphylaxis episodes | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **Timing** | Typically within 8-10 hours (range 1-72 hours) | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **Risk Factors** | Severe initial reaction, Delayed epinephrine, Unknown trigger | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
| **Observation Period** | 4-8 hours (longer if severe, reactive airway disease, or remote from care) | https://www.aaaai.org/conditions-treatments/allergies/anaphylaxis |
