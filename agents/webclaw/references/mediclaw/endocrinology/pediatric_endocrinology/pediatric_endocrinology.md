# Endocrinology - Pediatric Endocrinology

## Growth and Development
| Parameter | Assessment | Abnormal |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| Height | Growth chart (CDC/WHO) | <3rd percentile, >95th percentile, Crossing percentiles |
| Growth Velocity | cm/year | <5 cm/year (age 4-puberty) |
| Bone Age | X-ray of left hand/wrist | Delayed: Constitutional delay, Hypothyroidism, GH deficiency; Advanced: Precocious puberty |
| Mid-Parental Height | (Father + Mother ± 13 cm)/2 | Compare to child's predicted height |

## Short Stature - Differential
| Cause | Features | Bone Age | Growth Velocity | IGF-1 | Treatment |
|-------|----------|----------|-----------------|-------|-----------|
| Familial Short Stature | Parents short | Normal | Normal | Normal | Reassurance |
| Constitutional Delay | Delayed puberty, Family history | Delayed | Normal | Normal/Low | Reassurance; Consider testosterone (boys) |
| GH Deficiency | Slow growth, Hypoglycemia | Delayed | Low | Low | **GH replacement** |
| Hypothyroidism | Fatigue, Constipation, Cold intolerance | Delayed | Low | Low | Levothyroxine |
| Celiac Disease | Abdominal pain, Diarrhea | Delayed | Variable | Normal | Gluten-free diet |
| Turner Syndrome | Webbed neck, Shield chest, Lymphedema | Delayed | Low | Normal | GH + Estrogen replacement |
| Skeletal Dysplasia | Disproportionate short stature | Normal | Normal | Normal | Supportive |

## Growth Hormone Deficiency
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Diagnosis | Low IGF-1, IGFBP-3; Abnormal GH stimulation test (peak <10 ng/mL) |
| Workup | MRI pituitary (rule out tumor, structural defects) |
| Treatment | **Recombinant GH** 0.16-0.24 mg/kg/week divided daily |
| Monitoring | Growth velocity, IGF-1, Bone age, Thyroid function |
| Duration | Until growth plates fuse (bone age 14-15 in girls, 16-17 in boys) |

## Precocious Puberty
| Type | Definition | Causes | Treatment |
|------|------------|--------|-----------|
| Central (GnRH-dependent) | Breast development <8 years (girls), Testicular enlargement <9 years (boys) | Idiopathic (girls), CNS lesions (boys) | **GnRH agonist** (Leuprolide) |
| Peripheral (GnRH-independent) | Signs of puberty without GnRH activation | McCune-Albright, CAH, Ovarian/Testicular tumors | Treat underlying cause; Aromatase inhibitors |

## Delayed Puberty
| Definition | Girls: No breast development by age 13 or no menses by age 15; Boys: No testicular enlargement by age 14 |
|------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Causes | Constitutional delay (most common), Hypogonadotropic hypogonadism (Kallmann), Hypergonadotropic hypogonadism (Turner, Klinefelter), Chronic illness |
| Workup | LH, FSH, Estradiol/Testosterone, Bone age, Karyotype, Prolactin, MRI |
| Treatment | Constitutional delay: Reassurance or short course of sex steroids; Hypogonadism: Hormone replacement |

## Congenital Hypothyroidism
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Newborn Screening | TSH or T4 measured on heel stick at 24-48 hours of life |
| Diagnosis | Confirm with serum TSH and free T4 |
| Treatment | **Levothyroxine 10-15 mcg/kg/day** (start immediately) |
| Monitoring | TSH and T4 every 1-2 weeks initially, then monthly |
| Goal | Normalize T4 in 2 weeks, TSH in 1 month |

## Disorders of Sexual Development (DSD)
| Type | Karyotype | Gonads | Presentation | Management |
|------|-----------|--------|--------------|------------|
| 46,XX DSD | 46,XX | Ovaries | Ambiguous genitalia (CAH most common) | Treat CAH; Surgical reconstruction |
| 46,XY DSD | 46,XY | Testes | Undervirilization (Androgen insensitivity, 5-alpha reductase deficiency) | Hormone replacement; Gonadectomy if risk of malignancy |
| Sex Chromosome DSD | 45,X/46,XY, 46,XX/46,XY | Mixed | Turner, Klinefelter, Mixed gonadal dysgenesis | Hormone replacement; Gonadectomy |
