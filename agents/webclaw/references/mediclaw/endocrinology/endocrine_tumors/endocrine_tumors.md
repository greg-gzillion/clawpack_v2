# Endocrinology - Endocrine Tumors

## Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia (MEN) Syndromes
| Syndrome | Gene | Tumors | Screening | Prophylactic Surgery |
|----------|------|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| MEN1 | MEN1 (11q13) | Parathyroid (95%), Pancreatic NET (50%), Pituitary (40%) | Annual Ca, PTH, PRL, IGF-1, Gastrin, Insulin, MRI | No |
| MEN2A | RET (10q11) | Medullary thyroid cancer (100%), Pheochromocytoma (50%), Parathyroid (20%) | Annual calcitonin, Metanephrines, Ca | **Prophylactic thyroidectomy** |
| MEN2B | RET (10q11) | Medullary thyroid cancer (100%), Pheochromocytoma (50%), Mucosal neuromas, Marfanoid habitus | Annual calcitonin, Metanephrines | **Prophylactic thyroidectomy in infancy** |
| MEN4 | CDKN1B | Similar to MEN1 | Similar to MEN1 | No |

## Neuroendocrine Tumors (NETs) - Overview
| Tumor | Hormone | Clinical Syndrome | Diagnostic Marker | Treatment |
|-------|---------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Insulinoma | Insulin | Hypoglycemia (Whipple's triad) | Insulin, C-peptide, Proinsulin | Surgery; Diazoxide |
| Gastrinoma | Gastrin | Zollinger-Ellison (PUD, Diarrhea) | Fasting gastrin, Secretin stimulation | PPI; Surgery |
| Glucagonoma | Glucagon | Necrolytic migratory erythema, DM, DVT | Glucagon | Surgery; Octreotide |
| VIPoma | VIP | WDHA (Watery Diarrhea, Hypokalemia, Achlorhydria) | VIP | Surgery; Octreotide |
| Somatostatinoma | Somatostatin | DM, Gallstones, Steatorrhea | Somatostatin | Surgery |
| Carcinoid | Serotonin | Flushing, Diarrhea, Bronchospasm, Fibrosis | 5-HIAA (urine), Chromogranin A | Surgery; Octreotide |
| Non-Functioning NET | None | Mass effects, Metastases | Chromogranin A | Surgery; Everolimus, Sunitinib |

## Carcinoid Syndrome
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Flushing (70%), Diarrhea (70%), Abdominal pain, Bronchospasm, Right-sided heart failure (fibrosis) |
| Triggers | Alcohol, Stress, Exercise, Certain foods |
| Diagnosis | 24h urine 5-HIAA (elevated); Chromogranin A |
| Treatment | **Octreotide** (somatostatin analog); Surgery (if localized); Telotristat (for refractory diarrhea) |
| Carcinoid Crisis | **Octreotide 100-500 mcg IV bolus** pre-procedure |

## Insulinoma
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Fasting hypoglycemia; Whipple's triad (symptoms, low glucose, relief with glucose) |
| Diagnosis | Supervised 72-hour fast: Glucose <55 mg/dL, Insulin >=3 uU/mL, C-peptide >=0.6 ng/mL |
| Localization | CT/MRI, Endoscopic US, Calcium stimulation test |
| Treatment | Surgical resection; **Diazoxide** (medical management if unresectable) |

## Adrenocortical Carcinoma (ACC)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Hormone excess (Cushing's, Virilization) or mass effects |
| Diagnosis | CT (large, heterogeneous, irregular margins); Biopsy if needed |
| Treatment | Complete surgical resection; **Mitotane** (adjuvant or metastatic); EDP chemotherapy |
| Prognosis | Poor (5-year survival 30-40% for localized, <15% for metastatic) |
