# Endocrinology - Neuroendocrinology

## Hypothalamic-Pituitary Axis
| Hypothalamic Hormone | Pituitary Hormone | Target Gland | Peripheral Hormone |
|----------------------|-------------------|--------------|---------------------|
| TRH | TSH | Thyroid | T4, T3 |
| CRH | ACTH | Adrenal Cortex | Cortisol |
| GnRH | LH, FSH | Gonads | Testosterone, Estrogen |
| GHRH | GH | Liver, Bone | IGF-1 |
| Somatostatin | Inhibits GH, TSH | - | - |
| Dopamine | Inhibits Prolactin | Breast | - |

## Craniopharyngioma
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Origin | Rathke's pouch remnant (sellar/suprasellar) |
| Presentation | Headache, Visual field defects, Hypopituitarism, Diabetes insipidus |
| Diagnosis | MRI (cystic/solid mass, calcifications) |
| Treatment | Surgery (often subtotal); Radiation; Hormone replacement |
| Prognosis | High survival; Significant morbidity (panhypopituitarism, obesity) |

## Empty Sella Syndrome
| Type | Description | Management |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Primary | Defect in diaphragm sella; CSF fills sella | Usually incidental; Rarely causes hypopituitarism |
| Secondary | Post-surgery, Radiation, Apoplexy | Monitor for hypopituitarism; Hormone replacement if needed |

## Lymphocytic Hypophysitis
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Often peripartum; Headache, Visual changes, Hypopituitarism |
| Diagnosis | MRI (pituitary enlargement, stalk thickening) |
| Treatment | High-dose steroids (if vision threatened); Hormone replacement; Usually self-limited |

## Pineal Gland Disorders
| Condition | Hormone | Clinical Features | Treatment |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|-----------|
| Pineal Tumors | Melatonin, Neurotransmitters | Parinaud syndrome, Hydrocephalus, Precocious puberty | Surgery, Radiation |
| Pineal Cysts | - | Usually incidental | Observation |

## Melatonin and Circadian Rhythm Disorders
| Disorder | Features | Treatment |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Delayed Sleep Phase Syndrome | Late sleep onset/wake | Melatonin 0.5-3 mg in evening; Light therapy |
| Advanced Sleep Phase Syndrome | Early sleep onset/wake | Light therapy in evening |
| Jet Lag | Desynchronization | Melatonin at destination bedtime |
| Shift Work Disorder | Sleep disruption with night shifts | Melatonin before daytime sleep; Light exposure at work |

## Hypothalamic Obesity
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Causes | Craniopharyngioma, Hypothalamic tumors, Trauma, Radiation |
| Pathophysiology | Disruption of satiety centers, Hyperphagia, Low sympathetic tone |
| Treatment | Lifestyle modification; GLP-1 agonists; Setmelanotide (MC4R agonist); Bariatric surgery |
