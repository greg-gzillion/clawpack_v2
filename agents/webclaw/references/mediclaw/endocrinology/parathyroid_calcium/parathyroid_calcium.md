# Endocrinology - Parathyroid and Calcium Disorders

## Calcium Homeostasis
| Hormone | Source | Effect on Calcium | Effect on Phosphate | Mechanism |
|---------|--------|------------------|---------------------|-----------|
| PTH | Parathyroid | Increases | Decreases | Bone resorption, Renal reabsorption, 1,25(OH)2D synthesis |
| 1,25(OH)2D (Calcitriol) | Kidney (via 1-alpha-hydroxylase) | Increases | Increases | Intestinal absorption, Bone resorption |
| Calcitonin | Thyroid C-cells | Decreases | Decreases | Inhibits bone resorption |
| FGF23 | Bone (osteocytes) | Decreases | Decreases | Inhibits 1-alpha-hydroxylase, Increases phosphate excretion |

## Normal Laboratory Values
| Test | Normal Range |
|------|--------------|
| Total Calcium | 8.5-10.5 mg/dL |
| Ionized Calcium | 4.5-5.6 mg/dL (1.12-1.4 mmol/L) |
| Corrected Calcium | Total Ca + 0.8 x (4 - Albumin) |
| Phosphate | 2.5-4.5 mg/dL |
| Magnesium | 1.7-2.2 mg/dL |
| PTH | 10-65 pg/mL |
| 25(OH)Vitamin D | 30-100 ng/mL (sufficient) |
| 1,25(OH)2Vitamin D | 20-80 pg/mL |

## Hypercalcemia
| Severity | Calcium Level | Management |
|----------|---------------|------------|
| Mild | 10.5-12 mg/dL | Oral hydration; Treat underlying cause |
| Moderate | 12-14 mg/dL | IV fluids; Consider bisphosphonates |
| Severe | >14 mg/dL or Symptomatic | **Aggressive IVF**; **Calcitonin**; **Bisphosphonates**; Dialysis if refractory |

## Differential Diagnosis of Hypercalcemia
| Cause | PTH | Phosphate | 1,25(OH)2D | Urine Calcium |
|-------|-----|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Primary Hyperparathyroidism | High | Low | High/Normal | High |
| Malignancy (Humoral) | Low | Low | Low | High |
| Malignancy (Local osteolytic) | Low | Normal | Low | High |
| Granulomatous Disease | Low | High | High | High |
| Vitamin D Intoxication | Low | High | Normal | High |
| Milk-Alkali Syndrome | Low | High | Low | Low |
| Familial Hypocalciuric Hypercalcemia | High/Normal | Low/Normal | Normal | **Low (<100 mg/24h)** |
| Thiazide Diuretics | Low | Low | Normal | Low |
| Immobilization | Low | High | Low | High |

## Primary Hyperparathyroidism
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | "Bones, Stones, Groans, Psychiatric Overtones" (Osteoporosis, Kidney stones, Constipation, Depression) |
| Diagnosis | Hypercalcemia + Elevated or inappropriately normal PTH |
| Localization | Sestamibi scan, Neck ultrasound, 4D-CT |
| Surgical Indications | Age <50, Ca >1 mg/dL above normal, Osteoporosis, eGFR <60, Kidney stones |
| Surgery | Minimally invasive parathyroidectomy (MIP) or Bilateral neck exploration |
| Medical Management | Cinacalcet (lowers Ca, no effect on bone); Bisphosphonates (bone protection) |

## Hypocalcemia
| Severity | Calcium Level | Management |
|----------|---------------|------------|
| Mild | 7.5-8.5 mg/dL | Oral calcium; Vitamin D |
| Moderate | 7.0-7.5 mg/dL | IV calcium if symptomatic |
| Severe | <7.0 mg/dL or Symptomatic | **IV Calcium Gluconate** (1-2g over 10-20 min); Continuous infusion |

## Differential Diagnosis of Hypocalcemia
| Cause | PTH | Phosphate | 25(OH)D | Magnesium |
|-------|-----|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Hypoparathyroidism | Low | High | Normal | Normal |
| Pseudohypoparathyroidism | High | High | Normal | Normal |
| Vitamin D Deficiency | High | Low/Normal | Low | Normal |
| CKD | High | High | Low/Normal | Normal/Variable |
| Magnesium Deficiency | Low/Inappropriately normal | Normal/High | Normal | **Low** |
| Hungry Bone Syndrome | Low/Normal | Low | Normal | Normal/Low |
| Acute Pancreatitis | Low/Normal | Normal | Normal | Normal/Low |

## Hypoparathyroidism
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Causes | Post-surgical (most common), Autoimmune, Genetic (DiGeorge), Infiltrative |
| Diagnosis | Hypocalcemia + Hyperphosphatemia + Low/Inappropriately normal PTH |
| Treatment | Calcium carbonate (1-3g daily) + **Calcitriol** (0.25-1 mcg daily) |
| Goal | Calcium in low-normal range (avoid hypercalciuria and stones) |
| PTH Replacement | Recombinant PTH (Natpara) - restricted access program |

## Pseudohypoparathyroidism
| Type | Features | PTH | Calcium | Phosphate |
|------|----------|-----|---------|-----------|
| Type 1a | Albright hereditary osteodystrophy (AHO), Resistance to multiple hormones | High | Low | High |
| Type 1b | Renal resistance only, No AHO | High | Low | High |
| Type 2 | Normal cAMP response but defective phosphaturia | High | Low | High |

## Vitamin D Deficiency
| Severity | 25(OH)D Level | Management |
|----------|---------------|------------|
| Severe Deficiency | <10 ng/mL | Ergocalciferol 50,000 IU weekly x 8 weeks |
| Deficiency | 10-20 ng/mL | Ergocalciferol 50,000 IU weekly x 8 weeks or Cholecalciferol 2000-4000 IU daily |
| Insufficiency | 20-30 ng/mL | Cholecalciferol 800-2000 IU daily |
| Sufficiency | >30 ng/mL | Maintenance 600-800 IU daily |

## Paget's Disease of Bone
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Bone pain, Deformity, Fractures, Hearing loss, High-output heart failure |
| Diagnosis | Elevated Alkaline Phosphatase (bone-specific); X-ray (osteolytic lesions, thickened bone) |
| Treatment | **Bisphosphonates** (Zoledronic acid 5 mg IV once); Calcitonin (if bisphosphonate contraindicated) |
| Monitoring | Alkaline phosphatase every 3-6 months |
