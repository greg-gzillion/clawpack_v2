# Endocrinology - Pituitary Disorders

## Pituitary Anatomy and Physiology
| Lobe | Hormones | Cell Type | Percentage |
|------|----------|-----------|------------|
| Anterior (Adenohypophysis) | GH, PRL, ACTH, TSH, LH, FSH | Various | 80% |
| Posterior (Neurohypophysis) | ADH, Oxytocin | Axon terminals | 20% |
| Intermediate | MSH | Melanotrophs | Minimal in adults |

## Anterior Pituitary Hormones
| Hormone | Target | Function | Stimulation | Inhibition |
|---------|--------|----------|-------------|------------|
| GH | Liver, Bone, Muscle | Growth, IGF-1 production | GHRH, Ghrelin | Somatostatin |
| Prolactin | Breast | Lactation | TRH, Estrogen | Dopamine |
| TSH | Thyroid | T4/T3 synthesis | TRH | T4/T3 |
| ACTH | Adrenal cortex | Cortisol synthesis | CRH | Cortisol |
| LH | Gonads | Sex steroids, Ovulation | GnRH | Sex steroids |
| FSH | Gonads | Follicle development, Spermatogenesis | GnRH | Inhibin, Sex steroids |

## Pituitary Adenomas
| Type | Prevalence | Hormone | Presentation | Treatment |
|------|------------|---------|--------------|-----------|
| Prolactinoma | 40-50% | PRL | Galactorrhea, Amenorrhea, Hypogonadism | Dopamine agonists (Cabergoline) |
| Non-functioning | 30% | None | Mass effects, Hypopituitarism | Surgery if symptomatic |
| GH-secreting | 10-15% | GH | Acromegaly (adults), Gigantism (children) | Surgery, Octreotide, Pegvisomant |
| ACTH-secreting | 5-10% | ACTH | Cushing's disease | Surgery, Ketoconazole, Pasireotide |
| TSH-secreting | <1% | TSH | Hyperthyroidism | Surgery, Octreotide |
| Gonadotroph | 10% | FSH/LH | Usually non-functioning | Surgery if symptomatic |

## Prolactinoma Management
| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| Microprolactinoma (<10 mm) | Cabergoline 0.25-0.5 mg weekly; Monitor PRL |
| Macroprolactinoma (>10 mm) | Cabergoline 0.5-1 mg weekly; Visual field testing; MRI |
| Pregnancy | Discontinue dopamine agonist; Monitor for expansion |
| Resistant | Increase dose; Consider surgery or radiation |

## Acromegaly
| Component | Findings | Management |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| Clinical Features | Enlarged hands/feet, Coarse facies, Prognathism, Macroglossia, Carpal tunnel, HTN, Cardiomyopathy |
| Diagnosis | Elevated IGF-1; GH >1 ng/mL after 75g glucose load |
| Treatment | Transsphenoidal surgery; Somatostatin analogs (Octreotide, Lanreotide); GH antagonist (Pegvisomant) |
| Monitoring | IGF-1; MRI; Colonoscopy (increased CRC risk) |

## Cushing's Disease
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Diagnosis | Elevated 24h urine cortisol; Abnormal dexamethasone suppression; Elevated late-night salivary cortisol |
| ACTH Level | Elevated or "inappropriately normal" ACTH (pituitary source) |
| Localization | Pituitary MRI; IPSS (Inferior Petrosal Sinus Sampling) |
| Treatment | Transsphenoidal surgery; Ketoconazole, Metyrapone, Pasireotide; Bilateral adrenalectomy (last resort) |

## Hypopituitarism
| Deficiency | Diagnosis | Replacement |
|------------|-----------|-------------|
| ACTH | Low AM cortisol; Abnormal ACTH stim test | Hydrocortisone 15-25 mg/day divided |
| TSH | Low free T4; Inappropriately normal/low TSH | Levothyroxine (lower dose than primary) |
| LH/FSH | Low testosterone (men); Amenorrhea (women) | Testosterone; Estrogen/Progesterone |
| GH | Low IGF-1; Abnormal GH stimulation test | GH replacement |
| ADH | Diabetes insipidus (polyuria, hypernatremia) | Desmopressin (DDAVP) |

## Diabetes Insipidus (DI)
| Type | Mechanism | Diagnosis | Treatment |
|------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Central DI | Deficient ADH | Hypernatremia, Polyuria, Water deprivation test | Desmopressin (DDAVP) |
| Nephrogenic DI | Renal resistance to ADH | Same as central; No response to DDAVP | Thiazides, Low-solute diet |
| Primary Polydipsia | Excessive water intake | Low-normal sodium; Responds to water restriction | Behavioral modification |

## SIADH (Syndrome of Inappropriate Antidiuretic Hormone)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Diagnosis | Hyponatremia; Urine osmolality >100; Euvolemia; Normal adrenal/thyroid function |
| Causes | CNS disorders, Pulmonary disease, Medications, Malignancy (SCLC) |
| Treatment | Fluid restriction; Salt tablets; Vaptans (Tolvaptan); Treat underlying cause |
| Correction Rate | <8 mEq/L per 24 hours (risk of osmotic demyelination) |

## Pituitary Apoplexy
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Sudden severe headache, Visual loss, Oculomotor palsy, Hypopituitarism |
| Diagnosis | Pituitary MRI showing hemorrhage/infarction |
| Treatment | **High-dose steroids** (Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV); Neurosurgery consultation |
| Surgery Indications | Visual deterioration, Altered mental status |
