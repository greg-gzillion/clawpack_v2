# Endocrinology - Endocrine Emergencies

## Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Diagnostic Criteria | Glucose >250, pH <7.3, HCO3 <18, Ketones positive, Anion gap >12 |
| Fluids | 0.9% NaCl 15-20 mL/kg/hr first hour; Then 250-500 mL/hr |
| Insulin | Regular insulin 0.1 units/kg bolus; Then 0.1 units/kg/hr infusion |
| Potassium | If <3.3: Hold insulin, Replete K+; If 3.3-5.3: Add 20-30 mEq/L; If >5.3: No K+ |
| Bicarbonate | Only if pH <6.9 |
| Transition | When glucose <200 AND anion gap closed AND patient eating |

## Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Diagnostic Criteria | Glucose >600, Osmolality >320, pH >7.3, Ketones negative/low |
| Fluids | **More aggressive than DKA**; 6-9 L deficit typical |
| Insulin | Lower doses (0.05 units/kg/hr) |
| Electrolytes | Monitor K+ closely (risk of severe hypokalemia) |
| Thrombosis | Consider prophylactic anticoagulation |

## Hypoglycemia (Severe)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Altered mental status, Seizures, Coma |
| Diagnosis | Glucose <50 mg/dL (often <40 mg/dL) |
| Treatment | **D50 1 amp (25g) IV push**; OR **Glucagon 1 mg IM/IN** |
| Sulfonylurea-Induced | **Octreotide 50-100 mcg SC q6-12h**; Admit for observation |
| Refractory | Continuous D10 or D20 infusion; Consider hydrocortisone |

## Adrenal Crisis
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Hypotension (refractory to fluids), Hyponatremia, Hyperkalemia, Hypoglycemia, Fever, AMS |
| Diagnosis | Clinical (do NOT wait for labs); Random cortisol <15 mcg/dL confirms |
| Treatment | **Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV bolus**; Then 50 mg q6h or 100 mg q8h |
| Fluids | **Aggressive IVF** (D5NS preferred for hypoglycemia risk) |
| Precipitants | Infection, Surgery, Trauma, Steroid withdrawal |

## Thyroid Storm
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Fever, Tachycardia, CHF, AMS, GI symptoms (N/V/D) |
| Diagnosis | Burch-Wartofsky Score >45; Low TSH, High T4 |
| Treatment | **PTU** 500-1000 mg load then 250 mg q4h (blocks synthesis + T4->T3); **Iodine** (SSKI 5 drops q6h, 1 hr after PTU); **Propranolol** 1-2 mg IV q15min; **Hydrocortisone** 100 mg IV q8h |
| Supportive | Cooling, IVF, Treat precipitant |

## Myxedema Coma
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Hypothermia, Bradycardia, Hypotension, AMS, Coma |
| Diagnosis | High TSH, Low T4 |
| Treatment | **Levothyroxine IV** 200-400 mcg load then 50-100 mcg daily; **Hydrocortisone** 100 mg IV q8h (until adrenal insufficiency ruled out) |
| Supportive | Passive rewarming, IVF cautiously (risk of CHF) |
| Mortality | 30-60% despite treatment |

## Pituitary Apoplexy
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Sudden severe headache, Visual loss, Oculomotor palsy, Hypopituitarism |
| Diagnosis | Pituitary MRI (hemorrhage/infarction) |
| Treatment | **Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV** (do NOT wait for labs); Neurosurgery consult |
| Surgery Indications | Visual deterioration, Altered mental status |

## Hypercalcemic Crisis
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Severe hypercalcemia (>14 mg/dL) or symptomatic: AMS, Renal failure, ECG changes |
| Treatment | **Aggressive IVF** (200-300 mL/hr); **Calcitonin** 4 units/kg IM/SC q12h (rapid onset); **Bisphosphonates** (Zoledronic acid 4 mg IV or Pamidronate 60-90 mg IV) |
| Refractory | Dialysis; Denosumab |
| Monitoring | ECG (short QT), Renal function |

## Hypocalcemic Crisis
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Tetany, Paresthesias, Laryngospasm, Seizures, Prolonged QT |
| Diagnosis | Calcium <7.0 mg/dL or symptomatic |
| Treatment | **Calcium Gluconate 1-2g IV over 10-20 min**; Then continuous infusion (1-2 mg/kg/hr) |
| Monitoring | ECG (prolonged QT), Frequent calcium checks |
| Refractory | Check **Magnesium** (replete if low) |

## Pheochromocytoma Crisis
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Severe hypertension, Tachycardia, Headache, Diaphoresis |
| Treatment | **Phentolamine** 2.5-5 mg IV q5min; OR **Nicardipine** infusion; **Esmolol** (only after alpha-blockade) |
| Avoid | Beta-blockers before alpha-blockade (unopposed alpha effect) |
