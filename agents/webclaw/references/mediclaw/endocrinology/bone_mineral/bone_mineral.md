# Endocrinology - Bone and Mineral Disorders

## Osteoporosis - Diagnostic Criteria (WHO)
| Category | T-Score (BMD) |
|----------|---------------|
| Normal | >= -1.0 |
| Osteopenia | -1.0 to -2.4 |
| Osteoporosis | <= -2.5 |
| Severe Osteoporosis | <= -2.5 + Fragility fracture |

## Osteoporosis - Screening Indications
| Population | Recommendation |
|------------|----------------|
| Women >=65 | Routine DXA screening |
| Women <65 | If FRAX 10-year risk >=8.4% (or equivalent) |
| Men >=70 | Routine DXA screening |
| Men 50-69 | If risk factors present |
| Any Adult | After fragility fracture, Chronic steroid use, High-risk conditions |

## FRAX Tool Components
| Risk Factor | Points |
|-------------|--------|
| Age | Major contributor |
| Prior fragility fracture | Significant risk |
| Parental hip fracture | Genetic component |
| Current smoking | Modifiable |
| Glucocorticoid use | Dose-dependent |
| Rheumatoid arthritis | Secondary cause |
| Secondary osteoporosis | Various conditions |
| Alcohol >=3 units/day | Modifiable |
| Low BMI | <21 kg/m2 |

## Osteoporosis - Pharmacologic Treatment
| Drug Class | Examples | Mechanism | Fracture Reduction | Considerations |
|------------|----------|-----------|---------------------|----------------|
| Bisphosphonates (Oral) | Alendronate, Risedronate | Inhibit osteoclasts | 40-50% vertebral, 20-30% hip | Take on empty stomach, Remain upright 30-60 min |
| Bisphosphonates (IV) | Zoledronic acid | Inhibit osteoclasts | 40-50% vertebral, 30-40% hip | Flu-like symptoms, Acute phase reaction |
| Denosumab | Prolia | RANKL inhibitor | 50-60% vertebral, 40% hip | **Do not stop abruptly** (rebound fractures) |
| SERMs | Raloxifene | Estrogen agonist/antagonist | 30% vertebral, No hip benefit | Reduces breast cancer risk; Hot flashes |
| Teriparatide | Forteo | PTH analog (anabolic) | 65% vertebral, 50% non-vertebral | Daily injection; 2-year limit |
| Abaloparatide | Tymlos | PTHrP analog | 86% vertebral, 43% non-vertebral | Daily injection; 2-year limit |
| Romosozumab | Evenity | Anti-sclerostin | 73% vertebral, 38% hip | **Black box: CV risk**; 1-year course |

## Bisphosphonate Holiday
| Risk Level | Duration of Therapy | Holiday Duration |
|------------|---------------------|------------------|
| Low Risk | 3-5 years | Consider 2-3 year holiday |
| Moderate Risk | 5 years | Consider 1-2 year holiday |
| High Risk | 6-10 years | No holiday or short (1 year) |

## Atypical Femur Fracture (AFF) and ONJ
| Complication | Risk Factors | Management |
|--------------|--------------|------------|
| Atypical Femur Fracture | Long-term bisphosphonate use (>5 years), Asian race, Glucocorticoids | Stop bisphosphonate; Teriparatide; Surgical fixation |
| Osteonecrosis of Jaw (ONJ) | Dental procedures, Poor oral hygiene, Long-term bisphosphonates | Dental clearance before starting; Avoid invasive dental work |

## Osteomalacia and Rickets
| Cause | Labs | Treatment |
|-------|------|-----------|
| Vitamin D Deficiency | Low 25(OH)D, Low/Normal Ca, High PTH, High ALP | Vitamin D supplementation |
| Hypophosphatemic Rickets | Low Phosphate, High/Normal PTH, Low/Normal 25(OH)D | Phosphate + Calcitriol |
| CKD-MBD | High Phosphate, High PTH, Low 1,25(OH)2D | Phosphate binders, Calcitriol, Calcimimetics |
| Tumor-Induced Osteomalacia | Low Phosphate, High FGF23 | Resection of tumor; Phosphate + Calcitriol |

## Paget's Disease of Bone
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Bone pain, Deformity, Hearing loss, High-output heart failure, Pathologic fracture |
| Diagnosis | Elevated Alkaline Phosphatase (bone-specific); X-ray findings |
| Indications for Treatment | Symptoms, Alk Phos >2-4x ULN, Weight-bearing bone involvement, Skull involvement |
| Treatment | **Zoledronic acid 5 mg IV once** (preferred); Risedronate 30 mg daily x 2 months |
| Monitoring | Alkaline phosphatase every 6-12 months |
