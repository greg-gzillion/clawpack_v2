# Endocrinology - Diabetes and Metabolism

## Diabetes Mellitus - Diagnostic Criteria
| Test | Normal | Prediabetes | Diabetes |
|------|--------|-------------|----------|
| Fasting Plasma Glucose | <100 mg/dL | 100-125 mg/dL (IFG) | >=126 mg/dL |
| 2-hour OGTT | <140 mg/dL | 140-199 mg/dL (IGT) | >=200 mg/dL |
| HbA1c | <5.7% | 5.7-6.4% | >=6.5% |
| Random Glucose | - | - | >=200 mg/dL + symptoms |

## Type 1 vs Type 2 Diabetes
| Feature | Type 1 | Type 2 |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Pathophysiology | Autoimmune beta-cell destruction | Insulin resistance + relative deficiency |
| Age of Onset | Typically childhood/young adult | Typically adult (increasing in youth) |
| Body Habitus | Usually lean | Often overweight/obese |
| Ketosis Prone | Yes | Rare (but possible in stress) |
| Autoantibodies | GAD65, IA-2, ZnT8, Insulin | Absent |
| C-peptide | Low/Absent | Normal/High (initially) |
| Treatment | **Insulin required** | Lifestyle, Oral agents, GLP-1, Insulin |

## Glycemic Targets
| Population | HbA1c Goal | Fasting Glucose | Postprandial Glucose |
|------------|------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Most Non-Pregnant Adults | <7.0% | 80-130 mg/dL | <180 mg/dL |
| Older Adults (Healthy) | <7.5% | 90-130 mg/dL | <180 mg/dL |
| Older Adults (Complex/Unhealthy) | <8.0-8.5% | 100-180 mg/dL | <200-250 mg/dL |
| Pregnancy | <6.0-6.5% | <95 mg/dL | <120-140 mg/dL |

## Insulin Formulations
| Type | Onset | Peak | Duration | Examples |
|------|-------|------|----------|----------|
| Rapid-Acting | 10-20 min | 1-2 hrs | 3-5 hrs | Lispro, Aspart, Glulisine |
| Short-Acting | 30-60 min | 2-4 hrs | 5-8 hrs | Regular |
| Intermediate-Acting | 1-2 hrs | 4-8 hrs | 12-18 hrs | NPH |

| Ultra-Long-Acting | 1-2 hrs | No peak | >42 hrs | Degludec U200 |

## Insulin Dosing
| Component | Calculation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Total Daily Dose (TDD) | 0.5-0.7 units/kg (type 1); 0.5-1.5 units/kg (type 2) |
| Basal Insulin | 40-50% of TDD |
| Bolus Insulin | 50-60% of TDD divided pre-meals |
| Correction Factor | 1800 / TDD = mg/dL drop per 1 unit |
| Insulin:Carb Ratio | 500 / TDD = grams carb per 1 unit |

## Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Diagnostic Criteria | Glucose >250 mg/dL, pH <7.3, HCO3 <18, Ketones positive |
| Fluids | 0.9% NaCl 15-20 mL/kg/hr first hour; Then 250-500 mL/hr |
| Insulin | Regular insulin 0.1 units/kg bolus; Then 0.1 units/kg/hr infusion |
| Potassium | If K+ <3.3, hold insulin and replete; If 3.3-5.3, add 20-30 mEq/L |
| Bicarbonate | Only if pH <6.9 |
| Transition to SC | When glucose <200 AND anion gap closed AND patient eating |

## Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Diagnostic Criteria | Glucose >600 mg/dL, Osmolality >320 mOsm/kg, pH >7.3, Ketones negative/low |
| Fluids | **More aggressive than DKA**; 6-9 L deficit typical |
| Insulin | Lower doses than DKA (0.05 units/kg/hr) |
| Electrolytes | Monitor K+ closely (risk of severe hypokalemia) |
| Complications | Thrombosis (consider anticoagulation), Cerebral edema |

## Hypoglycemia
| Severity | Glucose | Management |
|----------|---------|------------|
| Mild (Alert) | <70 mg/dL | 15g fast-acting carbs; Recheck in 15 min |
| Severe (Unable to swallow) | <50 mg/dL | D50 1 amp IV OR Glucagon 1 mg IM/IN |
| Sulfonylurea-Induced | Variable | Octreotide 50-100 mcg SC q6-12h; Admit |

## Chronic Complications of Diabetes
| Complication | Screening | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| Retinopathy | Annual dilated eye exam | Laser, Anti-VEGF, Vitrectomy |
| Nephropathy | Annual urine albumin:creatinine ratio, eGFR | ACE-I/ARB, SGLT2 inhibitor |
| Neuropathy | Annual monofilament exam | Pain management, Foot care |
| Cardiovascular | Risk factor management | Statin, Aspirin, BP control |
| Foot Ulcers | Annual foot exam; Patient education | Offloading, Wound care, Antibiotics |

## Gestational Diabetes (GDM)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Screening | 1-hour 50g OGTT at 24-28 weeks; If abnormal, 3-hour 100g OGTT |
| Diagnostic Criteria | Fasting >=92, 1h >=180, 2h >=153, 3h >=140 (one abnormal = GDM) |
| Glycemic Targets | Fasting <95, 1h postprandial <140, 2h postprandial <120 |
| Treatment | Medical nutrition therapy; Insulin (preferred); Metformin (alternative) |
| Postpartum | 75g OGTT at 6-12 weeks; Annual screening for diabetes |
