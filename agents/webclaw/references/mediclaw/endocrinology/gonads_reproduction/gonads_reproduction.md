# Endocrinology - Gonadal and Reproductive Disorders

## Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Gonadal (HPG) Axis
| Level | Female | Male |
|-------|--------|------|
| Hypothalamus | GnRH (pulsatile) | GnRH (pulsatile) |
| Pituitary | LH, FSH | LH, FSH |
| Gonads | Estrogen, Progesterone | Testosterone |
| Inhibitors | Estrogen, Progesterone, Inhibin | Testosterone, Inhibin |

## Male Hypogonadism
| Type | LH/FSH | Testosterone | Causes | Treatment |
|------|--------|--------------|--------|-----------|
| Primary (Hypergonadotropic) | High | Low | Klinefelter (XXY), Mumps orchitis, Trauma, Chemo/Radiation | Testosterone replacement |
| Secondary (Hypogonadotropic) | Low/Normal | Low | Kallmann syndrome, Pituitary tumors, Hemochromatosis, Opioids | Testosterone or hCG/FSH (if fertility desired) |
| Late-Onset Hypogonadism | Variable | Low/Normal | Aging, Obesity, Comorbidities | Testosterone if symptomatic and confirmed low |

| Formulation | Dose | Frequency | Considerations |
|-------------|------|-----------|----------------|

## Testosterone Replacement - Monitoring
| Parameter | Goal | Frequency |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| Testosterone | Mid-normal range (400-700 ng/dL) | 3-6 months |
| Hematocrit | <54% | 3-6 months, then annually |
| PSA | Monitor for increase | Baseline, then per age guidelines |
| DRE | Monitor for prostate abnormalities | Per age guidelines |

## Male Infertility
| Cause | Evaluation | Treatment |
|-------|------------|-----------|
| Varicocele | Physical exam, Scrotal US | Varicocelectomy |
| Obstructive | Semen analysis (low volume, no sperm) | Surgical reconstruction |
| Hormonal | Low T, Low FSH/LH | hCG ｱ FSH |
| Genetic | Karyotype, Y microdeletion | IVF/ICSI, Donor sperm |
| Idiopathic | Rule out other causes | Empiric therapy, IVF/ICSI |

## Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Rotterdam Criteria | 2 of 3: Oligo/anovulation, Hyperandrogenism, Polycystic ovaries on US |
| Clinical Features | Irregular menses, Hirsutism, Acne, Obesity, Insulin resistance |
| Labs | Elevated testosterone, LH:FSH >2:1, Insulin resistance |
| Treatment | Lifestyle modification; OCPs (menstrual regulation); Metformin (insulin resistance); Spironolactone (hirsutism); Clomiphene/Letrozole (fertility) |
| Long-term Risks | Diabetes, Endometrial hyperplasia/cancer, Cardiovascular disease |

## Female Hypogonadism
| Type | LH/FSH | Estrogen | Causes | Treatment |
|------|--------|----------|--------|-----------|
| Primary (Hypergonadotropic) | High | Low | Turner syndrome (XO), Premature ovarian insufficiency, Autoimmune, Chemo/Radiation | Estrogen replacement |
| Secondary (Hypogonadotropic) | Low/Normal | Low | Functional hypothalamic amenorrhea, Pituitary tumors, Hyperprolactinemia | Treat underlying cause; Estrogen replacement |

## Premature Ovarian Insufficiency (POI)
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Definition | Menopause before age 40 |
| Causes | Autoimmune (20-30%), Genetic (Fragile X premutation, Turner), Iatrogenic, Idiopathic |
| Diagnosis | FSH >40 on two occasions >1 month apart |
| Treatment | Estrogen replacement (until age 50-51); Calcium/Vitamin D; Fertility (donor eggs) |
| Screening | Thyroid function, Adrenal antibodies, Bone density |

## Menopause
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Definition | 12 months of amenorrhea; Average age 51 |
| Symptoms | Vasomotor (hot flashes), Vaginal dryness, Sleep disturbance, Mood changes |
| Diagnosis | Clinical; FSH not routinely needed |
| Hormone Therapy | Estrogen ｱ Progesterone (if uterus present); Lowest effective dose for shortest duration |
| Contraindications | Breast cancer, Endometrial cancer, VTE, Active liver disease, Undiagnosed vaginal bleeding |
| Non-Hormonal Options | SSRIs/SNRIs, Gabapentin, Clonidine, Cognitive behavioral therapy |

## Hormone Therapy for Menopause
| Regimen | Indication | Formulations |
|---------|------------|--------------|
| Estrogen Only | Hysterectomy | Oral, Transdermal, Vaginal |
| Estrogen + Progesterone | Intact uterus | Continuous-combined, Cyclic |
| Progesterone Only | Contraindication to estrogen | Oral, IUD |
| SERMs | Osteoporosis, Vasomotor symptoms | Raloxifene, Bazedoxifene + Conjugated estrogens |

## Gynecomastia
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Causes | Physiologic (neonatal, pubertal, aging), Medications (spironolactone, antiandrogens, marijuana, alcohol), Hypogonadism, Hyperthyroidism, Liver disease, Tumors |
| Evaluation | Breast exam, Testicular exam, Liver function, Hormones (T, E2, LH, hCG, TSH, Prolactin) |
| Treatment | Treat underlying cause; Observation (often resolves); Tamoxifen (if painful/persistent); Surgery (if refractory) |

## Erectile Dysfunction
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Causes | Vascular (atherosclerosis, HTN), Neurologic (DM, spinal cord injury), Hormonal (hypogonadism, hyperprolactinemia), Medications, Psychogenic |
| Evaluation | History, Physical, Morning testosterone, Prolactin, Glucose, Lipids |
| Treatment | PDE5 inhibitors (Sildenafil, Tadalafil); Testosterone if hypogonadal; Vacuum devices; Intracavernosal injections; Penile implant |
