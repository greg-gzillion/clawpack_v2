# Diabetes Mellitus Type 2

## Definition
Chronic metabolic disorder characterized by insulin resistance and relative insulin deficiency

## Pathophysiology
- Insulin resistance in peripheral tissues (muscle, liver, fat)
- Progressive beta-cell dysfunction
- Increased hepatic glucose production

## Risk Factors
- Obesity (BMI =30)
- Family history
- Age =45 years
- Physical inactivity
- Gestational diabetes history
- PCOS
- Hypertension
- Dyslipidemia

## Diagnostic Criteria (any of the following)
- FPG =126 mg/dL (fasting 8+ hours)
- 2-h PG =200 mg/dL during OGTT
- HbA1c =6.5%
- Random PG =200 mg/dL with symptoms

## Symptoms
- Polyuria, polydipsia, polyphagia
- Blurred vision
- Fatigue
- Weight loss
- Recurrent infections

## Treatment

### Lifestyle (foundation for all)
- Medical nutrition therapy
- Weight loss (5-10% body weight)
- Physical activity (150 min/week)

### Pharmacotherapy

**First-line:**
- **Metformin** (500-2000 mg/day) - decreases hepatic glucose production

**Add-on therapies (if HbA1c >7% after 3 months):**
- **SGLT2 inhibitors** (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) - CV/renal benefits
- **GLP-1 agonists** (semaglutide, liraglutide) - weight loss, CV benefits
- **DPP-4 inhibitors** (sitagliptin, linagliptin) - neutral CV effects
- **Sulfonylureas** (glipizide, glyburide) - inexpensive, hypoglycemia risk
- **TZDs** (pioglitazone) - insulin sensitizer
- **Insulin** (basal, prandial, or premixed)

## Monitoring
- HbA1c every 3-6 months (goal <7%)
- Self-monitoring blood glucose
- Annual: lipids, renal function, eye exam, foot exam

## Complications
- Microvascular: retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy
- Macrovascular: CAD, stroke, PAD
