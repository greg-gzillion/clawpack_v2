# Endocrinology - Adrenal Disorders

## Adrenal Anatomy and Physiology
| Zone | Hormones | Regulation | Function |
|------|----------|------------|----------|
| Zona Glomerulosa | Aldosterone | Renin-Angiotensin, K+ | Sodium retention, Potassium excretion |
| Zona Fasciculata | Cortisol | ACTH | Stress response, Metabolism, Immune suppression |
| Zona Reticularis | Androgens (DHEA, DHEA-S) | ACTH | Precursors to testosterone/estrogen |
| Medulla | Epinephrine, Norepinephrine | Sympathetic nervous system | Fight or flight response |

## Cushing's Syndrome
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Clinical Features | Central obesity, Moon facies, Buffalo hump, Purple striae, Proximal myopathy, HTN, Diabetes, Osteoporosis |
| Screening Tests | 24h urine free cortisol (>3x normal); Late-night salivary cortisol (>normal); 1 mg overnight dexamethasone suppression (cortisol >1.8 mcg/dL = abnormal) |
| Confirmatory Tests | 2-day low-dose dexamethasone suppression; Midnight serum cortisol |
| ACTH Level | Low: Adrenal source; High: Pituitary or Ectopic source |
| Localization | Adrenal CT; Pituitary MRI; IPSS (for ACTH-dependent); Octreotide scan (ectopic) |
| Treatment | Surgery (adrenalectomy, pituitary adenomectomy); Medical (Ketoconazole, Metyrapone, Mitotane, Osilodrostat) |

## Differential Diagnosis of Cushing's Syndrome
| Cause | ACTH | Dexamethasone Suppression | CRH Stimulation | IPSS Gradient |
|-------|------|---------------------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Cushing's Disease | High/Normal | No suppression (high dose may suppress) | ACTH rises | >2 (central) |
| Ectopic ACTH | High | No suppression | No ACTH rise | <2 |
| Adrenal Adenoma/Carcinoma | Low | No suppression | No ACTH rise | <2 |
| Macronodular Hyperplasia | Low | Paradoxical rise | Variable | <2 |
| Pseudo-Cushing's | High/Normal | May suppress | Normal response | - |

## Adrenal Insufficiency
| Type | Cause | ACTH | Aldosterone | Clinical Features |
|------|-------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Primary (Addison's) | Autoimmune (80%), TB, Hemorrhage | High | Low | Hyperpigmentation, Salt craving, Hyperkalemia |
| Secondary | Pituitary/Hypothalamic disease, Steroid withdrawal | Low | Normal | No hyperpigmentation, Normal potassium |
| Tertiary | Hypothalamic CRH deficiency | Low | Normal | Same as secondary |

## Adrenal Insufficiency - Diagnosis
| Test | Interpretation |
|------|----------------|
| AM Cortisol | <3 mcg/dL = Adrenal insufficiency; >15 mcg/dL = Normal |
| ACTH Stimulation Test | Cortisol <18-20 mcg/dL at 30-60 min = Adrenal insufficiency |
| Insulin Tolerance Test | Gold standard but risky; Hypoglycemia stimulates ACTH/cortisol |
| Metyrapone Test | Assesses entire HPA axis |
| CRH Stimulation Test | Differentiates secondary vs tertiary |

## Adrenal Insufficiency - Treatment
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Maintenance | Hydrocortisone 15-25 mg/day divided (15 mg AM, 5-10 mg PM); Fludrocortisone 0.05-0.2 mg daily (primary only) |
| Stress Dosing | Double/triple dose for illness, injury, surgery |
| Adrenal Crisis | **Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV bolus**; Then 50 mg q6h or 100 mg q8h; **Aggressive IVF** |
| Monitoring | Clinical status; Electrolytes; Avoid over-replacement |
| Medical Alert | MedicAlert bracelet; Emergency hydrocortisone injection kit |

## Primary Aldosteronism
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Clinical Features | Hypertension, Hypokalemia (may be normokalemic), Metabolic alkalosis |
| Screening | Aldosterone:Renin Ratio (ARR) >20-30 + Aldosterone >15 ng/dL |
| Confirmatory Testing | Saline infusion; Oral sodium loading; Fludrocortisone suppression; Captopril challenge |
| Subtype Differentiation | Adrenal CT; **Adrenal vein sampling** (gold standard) |
| Treatment | Unilateral adenoma: Laparoscopic adrenalectomy; Bilateral hyperplasia: Mineralocorticoid antagonist (Spironolactone, Eplerenone) |

## Pheochromocytoma and Paraganglioma
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| "Rule of 10s" | 10% extra-adrenal, 10% bilateral, 10% malignant, 10% familial, 10% children |
| Clinical Features | Paroxysmal HTN, Headache, Palpitations, Diaphoresis, Pallor |
| Diagnosis | Plasma metanephrines (most sensitive); 24h urine metanephrines and catecholamines |
| Localization | CT/MRI abdomen; MIBG scan; PET-CT (DOTATATE) |
| Preoperative Management | **Alpha-blockade first** (Phenoxybenzamine 10 mg BID, titrate); Then **Beta-blockade** (only after alpha-blockade) |
| Surgery | Laparoscopic adrenalectomy |
| Postoperative | Monitor for hypotension; Lifelong surveillance |

## Adrenal Incidentaloma
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Prevalence | 4-7% on abdominal imaging |
| Evaluation | Is it functional? Is it malignant? |
| Functional Workup | 1 mg dexamethasone suppression (Cushing's); Plasma metanephrines (Pheo); ARR if hypertensive (Aldosteronism) |
| Imaging Features | Size >4 cm, Hounsfield units >10, Washout <50%, Heterogeneity (concerning) |
| Management | <1 cm + benign features: No follow-up; 1-4 cm: Follow imaging; >4 cm or suspicious: Consider adrenalectomy |

## Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (CAH)
| Type | Enzyme Deficiency | Hormone Profile | Clinical Features |
|------|------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| 21-Hydroxylase (90%) | CYP21A2 | High 17-OHP, Low Cortisol, High ACTH, High Androgens | Salt-wasting (75%), Simple virilizing (25%) |
| 11-beta-Hydroxylase | CYP11B1 | High 11-Deoxycortisol, High Androgens, Hypertension | Virilization + HTN |
| 17-alpha-Hydroxylase | CYP17A1 | Low Androgens, Low Cortisol, High Mineralocorticoids | HTN, Hypokalemia, Sexual infantilism |
| 3-beta-HSD | HSD3B2 | High DHEA, Low Androgens, Low Cortisol, Low Aldosterone | Salt-wasting, Ambiguous genitalia |
