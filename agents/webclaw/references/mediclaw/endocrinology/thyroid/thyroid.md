# Endocrinology - Thyroid Disorders

## Thyroid Physiology
| Component | Normal Range | Function |
|-----------|--------------|----------|
| TSH | 0.4-4.0 mIU/L | Stimulates T4/T3 production |
| Total T4 | 5-12 mcg/dL | Prohormone (converted to T3) |
| Free T4 | 0.8-1.8 ng/dL | Active form available to tissues |
| Total T3 | 80-200 ng/dL | Active hormone |
| Free T3 | 2.3-4.2 pg/mL | Most biologically active |
| Reverse T3 | 10-24 ng/dL | Inactive metabolite |
| Thyroglobulin | <33 ng/mL | Tumor marker (thyroid cancer) |
| Thyroid Antibodies | Variable | Autoimmune thyroid disease |

## Graves' Disease
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Pathophysiology | TSH receptor antibodies (TSI) stimulate thyroid |
| Clinical Features | Hyperthyroidism, Diffuse goiter, Ophthalmopathy, Dermopathy (pretibial myxedema) |
| Diagnosis | Low TSH, High T4, Positive TSI, High RAIU |
| Treatment Options | Antithyroid drugs (Methimazole, PTU); RAI ablation; Thyroidectomy |
| Beta-Blockers | Propranolol for symptomatic control |
| Ophthalmopathy | Selenium; Steroids; Orbital radiation; Surgery (severe) |

## Antithyroid Drugs
| Drug | Dose | Duration | Side Effects | Pregnancy |
|------|------|----------|--------------|-----------|
| Methimazole | 10-30 mg daily | 12-18 months | Agranulocytosis, Hepatotoxicity, Rash | 2nd/3rd trimester |
| PTU (Propylthiouracil) | 100-300 mg TID | 12-18 months | Hepatotoxicity (severe), Agranulocytosis, Vasculitis | **1st trimester only** |

## Hashimoto's Thyroiditis
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Pathophysiology | Autoimmune destruction of thyroid (TPO antibodies) |
| Clinical Features | Hypothyroidism, Goiter (firm, rubbery), May have transient hyperthyroidism |
| Diagnosis | Elevated TSH, Low T4, Positive TPO antibodies |
| Treatment | Levothyroxine (dose based on weight and age) |
| Monitoring | TSH every 6-8 weeks until stable, then annually |

## Levothyroxine Dosing
| Patient Population | Starting Dose | Titration |
|--------------------|---------------|-----------|
| Young, Healthy | 1.6 mcg/kg daily | TSH in 6-8 weeks |
| Elderly, Cardiac Disease | 25-50 mcg daily | Slow titration |
| Pregnancy | Increase by 30-50% | Monitor TSH each trimester |
| Post-Thyroidectomy (Cancer) | 2.2 mcg/kg daily | Goal TSH suppression |

## Thyroid Nodules
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Prevalence | 4-7% palpable; 50-70% on ultrasound |
| Malignancy Risk | 5-15% of nodules |
| Ultrasound Features | Microcalcifications, Irregular margins, Taller-than-wide, Hypoechoic |
| TI-RADS Classification | 1-5 (increasing risk) |
| FNA Indications | >1 cm (or >1.5 cm if spongiform); Any size with suspicious features |
| Bethesda Classification | I-VI (I: Non-diagnostic; II: Benign; V-VI: Malignant) |

## Thyroid Cancer Types
| Type | Frequency | Prognosis | Treatment |
|------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Papillary | 80% | Excellent | Thyroidectomy ± RAI |
| Follicular | 10-15% | Good | Thyroidectomy ± RAI |
| Medullary | 5% | Intermediate | Total thyroidectomy + LND |
| Anaplastic | <2% | Poor | Palliative; Clinical trials |
| Hurthle Cell | 3-5% | Variable | Thyroidectomy |

## Thyroid Storm
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Fever, Tachycardia, CHF, AMS, GI symptoms |
| Precipitants | Infection, Surgery, Trauma, RAI, Iodine contrast |
| Treatment | **PTU** (blocks synthesis + T4->T3 conversion); **Iodine** (1 hour after PTU); **Propranolol**; **Hydrocortisone**; Supportive |
| Burch-Wartofsky Score | >45 = Highly suggestive of storm |

## Myxedema Coma
| Component | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| Presentation | Hypothermia, Bradycardia, Hypotension, AMS, Coma |
| Precipitants | Infection, Cold exposure, Medications (opioids, sedatives) |
| Treatment | **Levothyroxine IV** (200-400 mcg load, then 50-100 mcg daily); **Hydrocortisone** (until adrenal insufficiency ruled out); Supportive |
| Mortality | 30-60% despite treatment |

## Subclinical Thyroid Disease
| Condition | TSH | Free T4 | Management |
|-----------|-----|---------|------------|
| Subclinical Hyperthyroidism | <0.1 | Normal | Treat if age >65, cardiac disease, osteoporosis |
| Subclinical Hypothyroidism | 5-10 | Normal | Treat if symptomatic, pregnancy, goiter, TPO+ |
| Subclinical Hypothyroidism | >10 | Normal | Treat regardless of symptoms |
