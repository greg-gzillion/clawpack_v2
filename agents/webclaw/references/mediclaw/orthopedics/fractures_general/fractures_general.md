# Orthopedics - Fractures - General Principles

## Fracture Management Principles
| Principle | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Reduction** | Restore anatomic alignment (closed vs. open) | https://ota.org/for-patients/fracture-information |
| **Immobilization** | Maintain reduction (cast, Splint, External fixator, Internal fixation) | https://ota.org/for-patients/fracture-information |
| **Rehabilitation** | Restore function (range of motion, Strengthening) | https://ota.org/for-patients/fracture-information |

## Types of Fracture Fixation
| Type | Description | Indications | URL |
|------|-------------|-------------|-----|
| **Cast/Splint** | Non-operative immobilization | **Stable**, **Non-displaced** or **minimally displaced** fractures | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/recovery/care-of-casts-and-splints/ |
| **External Fixation** | Pins through skin into bone, Connected to external frame | **Open fractures**, **Damage control**, **Periarticular fractures** | https://ota.org/for-patients/fracture-information |
| **Internal Fixation** | Plates, Screws, Intramedullary nails | **Displaced**, **Unstable**, **Intra-articular** fractures | https://ota.org/for-patients/fracture-information |
| **Intramedullary Nailing** | Rod inserted into medullary canal | **Long bone shaft fractures** (femur, Tibia, Humerus) | https://ota.org/for-patients/fracture-information |

## Compartment Syndrome
| Component | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Definition** | **Elevated intracompartmental pressure** ? **Impaired tissue perfusion** ? **Muscle and nerve ischemia** | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/compartment-syndrome/ |
| **Etiology** | **Fractures** (tibia, Forearm), **Crush injury**, **Reperfusion**, **Burns**, **Tight casts/dressings** | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/compartment-syndrome/ |
| **5 P's** | **Pain** (out of proportion, With passive stretch), **Paresthesia**, **Pallor**, **Pulselessness** (late), **Paralysis** (late) | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/compartment-syndrome/ |
| **Diagnosis** | **Compartment pressure measurement**; **>30 mmHg** or **?P (DBP - compartment pressure) <30 mmHg** = Fasciotomy | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/compartment-syndrome/ |
| **Treatment** | **Emergent fasciotomy** | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/compartment-syndrome/ |

## Open Fracture Management
| Step | Intervention | URL |
|------|--------------|-----|
| **1. Antibiotics** | **Cefazolin** (Type I-II); **Cefazolin + Gentamicin** (Type III); **Add Penicillin** for farm/soil contamination | https://ota.org/for-patients/fracture-information |
| **2. Tetanus Prophylaxis** | **Tdap** if >5 years since last dose | https://www.cdc.gov/tetanus/index.html |
| **3. Irrigation and Debridement** | **Emergent** surgical debridement within 24 hours | https://ota.org/for-patients/fracture-information |
| **4. Stabilization** | **External fixation** or **Internal fixation** | https://ota.org/for-patients/fracture-information |
| **5. Soft Tissue Coverage** | **Delayed primary closure**, **Skin graft**, **Flap** within 5-7 days | https://ota.org/for-patients/fracture-information |

## Nonunion and Malunion
| Condition | Definition | Risk Factors | Treatment | URL |
|-----------|------------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Delayed Union** | **Slower than expected healing** (4-6 months) | Smoking, Diabetes, Infection, Poor nutrition | **Observation**, **Bone stimulator** | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/nonunions/ |
| **Nonunion** | **No healing >9 months** without progression | Same as above + Inadequate stabilization | **Surgery** (revision fixation + bone graft) | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/nonunions/ |
| **Malunion** | **Healed in unacceptable position** | Inadequate reduction, Loss of reduction | **Observation** (if acceptable), **Osteotomy** | https://orthoinfo.aaos.org/en/diseases--conditions/malunion/ |

## Venous Thromboembolism (VTE) Prophylaxis
| Risk Level | Prophylaxis | URL |
|------------|-------------|-----|
| **Low** (Minor surgery, No risk factors) | **Early mobilization** | https://www.aaos.org/quality/guidelines/vte/ |
| **Moderate** (Major surgery, Age 40-60) | **LMWH**, **Aspirin**, **IPC** | https://www.aaos.org/quality/guidelines/vte/ |
| **High** (Major trauma, Hip fracture, Prior VTE) | **LMWH**, **Fondaparinux**, **Warfarin**, **DOAC** | https://www.aaos.org/quality/guidelines/vte/ |
