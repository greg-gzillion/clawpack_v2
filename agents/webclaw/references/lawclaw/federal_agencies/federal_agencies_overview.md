# Federal Agencies - Overview

## Federal Government Structure
The federal government consists of three branches: Executive, Legislative, and Judicial. The Executive Branch includes the President, Executive Office of the President, 15 Cabinet-level executive departments, and numerous independent agencies, regulatory commissions, and government corporations.

## Branches of Federal Government
| Branch | Description | Key Components |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Executive Branch | Headed by the President; enforces laws | White House; Cabinet Departments; Independent Agencies |
| Legislative Branch | Makes laws; appropriates funds | Congress; Library of Congress; GAO; CBO |
| Judicial Branch | Interprets laws; adjudicates disputes | Supreme Court; Circuit Courts; District Courts |

## Categories of Federal Agencies
| Category | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Executive Office of the President | Direct support to President | OMB; NSC; Council of Economic Advisers |
| Executive Departments | 15 Cabinet-level departments | State; Defense; Justice; Treasury; Homeland Security |
| Independent Agencies | Agencies outside executive departments | EPA; NASA; SSA; CIA; GSA |
| Regulatory Commissions | Independent regulatory bodies | SEC; FTC; FCC; FERC; CFTC |
| Government Corporations | Government-owned businesses | USPS; Amtrak; FDIC; TVA; Ex-Im Bank |
| Legislative Branch Agencies | Support Congress | GAO; CBO; Library of Congress; GPO |
| Judicial Branch Agencies | Support federal courts | Administrative Office of U.S. Courts; Federal Judicial Center |
