# U.S. Law Universities as Reference Sources - Overview

## Law Universities as Authoritative Legal References
U.S. law schools serve as primary sources of legal scholarship, analysis, and research guidance. Their law libraries, faculty scholarship repositories, research guides, law reviews, and specialized centers provide invaluable reference materials for legal practitioners, scholars, and students.

## Types of Law University Reference Sources
| Source Type | Description | Examples |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Law Libraries | Comprehensive legal collections with research guides | Harvard Law Library, Yale Law Library |
| Faculty Scholarship Repositories | Open-access collections of faculty publications | SSRN, institutional repositories |
| Law Reviews/Journals | Student-edited scholarly publications | Harvard Law Review, Yale Law Journal |
| Research Guides | Subject-specific legal research guides | Georgetown Law Library Guides |
| Centers and Institutes | Specialized research centers | Brennan Center (NYU), Berkman Klein Center (Harvard) |
| Clinics and Policy Labs | Practice-oriented research | Supreme Court Clinic (Stanford) |
| Blogs and Commentary | Faculty and institutional legal analysis | Volokh Conspiracy (UCLA), Lawfare (Brookings/Harvard) |
