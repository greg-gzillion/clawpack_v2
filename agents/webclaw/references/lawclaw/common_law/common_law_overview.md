# Common Law - Overview

## What is Common Law?
Common law is law developed by judges through court decisions (precedent) rather than through statutes or constitutions. It originated in England and forms the basis of the legal system in the United States (except Louisiana), Canada, Australia, and other former British colonies.

## Common Law vs. Civil Law
| Feature | Common Law | Civil Law |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Primary Source | Judicial decisions (precedent) | Codified statutes |
| Role of Judges | Interpret and create law | Apply code to facts |
| Stare Decisis | Binding precedent | Persuasive only |
| Jury Role | Significant (fact-finding) | Limited |
| Discovery | Extensive | Limited |
| Adversarial System | Party-driven | Judge-driven |

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|

| 2. Find Relevant Precedent | Search case law |
| 3. Extract Legal Rule | Identify ratio decidendi |
