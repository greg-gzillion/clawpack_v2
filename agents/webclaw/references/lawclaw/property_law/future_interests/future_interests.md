| Type | Definition | Example | URL |

## Rule Against Perpetuities (Common Law)
| Element | Description | URL |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Statement of Rule | No interest is good unless it must vest, if at all, no later than 21 years after some life in being at creation of interest | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/rule_against_perpetuities |
| Lives in Being | Persons alive at creation of interest who can affect vesting | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/rule_against_perpetuities |
| 21-Year Period | Additional 21 years after death of last life in being | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/rule_against_perpetuities |
| What-If Analysis | Interest valid only if certain to vest within period | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/rule_against_perpetuities |
