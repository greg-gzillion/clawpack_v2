# State Agencies - Complete 50 State Directory

## Overview of State Agencies
State agencies are the administrative arms of state government, responsible for implementing and enforcing state laws, regulations, and policies. Each state has a complex network of executive departments, regulatory commissions, boards, and independent agencies.

## Categories of State Agencies
| Category | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Executive Departments | Cabinet-level agencies headed by appointed secretaries | Department of Health, Department of Transportation |
| Regulatory Commissions | Independent bodies regulating specific industries | Public Utilities Commission, Insurance Commission |
| Professional Licensing Boards | Boards overseeing licensed professions | Medical Board, Bar Association, Accountancy Board |
| Environmental Agencies | Natural resource and environmental protection | Department of Environmental Quality, Fish and Wildlife |
| Health and Human Services | Public health and social services | Department of Health, Department of Social Services |
| Public Safety Agencies | Law enforcement and emergency management | State Police, Highway Patrol, Emergency Management |
| Education Agencies | K-12 and higher education administration | Department of Education, Board of Regents |
| Revenue and Tax Agencies | Tax collection and administration | Department of Revenue, Franchise Tax Board |
| Labor and Employment | Workforce development and worker protection | Department of Labor, Employment Security |
| Transportation Agencies | Roads, highways, transit, aviation | Department of Transportation, DMV |
| Agriculture Agencies | Farming, food safety, weights and measures | Department of Agriculture |
| Insurance and Banking | Financial regulation | Department of Insurance, Department of Banking |
| Energy and Utilities | Energy policy and utility regulation | Energy Commission, Public Service Commission |
| Veterans Affairs | Services for military veterans | Department of Veterans Affairs |
