# Federal Case Law - Overview

## What is Federal Case Law?
Federal case law consists of judicial decisions from federal courts interpreting the U.S. Constitution, federal statutes, federal regulations, and treaties. The federal court system operates alongside state court systems and has limited jurisdiction, hearing only cases involving federal questions, diversity of citizenship, or specific subject matter granted by Congress.
