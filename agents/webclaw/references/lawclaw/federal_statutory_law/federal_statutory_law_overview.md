# Federal Statutory Law - Overview

## What is Federal Statutory Law?
Federal statutory law consists of laws enacted by the United States Congress and signed by the President (or passed over veto). These laws are published chronologically as session laws in the Statutes at Large and codified by subject matter in the United States Code (U.S.C.). Federal statutes govern matters within Congress's enumerated powers under the Constitution.
