# Federal Statutory Law - Reauthorization and Sunset Provisions

## What are Reauthorization and Sunset Provisions?
Many federal programs and agencies are established with limited durations and must be periodically reauthorized by Congress. Sunset provisions automatically terminate programs or authorities on a specified date unless Congress acts to extend them.

## Authorization Duration Types
| Type | Description | Example | URL |
|------|-------------|---------|-----|
| Permanent Authorization | No expiration; continues indefinitely | Social Security Act; Clean Air Act | https://www.congress.gov |
| Multi-Year Authorization | Fixed term; must be reauthorized | Farm Bill (5 years); FAA (5 years) | https://www.congress.gov |
| Annual Authorization | Must be reauthorized each year | Intelligence Authorization Act | https://www.congress.gov |
| Sunset Provision | Automatic termination unless extended | Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (individual provisions) | https://www.congress.gov |

## Continuing Resolutions and Lapsed Appropriations
| Scenario | Description | Consequence | URL |
|----------|-------------|-------------|-----|
| Continuing Resolution (CR) | Temporary funding extension | Programs continue at prior levels | https://www.congress.gov/help/learn-about-the-legislative-process/continuing-resolution |
| Authorization Lapse | Program authority expires | May continue if appropriated; new activities limited | https://www.congress.gov |
| Appropriations Lapse | Funding expires | Government shutdown; essential services continue | https://www.gao.gov |
| Full Funding Gap | Both authorization and appropriations lapse | Program ceases operations | https://www.congress.gov |
