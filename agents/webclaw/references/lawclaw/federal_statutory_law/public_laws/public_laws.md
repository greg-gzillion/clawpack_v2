# Federal Statutory Law - Public Laws

## What are Public Laws?
Public laws are acts of Congress that affect the general public or classes of citizens. They are numbered sequentially within each Congress (e.g., Pub. L. 117-2) and are first published as slip laws before being compiled in the Statutes at Large.

## Public Law Numbering System
| Component | Example | Description | URL |
|-----------|---------|-------------|-----|
| Congress Number | 117 | The Congress that enacted the law | https://www.congress.gov |
| Law Number | 2 | Sequential number within that Congress | https://www.congress.gov |
| Full Citation | Pub. L. 117-2 | Complete public law citation | https://www.congress.gov/public-laws/ |

## Public Law vs. Private Law
| Type | Description | Example | URL |
|------|-------------|---------|-----|
| Public Law | Affects general public or classes of citizens | Pub. L. 117-2 | https://www.congress.gov/public-laws/ |
| Private Law | Affects specific individuals or entities | Priv. L. 117-1 | https://www.congress.gov/private-laws/ |
