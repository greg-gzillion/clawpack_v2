# Federal Statutory Law - Appropriations and Authorizations

## The Dual Process of Federal Spending
| Type | Description | Purpose | URL |
|------|-------------|---------|-----|
| Authorization | Establishes or continues federal program or agency | Creates legal authority; sets funding ceilings | https://www.congress.gov/help/learn-about-the-legislative-process/authorization-and-appropriation |
| Appropriation | Provides actual funding for authorized programs | Makes funds available for obligation | https://www.congress.gov/help/learn-about-the-legislative-process/authorization-and-appropriation |
| Relationship | Authorization required before appropriation | "Authorize first, appropriate later" | https://www.congress.gov/help/learn-about-the-legislative-process/authorization-and-appropriation |

## Constitutional Basis
| Provision | Text | URL |
|-----------|------|-----|
| Article I, Section 9, Clause 7 | "No Money shall be drawn from the Treasury, but in Consequence of Appropriations made by Law" | https://www.law.cornell.edu/constitution/articlei |

## Types of Appropriations

| Permanent Appropriations | Indefinite | Mandatory spending (e.g., Social Security, Medicare) | https://www.congress.gov/appropriations |
| Continuing Resolution (CR) | Temporary | Funds government when regular appropriations not enacted | https://www.congress.gov/help/learn-about-the-legislative-process/continuing-resolution |

## Appropriations Process Timeline
| Date | Action | URL |
|------|--------|-----|
| First Monday in February | President submits budget request | https://www.whitehouse.gov/omb/budget/ |
| February-April | Congressional budget resolution | https://www.congress.gov/help/learn-about-the-legislative-process/budget-resolution |
| Spring-Summer | Appropriations subcommittee hearings and markups | https://www.appropriations.senate.gov |
| Summer-Fall | Floor consideration of appropriations bills | https://www.congress.gov/appropriations |
| October 1 | Fiscal year begins; CR if appropriations not enacted | https://www.congress.gov/help/learn-about-the-legislative-process/continuing-resolution |
