# Federal Statutory Law - Codification

## What is Codification?
Codification is the process of collecting and organizing laws by subject matter into a systematic code. The United States Code is the official codification of general and permanent federal laws, organized into 54 titles by subject.

## The Codification Process
| Stage | Description | URL |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| 1. Enactment | Congress passes and President signs bill | https://www.congress.gov |
| 2. Slip Law | Individual law published as slip law | https://www.govinfo.gov/app/collection/PLAW |
| 3. Statutes at Large | Laws compiled chronologically for each Congress | https://www.govinfo.gov/app/collection/STATUTE |
| 4. Codification | Office of the Law Revision Counsel organizes into U.S. Code | https://uscode.house.gov |
| 5. Classification | Provisions assigned to appropriate U.S.C. titles and sections | https://uscode.house.gov/about_classification.xhtml |
| 6. Publication | U.S. Code published every 6 years; supplements annually | https://uscode.house.gov |
