# Bankruptcy Law - Overview

## What is Bankruptcy?
Bankruptcy is a federal court process that allows individuals and businesses to eliminate or repay debts under the protection of the bankruptcy court. Bankruptcy is governed by the Bankruptcy Code (11 U.S.C.).

## Bankruptcy Chapters Overview
| Chapter | Type | Who Files | Key Feature |
|---------|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Chapter 7** | Liquidation | Individuals & businesses | Assets sold to pay creditors; dischargeable debts eliminated |
| **Chapter 11** | Reorganization | Businesses & high-income individuals | Debtor reorganizes; continues operations under court supervision |
| **Chapter 12** | Family Farmer/Fisherman | Family farmers & fishermen | Specialized reorganization for agricultural operations |
| **Chapter 13** | Wage Earner Plan | Individuals with regular income | Repayment plan over 3-5 years; keep assets |

## The Bankruptcy Court System
- **94 Bankruptcy Courts**: One in each federal judicial district
- **Bankruptcy Judges**: Appointed for 14-year terms by Court of Appeals
- **Bankruptcy Appellate Panels (BAPs)**: Optional appellate review in some circuits
- **U.S. Trustee Program**: DOJ oversight of bankruptcy administration

## Key Players in Bankruptcy
| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Debtor** | Person/business filing for bankruptcy |
| **Creditor** | Entity owed money by debtor |
| **Trustee** | Administers estate; liquidates assets (Ch. 7) or oversees plan (Ch. 13) |
| **U.S. Trustee** | DOJ official; monitors fraud and abuse |
| **Creditors' Committee** | Represents unsecured creditors (Ch. 11) |
| **Bankruptcy Judge** | Presides over contested matters |

## The Automatic Stay (11 U.S.C. § 362)
- **Immediate effect** upon filing bankruptcy petition
- **Stops**: Collections, lawsuits, foreclosures, repossessions, garnishments
- **Exceptions**: Criminal proceedings, certain family law matters, tax audits
- **Violations**: Actual damages, punitive damages, attorney fees
