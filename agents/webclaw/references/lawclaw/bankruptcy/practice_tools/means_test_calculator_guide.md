# Bankruptcy Means Test - Calculation Guide

## Overview
The "means test" determines eligibility for Chapter 7 and the applicable commitment period for Chapter 13. It compares the debtor's income to state median and calculates disposable income.

## Official Forms
- **Form 122A-1**: Chapter 7 Statement of Current Monthly Income
- **Form 122A-2**: Chapter 7 Means Test Calculation
- **Form 122C-1**: Chapter 13 Statement of Current Monthly Income
- **Form 122C-2**: Chapter 13 Calculation of Disposable Income

## Step 1: Calculate Current Monthly Income (CMI)
CMI = Average monthly income for 6 months pre-filing from ALL sources except Social Security.

| Included | Excluded |
|----------|----------|
| Wages, salary, tips | Social Security benefits |
| Self-employment income | Payments to victims of war crimes/crimes against humanity |
| Rental income | Foster care payments (state) |
| Unemployment compensation | |
| Pension/retirement | |
| Regular gifts from family | |
| Child support/alimony received | |

**Formula**: (Total income for 6 months) ÷ 6 = CMI

## Step 2: Compare to State Median
- **CMI = State Median**: No presumption of abuse (Ch. 7); 36-month plan (Ch. 13)
- **CMI > State Median**: Must complete full means test

## Step 3: Calculate Disposable Income (Above-Median Debtors)

### Allowed Expenses
| Expense | Amount | Source |
|---------|--------|--------|
| **National Standards** | Fixed by IRS | Food, clothing, household supplies, personal care |
| **Local Standards** | Varies by location | Housing, utilities, transportation |
| **Actual Expenses** | Actual amount | Taxes, mandatory payroll deductions, health insurance, disability insurance |
| **Secured Debt Payments** | Contractual amount | Mortgage, car loans (for property debtor keeps) |
| **Priority Claims** | Full amount | Child support, alimony, tax debts |
| **Administrative Expenses** | Actual | Chapter 13 trustee fee (10%) |

## Step 4: Determine Outcome

### Chapter 7 Presumption of Abuse
- **CMI - Allowed Expenses = Disposable Income**
- Multiply by 60 (months)
- **If = $8,175** (as of 2024): Presumption of abuse arises
- **If < $8,175**: No presumption of abuse

### Chapter 13 Disposable Income
- **CMI - Allowed Expenses = Monthly Disposable Income**
- Must be paid to unsecured creditors for:
  - **36 months** (below-median debtors)
  - **60 months** (above-median debtors)

## Special Circumstances Rebuttal
Debtor may rebut presumption of abuse by showing "special circumstances":
- **Serious medical condition**
- **Active military duty**
- **Other circumstances beyond debtor's control**

Must provide:
- Documentation of circumstance
- Itemization of additional expenses
- Explanation why expenses are reasonable

## Marital Adjustment
- **Non-filing spouse's income**: Excluded from CMI
- **Non-filing spouse's expenses**: May be deducted if spouse contributes to household
- **Marital deduction**: CMI - (non-filing spouse's income not contributed to household)
