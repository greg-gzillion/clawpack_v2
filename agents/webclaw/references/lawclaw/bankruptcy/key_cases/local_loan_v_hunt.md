# Local Loan Co. v. Hunt, 292 U.S. 234 (1934)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 292 U.S. 234 (1934)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: April 30, 1934
**Vote**: 9-0 (Sutherland writing)

## Issue
Whether an assignment of future wages as security for a loan is enforceable after the debtor receives a bankruptcy discharge.

## Facts
- Hunt borrowed $300 from Local Loan Co., assigning future wages as security
- Hunt filed bankruptcy and received discharge
- Local Loan Co. sought to garnish Hunt's wages post-discharge
- Lower courts held assignment unenforceable

## Holding
**No. The assignment of future wages was not enforceable after discharge.** The bankruptcy discharge gives the debtor a "fresh start" unencumbered by pre-petition debts.

## Key Quote
> "One of the primary purposes of the bankruptcy act is to 'relieve the honest debtor from the weight of oppressive indebtedness and permit him to start afresh free from the obligations and responsibilities consequent upon business misfortunes.'"

## Reasoning
1. **Fresh Start Policy**: Bankruptcy designed to give debtors new beginning
2. **Wage Assignments**: Unearned wages are not property that can be assigned pre-petition
3. **Post-Petition Earnings**: Belong to debtor, not pre-petition creditors
4. **Discharge Injunction**: Bars collection of discharged debts from post-petition earnings

## The "Fresh Start" Doctrine
- Debtor entitled to "new opportunity in life and a clear field for future effort"
- Post-petition earnings protected from pre-petition creditors
- Foundation for modern bankruptcy policy

## Distinction: Security Interests vs. Wage Assignments
| Enforceable After Discharge | Not Enforceable After Discharge |
|----------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Consensual liens on existing property | Assignment of future wages |
| Mortgages on real estate | Garnishment of post-petition earnings |
| Security interests in existing collateral | Claims against exempt property |

## Modern Application
- **§ 524(a)**: Discharge injunction protects all post-petition earnings
- **Reaffirmation Agreements**: Narrow exception requiring court approval
- **Chapter 13**: Debtor may voluntarily commit future income to plan
