# Till v. SCS Credit Corp., 541 U.S. 465 (2004)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 541 U.S. 465 (2004)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: May 17, 2004
**Vote**: 5-4 (Stevens writing for plurality)

## Issue
What interest rate should be used when a Chapter 13 debtor "cramdowns" a secured creditor's claim by paying it over time?

## Facts
- Till purchased used truck, financed by SCS Credit Corp. at 21% interest
- Till filed Chapter 13, proposed to pay SCS the truck's value ($4,000) over time
- SCS objected, demanding 21% contract rate
- Lower courts approved 9.5% "prime-plus" rate

## Holding
**The "formula approach" (prime-plus) is proper.** The cramdown interest rate should be the national prime rate plus a risk adjustment (typically 1-3%).

## Key Quote
> "The formula approach begins with the national prime rate and adjusts upward to account for risk... This approach is familiar, objective, and minimizes litigation."

## Four Approaches Considered
| Approach | Description | Court's View |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Contract Rate** | Rate in original agreement | Rejected - gives windfall to secured creditor |
| **Formula (Prime-Plus)** | Prime + risk adjustment (1-3%) | **ADOPTED** (plurality) |
| **Presumptive Contract Rate** | Contract rate unless debtor rebuts | Rejected |
| **Cost of Funds** | Creditor's actual borrowing cost | Rejected - too complex |

## Plurality Reasoning
1. **§ 1325(a)(5)(B)(ii)**: Secured creditor entitled to "value, as of the effective date"
2. **Present Value**: Interest compensates for time value of money, not contract rights
3. **Risk Adjustment**: Debtor's financial condition justifies modest increase over prime
4. **Judicial Efficiency**: Formula approach reduces litigation

## Concurrence (Thomas)
- No plurality opinion on proper rate
- Agreed only that contract rate and cost-of-funds were incorrect

## Dissent (Scalia)
- "Value" means fair market value of the claim, not present value calculation
- Court should not legislate interest rates

## Practical Application
| Chapter | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| **Chapter 13** | § 1325(a)(5) cramdown of secured claims |
| **Chapter 11** | § 1129(b) cramdown uses same Till rate |
| **Chapter 12** | Similar analysis |

## Post-Till Developments
- **1-3% Risk Adjustment**: Typically applied
- **Higher Adjustments**: Permitted if debtor poses unusual risk
- **Chapter 11**: Courts apply Till to individual debtors; split on corporate debtors
