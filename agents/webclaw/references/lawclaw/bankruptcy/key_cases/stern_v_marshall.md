# Stern v. Marshall, 564 U.S. 462 (2011)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 564 U.S. 462 (2011)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: June 23, 2011
**Vote**: 5-4 (Roberts writing for majority)

## Issue
Whether bankruptcy courts have constitutional authority to enter final judgments on state law counterclaims that are not resolved in the process of ruling on a creditor's proof of claim.

## Facts
- Vickie Lynn Marshall (Anna Nicole Smith) filed bankruptcy
- Pierce Marshall filed defamation claim and proof of claim
- Vickie filed counterclaim for tortious interference with expected inheritance
- Bankruptcy court entered final judgment for Vickie on counterclaim ($449 million)
- Pierce appealed, arguing bankruptcy court lacked constitutional authority

## Holding
**No. Bankruptcy courts lack constitutional authority to enter final judgment on state law counterclaims.** Such claims must be adjudicated by Article III courts.

## Key Quote
> "Congress may not bypass Article III simply because a proceeding may have some bearing on a bankruptcy case... The question is whether the action at issue stems from the bankruptcy itself or would necessarily be resolved in the claims allowance process."

## Reasoning
1. **Article III vs. Article I**: Bankruptcy judges are Article I judges without life tenure or salary protection
2. **Public vs. Private Rights**: Adjudication of private rights requires Article III court
3. **Claims Allowance Process**: Counterclaim not necessarily resolved in ruling on proof of claim
4. **Consent**: Parties cannot consent to non-Article III adjudication of Article III claims

## Significance
- **Constitutional Limits**: Bankruptcy courts cannot enter final judgment on "Stern claims"
- **Procedural Fix**: Courts now treat Stern claims as "non-core" or issue proposed findings
- **28 U.S.C. § 157**: Bankruptcy courts may hear but not finally determine certain matters
- **Subsequent Cases**: Executive Benefits Ins. Agency v. Arkison (2014) - Article III review required

## Practical Implications
- Bankruptcy judges issue **proposed findings and conclusions** on Stern claims
- District courts review **de novo**
- Parties may **consent** to final adjudication by bankruptcy court (post-Wellness Int'l)
