# Discharge and Dischargeability - 11 U.S.C. ｧｧ 523, 727, 1328

## Overview
The bankruptcy discharge releases the debtor from personal liability for dischargeable debts and operates as a permanent injunction against collection.

## Chapter 7 Discharge (ｧ 727)
### Grounds for Denial of Discharge
| Ground | Citation | Description |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Fraudulent transfers** | ｧ 727(a)(2) | Transferred/hid property within 1 year with intent to defraud |
| **Inadequate records** | ｧ 727(a)(3) | Failed to keep books/records from which financial condition can be determined |
| **False oath/account** | ｧ 727(a)(4) | Knowingly made false statement under oath |
| **Lost assets** | ｧ 727(a)(5) | Failed to explain loss of assets |
| **Refusal to testify** | ｧ 727(a)(6) | Refused to obey court order or testify |
| **Prior discharge** | ｧ 727(a)(8) | Received Ch. 7 discharge within 8 years |
| **Waiver** | ｧ 727(a)(10) | Waived discharge in writing |

### Revocation of Discharge (ｧ 727(d))
- **Within 1 year**: If discharge obtained through fraud
- **Within 1 year**: If debtor acquired estate property and failed to report

## Chapter 13 Discharge (ｧ 1328)
### Full Compliance Discharge (Broader than Ch. 7)
- **Discharges**:
  - Willful/malicious injury claims
  - Divorce property settlements (not DSOs)
  - Certain tax penalties
  - Condominium/HOA fees
- **Still Non-Dischargeable**:
  - Domestic support obligations
  - Student loans (unless undue hardship)
  - DUI personal injury claims
  - Criminal fines/restitution

### Hardship Discharge (ｧ 1328(b))
Requirements:
1. Debtor's failure to complete plan due to circumstances beyond control
2. Creditors received at least Chapter 7 liquidation value
3. Modification of plan not practicable
4. **Not applicable to Ch. 7 conversion**

## Exceptions to Discharge (ｧ 523)
### Automatically Non-Dischargeable (No adversary proceeding required)
| Exception | Citation | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Certain taxes** | ｧ 523(a)(1) | Priority taxes, recent tax returns |
| **Fraud/misrepresentation** | ｧ 523(a)(2)(A) | Actual fraud, not constructive |
| **Luxury goods/presumptions** | ｧ 523(a)(2)(C) | $800+ luxury goods within 90 days |
| **Unscheduled debts** | ｧ 523(a)(3) | Creditor didn't receive notice |
| **Fraud/fiduciary duty** | ｧ 523(a)(4) | Embezzlement, larceny |
| **Domestic support** | ｧ 523(a)(5) | Alimony, maintenance, support |
| **Willful/malicious injury** | ｧ 523(a)(6) | Intentional torts |

### Requires Adversary Proceeding
| Exception | Citation | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Student loans** | ｧ 523(a)(8) | Unless "undue hardship" (Brunner test) |

## Discharge Injunction (ｧ 524)
- Permanent injunction against collection of discharged debts
- **Violations**: Contempt of court sanctions
- **Reaffirmation**: Debtor may voluntarily repay (ｧ 524(c))

## Key Cases
| Case | Holding |
|------|---------|
| **Grogan v. Garner** (1991) | Preponderance standard for ｧ 523 exceptions |
| **Cohen v. de la Cruz** (1998) | ｧ 523(a)(2) bars discharge of all liability including punitive damages |
| **Bullock v. BankChampaign** (2013) | Defalcation requires intentional wrong or reckless disregard |
| **Brunner v. NY Higher Ed.** (2d Cir. 1987) | Undue hardship test for student loans |
