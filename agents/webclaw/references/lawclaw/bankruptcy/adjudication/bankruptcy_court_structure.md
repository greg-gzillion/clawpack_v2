# Bankruptcy Court Structure and Jurisdiction

## Overview
Bankruptcy courts are units of the federal district courts with exclusive jurisdiction over bankruptcy cases under 28 U.S.C. § 1334.

## Constitutional Status
| Feature | Bankruptcy Judges | Article III Judges |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Appointment** | Courts of Appeals (14-year terms) | President/Senate (life tenure) |
| **Salary Protection** | No constitutional protection | Cannot be diminished |
| **Final Adjudication** | Limited by Stern v. Marshall | Full Article III power |
| **Core Proceedings** | May enter final judgments | N/A |
| **Non-Core/Stern Claims** | Proposed findings only | Final judgment |

## Jurisdiction (28 U.S.C. § 1334)
| Type | Scope | Example |
|------|-------|---------|
| **"Arising Under"** | Claims created by Bankruptcy Code | Preference actions (§ 547), fraudulent transfers (§ 548) |
| **"Arising In"** | Administrative matters unique to bankruptcy | Allowance of fees, turnover motions |
| **"Related To"** | Non-bankruptcy claims affecting estate | Breach of contract claims, tort claims |

## Core vs. Non-Core Proceedings (28 U.S.C. § 157)
| Core Proceedings (Bankruptcy court may enter final judgment) | Non-Core/Stern Claims (Proposed findings only) |
|-------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Allowance/disallowance of claims | State law contract claims |
| Avoidance of preferences (§ 547) | Personal injury tort claims |
| Fraudulent transfers (§ 548) | Wrongful death claims |
| Automatic stay violations (§ 362(k)) | Claims against non-debtors |
| Plan confirmation | Claims not resolved in claims allowance |
| Use/sale/lease of estate property | Stern v. Marshall counterclaims |

## Bankruptcy Appellate Panels (BAPs)
- **Optional** in circuits that have established them (1st, 6th, 8th, 9th, 10th)
- **3-judge panels** of bankruptcy judges from other districts
- **Appeal directly to Circuit** if parties consent
- **No BAP**: Appeal directly to District Court

## Withdrawal of Reference (28 U.S.C. § 157(d))
District court may withdraw reference for:
- **Mandatory Withdrawal**: Resolution requires consideration of both bankruptcy law and other federal laws
- **Permissive Withdrawal**: "Cause shown" (jury trial right, judicial economy)
