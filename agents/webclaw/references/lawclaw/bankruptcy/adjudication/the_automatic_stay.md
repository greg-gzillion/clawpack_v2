# The Automatic Stay - 11 U.S.C. § 362

## Overview
The automatic stay is an injunction that arises automatically upon filing a bankruptcy petition. It stops most collection actions against the debtor and estate property.

## Scope of Stay (§ 362(a))
The stay prohibits:
| Action | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **Judicial proceedings** | Lawsuits, arbitrations, administrative proceedings |
| **Enforcement of judgments** | Garnishments, levies, execution |
| **Acts to obtain possession** | Foreclosure, repossession, eviction |
| **Acts to create/perfect liens** | Recording judgment liens, UCC filings |
| **Setoff** | Bank account freezes, withholding funds |
| **Tax Court proceedings** | Pre-petition tax years |

## Exceptions to Stay (§ 362(b))
The stay does NOT apply to:
| Exception | Citation |
|-----------|----------|
| **Criminal proceedings** | § 362(b)(1) |
| **Domestic support obligations** (from post-petition income) | § 362(b)(2)(B) |
| **Tax audits/assessments** (but not collection) | § 362(b)(9) |
| **Regulatory enforcement** (police power) | § 362(b)(4) |
| **Eviction where landlord obtained judgment pre-petition** | § 362(b)(22) |
| **Eviction for endangerment/drugs** | § 362(b)(23) |

## Duration of Stay
| Event | Stay Terminates |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Property no longer estate property** | When property leaves estate (sale, abandonment) |
| **Case closed/dismissed** | Immediately |
| **Discharge granted** | Discharge injunction replaces stay (§ 524) |
| **Relief from stay granted** | As ordered by court |

## Relief from Stay (§ 362(d))
Court shall grant relief for "cause," including:
- **Lack of adequate protection** of secured creditor's interest
- **No equity in property** and property not necessary for reorganization
- **Single asset real estate**: Specific timelines apply (§ 362(d)(3))

### Adequate Protection (§ 361)
Secured creditor entitled to protection against decrease in collateral value:
- **Cash payments** to cover depreciation
- **Additional/replacement liens**
- **Other relief** providing "indubitable equivalent"

## Violations of Stay (§ 362(k))
- **Individual debtor** may recover:
  - **Actual damages**
  - **Punitive damages** (if willful)
  - **Attorney's fees and costs**
- **Willful violation**: Creditor knew of stay and intentionally acted

## Key Cases
| Case | Holding |
|------|---------|
| **FCC v. NextWave** (2003) | Regulatory license revocation violates stay |
| **Citizens Bank v. Strumpf** (1995) | Administrative freeze not a setoff violation |
| **Weber v. SEFCU** (2d Cir. 2013) | Post-petition account freeze violates stay |
