# Bankruptcy Exemptions - Federal and State

## Overview
Exemptions allow debtors to protect certain property from liquidation in bankruptcy. Debtors may choose between federal exemptions (ｧ 522(d)) and state exemptions unless the state has "opted out."

## Federal Exemptions (ｧ 522(d))
| Exemption | Amount (as of 2024) | Description |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Homestead** | $27,900 | Real or personal property used as residence |
| **Motor Vehicle** | $4,450 | One motor vehicle |
| **Household Goods** | $700 per item / $14,875 aggregate | Furniture, appliances, clothing, books |
| **Jewelry** | $1,875 | Jewelry held primarily for personal/family use |
| **Tools of Trade** | $2,800 | Implements, professional books, tools of trade |
| **Wildcard** | $1,475 + unused homestead (up to $13,950) | Any property |
| **Health Aids** | Unlimited | Prescribed health aids |
| **Life Insurance** | Unlimited (loan value up to $14,875) | Unmatured life insurance contract |
| **Retirement Funds** | Unlimited | Tax-exempt retirement accounts (ｧ 522(b)(3)(C)) |
| **Personal Injury** | $27,900 | Recovery for personal bodily injury (excluding pain/suffering) |
| **Lost Earnings** | Varies | Reasonably necessary support payments |

*Amounts adjust every 3 years (next adjustment April 2025)*

## Key State Exemption Highlights
| State | Homestead | Wildcard | Notes |
|-------|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Texas** | Unlimited (urban: 10 acres; rural: 100-200 acres) | $50,000+ | No federal choice; extremely generous |
| **Florida** | Unlimited (1/2 acre urban; 160 acres rural) | $5,000 | Must own 1,215+ days for full protection |
| **California** | $300,000-$600,000 (varies by county) | $31,950 | Two systems: System 1 (ｧ 704) or System 2 (ｧ 703) |
| **New York** | $179,975-$358,950 (varies by county) | $11,975 | Homeowner vs. non-homeowner exemptions |
| **Colorado** | $105,000 (age 60+/disabled) / $75,000 (others) | None | Separate wildcard for some property |

## Tenancy by the Entirety
In some states, property held as "tenants by the entirety" is exempt from individual debtor's creditors:
- **Available in**: Alaska, Arkansas, Delaware, Florida, Hawaii, Illinois, Indiana, Kentucky, Maryland, Massachusetts, Michigan, Mississippi, Missouri, New Jersey, New York, North Carolina, Ohio, Oklahoma, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, Tennessee, Vermont, Virginia, Wyoming

## Retirement Account Exemption (ｧ 522(b)(3)(C))
- **Unlimited exemption** for tax-exempt retirement accounts:
  - 401(k), 403(b)
  - IRA (Roth and Traditional) - capped at $1,512,350 (as of 2024, adjusts triennially)
  - SEP and SIMPLE IRAs
  - Defined benefit plans
  - 457 plans

## Wildcard Exemption (ｧ 522(d)(5))
- $1,475 + up to $13,950 of unused homestead exemption
- Can be applied to any property
- Often used to protect cash, tax refunds, or additional vehicle equity

## Avoiding Exemption Issues
- **Domicile Requirement**: Use exemptions of state where debtor lived for 730 days pre-filing (ｧ 522(b)(3)(A))
- **If moved recently**: Use exemptions of state where debtor lived for majority of 180 days preceding the 730-day period
- **Homestead Cap**: $189,050 (as of 2024) if property acquired within 1,215 days pre-filing (ｧ 522(p))
