# Chapter 13 Bankruptcy - Wage Earner Plan

## Overview
Chapter 13 allows individuals with regular income to propose a repayment plan to pay creditors over 3-5 years while keeping their assets.

## Eligibility (§ 109(e))
- **Only Individuals**: No corporations or partnerships
- **Regular Income**: Wages, self-employment, benefits, rental income
- **Debt Limits** (adjusted triennially):
  - Unsecured debts: < $465,275
  - Secured debts: < $1,395,875

## The Chapter 13 Process
1. **File Petition**: Including proposed plan
2. **Automatic Stay**: Protects debtor and co-debtors (§ 1301)
3. **Trustee Appointed**: Standing Chapter 13 trustee
4. **§ 341 Meeting**: Debtor attends, trustee questions
5. **Plan Confirmation**: Court approves plan (§ 1325)
6. **Plan Payments**: Debtor makes payments to trustee (3-5 years)
7. **Discharge**: Upon completion of plan payments

## Plan Requirements (§ 1325)
- **Good Faith**: Plan proposed in good faith
- **Feasibility**: Debtor can make payments
- **Best Interests Test**: Unsecured creditors receive = Chapter 7 liquidation value
- **Disposable Income**: All projected disposable income devoted to plan
- **Secured Claims**: Must provide for retention of lien and payment of claim value

## Disposable Income (Form 122C)
- **Above-Median Debtors**: 5-year plan commitment period
- **Below-Median Debtors**: 3-year plan commitment period
- **Calculation**: Current Monthly Income minus allowed expenses

## Discharge (§ 1328)
- **Full Compliance Discharge**: Broader than Chapter 7
  - Discharges: Some debts non-dischargeable in Ch. 7 (willful injury, divorce property settlements)
- **Hardship Discharge**: If debtor cannot complete plan due to circumstances beyond control
- **Non-Dischargeable**: Certain taxes, domestic support, student loans, DUI claims

## Advantages Over Chapter 7
| Chapter 13 | Chapter 7 |
|------------|-----------|
| Keep all assets | Lose non-exempt assets |
| Cure mortgage arrears | Foreclosure only temporarily delayed |
| Strip wholly unsecured junior liens | Liens survive bankruptcy |
| Co-debtor stay protects co-signers | No co-debtor protection |
| Broader discharge | Narrower discharge |
