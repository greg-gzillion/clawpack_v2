# Chapter 7 Bankruptcy - Liquidation

## Overview
Chapter 7 provides for liquidation of the debtor's non-exempt assets by a trustee, with proceeds distributed to creditors. Most individual debtors receive a discharge of dischargeable debts.

## Eligibility
- **Individuals**: Must pass "means test" (income below state median or no disposable income)
- **Businesses**: Any entity eligible (corporations, LLCs, partnerships)
- **Exceptions**: Railroads, insurance companies, banks, credit unions (other statutes govern)

## The Chapter 7 Process
1. **File Petition**: Voluntary or involuntary
2. **Automatic Stay**: Immediate protection (§ 362)
3. **Trustee Appointed**: Interim trustee from panel
4. **§ 341 Meeting**: Meeting of creditors (debtor must attend)
5. **Trustee Administers Estate**: Liquidates non-exempt assets
6. **Distribution**: Pro rata to creditors per priority scheme
7. **Discharge**: Usually 60-90 days after § 341 meeting
8. **Case Closed**: Trustee files final report

## Exemptions
Debtors may keep certain property:
- **Federal Exemptions** (§ 522(d)): Homestead, vehicle, household goods, tools of trade
- **State Exemptions**: States may "opt out" of federal exemptions
- **Wildcard Exemption**: Up to $1,475 plus unused homestead (amounts adjust triennially)

## Means Test (Form 122A)
- **Presumption of Abuse**: If debtor's income exceeds state median and disposable income > certain threshold
- **Consequences**: Case may be dismissed or converted to Chapter 13

## Dischargeable vs. Non-Dischargeable Debts
| Dischargeable | Non-Dischargeable (§ 523) |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| Credit card debt | Certain taxes |
| Medical bills | Student loans (unless undue hardship) |
| Personal loans | Domestic support obligations |
| Business debts | Fraud/deception debts |
| Deficiency judgments | Willful/malicious injury |
| | Fines/penalties to government |
