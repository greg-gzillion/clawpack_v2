# Trusts and Estates Law - Charitable Trusts

## Charitable Trust Requirements
| Element | Description | URL |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Charitable Purpose | Relief of poverty; advancement of education/religion; promotion of health; governmental purposes | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/charitable_trust |
| Indefinite Beneficiaries | Benefit must be to community or substantial class | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/charitable_trust |
| Perpetual Duration | Rule Against Perpetuities does not apply | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/charitable_trust |
| Cy Pres Doctrine | Court may modify trust when purpose impossible/impracticable | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/cy_pres_doctrine |

## Cy Pres Doctrine
| Element | Description | URL |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Definition | "As near as possible" - modification of charitable trust | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/cy_pres_doctrine |
| General Charitable Intent | Settlor had general charitable intent (not specific) | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/cy_pres_doctrine |
| Impracticability | Original purpose impossible, impracticable, or illegal | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/cy_pres_doctrine |
| UTC § 413 | Cy pres authorized without finding general charitable intent | https://www.uniformlaws.org/acts/trust-code |

## Charitable Trust Enforcement
| Party | Standing | Description | URL |
|-------|----------|-------------|-----|
| Attorney General | Primary enforcer | Represents public interest in charitable trusts | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/charitable_trust |
| Settlor | Standing to enforce | May bring action to enforce trust | https://www.uniformlaws.org/acts/trust-code |
| Beneficiary | Limited standing | Generally no standing unless special interest | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/charitable_trust |
| Co-Trustee | Standing | May bring action against co-trustee | https://www.uniformlaws.org/acts/trust-code |

## Types of Charitable Trusts
| Type | Description | URL |
|------|-------------|-----|

| Charitable Lead Trust (CLT) | Income to charity; remainder to non-charitable beneficiaries | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/charitable_lead_trust |
