# Legal Ethics - Advertising and Solicitation (Rules 7.1-7.5)

## Rule 7.1: Communications Concerning a Lawyer's Services
| Topic | Description | URL |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| False or Misleading Prohibited | No false or misleading communications | https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/rule_7_1_communications_concerning_a_lawyers_services/ |
| Material Misrepresentation | No material misrepresentation of fact or law | Varies |
| Unsubstantiated Comparisons | Cannot make unsubstantiated comparisons to other lawyers | Varies |
| Misleading Omissions | Omission of necessary fact may be misleading | Varies |
| Unverified Results | Cannot promise or imply specific results | Varies |
| Specialization Claims | "Specialist" claim requires certification by approved organization | Varies |

## Rule 7.2: Advertising
| Topic | Description | URL |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Permitted Advertising | May advertise through written, recorded, or electronic communication | https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/rule_7_2_advertising/ |
| Payment for Recommendations | Cannot give anything of value for recommending services except: (1) reasonable advertising costs; (2) non-profit referral service; (3) law practice sale | Varies |
| Lead Generation Services | Must comply with rules; cannot pay for leads that are recommendations | Varies |
| Reciprocal Referral Agreements | Permitted if not exclusive and client informed | Varies |
| Record Retention | Some states require copies of advertisements for specified period | Varies |

## Rule 7.3: Solicitation of Clients
| Topic | Description | URL |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Definition | Direct contact with specific person to offer representation | https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/rule_7_3_solicitation_of_clients/ |
| In-Person Solicitation | Prohibited unless: (1) other lawyer; (2) family/close personal relationship; (3) prior professional relationship | Varies |
| Live Telephone Solicitation | Prohibited unless same exceptions as in-person | Varies |
| Written Solicitation | Permitted if not false/misleading; must include "Advertising Material" | Varies |
| Targeted Direct Mail | Permitted with proper labeling | Varies |
| 30-Day Accident Rule | Some states prohibit solicitation within 30 days of accident/disaster | State law |
| Real-Time Electronic Contact | Generally treated like live telephone (prohibited unless exception) | Varies |

## Rule 7.4: Communication of Fields of Practice
| Topic | Description | URL |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Field of Practice | May communicate areas of practice | https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/rule_7_4_communication_of_fields_of_practice_and_specialization/ |
| Certified Specialist | May state certified specialist if: (1) approved organization; (2) organization identified | Varies |
| Patent/Trademark/Admiralty | May designate practice in these areas | Varies |

## Rule 7.5: Firm Names and Letterheads
| Topic | Description | URL |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Trade Names | Permitted if not misleading | https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/rule_7_5_firm_names_and_letterheads/ |
| Deceased/Retired Partners | May use name if there has been a successor or the name has been in the firm name for a substantial period of time | Varies |
| Public Office Names | Cannot imply connection with government agency | Varies |
| "Of Counsel" | Permitted for close, ongoing relationship | Varies |
| Multistate Firms | May use same name in each jurisdiction if not misleading | Varies |
| Domain Names | Subject to Rule 7.1 (not misleading) | Varies |

## Key Constitutional Cases on Lawyer Advertising
| Case | Citation | Holding | URL |
|------|----------|---------|-----|
| Bates v. State Bar of Arizona | 433 U.S. 350 (1977) | Lawyer advertising is commercial speech protected by First Amendment | https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/433/350/ |
| Ohralik v. Ohio State Bar Ass'n | 436 U.S. 447 (1978) | In-person solicitation may be prohibited | https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/436/447/ |
| Shapero v. Kentucky Bar Ass'n | 486 U.S. 466 (1988) | Targeted direct mail solicitation is protected | https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/486/466/ |
| Florida Bar v. Went For It, Inc. | 515 U.S. 618 (1995) | 30-day ban on accident victim solicitation constitutional | https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/515/618/ |
