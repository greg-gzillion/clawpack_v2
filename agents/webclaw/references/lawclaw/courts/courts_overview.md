# Courts - Overview

## The U.S. Court System
The United States has a dual court system consisting of federal courts and state courts. Each system has its own jurisdiction, structure, and procedures.

## Dual Court System
| System | Jurisdiction | URL |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| Federal Courts | Federal questions, diversity, U.S. as party | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/federal_courts |
| State Courts | General jurisdiction; most cases | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/state_courts |
| Concurrent Jurisdiction | Both federal and state courts may hear | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/concurrent_jurisdiction |
