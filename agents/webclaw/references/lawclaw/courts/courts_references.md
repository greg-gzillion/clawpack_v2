## Federal Court System

### Supreme Court of the United States (SCOTUS)
- **Website**: https://www.supremecourt.gov
- **Jurisdiction**: Final appellate authority
- **Composition**: 9 Justices (1 Chief, 8 Associate)
- **Term**: First Monday in October to late June/early July
- **Certiorari**: Requires 4 votes to grant review
- **Decisions**: Binding precedent nationwide
- **Notable Cases**:
  - Marbury v. Madison (1803) - Judicial review
  - Brown v. Board of Education (1954) - Desegregation
  - Roe v. Wade (1973) - Abortion rights
  - Citizens United v. FEC (2010) - Campaign finance

### District Courts (94 Districts)
- **Structure**: At least one district per state
- **Largest**: Central District of California (27 judges)
- **Smallest**: District of North Dakota (1 judge)
- **Bankruptcy Courts**: Adjunct to district courts
- **Magistrate Judges**: Assist district judges

## Specialized Federal Courts

### US Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit
- **Jurisdiction**: Patents, international trade, federal claims, veterans claims
- **Location**: Washington, DC
- **Judges**: 12 active, senior judges
- **Appeals from**: Patent Trial and Appeal Board, Court of International Trade, Court of Federal Claims

### US Court of International Trade
- **Jurisdiction**: Customs and international trade disputes
- **Location**: New York City
- **Judges**: 9
- **Appeals**: Federal Circuit

### US Court of Federal Claims
- **Jurisdiction**: Monetary claims against US government
- **Location**: Washington, DC
- **Judges**: 16
- **Appeals**: Federal Circuit

### US Tax Court
- **Jurisdiction**: Federal tax disputes before payment
- **Location**: Washington, DC (nationwide sessions)
- **Judges**: 19
- **Appeals**: Circuit where taxpayer resides

### US Court of Appeals for Veterans Claims
- **Jurisdiction**: VA benefits decisions
- **Location**: Washington, DC
- **Judges**: 7
- **Appeals**: Federal Circuit

### US Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces (CAAF)
- **Jurisdiction**: Military appeals
- **Location**: Washington, DC
- **Judges**: 5 civilian
- **Appeals**: Supreme Court

## State Court Systems

### Structure (Typical - varies by state)
1. **Courts of Last Resort** (State Supreme Courts)
   - Final authority on state law
   - 50 state supreme courts + DC, Puerto Rico
   - Usually 5-9 justices

2. **Intermediate Appellate Courts**
   - 40 states have intermediate appeals
   - Mandatory jurisdiction
   - 3-judge panels typically

3. **Trial Courts of General Jurisdiction**
   - Major civil/criminal cases
   - Called Superior Court, Circuit Court, District Court
   - Jury trials available

4. **Limited Jurisdiction Courts**
   - Small claims (typically <,000)
   - Probate courts (wills, estates)
   - Family courts (divorce, custody, adoption)
   - Juvenile courts (minors)
   - Traffic courts
   - Municipal courts (city ordinances)

### Notable State Courts
- **Delaware Court of Chancery**: Business/commercial law
- **Texas Court of Criminal Appeals**: Highest for criminal cases
- **Oklahoma Court of Criminal Appeals**: Separate from civil
- **New York Court of Appeals**: Highest court (unusual naming)
- **California Supreme Court**: Largest state jurisdiction

## Tribal Courts

### Overview
- **Sovereign authority**: Tribal nations have inherent sovereignty
- **Jurisdiction**: Tribal lands and members
- **Types**:
  - Traditional courts (customary law)
  - CFR courts (Code of Federal Regulations)
  - Peacemaker courts (restorative justice)

### Notable Tribal Courts
- **Navajo Nation Supreme Court**: Largest tribal court system
- **Cherokee Nation Judicial Branch**: Comprehensive system
- **Oklahoma Tribal Courts**: Many after McGirt v. Oklahoma

### Key Legislation
- **Indian Civil Rights Act (1968)**: Due process protections
- **Tribal Law and Order Act (2010)**: Enhanced sentencing
- **VAWA 2013**: Tribal jurisdiction over non-Indians

## Military Courts

### Court-Martial Types
1. **Summary Court-Martial**: Minor offenses (1 officer)
2. **Special Court-Martial**: Misdemeanor (3 members + military judge)
3. **General Court-Martial**: Felony (5+ members + military judge)

### Appeals Process
1. **Convening Authority**: Commander review
2. **Court of Criminal Appeals**: Each branch has one
3. **Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces (CAAF)**
4. **Supreme Court** (certiorari)

### Uniform Code of Military Justice (UCMJ)
- 134 articles
- Governs all service members
- Separate from civilian law

## Administrative Courts & Tribunals

### Administrative Law Judges (ALJs)
- **Agency adjudication**: Social Security, OSHA, NLRB, FCC
- **Appeals**: Agency head → Federal court
- **Due process**: Required for benefits termination

### Major Administrative Courts

- **National Labor Relations Board (NLRB)**: Labor disputes
- **Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC)**: Discrimination
- **Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC)**: Energy regulation
- **Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)**: Administrative proceedings

## Court Procedures

### Civil Procedure (Federal Rules)
- **Filing**: Complaint, summons
- **Service of process**: Defendant notification
- **Motions**: Rule 12(b) dismissals, Rule 56 summary judgment
- **Discovery**: Depositions, interrogatories, document requests
- **Trial**: Jury selection, evidence, verdict
- **Judgment**: Final determination
- **Appeal**: Notice within 30 days

### Criminal Procedure (Federal Rules)
- **Initial appearance**: Bail determination
- **Preliminary hearing**: Probable cause
- **Grand jury**: Indictment (felonies)
- **Arraignment**: Enter plea
- **Discovery**: Evidence exchange
- **Trial**: Speedy trial right (6th Amendment)
- **Sentencing**: Guidelines (USSC)
- **Appeal**: Direct appeal, habeas corpus

## Best Practices

### For Litigators
1. Know local court rules (often differ from federal)
2. Check judge's standing orders
3. File electronically when possible
4. Use proper citation format (Bluebook)
5. Meet all deadlines strictly

### For Pro Se Litigants
1. Visit court self-help center
2. Review court website resources
3. Consider legal aid if eligible
4. Understand filing fees
5. Follow formatting rules exactly

### For Researchers
1. Use multiple research platforms
2. Shepardize/KeyCite all cases
3. Check for subsequent history
4. Note procedural posture
5. Update with recent decisions
