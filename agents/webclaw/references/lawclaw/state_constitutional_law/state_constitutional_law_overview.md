# State Constitutional Law - Overview

## What is State Constitutional Law?
State constitutional law governs the interpretation and application of state constitutions, which are the fundamental governing documents of each state. State constitutions often provide greater individual rights protections than the federal Constitution, and state courts may interpret their state constitutions independently of federal constitutional law under the doctrine of independent state grounds.

## Key Principles of State Constitutional Law
| Principle | Description | URL |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Independent State Grounds | State courts may interpret state constitutions to provide greater rights than federal Constitution | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/independent_and_adequate_state_grounds |
| Federal Floor | Federal Constitution provides minimum protections; states may provide more | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/federal_floor |
| Lockstep Doctrine | Some states interpret state constitutional provisions identically to federal counterparts | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/lockstep_doctrine |
| Primacy of Text | State courts focus on text and original meaning of state constitution | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/constitutional_interpretation |
