# Motor Vehicle Manufacturers Ass'n v. State Farm Mutual Auto. Ins. Co., 463 U.S. 29 (1983)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 463 U.S. 29 (1983)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: June 24, 1983
**Vote**: 5-4 (White writing for majority)

## Issue
Standard for reviewing agency rescission or modification of existing rules.

## Facts
- NHTSA required passive restraints (airbags or automatic seatbelts)
- Agency rescinded requirement after election
- State Farm challenged rescission as arbitrary and capricious

## Holding
Agency rescission of rule subject to same "arbitrary and capricious" review as rule promulgation. Agency must provide reasoned explanation for policy change.

## Key Principles
1. **Same Standard Applies**: Rescission reviewed under same standard as promulgation
2. **Reasoned Explanation Required**: Agency must explain policy reversal
3. **Consider Alternatives**: Agency must consider obvious alternatives
4. **Factual Basis**: Must be supported by record evidence

## Key Quote
> "An agency changing its course must supply a reasoned analysis... [and] must examine the relevant data and articulate a satisfactory explanation for its action including a rational connection between the facts found and the choice made."

## Significance
- Prevents agencies from changing policies based solely on political shifts
- Requires empirical or reasoned justification for reversals
- Applied to Trump, Biden, and future administration policy reversals
- **FCC v. Fox Television Stations (2009)** refined but didn't overturn

## The "State Farm" Factors for Agency Policy Change
1. Acknowledge policy change
2. Provide reasoned explanation
3. Consider reliance interests
4. Address factual premises of prior policy
5. Consider obvious alternatives

## Key Cases Applying State Farm
- **FCC v. Fox Television Stations (2009)** - No heightened standard for policy changes
- **Encino Motorcars v. Navarro (2016)** - Inadequate explanation for reversal
- **DHS v. Regents (2020)** - DACA rescission arbitrary and capricious
