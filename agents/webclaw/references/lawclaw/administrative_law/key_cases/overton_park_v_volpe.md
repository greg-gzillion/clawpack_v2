# Citizens to Preserve Overton Park v. Volpe, 401 U.S. 402 (1971)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 401 U.S. 402 (1971)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: March 2, 1971
**Vote**: 6-2 (Marshall writing, Black and Brennan concurring)

## Issue
Standard of judicial review for informal agency action under APA § 706.

## Facts
- Secretary of Transportation approved highway through Overton Park (Memphis)
- Statute prohibited highways through parks if "feasible and prudent" alternative exists
- Citizens challenged, seeking judicial review

## Holding
Informal agency action reviewed under "arbitrary and capricious" standard, requiring "hard look" at agency reasoning.

## Key Principles Established
1. **Presumption of Reviewability**: Agency action presumptively reviewable
2. **Hard Look Doctrine**: Court must engage in "thorough, probing, in-depth review"
3. **Record-Based Review**: Review confined to administrative record
4. **Remand for Explanation**: Inadequate record requires remand, not de novo fact-finding

## The "Hard Look" Standard
- Court examines whether agency:
  - Considered relevant factors
  - Made clear error of judgment
  - Articulated rational connection between facts and decision

## Key Quote
> "The court must consider whether the decision was based on a consideration of the relevant factors and whether there has been a clear error of judgment... Although this inquiry into the facts is to be searching and careful, the ultimate standard of review is a narrow one."

## Significance
- Foundation of modern arbitrary/capricious review
- Balanced deference with meaningful judicial oversight
- Template for environmental and administrative litigation
- Cited in thousands of APA cases
