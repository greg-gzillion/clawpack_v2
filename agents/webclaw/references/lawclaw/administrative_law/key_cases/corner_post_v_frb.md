# Corner Post, Inc. v. Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System, 144 S. Ct. 2440 (2024)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 144 S. Ct. 2440 (2024)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: July 1, 2024
**Vote**: 6-3 (Barrett writing for majority, Jackson dissenting)

## Issue
When does the 6-year statute of limitations for APA claims begin to run?

## Facts
- Federal Reserve issued Regulation II in 2011 setting debit card interchange fees
- Corner Post incorporated in 2017, began accepting debit cards
- Corner Post sued in 2021 challenging Regulation II
- Lower courts dismissed as time-barred (more than 6 years after regulation issued)

## Holding
**APA's 6-year statute of limitations (28 U.S.C. ｧ 2401(a)) runs from when plaintiff is injured, not when agency acts.** New businesses can challenge old regulations.

## Key Quote
> "An APA plaintiff's claim accrues when the plaintiff is injured by final agency action, not when the agency acts... The limitations period begins to run when the plaintiff has the right to bring suit."

## Significance
- **Massive Expansion of APA Review**: Any new business can challenge old regulations
- **Regulatory Uncertainty**: Decades-old rules now vulnerable to challenge
- **Industry-Specific Impact**: Financial services, environmental, healthcare rules all exposed
- **Forum Shopping**: May encourage incorporation to reset limitations clock

## The Statute
- **28 U.S.C. ｧ 2401(a)**: "Every civil action commenced against the United States shall be barred unless the complaint is filed within six years after the right of action first accrues."

## Before Corner Post
- Clock started when regulation became final (for everyone)
- Facial challenges had to be brought within 6 years
- As-applied challenges accrued at time of application

## After Corner Post
- Clock starts when plaintiff is first injured
- New market entrants can challenge old rules
- Potentially unlimited window for regulatory challenges

## Practical Implications
- Financial institutions: Challenge Regulation II (interchange fees)
- Environmental groups: Challenge old EPA rules
- Healthcare providers: Challenge CMS regulations
- Startups: Challenge incumbent-favoring regulations

## Combined with Loper Bright
- **Loper Bright**: Courts exercise independent judgment (no Chevron deference)
- **Corner Post**: Old regulations can be challenged by new plaintiffs
- **Result**: Perfect storm for regulatory litigation explosion
