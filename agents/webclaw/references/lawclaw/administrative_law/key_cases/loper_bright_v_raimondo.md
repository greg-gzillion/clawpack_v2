# Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo, 144 S. Ct. 2244 (2024)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 144 S. Ct. 2244 (2024)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: June 28, 2024
**Vote**: 6-3 (Roberts writing for majority, Kagan dissenting)

## Issue
Whether Chevron deference should be overruled.

## Facts
- Magnuson-Stevens Act required fishing vessels to carry federal observers
- NMFS interpreted statute to require industry funding of observers
- Fishermen challenged, arguing agency interpretation exceeded authority

## Holding
**Chevron is OVERRULED.** Courts must exercise independent judgment in statutory interpretation. Agency interpretations may be persuasive (Skidmore) but not binding.

## Key Quote
> "Chevron is overruled. Courts must exercise their independent judgment in deciding whether an agency has acted within its statutory authority... The deference that Chevron requires of courts reviewing agency action cannot be squared with the APA."

## Reasoning
1. **APA § 706**: Courts shall "decide all relevant questions of law"
2. **Article III**: Judicial power to "say what the law is" (Marbury v. Madison)
3. **Separation of Powers**: Agencies execute, courts interpret
4. **Chevron's Failures**: Unworkable, unpredictable, encourages agency overreach

## What Survives
- **Skidmore Deference**: Agency interpretations persuasive based on thoroughness, validity, consistency
- **Auer/Kisor Deference**: Agency interpretations of own regulations (narrowly)
- **State Farm**: Arbitrary/capricious review of policy decisions
- **Major Questions Doctrine**: Clear congressional authorization for major actions

## What Changes
| Before (Chevron) | After (Loper Bright) |
|------------------|---------------------|
| Deference to reasonable agency interpretations | Independent judicial judgment |
| Agency fills statutory gaps | Courts interpret statutes |
| Two-step framework | Best reading of statute |

## Practical Implications
- More aggressive statutory challenges to agency rules
- Courts may strike down more agency interpretations
- Congress must draft more precisely
- Agency guidance less authoritative
