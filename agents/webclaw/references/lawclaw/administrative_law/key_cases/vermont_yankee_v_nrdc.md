# Vermont Yankee Nuclear Power Corp. v. NRDC, 435 U.S. 519 (1978)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 435 U.S. 519 (1978)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: April 3, 1978
**Vote**: 9-0

## Issue
Whether courts may impose additional procedural requirements on agency rulemaking beyond those in the APA.

## Facts
- NRC conducted rulemaking on nuclear waste environmental effects
- D.C. Circuit vacated rule for inadequate procedures
- Court required more than APA notice-and-comment minimum

## Holding
**No. Courts cannot impose extra procedural requirements on agency rulemaking beyond the APA.**

## Key Quote
> "Agencies are free to grant additional procedural rights in the exercise of their discretion, but reviewing courts are generally not free to impose them if the agencies have not chosen to grant them."

## Significance
- **Anti-judicial activism** in administrative procedure
- Agencies control their own procedures (within APA)
- Courts review outcomes, not process design
- Distinguished between rulemaking and adjudication procedures

## Relationship to Other Cases
- **Chenery I & II**: Courts review reasons given by agency, cannot supply new ones
- **State Farm (1983)**: Hard look at agency reasoning, but not procedures
- **Perez v. Mortgage Bankers (2015)**: Interpretive rules exempt from notice-and-comment

## Practical Implications
- Challengers cannot demand extra procedures APA doesn't require
- Focus on substantive arbitrariness, not procedural inadequacy
- Agencies may voluntarily add procedures, but courts won't mandate them
