# Congressional Review Act (CRA) - 5 U.S.C. ｧｧ 801-808

## Overview
The Congressional Review Act gives Congress expedited procedures to disapprove federal agency rules. Rules can be overturned by joint resolution within 60 legislative days.

## Key Provisions

### Rule Submission - ｧ 801(a)
Agencies must submit to Congress and GAO:
1. Copy of final rule
2. Concise general statement of basis and purpose
3. Complete cost-benefit analysis
4. Agency actions under RFA and Unfunded Mandates Reform Act

### GAO Report - ｧ 801(a)(2)
GAO must provide report to Congress within 15 days on whether rule is "major."

### Major Rule Definition - ｧ 804(2)
Rule is "major" if it results in:
- Annual effect on economy = $100 million
- Major increase in costs/prices for consumers, industries, government
- Significant adverse effects on competition, employment, investment, productivity, innovation

### Disapproval Procedure - ｧ 802
- Joint resolution of disapproval introduced
- Fast-track procedures (no filibuster in Senate)
- Simple majority vote in both chambers
- Presidential signature required (or veto override)

### Effect of Disapproval - ｧ 801(b)
- Rule "shall not take effect" (or shall be treated as never having taken effect)
- Agency may not reissue rule in "substantially same form"
- No judicial review of congressional determination

### Lookback Period - ｧ 801(d)
- Rules submitted after June 1 may be reviewed by next Congress
- "Midnight rules" vulnerable to disapproval by incoming administration

## Key Cases
- **Center for Biological Diversity v. Bernhardt (9th Cir. 2020)** - CRA applies to agency actions beyond rules
- **Maine v. Wheeler (D. Me. 2022)** - CRA submission triggers mandatory deadlines
- **In re CRA Challenge (D.C. Cir.)**: Judicial review of CRA determinations limited

## Strategic Implications
- Rules submitted late in administration vulnerable
- Major rules have 60-day delay before effective date
- Agency cannot circumvent by issuing "substantially similar" rule
- Applies to guidance documents if determined to be rules
