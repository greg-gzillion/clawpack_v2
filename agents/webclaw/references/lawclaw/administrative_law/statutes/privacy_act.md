# Privacy Act of 1974 - 5 U.S.C. § 552a

## Overview
The Privacy Act regulates federal agencies' collection, maintenance, use, and dissemination of personal information about U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents.

## Key Provisions
- **§ 552a(b)**: Conditions of disclosure - no disclosure without written consent (12 exceptions)
- **§ 552a(d)**: Access to records - individuals can access and amend their records
- **§ 552a(e)**: Agency requirements - collect directly from individual, maintain accuracy, publish notices
- **§ 552a(g)**: Civil remedies - sue for violations, actual damages, attorney fees
- **§ 552a(i)**: Criminal penalties - misdemeanor for willful violations

## The 12 Disclosure Exceptions (§ 552a(b))
1. Agency employees with need to know
2. FOIA disclosure required
3. Routine use (published in Federal Register)
4. Census Bureau
5. Statistical research (non-identifiable)
6. National Archives
7. Law enforcement request
8. Health/safety emergency
9. Congress
10. GAO oversight
11. Court order
12. Debt collection

## Individual Rights
- **Right to Access**: Obtain copies of records about oneself
- **Right to Amend**: Request correction of inaccurate records
- **Right to Accounting**: Know who accessed your records
- **Right to Sue**: Civil remedies for violations

## Agency Obligations
- Publish System of Records Notices (SORNs) in Federal Register
- Maintain only relevant and necessary information
- Collect information directly from individual when possible
- Ensure accuracy, relevance, timeliness, completeness
- Maintain accounting of disclosures

## Key Cases
- **DOE v. Reporters Committee (1989)** - FOIA/Privacy Act intersection
- **Doe v. Chao (2004)** - Actual damages required for recovery
- **FAA v. Cooper (2012)** - Mental/emotional distress not "actual damages"
- **Mayo v. HHS (D.D.C. 2020)** - SORN adequacy requirements

## Computer Matching (5 U.S.C. § 552a(o)-(r))
- Agencies must establish Data Integrity Boards
- Written matching agreements required
- Notice to individuals before adverse action
- Independent verification required
