# Paperwork Reduction Act (PRA) - 44 U.S.C. ÏÏ 3501-3521

## Overview
The Paperwork Reduction Act requires agencies to obtain OMB approval before collecting information from 10 or more persons. It establishes OIRA as the central clearance authority.

## Official Resources

## Key Requirements

### Information Collection Request (ICR) - Ï 3507
Agency must submit to OIRA for approval:
1. Description of information to be collected
2. Need for collection and intended use
3. Burden estimate (hours and cost)
4. Whether collection uses automated/electronic methods
5. Whether collection affects small entities

### OMB Control Number
- Every approved collection receives unique control number
- Must be displayed on collection instrument
- Valid for 3 years maximum (then renewal required)

### Public Notice - Ï 3506(c)(2)
- 60-day notice in Federal Register
- 30-day notice before final submission to OMB
- Opportunity for public comment

## Exemptions - Ï 3518
PRA does not apply to:
1. Collections during criminal/civil investigations/prosecutions
2. Collections during administrative actions involving specific parties
3. Collections required by subpoena or court order
4. Collections of 9 or fewer respondents

## Burden Calculation
- **Burden**: Time, effort, financial resources to comply
- **Respondent**: Individual, household, business, non-profit, government
- **Annual Reporting**: Agencies report total burden hours in ICR

## Key Cases
- **Dole v. United Steelworkers (1990)** - PRA applies to third-party disclosures
- **Sacramento Grazing Ass'n v. United States (Fed. Cir. 2018)** - PRA violation doesn't invalidate collection
- **California v. Azar (9th Cir. 2019)** - PRA applies to grant conditions
- **Career Colleges & Sch. of Tex. v. DOE (5th Cir. 2023)** - PRA notice requirements

## Practical Implications
| Issue | PRA Requirement |
|-------|-----------------|
| Agency form | Must display OMB control number and expiration |
| Online survey | Counts toward 10-person threshold |
| Third-party disclosure | Covered if agency mandates disclosure |
| Voluntary collection | Still requires OMB approval |
| Failure to comply | Public protection - no penalty for non-response |

## PRA Violations - Public Protection (Ï 3512)
- Person cannot be penalized for failing to respond to unapproved collection
- But this is defense, not affirmative claim
- Does not invalidate agency action
