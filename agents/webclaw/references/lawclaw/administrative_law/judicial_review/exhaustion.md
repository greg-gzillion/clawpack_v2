# Exhaustion of Administrative Remedies

## Overview
Parties must complete all available administrative remedies before seeking judicial review.

## General Rule - McKart v. United States (1969)
- Exhaustion required unless exceptions apply
- Purpose: Agency expertise, record development, judicial efficiency

## Official Resources
- **APA ｧ 704 (Final Agency Action)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/704
- **ACUS Exhaustion Report**: https://www.acus.gov/report/exhaustion-administrative-remedies

## Exceptions to Exhaustion
1. **Futility**: Agency has predetermined issue
   - **Key Case**: Honig v. Doe (1988)
2. **Inadequate Remedy**: Agency cannot provide relief
   - **Key Case**: Bowen v. City of New York (1986)
3. **Irreparable Injury**: Delay would cause harm
   - **Key Case**: Mathews v. Eldridge (1976)
4. **Constitutional Claims**: Agency cannot resolve
   - **Key Case**: Thunder Basin Coal Co. v. Reich (1994)
5. **Exceeded Authority**: Agency acted ultra vires
   - **Key Case**: Leedom v. Kyne (1958)

## Statutory Exhaustion Requirements
- **Medicare Act**: 42 U.S.C. ｧ 405(g) - Mandatory, jurisdictional
- **IDEA**: 20 U.S.C. ｧ 1415(l) - Exhaustion required
- **Veterans Benefits**: 38 U.S.C. ｧ 511 - No judicial review of benefits decisions
- **FOIA**: 5 U.S.C. ｧ 552(a)(6) - Exhaustion required before suit

## Key Cases
- **McKart v. United States (1969)** - Selective Service exhaustion
- **McCarthy v. Madigan (1992)** - Prisoner exhaustion (Bivens)
- **Woodford v. Ngo (2006)** - PLRA requires proper exhaustion
- **Ross v. Blake (2016)** - PLRA exhaustion when remedy unavailable
- **Carr v. Saul (2021)** - Appointments Clause claims need not exhaust

## Issue Exhaustion vs. Remedy Exhaustion
- **Issue Exhaustion**: Must raise specific arguments before agency
- **Waiver**: Arguments not raised may be waived
- **Key Case**: Sims v. Apfel (2000) - SSA Appeals Council proceedings

## Prison Litigation Reform Act (PLRA)
- 42 U.S.C. ｧ 1997e(a) - Mandatory exhaustion
- Must exhaust "available" remedies
- Proper exhaustion required (procedural compliance)
- **Key Cases**: Booth v. Churner (2001), Porter v. Nussle (2002)

## Administrative Procedure Act
- Exhaustion required only when statute mandates
- Common law exhaustion doctrine applies otherwise
- **Key Case**: Darby v. Cisneros (1993)
