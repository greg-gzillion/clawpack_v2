# Scope of Judicial Review - APA ｧ 706

## Overview
APA ｧ 706 defines the scope of judicial review of agency action - what courts can review and what remedies are available.

## Official Resources
- **APA ｧ 706 (Full Text)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/706
- **APA ｧ 701 (Application)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/701

## Reviewable Agency Actions (ｧ 706)
### Compel Agency Action (ｧ 706(1))
- Action "unlawfully withheld or unreasonably delayed"
- **Key Case**: Telecommunications Research & Action Center v. FCC (D.C. Cir. 1984) - TRAC factors

### Set Aside Agency Action (ｧ 706(2))
Courts shall set aside agency action found to be:

**(A) Arbitrary, capricious, an abuse of discretion, or otherwise not in accordance with law**
- Most common ground for challenge
- **Key Case**: Motor Vehicle Mfrs. Ass'n v. State Farm (1983)

**(B) Contrary to constitutional right, power, privilege, or immunity**
- Constitutional challenges
- Due process, equal protection, First Amendment

**(C) In excess of statutory jurisdiction, authority, or limitations**
- Agency exceeded delegated authority
- **Key Case**: FDA v. Brown & Williamson (2000)

**(D) Without observance of procedure required by law**
- Procedural APA violations
- Agency failed to follow required procedures

**(E) Unsupported by substantial evidence**
- Formal rulemaking/adjudication only
- **Key Case**: Universal Camera v. NLRB (1951)

**(F) Unwarranted by the facts to the extent that facts are subject to trial de novo**
- De novo review (rare)
- Inadequate agency fact-finding

## Exceptions to Judicial Review (ｧ 701)
1. **Statutory Preclusion**: Statute bars judicial review
2. **Committed to Agency Discretion**: No meaningful standard for review
   - **Key Case**: Heckler v. Chaney (1985) - Enforcement discretion
   - **Key Case**: Lincoln v. Vigil (1993) - Lump-sum appropriations

## TRAC Factors - Unreasonable Delay
1. Rule of reason (timetable)
2. Congressional timetable
3. Human health/welfare vs. economic regulation
4. Competing agency priorities
5. Interests prejudiced by delay
6. Improper agency motive not required

## Remand Without Vacatur
- Court finds error but leaves rule in place
- Agency must correct error on remand
- **Factors**: Severity of error, disruptive consequences
- **Key Case**: Allied-Signal v. NRC (D.C. Cir. 1993)

## Vacatur
- Default remedy for unlawful agency action
- **Nationwide Vacatur Debate**: Universal vs. party-specific relief
- **Key Case**: United States v. Texas (2023) - Vacatur debate

## Record Review
- Judicial review based on administrative record
- Extra-record evidence limited
- **Exceptions**: Bad faith, need for explanation, inadequate record
