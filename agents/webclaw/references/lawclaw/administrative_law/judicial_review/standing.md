# Standing in Administrative Law - Judicial Review

## Overview
Standing determines who may challenge agency action in federal court. Article III requires a "case or controversy."

## Constitutional Standing (Lujan Test)
**Lujan v. Defenders of Wildlife (1992)** - Three-part test:
1. **Injury in Fact**: Concrete, particularized, actual or imminent
2. **Causation**: Injury fairly traceable to challenged agency action
3. **Redressability**: Likely that favorable court decision will remedy injury

## Prudential Standing (Non-Constitutional Limits)
- **Zone of Interests Test**: Plaintiff's interest must be within zone of interests protected by statute
- **Key Case**: Association of Data Processing Service Orgs. v. Camp (1970)
- **Key Case**: Clarke v. Securities Industry Ass'n (1987)

## Key Administrative Law Standing Cases
- **Sierra Club v. Morton (1972)** - Environmental standing, aesthetic injury
- **Lujan v. Defenders of Wildlife (1992)** - No standing for generalized grievance
- **Massachusetts v. EPA (2007)** - State standing, greenhouse gas regulation
- **Summers v. Earth Island Institute (2009)** - No standing for procedural injury alone
- **Clapper v. Amnesty International (2013)** - Speculative injury insufficient
- **Spokeo, Inc. v. Robins (2016)** - Concrete injury required (not just statutory violation)
- **TransUnion LLC v. Ramirez (2021)** - Class members without concrete injury lack standing

## Associational Standing
Organizations may sue on behalf of members if:
1. Members would have standing individually
2. Interests at stake germane to organization's purpose
3. Individual members' participation not required
- **Key Case**: Hunt v. Washington Apple Advertising Comm'n (1977)

## State Standing
- States receive "special solicitude" in standing analysis
- Parens patriae standing for quasi-sovereign interests
- **Key Case**: Massachusetts v. EPA (2007)

## Procedural Standing
- Lower threshold for procedural rights violations
- Must show agency action threatens concrete interest
- **Key Case**: Lujan v. Defenders of Wildlife (1992) - Footnote 7

## Prudential Standing Exceptions
- Competitor standing (hardware store vs. bank)
- Aesthetic/recreational standing
- Informational standing (FOIA, FACA)
