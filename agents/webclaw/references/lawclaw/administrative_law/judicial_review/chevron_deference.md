# Chevron Deference - Chevron U.S.A., Inc. v. Natural Resources Defense Council, Inc., 467 U.S. 837 (1984)

## Overview
Chevron deference was a doctrine of judicial review requiring courts to defer to reasonable agency interpretations of ambiguous statutes.

**Note: Chevron was OVERTURNED in Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo, 144 S. Ct. 2244 (2024).**

## The Two-Step Framework (Historical)
1. **Step One**: Has Congress directly spoken to the precise question?
   - If yes ? Court follows congressional intent
   - If ambiguous ? Proceed to Step Two

2. **Step Two**: Is the agency's interpretation reasonable?
   - If yes ? Court defers to agency
   - If no ? Court may substitute its own interpretation

## Key Cases
- **Chevron v. NRDC** (1984) - Established the doctrine
- **United States v. Mead Corp.** (2001) - Limited Chevron's scope
- **Loper Bright v. Raimondo** (2024) - Overturned Chevron

## Post-Chevron Era
- Skidmore deference remains (persuasiveness, not binding)
- Courts now exercise independent judgment
- Agency interpretations may still be persuasive
