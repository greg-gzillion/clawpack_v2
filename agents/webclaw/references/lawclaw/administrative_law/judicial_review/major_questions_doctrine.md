# Major Questions Doctrine - Administrative Law

## Overview
Courts require clear congressional authorization for agency actions of "vast economic and political significance."

## Official Resources
- **CRS Major Questions Report**: https://crsreports.congress.gov/product/pdf/LSB/LSB11034
- **SCOTUS Blog Coverage**: https://www.scotusblog.com/case-files/cases/west-virginia-v-environmental-protection-agency/

## The Doctrine Defined
- **West Virginia v. EPA (2022)**: Clear congressional authorization required for major questions
- **"Major Questions"**: Vast economic/political significance
- **Agencies cannot discover unheralded powers in vague statutory language**

## Key Cases
- **FDA v. Brown & Williamson Tobacco Corp. (2000)** - FDA cannot regulate tobacco (first articulation)
- **Whitman v. American Trucking Ass'ns (2001)** - MQD mentioned in context
- **King v. Burwell (2015)** - ACA tax credits (Chevron step zero)
- **Utility Air Regulatory Group v. EPA (2014)** - GHG permitting limits
- **West Virginia v. EPA (2022)** - EPA cannot restructure energy grid
- **Biden v. Nebraska (2023)** - Student loan forgiveness struck down
- **Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo (2024)** - Chevron overturned, MQD remains

## Elements of Major Questions Doctrine
1. Agency claims transformative authority
2. Limited statutory basis (vague/generic language)
3. Vast economic/political significance
4. Traditional state domain or novel area
5. Congress unlikely to delegate implicitly

## Relationship to Chevron
- Chevron deference **overturned** (Loper Bright, 2024)
- Major Questions Doctrine **survives** independently
- Both limit agency interpretive authority

## Post-Chevron Landscape
| Doctrine | Status | Effect |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Chevron | Overturned | Courts exercise independent judgment |
| Major Questions | Active | Clear statement required |
| Skidmore | Active | Persuasive weight only |

## Impact on Agency Action
- **EPA**: Climate regulations limited
- **OSHA**: Vaccine mandate struck down (NFIB v. OSHA, 2022)
- **CDC**: Eviction moratorium struck down (Alabama Ass'n of Realtors v. HHS, 2021)
- **Education**: Student loan forgiveness struck down

## Criticism and Support
- **Critics**: Judicial overreach, anti-regulatory bias
- **Supporters**: Democratic accountability, nondelegation enforcement
- **Academic Debate**: Harvard Law Review, Yale Law Journal symposia

## Practice Implications
- Challenge agency rules on MQD grounds when:
  - Agency asserts novel authority
  - Statutory basis is general/vague
  - Economic/political impact is significant
- Cite West Virginia v. EPA and Biden v. Nebraska
