# Standards of Judicial Review - Administrative Law

## Overview - APA ｧ 706
Judicial review standards determine how much deference courts give to agency actions.

## Official Resources
- **APA ｧ 706 (Full Text)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/706
- **DOJ Civil Division - Appellate**: https://www.justice.gov/civil/appellate-staff

## Standards of Review

### 1. Arbitrary and Capricious (APA ｧ 706(2)(A))
- **Standard**: Agency action must be rational, based on relevant factors
- **Key Case**: Motor Vehicle Mfrs. Ass'n v. State Farm (1983)
- **Application**: Most informal rulemaking and adjudication
- **Hard Look Review**: Courts examine agency reasoning thoroughly

### 2. Substantial Evidence (APA ｧ 706(2)(E))
- **Standard**: Evidence that a reasonable mind might accept as adequate
- **Application**: Formal rulemaking and adjudication (hearing required)
- **Key Case**: Universal Camera Corp. v. NLRB (1951)

### 3. De Novo Review (APA ｧ 706(2)(F))
- **Standard**: Court decides matter independently
- **Application**: Limited - when agency action is adjudicatory and inadequate

### 4. Statutory Interpretation - Post-Chevron
- **Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo** (2024): Courts exercise independent judgment
- **Skidmore Deference** (Skidmore v. Swift & Co., 1944): Agency interpretation persuasive based on thoroughness, validity, consistency
- **Kisor v. Wilkie** (2019): Auer deference for ambiguous regulations (narrowly applied)

## Scope of Review (APA ｧ 706)
- **ｧ 706(1)**: Compel agency action unlawfully withheld or unreasonably delayed
- **ｧ 706(2)**: Set aside agency action that is:
  - (A) Arbitrary, capricious, abuse of discretion
  - (B) Contrary to constitutional right
  - (C) In excess of statutory jurisdiction
  - (D) Without observance of procedure required by law
  - (E) Unsupported by substantial evidence
  - (F) Unwarranted by facts (de novo review)

## Key Cases on Standard of Review
- **Citizens to Preserve Overton Park v. Volpe** (1971): Hard look doctrine
- **Baltimore Gas & Electric v. NRDC** (1983): Agency scientific expertise deference
- **FCC v. Fox Television Stations** (2009): Policy changes require reasoned explanation
- **DHS v. Regents of Univ. of Cal.** (2020): DACA rescission arbitrary & capricious

## Procedural Requirements for Review
- **Standing**: Lujan v. Defenders of Wildlife (1992)
- **Ripeness**: Abbott Laboratories v. Gardner (1967)
- **Exhaustion**: Administrative remedies must be exhausted
- **Finality**: Agency action must be final (APA ｧ 704)

## Remedies (APA ｧ 706)
- **Compel agency action** (unreasonably delayed)
- **Set aside unlawful agency action**
- **Remand to agency** (most common)
- **Vacatur** (universal or party-specific)
