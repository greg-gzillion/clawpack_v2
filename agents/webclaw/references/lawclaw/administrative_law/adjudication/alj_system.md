# Administrative Law Judge (ALJ) System

## Overview
ALJs preside over formal adjudications and are independent decision-makers within federal agencies.

## ALJ Appointment
- **Lucia v. SEC (2018)**: ALJs are "inferior officers" under Appointments Clause
- Must be appointed by President, courts, or department heads
- Competitive examination through OPM
- **Executive Order 13843 (2018)**: Removed competitive service requirements

## ALJ Independence
- **APA ｧ 554(d)**: Separation of functions
  - ALJ may not consult with investigative/prosecutorial staff
  - Ex parte communications prohibited
- **APA ｧ 556(b)**: ALJ disqualification for bias
- **APA ｧ 7521**: Removal only for "good cause" after MSPB hearing
- **Key Case**: Butz v. Economou (1978) - ALJ absolute immunity

## ALJ Authority (ｧ 556(c))
- Administer oaths and affirmations
- Issue subpoenas (if authorized by statute)
- Rule on offers of proof and evidence
- Regulate course of hearing
- Hold conferences for settlement/simplification
- Dispose of procedural requests
- Make initial or recommended decisions

## The ALJ Decision
- **Initial Decision**: Becomes final unless appealed
- **Recommended Decision**: Certified to agency head for final decision
- **APA ｧ 557(c)**: Must include:
  - Findings of fact
  - Conclusions of law
  - Reasons for decision
  - Order

## Agency Appeal Process
- Appeal to agency head or appeals board
- Agency may review ALJ decision sua sponte
- Final agency decision subject to judicial review

## Key Cases
- **Ramspeck v. Federal Trial Examiners Conference** (1953) - ALJ independence
- **Nash v. Bowen** (2d Cir. 1989) - ALJ decisional independence
- **Free Enterprise Fund v. PCAOB** (2010) - Removal protections
- **Lucia v. SEC** (2018) - Appointments Clause applies to ALJs
- **Carr v. Saul** (2021) - Exhaustion not required for Appointments Clause claims
