# Informal Adjudication - APA § 555

## Overview
Informal adjudication covers all agency adjudications not required to be "on the record." This is the vast majority of agency decisions.

## Official Resources
- **APA § 555 (Ancillary Matters)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/555
- **ACUS Informal Adjudication Guide**: https://www.acus.gov/report/informal-adjudication-best-practices

## Types of Informal Adjudication
- Licensing decisions
- Permit approvals/denials
- Benefits determinations
- Immigration decisions (many)
- Government contract awards
- Personnel actions
- Inspection and compliance determinations

## Procedural Rights (§ 555)
- Right to appear with counsel (§ 555(b))
- Right to prompt notice of denial (§ 555(e))
- Right to reasons for denial
- Right to appeal to superior agency authority
- Access to agency records relevant to proceeding

## Constitutional Due Process
- **When Due Process Applies**: Property or liberty interest at stake
- **Mathews v. Eldridge (1976) Balancing Test**:
  1. Private interest affected
  2. Risk of erroneous deprivation
  3. Government's interest (including fiscal/administrative burden)

## Key Cases
- **Board of Regents v. Roth** (1972) - Property interest definition
- **Goldberg v. Kelly** (1970) - Welfare termination requires hearing
- **Mathews v. Eldridge** (1976) - Disability benefits termination (post-termination hearing sufficient)
- **Cleveland Board of Education v. Loudermill** (1985) - Public employment property interest

## Agency-Specific Procedures
- **Visa Denials**: 22 CFR Part 41
- **FOIA Appeals**: 5 U.S.C. § 552(a)(6)
- **SSA Benefits**: 20 CFR Part 404
- **Veterans Benefits**: 38 CFR Part 3
- **Patent Applications**: 37 CFR Part 1

## Judicial Review of Informal Adjudication
- **Standard**: Arbitrary and capricious (APA § 706(2)(A))
- **Record**: Agency must provide administrative record
- **Key Case**: Citizens to Preserve Overton Park v. Volpe (1971) - Review based on administrative record

## Best Practices for Agencies
- Provide clear notice of requirements
- Give opportunity to submit evidence
- Explain reasons for decision
- Allow internal appeal/reconsideration
- Maintain adequate record for judicial review
