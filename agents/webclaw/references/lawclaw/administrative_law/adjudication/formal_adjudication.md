# Formal Adjudication - APA §§ 554, 556-557

## Overview
Formal adjudication is a trial-type hearing required by statute when agency action must be "on the record after opportunity for an agency hearing."

## When Formal Adjudication Required
- Statute explicitly requires "on the record" hearing
- Triggering language: "after hearing," "on the record after hearing"
- **Key Case**: Seacoast Anti-Pollution League v. Costle (1st Cir. 1978)
- **Key Case**: Chemical Waste Management v. EPA (D.C. Cir. 1989)

## Procedural Requirements

### Notice (§ 554(b))
- Time, place, and nature of hearing
- Legal authority and jurisdiction
- Matters of fact and law asserted

### Hearing Rights (§ 554(c))
- Right to present evidence
- Right to cross-examine witnesses
- Right to be represented by counsel
- Right to proposed findings and conclusions

### Administrative Law Judge (ALJ) (§ 556)
- Presiding officer must be impartial
- Separation of functions required
- ALJ has subpoena power
- ALJ makes initial or recommended decision

### Burden of Proof (§ 556(d))
- Proponent of rule or order has burden of proof
- Preponderance of evidence standard (usually)
- Some statutes require "substantial evidence"

### Evidence (§ 556(d))
- Any oral or documentary evidence may be received
- Irrelevant, immaterial, unduly repetitious evidence excluded
- Hearsay admissible but cannot be sole basis for decision
- **Key Case**: Richardson v. Perales (1971) - Hearsay can constitute substantial evidence

### Ex Parte Communications (§ 557(d))
- Prohibited between interested parties and decision-makers
- Must be placed on public record if occurs
- **Key Case**: PATCO v. FLRA (D.C. Cir. 1982)

## Key Cases on Formal Adjudication
- **Wong Yang Sung v. McGrath** (1950) - APA applies to deportation hearings
- **Marcello v. Bonds** (1955) - Statutory exceptions to APA
- **Butz v. Economou** (1978) - ALJ absolute immunity
- **Lucia v. SEC** (2018) - SEC ALJs are "Officers of the United States" requiring Appointments Clause compliance

## Constitutional Considerations
- **Due Process Clause**: Mathews v. Eldridge (1976) - Balancing test
- **Appointments Clause**: Lucia v. SEC (2018) - ALJs must be appointed by President, courts, or department heads
- **Seventh Amendment**: Atlas Roofing v. OSHRC (1977) - No jury right in agency adjudication
