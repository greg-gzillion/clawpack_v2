# Due Process in Administrative Adjudication

## Overview
The Fifth Amendment Due Process Clause protects against deprivation of "life, liberty, or property" without due process of law in federal administrative proceedings.

## Official Resources
- **Fifth Amendment**: https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/amendment-5/
- **Fourteenth Amendment (States)**: https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/amendment-14/

## Threshold Questions
1. **Is there a protected interest?**
   - Life, liberty, or property
2. **Has there been a deprivation?**
   - Government action affecting interest
3. **What process is due?**
   - Mathews v. Eldridge balancing test

## Protected Interests

### Property Interests
- **Entitlements**: Welfare benefits, disability benefits
- **Licenses**: Professional licenses, permits
- **Employment**: Public employment (if property interest created)
- **Key Case**: Board of Regents v. Roth (1972) - Property interest defined by "legitimate claim of entitlement"

### Liberty Interests
- **Reputation**: Damage to reputation plus tangible harm
- **Key Case**: Wisconsin v. Constantineau (1971) - "Badge of infamy"
- **Key Case**: Paul v. Davis (1976) - Reputation alone insufficient

## Mathews v. Eldridge (1976) Balancing Test
Courts weigh three factors:
1. **Private Interest**: Importance of interest to individual
2. **Risk of Error**: Value of additional procedures
3. **Government Interest**: Fiscal and administrative burden

## Procedural Requirements (Common)
- **Notice**: Adequate notice of issues and hearing
- **Opportunity to be Heard**: Meaningful opportunity to present case
- **Impartial Decision-Maker**: No bias or conflict
- **Right to Counsel**: Not always required, but allowed in formal proceedings
- **Confrontation/Cross-Examination**: Required in formal adjudications
- **Written Decision**: Explanation of reasons

## Key Cases by Context

### Welfare Benefits
- **Goldberg v. Kelly (1970)**: Pre-termination hearing required

### Disability Benefits
- **Mathews v. Eldridge (1976)**: Post-termination hearing sufficient

### Public Employment
- **Cleveland Board of Education v. Loudermill (1985)**: Pre-termination opportunity to respond

### Immigration
- **Landon v. Plasencia (1982)**: Mathews balancing applies
- **Department of Homeland Security v. Thuraissigiam (2020)**: Expedited removal

### Licensing
- **Barry v. Barchi (1979)**: Horse trainer license suspension
- **FDIC v. Mallen (1988)**: Bank officer suspension

### Student Discipline
- **Goss v. Lopez (1975)**: Public school suspensions require due process
