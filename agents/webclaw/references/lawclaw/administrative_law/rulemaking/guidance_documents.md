# Guidance Documents vs. Legislative Rules

## Overview
Guidance documents (interpretive rules, policy statements, manuals) are not subject to notice-and-comment but also lack binding legal effect.

## Official Resources
- **APA § 553(b)(A)** - Exception for interpretive rules and policy statements
- **OMB Good Guidance Practices**: https://www.whitehouse.gov/omb/information-regulatory-affairs/good-guidance-practices/
- **DOJ Guidance Portal**: https://www.justice.gov/guidance

## Types of Non-Binding Documents

### Interpretive Rules
- Advise public of agency's interpretation of statute/regulation
- No independent legal effect
- Cannot create new legal obligations
- **Key Case**: Perez v. Mortgage Bankers Ass'n (2015)

### Policy Statements
- Announce agency's enforcement priorities/discretion
- No binding effect on public
- **Key Case**: General Electric Co. v. EPA (D.C. Cir. 2002)

### Guidance Documents
- Manuals, bulletins, advisory opinions
- No notice-and-comment required
- Cannot bind regulated parties

## Binding vs. Non-Binding Test
**When does "guidance" become a legislative rule?**
1. Would rule be valid basis for enforcement action?
2. Does it impose new rights or obligations?
3. Is it published in CFR?
4. Does agency claim binding effect?
5. Is it necessary to close statutory gap?

## Key Cases
- **Appalachian Power Co. v. EPA (D.C. Cir. 2000)**: Guidance can be final agency action
- **Perez v. Mortgage Bankers Ass'n (2015)**: Interpretive rules exempt from notice-and-comment
- **Azar v. Allina Health Services (2019)**: Medicare guidance required notice-and-comment

## Agency Good Guidance Practices (EO 13891)
- Public access to all guidance documents
- Single searchable portal for guidance
- Notice and comment for significant guidance
- OMB oversight of guidance development

## Practical Implications
| Feature | Legislative Rule | Guidance Document |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Notice-and-Comment | Required | Not required |
| Binding Effect | Yes | No (persuasive only) |
| Judicial Review | APA § 706 | Limited (final agency action?) |
| CFR Publication | Required | Not required |

## Challenging Guidance Documents
- **Arbitrary and Capricious**: If agency treats as binding
- **Procedural Challenge**: If guidance is actually legislative rule
- **Final Agency Action**: Guidance may be reviewable if it determines rights/obligations
