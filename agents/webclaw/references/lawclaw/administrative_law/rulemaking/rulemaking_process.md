## Types of Rulemaking
- **Informal/Notice-and-Comment (APA ｧ 553)**: Most common
- **Formal Rulemaking (APA ｧｧ 556-557)**: When statute requires hearing
- **Negotiated Rulemaking (Neg Reg)**: https://www.epa.gov/adr/negotiated-rulemaking
- **Direct Final Rulemaking**: When non-controversial
- **Interim Final Rules**: Emergency situations

## Rulemaking Process Steps
1. **Congressional Authorization** - Enabling statute
2. **Proposed Rule (NPRM)** - Federal Register publication
3. **Public Comment Period** - Usually 30-60 days
4. **Agency Review** - Analysis of comments
5. **OIRA Review** - For significant rules (EO 12866)
6. **Final Rule** - Federal Register publication
7. **Effective Date** - Usually 30-60 days after publication
8. **Judicial Review** - Challenge in federal court

## Key Administrative Law Cases on Rulemaking
- **Vermont Yankee v. NRDC** (1978) - Courts cannot impose extra procedures
- **Motor Vehicle Mfrs. Ass'n v. State Farm** (1983) - Arbitrary & capricious review
- **Perez v. Mortgage Bankers Ass'n** (2015) - Interpretive rules not subject to notice-and-comment
