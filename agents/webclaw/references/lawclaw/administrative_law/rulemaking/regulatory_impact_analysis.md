# Regulatory Impact Analysis - EO 12866

## Overview
Executive Order 12866 requires agencies to analyze costs and benefits of "significant" regulatory actions and submit them to OIRA for review.

## Significance Determinations

### Economically Significant (Section 3(f)(1))
- Annual effect on economy = $200 million
- Adversely affects economy, productivity, competition, jobs, environment, public health

### Significant Regulatory Action (Section 3(f))
- Creates serious inconsistency with other agency actions
- Materially alters budgetary impacts of entitlements/grants
- Raises novel legal or policy issues

## Required Analysis for Significant Rules
1. **Cost-Benefit Analysis**: Quantify and monetize to extent possible
2. **Alternatives Assessment**: Consider alternative regulatory approaches
3. **Distributional Effects**: Impact on different groups
4. **Risk Assessment**: Scientific/technical analysis of risks addressed

## OIRA Review Process
1. Agency submits draft rule and RIA to OIRA
2. OIRA has 90 days to review (extendable)
3. OIRA coordinates interagency review
4. "Return Letter" if rule inadequate
5. OIRA concludes review ? agency publishes

## Circular A-4 (2023 Revision)
Key changes in 2023 revision:
- **Discount Rates**: Lower rates for intergenerational effects
- **Distributional Analysis**: Greater focus on equity
- **Climate Impacts**: Guidance on valuing greenhouse gas effects
- **Behavioral Economics**: Consideration of cognitive biases

## Regulatory Flexibility Act (RFA)
- **Small Business Analysis**: Required if rule affects small entities
- **Initial Regulatory Flexibility Analysis**: With NPRM
- **Final Regulatory Flexibility Analysis**: With final rule
- **Certification**: Agency may certify no significant impact
- **Chief Counsel for Advocacy (SBA)** review

## Paperwork Reduction Act (PRA)
- OMB clearance for information collections
- Control number required on forms
- 10+ respondents triggers PRA requirements

## Judicial Review
- **Compliance with EO 12866 not judicially reviewable**
- But RIA informs arbitrary/capricious review under APA
- Inadequate analysis may support State Farm challenge

## Key Cases
- **Michigan v. EPA (2015)**: Agency must consider cost before regulating
- **Business Roundtable v. SEC (D.C. Cir. 2011)**: Inadequate cost-benefit analysis
- **American Petroleum Institute v. EPA (D.C. Cir. 2012)**: RIA must address alternatives
