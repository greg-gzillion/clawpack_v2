# Notice-and-Comment Rulemaking - APA § 553

## Overview
Notice-and-comment is the standard rulemaking procedure under APA § 553. Agencies must publish notice, accept public comments, and publish final rules with statement of basis and purpose.

## Official Resources
- **APA § 553 (Rulemaking)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/553
- **ACUS Rulemaking Guide**: https://www.acus.gov/research-projects/rulemaking-guide

## Notice Requirements (§ 553(b))
Must include:
1. **Time, place, and nature** of rulemaking proceedings
2. **Legal authority** under which rule is proposed
3. **Terms or substance** of proposed rule or description of subjects and issues

**Publication**: Federal Register (30+ days before effective date)

## Comment Period (§ 553(c))
- Agency must give "interested persons an opportunity to participate"
- **Minimum**: 30 days (60 recommended for major rules)
- **Extension**: Often granted for complex rules
- **Late Comments**: May be considered if time permits

## Final Rule Requirements (§ 553(c)-(d))
- **Concise General Statement**: Basis and purpose of rule
- **Response to Comments**: Address significant issues raised
- **Logical Outgrowth**: Final rule must be within scope of proposal
- **Publication**: 30+ days before effective date

## Exceptions to Notice-and-Comment (§ 553(a)-(b))
1. **Military or Foreign Affairs**: Exempt from APA requirements
2. **Agency Management or Personnel**: Internal agency matters
3. **Public Property, Loans, Grants, Benefits, or Contracts**: Exempt

## Good Cause Exception (§ 553(b)(B))
Agency may dispense with notice-and-comment when:
- **Impracticable**: Emergency situations
- **Unnecessary**: Minor technical amendments
- **Contrary to Public Interest**: Immediate action needed

## Key Cases
- **Vermont Yankee v. NRDC (1978)**: Courts cannot impose extra procedures
- **Perez v. Mortgage Bankers Ass'n (2015)**: Interpretive rules exempt
- **Little Sisters of the Poor v. Pennsylvania (2020)**: Good cause exception

## Best Practices
- Publish clear, specific notice
- Provide adequate comment period
- Respond to significant comments in preamble
- Explain any changes from proposal
- Ensure final rule is logical outgrowth of proposal
