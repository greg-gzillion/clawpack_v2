# Model State Administrative Procedure Act (MSAPA)

## Overview
The Model State Administrative Procedure Act provides a template for state administrative procedure laws. Most states have adopted some version of the MSAPA.

## Key Provisions of MSAPA

### Article 1 - General Provisions
- Definitions of agency, rule, order, contested case
- Applicability to state agencies
- Relationship to other statutes

### Article 2 - Rulemaking
- **Notice-and-Comment**: Similar to federal APA ｧ 553
- **Emergency Rules**: 120-day maximum duration
- **Incorporation by Reference**: Permitted with availability
- **Rulemaking Record**: Must be maintained for judicial review

### Article 3 - Adjudication
- **Contested Cases**: Trial-type hearings required
- **ALJ System**: Central panel vs. agency ALJs
- **Ex Parte Communications**: Prohibited
- **Separation of Functions**: ALJs independent from investigative staff

### Article 4 - Judicial Review
- **Exhaustion**: Must exhaust administrative remedies
- **Standards of Review**: Similar to federal APA ｧ 706
- **Record Review**: Based on agency record

## Key Differences from Federal APA
| Feature | Federal APA | MSAPA |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Central ALJ Panel | No (agency-specific) | Optional |
| Emergency Rule Duration | Unlimited | 120-180 days |
| Rulemaking Record | Not required | Required |
| Legislative Review | Congressional Review Act | State legislative review |
| Cost-Benefit Analysis | EO 12866 | State-specific requirements |

## Central Panel States
States with centralized ALJ corps:
- **California**: Office of Administrative Hearings (OAH)
- **Texas**: State Office of Administrative Hearings (SOAH)
- **Florida**: Division of Administrative Hearings (DOAH)
- **Washington**: Office of Administrative Hearings (OAH)
- **Arizona**: Office of Administrative Hearings (OAH)
- **Colorado**: Office of Administrative Courts (OAC)
- **New Jersey**: Office of Administrative Law (OAL)
