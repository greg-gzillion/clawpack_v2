# Key State Administrative Law Cases

## California
- **Yamaha Corp. of America v. State Bd. of Equalization (1998)** 19 Cal.4th 1
  - Agency interpretations of statutes reviewed de novo; regulations given deference
- **Western States Petroleum Ass'n v. Superior Court (1995)** 9 Cal.4th 559
  - Administrative record is exclusive basis for judicial review
- **Friends of the Eel River v. North Coast R.R. Authority (2017)** 3 Cal.5th 677
  - CEQA exemptions and categorical determinations

## New York
- **Matter of New York State Superfund Coalition v. NYS DEC (2019)** 173 A.D.3d 1463
  - Arbitrary and capricious review of agency determinations
- **Matter of Acevedo v. New York State Dept. of Motor Vehicles (2014)** 22 N.Y.3d 678
  - Standard of review for ALJ determinations
- **Matter of Pell v. Bd. of Educ. (1974)** 34 N.Y.2d 222
  - Penalty must be proportionate to offense

## Texas
- **Texas Dep't of Ins. v. Reconveyance Servs., Inc. (2010)** 306 S.W.3d 256
  - Agency rules cannot exceed statutory authority
- **Railroad Comm'n v. Texas Citizens for a Safe Future (2011)** 336 S.W.3d 619
  - Contested case procedures under Texas APA
- **City of Dallas v. Stewart (2011)** 361 S.W.3d 562
  - Substantial evidence review in administrative appeals

## Florida
- **Dept. of Highway Safety v. Schluter (1997)** 705 So.2d 81
  - Agency cannot use rulemaking to bypass legislative intent
- **Florida Dep't of Revenue v. Piccadilly Cafeterias (2008)** 554 U.S. 33
  - Stamp tax exemption interpretation
- **Cook v. Florida Parole & Probation Comm'n (1979)** 370 So.2d 440
  - Due process in administrative hearings

## Illinois
- **AFSCME v. State Labor Relations Bd. (2016)** 2016 IL 118562
  - Deference to agency interpretations of statutes
- **Wade v. City of North Chicago Police Pension Bd. (2006)** 226 Ill.2d 485
  - Manifest weight of evidence standard
- **Illinois Bell Tel. Co. v. Illinois Commerce Comm'n (1998)** 306 Ill.App.3d 238
  - Agency expertise deference

## Massachusetts
- **Goldberg v. Board of Health of Granby (2005)** 444 Mass. 627
  - Local board health regulations review
- **Town of Falmouth v. Civil Service Comm'n (2006)** 447 Mass. 814
  - Judicial review of civil service decisions
- **Moot v. Department of Environmental Protection (2008)** 448 Mass. 340
  - Wetlands protection regulations

## New Jersey
- **In re NJPDES Permit No. NJ0025241 (2005)** 185 N.J. 474
  - Deference to agency technical expertise
- **Mazza v. Cavicchia (1954)** 15 N.J. 498
  - Due process in administrative hearings
- **In re Virtua-West Jersey Hosp. (2009)** 194 N.J. 413
  - Certificate of need review

## Pennsylvania
- **Popowsky v. Pennsylvania Public Utility Comm'n (2005)** 589 Pa. 605
  - Agency must follow its own regulations
- **Seeton v. Pennsylvania Game Comm'n (2008)** 597 Pa. 481
  - Agency rulemaking authority limits
- **Bureau of Professional & Occupational Affairs v. State Bd. of Physical Therapy (1999)** 728 A.2d 340
  - Substantial evidence standard

## Ohio
- **State ex rel. Celebrezze v. National Lime & Stone Co. (1994)** 68 Ohio St.3d 377
  - Agency cannot modify clear statutory language
- **VFW Post 8587 v. Ohio Liquor Control Comm'n (1998)** 83 Ohio St.3d 79
  - Reliable, probative, substantial evidence standard
- **University of Toledo v. Heiny (1987)** 30 Ohio St.3d 143
  - Agency deference on technical matters

## Michigan
- **Michigan Farm Bureau v. Dep't of Environmental Quality (2012)** 491 Mich. 899
  - Agency cannot expand statutory authority by rule
- **In re MCI Telecom. Complaint (1999)** 460 Mich. 396
  - Agency deference on factual findings
- **Northwestern Nat'l Cas. Co. v. Comm'r of Ins. (1999)** 231 Mich.App. 483
  - Statutory interpretation de novo

## Washington
- **Hillis v. State Dep't of Ecology (1997)** 131 Wash.2d 373
  - Error of law standard in APA review
- **Tapper v. Employment Security Dep't (1993)** 122 Wash.2d 397
  - Agency cannot amend rules without rulemaking
- **Postema v. Pollution Control Hearings Bd. (2000)** 142 Wash.2d 68
  - Deference to agency factual findings

## General Principles Across States
| Principle | States Following |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Deference to agency legal interpretations | Varies (some de novo, some deferential) |
| Substantial evidence for factual findings | Nearly all states |
| Exhaustion required | All states |
| Record-based review | All states |
| Central ALJ panel | ~30 states |
