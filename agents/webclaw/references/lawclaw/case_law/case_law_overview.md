# Case Law Research - Overview

## What is Case Law?
Case law (also known as common law or precedent) is the body of law created by judicial decisions. Under the doctrine of stare decisis, courts follow precedents established by higher courts in the same jurisdiction.

## The Federal Court System
| Court | Number | Jurisdiction | Appeal To |
|-------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **U.S. Supreme Court** | 1 | Nationwide; discretionary review | Final |
| **U.S. Courts of Appeals** | 13 Circuits | Geographic/circuit | Supreme Court |
| **U.S. District Courts** | 94 Districts | Federal trial court | Court of Appeals |
| **Bankruptcy Courts** | 94 | Bankruptcy matters | District Court/BAP |
| **Specialized Courts** | Various | Tax, Federal Claims, Veterans, etc. | Federal Circuit |

| **Trial Courts** | Superior / District / Circuit / County | Original jurisdiction |
| **Limited Jurisdiction** | Municipal / Justice / Magistrate | Small claims, traffic |

## Precedent Hierarchy
### Binding Precedent (Mandatory Authority)
- Courts must follow decisions of **higher courts in the same jurisdiction**
- U.S. Supreme Court decisions bind ALL courts on federal law
- Circuit Court decisions bind District Courts within that circuit
- State Supreme Court decisions bind all lower state courts

### Persuasive Precedent (Non-Binding Authority)
- Decisions from **other jurisdictions** (other circuits, other states)
- Decisions from **lower courts** in same jurisdiction
- **Dicta** (statements not necessary to the holding)
- **Dissenting opinions**

## Types of Judicial Opinions
| Type | Author | Authority |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Majority Opinion** | Justice(s) in the majority | Binding precedent |
| **Concurring Opinion** | Justice who agrees but for different reasons | Persuasive |
| **Dissenting Opinion** | Justice in the minority | Persuasive only |
| **Per Curiam** | Unsigned, by the court | Binding |
| **Memorandum Opinion** | Brief, often unpublished | Limited/non-precedential |
