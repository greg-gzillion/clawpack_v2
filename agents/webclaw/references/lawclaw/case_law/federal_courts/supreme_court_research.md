# U.S. Supreme Court Case Research

## Overview
The Supreme Court of the United States is the highest court in the federal judiciary. It has ultimate appellate jurisdiction over all federal and state court cases involving federal law.

## Types of Supreme Court Opinions
| Type | Description | Binding? |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Majority Opinion** | Opinion of the Court (5+ justices) | Yes |
| **Plurality Opinion** | Most votes but <5; narrowest grounds controls | Yes (limited) |
| **Concurring Opinion** | Agrees with result, different reasoning | Persuasive |
| **Dissenting Opinion** | Disagrees with majority | Persuasive |
| **Per Curiam** | Unsigned, unanimous or clear outcome | Yes |
| **Memorandum Opinion** | Summary disposition | Yes |
| **Orders List** | Procedural rulings (cert grants/denials) | Varies |

## Certiorari Process
| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Petition for Certiorari** | Party asks Court to hear case |
| **Brief in Opposition** | Respondent opposes cert |
| **Conference** | Justices vote on cert (Rule of Four) |
| **Cert Granted** | ~1-2% of petitions |
| **Merits Briefing** | Full briefing on merits |
| **Oral Argument** | Typically 1 hour |
| **Opinion Issued** | Majority, concurrences, dissents |

## Supreme Court Research Strategies
### Finding Cases on a Topic
1. Start with secondary sources (treatises, law reviews)
2. Use West Key Number or Lexis Headnote systems
3. Search by keyword in Google Scholar
4. Use citators to find citing cases

### Shepardizing/KeyCiting Supreme Court Cases
- **Verify validity**: Has case been overruled?
- **Find citing cases**: Who has cited this case?
- **Treatment indicators**: Positive, negative, neutral
