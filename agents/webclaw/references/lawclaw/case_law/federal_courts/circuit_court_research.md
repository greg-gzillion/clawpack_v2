# Circuit Court Case Law Research

## Overview
The 13 U.S. Courts of Appeals are the intermediate appellate courts in the federal system. Their decisions are binding on district courts within their circuit.

## Circuit Precedent Rules
| Rule | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Binding Precedent** | Panel must follow prior circuit precedent |
| **En Banc Review** | Full circuit may overrule panel decision |
| **Circuit Split** | Different circuits may reach different conclusions |
| **Supreme Court Review** | Resolves circuit splits |

- **Binding on**: USPTO, ITC, Court of Federal Claims, MSPB

### D.C. Circuit
- **Website**: https://www.cadc.uscourts.gov
- **Notable for**: Administrative law, regulatory challenges

## Circuit Split Analysis
### What is a Circuit Split?
When two or more circuits reach different conclusions on the same legal issue, creating a conflict that may warrant Supreme Court review.

### Finding Circuit Splits
| Resource | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **SCOTUSblog Circuit Split Tracker** | Pending splits |
