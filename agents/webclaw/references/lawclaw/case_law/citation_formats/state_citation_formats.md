# State Citation Formats - Case Law

## Overview
States have varying citation requirements. Some require official reporter citations, others accept unofficial reporters, and many now use neutral citations.

## State-Specific Citation Rules

### California
- **Official Reports**: Cal., Cal. 2d, Cal. 3d, Cal. 4th, Cal. 5th
- **Citation format**: *People v. Smith* (2020) 10 Cal. 5th 123, 130
- **Unpublished opinions**: Not citable (CRC 8.1115)

### New York
- **Official Reports**: N.Y., N.Y.2d, N.Y.3d
- **Citation format**: *People v. Jones*, 34 N.Y.3d 456, 460 (2019)
- **Appellate Division**: *Smith v. Brown*, 150 A.D.3d 789 (2d Dep't 2017)

### Texas
- **Unofficial Reports**: S.W., S.W.2d, S.W.3d
- **Supreme Court**: *In re Smith*, 600 S.W.3d 123, 125 (Tex. 2020)
- **Court of Criminal Appeals**: *Jones v. State*, 550 S.W.3d 234 (Tex. Crim. App. 2018)

### Florida
- **Unofficial Reports**: So., So. 2d, So. 3d
- **Supreme Court**: *Smith v. State*, 300 So. 3d 123, 125 (Fla. 2020)
- **District Courts**: *Jones v. Brown*, 250 So. 3d 456 (Fla. 3d DCA 2018)

### Illinois
- **Official Reports**: Ill., Ill. 2d
- **Unofficial**: N.E., N.E.2d, N.E.3d
- **Citation format**: *People v. Smith*, 2020 IL 123456, ｶ 15
