# Neutral Citation Format - Case Law

## Overview
Neutral citations (also called vendor-neutral or public domain citations) are medium-independent citations that identify cases by court, year, and sequential number rather than by print reporter volume and page.

## Official Resources
- **AALL Universal Citation Guide**: https://www.aallnet.org/advocacy/legal-information/universal-citation-guide/
- **ABA Neutral Citation Standards**: https://www.americanbar.org/groups/judicial/publications/appellate_issues/

## Neutral Citation Format
### General Format
`[Year] [Court Abbreviation] [Sequential Number]`

### Examples
| Court | Neutral Citation |
|-------|------------------|
| **U.S. Supreme Court** | 2021 U.S. 12 |
| **1st Circuit** | 2020 1st Cir. 45 |
| **9th Circuit** | 2019 9th Cir. 234 |
| **California Supreme Court** | 2022 Cal. 15 |
| **New York Court of Appeals** | 2021 N.Y. 8 |

## Pinpoint Citations with Neutral Citations
| Type | Format | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Page** | YYYY U.S. 12, at *5 | 2023 U.S. 12, at *8 |
| **Paragraph** | YYYY U.S. 12, ¶ 15 | 2023 U.S. 12, ¶ 15 |
| **Section** | YYYY U.S. 12, § III | 2023 U.S. 12, § III |

## Combining Neutral and Traditional Citations
`*Smith v. Jones*, 2023 U.S. 12, ¶ 15, 143 S. Ct. 1234, 1238 (2023)`

## Advantages of Neutral Citations
| Advantage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Medium-independent** | Works in print and online |
| **Immediate citation** | Available upon release (no waiting for reporter) |
| **Free access** | No proprietary database required |
| **Universal** | Same citation for all sources |
