# Citator Guide - Verifying Case Law Authority

## Overview
Citators are essential tools for verifying whether a case remains "good law" and for finding cases that have cited your case. They show subsequent appellate history and treatment by other courts.

## Major Citators
| Citator | Database | Cost | Best For |
|---------|----------|------|----------|
| **KeyCite** | Westlaw | Paid | Comprehensive, detailed treatment indicators |
| **Shepard's** | LexisNexis | Paid | Original citator, trusted authority |
| **BCite** | Bloomberg Law | Paid | Integrated with legal analytics |
| **CourtListener Citator** | Free | Free | Basic citation network, open access |
| **Google Scholar** | Free | Free | Limited citator ("How cited" feature) |

## Citator Symbols and Meanings
### KeyCite (Westlaw)
| Symbol | Meaning | Action Required |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| ?? **Red Flag** | Case no longer good law for at least one point | Do NOT rely on this case |
| ?? **Yellow Flag** | Negative history (criticized, limited, questioned) | Read negative treatment carefully |
| ?? **Blue H** | Appellate history | Review history for effect |
| ?? **Green C** | Citing references (no negative treatment) | Safe to rely on |

### Shepard's (LexisNexis)
| Symbol | Meaning | Action Required |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| ?? **Red Stop Sign** | Overruled or reversed | Do NOT rely on this case |
| ?? **Yellow Triangle** | Possible negative treatment | Investigate further |
| ?? **Orange Q** | Validity questioned | Read citing cases |
| ?? **Green Diamond** | Positive treatment | Safe to rely on |

## Negative Treatment Types
| Treatment | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Overruled** | Higher court expressly rejects holding | *Brown* overruled *Plessy* |
| **Reversed** | Appellate court reverses lower court | District court reversed by Circuit |
| **Criticized** | Court questions reasoning | "We doubt the wisdom of..." |
| **Limited** | Holding restricted to specific facts | "We limit *Smith* to its facts" |
| **Distinguished** | Court finds case factually different | "Unlike *Jones*, here..." |
| **Abrogated** | Statute or rule supersedes case | Statute abrogates common law rule |
| **Superseded** | Later case or statute replaces | Rule superseded by amendment |

## How to Shepardize/KeyCite a Case

### Step 1: Retrieve the Case
- Enter citation or party name in your database

### Step 2: Check the Citator Signal
- **Red flag/stop sign**: Case has been overruled or reversed - investigate immediately
- **Yellow flag/triangle**: Case has negative history - read citing cases
- **No flag**: No direct negative history (but still check citing cases)

### Step 3: Review Direct History
- **Prior history**: What happened before this decision?
- **Subsequent history**: What happened on appeal?
- **Rehearing**: Was rehearing granted or denied?

### Step 4: Analyze Citing Cases
- **Filter by treatment**: Negative, positive, neutral
- **Filter by depth of treatment**: Discussed, cited, mentioned
- **Filter by jurisdiction**: Binding vs. persuasive authority

### Step 5: Verify Headnote/Key Number Treatment
- Each headnote may have different treatment
- A case may be good law on one point but overruled on another

## Free Citator Alternatives
| Resource | Coverage | Limitations |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| **CourtListener** | Federal and state appellate | Basic citation network, no treatment analysis |
| **Google Scholar** | Federal and state appellate | "How cited" feature, limited filtering |
| **Casetext** | Limited free access | Some citator features in paid tier |
| **Fastcase** | Free through many bar associations | Limited Authority Check feature |

## Practical Tips
- **Always Shepardize/KeyCite before citing a case**
- **Check all headnotes** - a case may be partially overruled
- **Review negative treatment** - don't just rely on the flag
- **Consider jurisdiction** - treatment in other circuits may be persuasive only
- **Update before filing** - citator status can change

## Common Pitfalls
| Pitfall | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Relying on overruled case | Always check citator |
| Missing implicit overruling | Read citing cases, not just flags |
| Ignoring jurisdictional limits | Filter by binding authority |
| Not checking all headnotes | Each point of law may have different treatment |
