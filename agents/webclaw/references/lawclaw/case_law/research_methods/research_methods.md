# Case Law Research Methods

## Starting Your Research
### 1. Identify the Legal Issue
- Frame the issue as a question
- Identify relevant jurisdiction (federal vs. state, which circuit/state)
- Determine if issue is civil or criminal

### 2. Find a Starting Point
| Method | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| **Secondary Sources** | Unfamiliar area of law |
| **Known Case** | Expanding from cited case |
| **Statute/Regulation** | Statutory interpretation questions |
| **Keyword Search** | Specific fact patterns |

### 3. Use Citators to Expand Research
| Citator | Database | Function |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **KeyCite** | Westlaw | Verify validity, find citing cases |
| **Shepard's** | LexisNexis | Verify validity, find citing cases |
| **BCite** | Bloomberg Law | Verify validity, find citing cases |
| **CourtListener Citator** | Free | Basic citation network |

## Research Strategies

### The "One Good Case" Method
1. Find one highly relevant case
2. Read the case carefully
3. Use citator to find cases citing your case
4. Review cases cited within your case
5. Identify key headnotes/topics and search those

### Keyword Search Tips
| Tip | Example |
|-----|---------|
| **Use Boolean operators** | "summary judgment" AND "employment discrimination" |
| **Use proximity operators** | "hostile work environment" /s "sexual harassment" |
| **Limit by jurisdiction** | court:"9th Circuit" |
| **Limit by date** | after:2020 |
| **Search headnotes/syllabus** | More precise than full-text |

### Topic/Headnote Searching
- **West Key Number System**: https://www.westlaw.com
- **Lexis Headnotes**: LexisNexis
- **Free alternative**: CourtListener opinion clusters

## Evaluating Authority

### Is the Case Still Good Law?
| Check | Tool |
|-------|------|
| **Has it been overruled?** | KeyCite/Shepard's red flag |
| **Has it been criticized?** | Citing cases with negative treatment |
| **Has it been superseded by statute?** | Check statutory annotations |
| **Is it from a higher court?** | Binding vs. persuasive |

### Negative Treatment Indicators
| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Red Flag** | Overruled, reversed, superseded |
| **Yellow Flag** | Criticized, limited, questioned, distinguished |
| **Blue H** | History (appeal history) |
| **Green C** | Citing references (no negative treatment) |

## Finding Cases by Citation
### Parallel Citations
A single case may have multiple citations:
- **Official reporter**: `410 U.S. 113`
- **Unofficial reporter**: `93 S. Ct. 705`
- **LEXIS/NEXIS**: `1973 U.S. LEXIS 40`

### Citation Formats
| Court | Official Cite | Unofficial Cite |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| **U.S. Supreme Court** | 410 U.S. 113 | 93 S. Ct. 705, 35 L. Ed. 2d 147 |
| **9th Circuit** | 829 F.3d 1079 | 2016 WL 3982456 |
| **California Supreme Court** | 61 Cal. 4th 339 | 189 Cal. Rptr. 3d 1 |

## Free Research Workflow
1. **Start with Google Scholar**: Broad keyword search
2. **Refine with CourtListener**: Filter by jurisdiction, date
3. **Check citing cases**: CourtListener citation network
4. **Verify on official court website**: Download official version
5. **Update with free citator**: CourtListener shows subsequent history
