# Uniform Arbitration Act (UAA) and Revised Uniform Arbitration Act (RUAA)

## Overview
The UAA (1955) and RUAA (2000) provide model state laws for arbitration. Most states have adopted some version.

## UAA vs. RUAA Comparison
| Feature | UAA (1955) | RUAA (2000) |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| **Adopted by** | 49 states | 23 states + DC |
| **Provisional remedies** | Unclear | Courts may grant |
| **Discovery** | Not addressed | Arbitrator may permit |
| **Immunity** | Not addressed | Arbitrators immune |
| **Consolidation** | Not addressed | Allowed if agreement |

## Key RUAA Provisions
| Section | Topic |
|---------|-------|
| **§ 4** | Notice requirements |
| **§ 8** | Provisional remedies (courts may grant) |
| **§ 14** | Arbitrator immunity |
| **§ 15** | Arbitration costs and fees |
| **§ 17** | Discovery procedures |
| **§ 23** | Vacating award (similar to FAA) |
| **§ 24** | Modification/correction of award |

## State Adoption Status
| State | Version | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------|
| California | California Arbitration Act | Similar to RUAA |
| New York | CPLR Article 75 | Unique provisions |
| Texas | Texas Arbitration Act | Based on UAA |
| Florida | Florida Arbitration Code | RUAA with modifications |
| Illinois | Illinois Uniform Arbitration Act | Based on UAA |

## FAA Preemption
- FAA preempts inconsistent state arbitration laws
- State laws may supplement where FAA is silent
- **Key Case**: Volt Information Sciences v. Stanford (1989) - Parties may choose state rules
