# Federal Arbitration Act (FAA) - 9 U.S.C. §§ 1-16

## Overview
The FAA establishes a federal policy favoring arbitration and provides for the enforcement of arbitration agreements.

## Key Provisions
| Section | Topic | Summary |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **§ 1** | "Maritime transactions" and "commerce" defined | Excludes "contracts of employment of seamen, railroad employees, or any other class of workers engaged in foreign or interstate commerce" |
| **§ 2** | Validity of arbitration agreements | Written arbitration agreements "valid, irrevocable, and enforceable" |
| **§ 3** | Stay of proceedings | Court shall stay trial pending arbitration |
| **§ 4** | Compelling arbitration | Court shall compel arbitration upon petition |
| **§ 5** | Appointment of arbitrators | Court may appoint if method fails |
| **§ 9** | Confirmation of award | Court must confirm unless vacated, modified, or corrected |
| **§ 10** | Grounds for vacatur | Corruption, fraud, evident partiality, misconduct, exceeding powers |
| **§ 11** | Modification of award | Evident miscalculation, imperfect form, exceeding authority |
| **§ 16** | Appeals | Interlocutory appeals allowed for certain orders |

## Key Supreme Court Cases
| Case | Citation | Holding |
|------|----------|---------|
| **AT&T Mobility v. Concepcion** | 563 U.S. 333 (2011) | FAA preempts state laws prohibiting class arbitration waivers |
| **Epic Systems v. Lewis** | 138 S. Ct. 1612 (2018) | Class action waivers in employment agreements enforceable |
| **Viking River Cruises v. Moriana** | 142 S. Ct. 1906 (2022) | FAA preempts California PAGA claim stacking |
| **Southland Corp. v. Keating** | 465 U.S. 1 (1984) | FAA applies in state courts |
| **Moses H. Cone Hospital v. Mercury Constr.** | 460 U.S. 1 (1983) | Strong federal policy favoring arbitration |

## Section 1 Exemption ("Transportation Workers")
- Applies to workers engaged in interstate commerce
- **Key Cases**: Circuit City v. Adams (2001), Southwest Airlines v. Saxon (2022)
- **Bissonnette v. LePage Bakeries** (2024): Exemption applies to any worker "actively engaged" in interstate transportation

## Preemption
- FAA preempts state laws that "stand as an obstacle" to arbitration
- **Key Case**: Kindred Nursing Centers v. Clark (2017) - Power of attorney can bind to arbitration
