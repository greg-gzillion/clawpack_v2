# Epic Systems Corp. v. Lewis, 138 S. Ct. 1612 (2018)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 138 S. Ct. 1612 (2018)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: May 21, 2018
**Vote**: 5-4 (Gorsuch writing for majority)

## Issue
Whether the National Labor Relations Act's protection of "concerted activities" overrides the FAA's mandate to enforce arbitration agreements, rendering class action waivers in employment agreements unenforceable.

## Facts
- Employees signed arbitration agreements with class action waivers
- Employees brought FLSA wage-and-hour class actions in federal court
- Employers moved to compel individual arbitration
- Seventh and Ninth Circuits held waivers violated NLRA

## Holding
**No. The FAA requires enforcement of arbitration agreements according to their terms.** The NLRA does not override the FAA's mandate. Class action waivers in employment agreements are enforceable.

## Key Quote
> "The Arbitration Act requires courts to enforce arbitration agreements according to their terms... This Court has never read a right to class actions into the NLRA."

## Reasoning
1. **FAA requires enforcement**: Courts must enforce arbitration agreements "as written"
2. **NLRA silent on class actions**: Section 7 protects "concerted activities" but not class litigation
3. **Chevron deference rejected**: NLRB interpretation not entitled to deference
4. **Congressional intent**: NLRA (1935) predates modern class action practice (1966)

## Significance
- **Employers can require individual arbitration**: Class action waivers enforceable
- **100+ million workers affected**: Most non-union employees subject to arbitration
- **Combined with Concepcion**: Broad FAA preemption across consumer and employment contexts

## Dissent (Ginsburg)
- Class actions essential for small-value claims
- NLRA protects collective action in all forums
- Majority undermines worker rights and access to justice
