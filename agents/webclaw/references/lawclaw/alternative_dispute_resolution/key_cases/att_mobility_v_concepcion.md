# AT&T Mobility LLC v. Concepcion, 563 U.S. 333 (2011)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 563 U.S. 333 (2011)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: April 27, 2011
**Vote**: 5-4 (Scalia writing for majority)

## Issue
Whether the Federal Arbitration Act preempts a state law that conditions the enforcement of an arbitration agreement on the availability of class-wide arbitration procedures.

## Facts
- AT&T Mobility included an arbitration clause in its customer agreement that waived class arbitration
- California's Discover Bank rule held class arbitration waivers in consumer contracts unconscionable
- Concepcion sued AT&T for false advertising, seeking class action status

## Holding
**Yes. The FAA preempts the Discover Bank rule.** States cannot condition enforcement of arbitration agreements on the availability of class procedures.

## Key Quote
> "Requiring the availability of classwide arbitration interferes with fundamental attributes of arbitration and thus creates a scheme inconsistent with the FAA."

## Reasoning
1. **Class arbitration sacrifices informality**: Makes process slower and more costly
2. **Class arbitration requires procedural formality**: Rules of evidence, class certification procedures
3. **Class arbitration increases risk to defendants**: No effective review of large class awards
4. **States cannot require procedures inconsistent with arbitration's fundamental attributes**

## Significance
- **Landmark preemption decision**: FAA broadly preempts state laws hostile to arbitration
- **Class action waivers enforceable**: In consumer and employment contracts
- **15,000+ citing references**: Most cited arbitration case in decades
- **Led to Epic Systems (2018)**: Extended to employment class actions

## Dissent (Breyer)
- FAA's "saving clause" allows generally applicable contract defenses
- Discover Bank was a neutral rule applied to all contracts
- Majority "federalizes" contract law contrary to FAA text
