# Mediation Procedure - ADR

## Overview
Mediation is a facilitated negotiation where a neutral third party helps disputants reach a mutually acceptable resolution. It is non-binding unless agreement is reached.

## The Mediation Process
1. **Agreement to Mediate**: Contractual clause or voluntary submission
2. **Mediator Selection**: Parties agree on neutral
3. **Pre-Mediation Submissions**: Position statements, key documents
4. **Joint Session**: All parties together with mediator
5. **Private Caucuses**: Mediator meets separately with each side
6. **Negotiation**: Shuttle diplomacy between rooms
7. **Settlement Agreement**: Written and signed by all parties
8. **Enforcement**: Contract law, not arbitration award

## Mediation Models
| Model | Description | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Facilitative** | Mediator helps parties communicate | Relationship preservation |
| **Evaluative** | Mediator assesses case strengths | Legal disputes |
| **Transformative** | Focus on empowerment and recognition | Deep conflicts |
| **Narrative** | Reframing stories and perspectives | Identity-based disputes |

## Confidentiality
- **Federal Rule of Evidence 408**: Settlement communications inadmissible
- **Uniform Mediation Act**: Privilege for mediation communications
- **State Mediation Privileges**: Vary by state
- **Exceptions**: Threats of harm, fraud, attorney misconduct

## Mediator Ethics and Standards
- **ABA Model Standards of Conduct for Mediators** (2005)
- **AAA/ABA/ACR Model Standards**: https://www.americanbar.org/groups/dispute_resolution/resources/ModelStandards/
- Core principles: Self-determination, impartiality, confidentiality, competence

## Mediation Clauses (Sample)
Any dispute arising out of or relating to this agreement shall first be submitted to mediation. The parties agree to participate in good faith in at least one full day of mediation with a mediator mutually selected by the parties. If the dispute is not resolved within 30 days of the mediation, either party may pursue arbitration or litigation as provided herein.
