# Massachusetts Constitution of 1780

## Overview
| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Current Constitution | Constitution of Massachusetts (1780) |
| Previous Constitutions | None |
| Amendments | 120+ amendments (Articles of Amendment) |
| Length | ~42,000 words |
| Distinction | **Oldest functioning written constitution in the world** |

## Key Provisions
| Part | Subject |
|------|---------|
| Preamble | |
| Part the First | Declaration of the Rights of the Inhabitants of Massachusetts |
| Part the Second | The Frame of Government |
| Chapter I | The Legislative Power |
| Chapter II | Executive Power |
| Chapter III | Judiciary Power |
| Chapter IV | Delegates to Congress |
| Chapter V | The University at Cambridge, and Encouragement of Literature |
| Chapter VI | Oaths and Subscriptions; Incompatibility of Offices |
| Articles of Amendment | Amendments 1-120 |

## Unique Features
- **Oldest functioning written constitution (1780)**
- Influenced U.S. Constitution
- John Adams was principal author
- Strong education provisions (Chapter V)
- Right to "clean air and water" (Amend. Art. XLIX)
