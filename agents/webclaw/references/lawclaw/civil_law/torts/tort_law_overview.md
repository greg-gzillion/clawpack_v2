# Tort Law - Overview

## What is a Tort?
A tort is a civil wrong (other than breach of contract) that causes harm or loss, for which the law provides a remedy, typically monetary damages.

## Categories of Torts
| Category | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Intentional Torts** | Deliberate acts causing harm | Battery, assault, false imprisonment, trespass |
| **Negligence** | Failure to exercise reasonable care | Auto accidents, medical malpractice, slip and fall |
| **Strict Liability** | Liability without fault | Abnormally dangerous activities, products liability |

## Intentional Torts
| Tort | Elements | Defenses |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Battery** | Harmful/offensive contact; intent | Consent, self-defense, defense of others |
| **Assault** | Reasonable apprehension of imminent battery; intent | Same as battery |
| **False Imprisonment** | Intentional confinement within boundaries | Consent, privilege (shopkeeper's privilege) |
| **Trespass to Land** | Intentional entry onto land of another | Consent, necessity, privilege |
| **Trespass to Chattels** | Intentional interference with personal property | Consent, privilege |
| **Conversion** | Substantial interference with personal property | Same |
| **Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress (IIED)** | Extreme and outrageous conduct; severe emotional distress | First Amendment (limited) |

## Negligence
### Elements
| Element | Description | Key Cases |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| **Duty** | Legal obligation to conform to standard of care | *Palsgraf v. Long Island R.R.* |
| **Breach** | Failure to meet standard of care | *United States v. Carroll Towing* (Hand Formula) |
| **Causation** | Actual cause + proximate cause | *Palsgraf* (foreseeability) |
| **Damages** | Actual harm or loss | Must be proven |

### Duty of Care
| Relationship | Standard |
|--------------|----------|
| **General** | Reasonable person under circumstances |
| **Professionals** | Customary practice of profession |
| **Children** | Reasonable child of similar age/experience |
| **Landowners** | Varies by entrant status (invitee, licensee, trespasser) |
| **Common Carriers** | Highest degree of care |

### Causation
| Type | Test | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Actual Cause** | "But for" test | Would harm have occurred without breach? |
| **Proximate Cause** | Foreseeability | Was harm within scope of risk? |
| **Substantial Factor** | Multiple causes | Each sufficient to cause harm |

### Defenses to Negligence
| Defense | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Contributory Negligence** | Complete bar (minority rule) |
| **Comparative Negligence** | Damages reduced by plaintiff's fault % |
| **Assumption of Risk** | Express (contract) or implied (conduct) |
| **Statute of Limitations** | Typically 1-3 years from injury |

## Strict Liability
| Category | Standard | Key Cases |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Abnormally Dangerous Activities** | Rylands v. Fletcher rule | Blasting, keeping wild animals |
| **Products Liability** | Defective product causing injury | Restatement (Third) of Torts |
| **Ultrahazardous Activities** | Cannot be made safe even with care | Explosives, toxic chemicals |

## Products Liability Theories
| Theory | Elements | Strict Liability? |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| **Negligence** | Duty, breach, causation, damages | No |
| **Strict Liability (Restatement 402A)** | Defective product unreasonably dangerous | Yes |
| **Breach of Warranty** | UCC Article 2 warranties | Yes (no fault) |

## Damages in Tort
| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Compensatory** | Actual losses | Medical bills, lost wages, pain/suffering |
| **Punitive** | Punish egregious conduct | Intentional torts, gross negligence |
| **Nominal** | Token amount for technical violation | $1 for trespass without harm |

## Key Tort Cases
| Case | Citation | Issue |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Palsgraf v. Long Island R.R.** | 162 N.E. 99 (N.Y. 1928) | Proximate cause; foreseeability |
| **United States v. Carroll Towing** | 159 F.2d 169 (2d Cir. 1947) | Hand Formula (B < PL) |
| **MacPherson v. Buick Motor Co.** | 111 N.E. 1050 (N.Y. 1916) | Privity not required for negligence |
| **Escola v. Coca Cola Bottling Co.** | 150 P.2d 436 (Cal. 1944) | Strict products liability (Traynor concurrence) |
| **Tarasoff v. Regents of Univ. of Cal.** | 551 P.2d 334 (Cal. 1976) | Duty to warn third parties |
