# Civil Law - Overview

## What is Civil Law?
Civil law governs disputes between private parties (individuals, businesses, organizations) involving non-criminal matters. It encompasses contracts, torts, property, family law, and remedies.

## Civil Law vs. Criminal Law
| Feature | Civil Law | Criminal Law |
|---------|-----------|--------------|
| **Purpose** | Compensate victim; resolve disputes | Punish offender; protect society |
| **Parties** | Plaintiff vs. Defendant | State vs. Defendant |
| **Burden of Proof** | Preponderance of evidence | Beyond reasonable doubt |
| **Remedy** | Damages, injunction, specific performance | Fine, imprisonment, probation |
| **Jury Verdict** | Not always unanimous (varies by state) | Unanimous (federal) |
| **Right to Counsel** | No constitutional right (must hire) | Constitutional right (appointed if indigent) |

## Major Areas of Civil Law
| Area | Description | Key Sources |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Contracts** | Agreements between parties | Common law, UCC |
| **Torts** | Civil wrongs causing harm | Common law, Restatements |
| **Property** | Rights in real and personal property | Common law, statutes |
| **Remedies** | Relief awarded to prevailing party | Common law, equity |
| **Civil Procedure** | Rules governing civil litigation | FRCP, state rules |

## Sources of Civil Law
| Source | Description | Authority |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Common Law** | Judge-made law through precedent | Binding within jurisdiction |
| **Restatements** | Scholarly summaries of common law | Persuasive authority |
| **Statutes** | Legislative enactments | Binding law |
| **Uniform Laws** | Model statutes (UCC, etc.) | Adopted by states |
| **Constitution** | Federal and state constitutions | Supreme law |

## Civil Court System
| Court Level | Jurisdiction | Appeal To |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Small Claims** | Limited amount (varies by state: $2,500-$25,000) | Limited appeal |
| **State Trial Courts** | General civil jurisdiction | State appellate courts |
| **Federal District Courts** | Diversity ($75,000+) or federal question | Circuit Courts |
| **State Appellate Courts** | Review trial court decisions | State Supreme Court |
| **U.S. Courts of Appeals** | Review district court decisions | U.S. Supreme Court |

## Key Civil Law Doctrines
| Doctrine | Area | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| **Stare Decisis** | All | Courts follow precedent |
| **Res Judicata** | Procedure | Claim preclusion - cannot relitigate same claim |
| **Collateral Estoppel** | Procedure | Issue preclusion - cannot relitigate same issue |
| **Erie Doctrine** | Procedure | Federal courts apply state substantive law in diversity |
| **Economic Loss Rule** | Torts | Contract remedies for purely economic loss |
