# Remedies - Contempt and Enforcement

## Civil Contempt
Civil contempt is coercive and remedial, designed to compel compliance with court orders.

- **Cornell LII - Civil Contempt**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/civil_contempt
- **28 U.S.C. § 1826 (Recalcitrant Witnesses)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/1826

## Criminal Contempt
Criminal contempt is punitive, designed to vindicate the court's authority.

- **Cornell LII - Criminal Contempt**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/criminal_contempt
- **FRCP Rule 42 (Criminal Contempt)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_42

## Civil vs. Criminal Contempt
| Feature | Civil Contempt | Criminal Contempt |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Purpose | Coerce compliance | Punish disobedience |
| Sanction | Conditional (purgeable) | Unconditional (fixed) |
| Burden of Proof | Clear and convincing | Beyond reasonable doubt |
| Right to Counsel | No constitutional right | Constitutional right |

- **Bagwell v. International Union (1994)**: https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/512/821/
