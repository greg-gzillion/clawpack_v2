# Remedies - Compensatory Damages

## Expectation Damages
Expectation damages put the plaintiff in the position they would have been in had the contract been performed.

- **Cornell LII - Expectation Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/expectation_damages
- **Restatement (Second) of Contracts § 347**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/347
- **UCC § 2-713 (Buyer's Damages)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-713
- **UCC § 2-708 (Seller's Damages)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-708

## Reliance Damages
Reliance damages reimburse the plaintiff for costs incurred in reliance on the contract.

- **Cornell LII - Reliance Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/reliance_damages
- **Restatement (Second) of Contracts § 349**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/349

## Restitution Damages
Restitution prevents unjust enrichment by returning benefits conferred.

- **Cornell LII - Restitution**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/restitution
- **Restatement (Third) of Restitution**: https://www.ali.org/publications/show/restitution-and-unjust-enrichment-third/

## Consequential Damages
Consequential damages compensate for foreseeable losses beyond the contract itself.

- **Cornell LII - Consequential Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/consequential_damages
- **UCC § 2-715(2)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-715
- **Hadley v. Baxendale Rule**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/supremecourt/text/156/1

## Incidental Damages
Incidental damages cover expenses incurred as a result of the breach.

- **Cornell LII - Incidental Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/incidental_damages
- **UCC § 2-710 (Seller)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-710
- **UCC § 2-715(1) (Buyer)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-715

## Certainty Requirement
Damages must be proven with reasonable certainty.

- **Cornell LII - Certainty of Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/certainty_of_damages
- **Restatement (Second) of Contracts § 352**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/352

## Mitigation of Damages
The non-breaching party must take reasonable steps to reduce damages.

- **Cornell LII - Mitigation of Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/mitigation_of_damages
- **Restatement (Second) of Contracts § 350**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/350
