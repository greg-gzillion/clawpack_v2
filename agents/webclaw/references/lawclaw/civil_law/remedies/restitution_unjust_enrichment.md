# Remedies - Restitution and Unjust Enrichment

## Unjust Enrichment
Unjust enrichment occurs when one party benefits at another's expense under circumstances making retention unjust.

- **Cornell LII - Unjust Enrichment**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/unjust_enrichment
- **Restatement (Third) of Restitution § 1**: https://www.ali.org/publications/show/restitution-and-unjust-enrichment-third/

## Constructive Trust
A constructive trust is an equitable remedy imposed to prevent unjust enrichment.

- **Cornell LII - Constructive Trust**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/constructive_trust
- **Restatement (Third) of Restitution § 55**: https://www.ali.org/publications/show/restitution-and-unjust-enrichment-third/

## Equitable Lien
An equitable lien is a security interest imposed on property to secure payment for benefits conferred.

- **Cornell LII - Equitable Lien**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/equitable_lien

## Tracing
Tracing allows a plaintiff to follow property into its product or substitute.

- **Cornell LII - Tracing**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/tracing
- **Commingled Funds**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/commingling

## Subrogation
Subrogation allows a party who paid another's debt to step into the creditor's shoes.

- **Cornell LII - Subrogation**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/subrogation
- **Equitable Subrogation**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/equitable_subrogation
