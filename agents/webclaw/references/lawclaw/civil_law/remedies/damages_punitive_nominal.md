# Remedies - Punitive, Nominal, and Liquidated Damages

## Punitive Damages
Punitive damages punish egregious conduct and deter future wrongdoing.

- **Cornell LII - Punitive Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/punitive_damages
- **BMW v. Gore (1996)**: https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/517/559/
- **State Farm v. Campbell (2003)**: https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/538/408/
- **Due Process Limits on Punitive Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/punitive_damages#due_process

## Nominal Damages
Nominal damages are a small amount awarded when a legal wrong occurred but no actual damages are proven.

- **Cornell LII - Nominal Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/nominal_damages
- **UCC § 2-713 (Nominal Damages)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-713

## Liquidated Damages
Liquidated damages are an agreed-upon sum for breach, provided it is reasonable and not a penalty.

- **Cornell LII - Liquidated Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/liquidated_damages
- **UCC § 2-718**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-718
- **Restatement (Second) of Contracts § 356**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/356
- **Penalty Doctrine**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/penalty_doctrine

## Statutory Damages
Statutory damages are damages set by statute, often available when actual damages are difficult to prove.

- **Cornell LII - Statutory Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/statutory_damages
- **Copyright Act Statutory Damages (17 U.S.C. § 504)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/17/504

## Treble Damages
Treble damages triple the actual damages as a penalty for certain statutory violations.

- **Cornell LII - Treble Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/treble_damages
- **Clayton Act (Antitrust)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/15
- **RICO**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1964
