# Contract Interpretation - Rules and Principles

## Parol Evidence Rule
Extrinsic evidence is inadmissible to vary or contradict a fully integrated written agreement.

- **Cornell LII - Parol Evidence Rule**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/parol_evidence_rule
- **UCC § 2-202**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-202
- **Restatement (Second) § 213**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/213

## Plain Meaning Rule
Words in a contract are given their ordinary and natural meaning.

- **Cornell LII - Plain Meaning Rule**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/plain_meaning_rule
- **Restatement (Second) § 201-202**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/201

## Contra Proferentem
Ambiguous contract terms are construed against the drafter.

- **Cornell LII - Contra Proferentem**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/contra_proferentem
- **Restatement (Second) § 206**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/206

## Course of Performance
The conduct of parties under a particular contract showing their understanding of its terms.

- **Cornell LII - Course of Performance**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/course_of_performance
- **UCC § 2-208**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-208

## Course of Dealing
The conduct of parties in prior transactions showing a common basis of understanding.

- **Cornell LII - Course of Dealing**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/course_of_dealing
- **UCC § 1-303**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/1/1-303

## Usage of Trade
A practice or method of dealing having such regularity in a place, vocation, or trade as to justify an expectation.

- **Cornell LII - Usage of Trade**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/usage_of_trade
- **UCC § 1-303(c)**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/1/1-303
