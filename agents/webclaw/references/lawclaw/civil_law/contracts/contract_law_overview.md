# Contract Law - Overview

## What is a Contract?
A contract is a legally enforceable agreement between two or more parties. Contract law governs the formation, performance, and enforcement of agreements.

## Elements of a Valid Contract
| Element | Description | Key Cases |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| **Offer** | Manifestation of willingness to enter bargain | *Lefkowitz v. Great Minneapolis Surplus Store* |
| **Acceptance** | Unconditional agreement to terms of offer | *Carlill v. Carbolic Smoke Ball Co.* |
| **Consideration** | Bargained-for exchange of value | *Hamer v. Sidway* |
| **Mutual Assent** | Meeting of the minds | *Lucy v. Zehmer* |
| **Capacity** | Legal ability to contract | Minors, mental incapacity |
| **Legality** | Purpose must be lawful | Public policy limitations |

## Sources of Contract Law
| Source | Applies To | Key Provisions |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| **Common Law** | Services, real estate, employment | Restatement (Second) of Contracts |
| **UCC Article 2** | Sale of goods | Perfect tender rule, battle of forms |
| **CISG** | International sale of goods | Applies unless parties opt out |

## Contract Formation
### Offer
- Must be definite and certain
- Must show intent to be bound
- Distinguished from preliminary negotiations
- **Termination**: Revocation, rejection, counteroffer, lapse of time, death/incapacity

### Acceptance
- Must be unequivocal
- **Mailbox Rule**: Acceptance effective upon dispatch
- **Mirror Image Rule** (Common Law): Acceptance must match offer exactly
- **Battle of Forms** (UCC 2-207): Acceptance with additional terms may form contract

### Consideration
- **Bargained-for exchange**
- Must be legally sufficient (not past consideration)
- **Exceptions**: Promissory estoppel (ｧ 90), moral obligation

## Defenses to Formation
| Defense | Description | Effect |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Mistake** | Unilateral (generally no defense) or Mutual (voidable) | Voidable |
| **Misrepresentation** | Fraudulent or material | Voidable |
| **Duress** | Improper threat | Voidable |
| **Undue Influence** | Exploitation of relationship | Voidable |
| **Unconscionability** | Grossly unfair terms | Voidable/Unenforceable |
| **Statute of Frauds** | Certain contracts must be in writing | Unenforceable |

## Statute of Frauds (Writing Required)
| Type of Contract | UCC/Common Law |
|------------------|----------------|
| **Marriage** | Common Law |
| **Year** (cannot be performed within 1 year) | Common Law |
| **Land** (sale of real property) | Common Law |
| **Executor** (promise to pay estate debt) | Common Law |
| **Goods** ($500+) | UCC 2-201 |
| **Suretyship** (promise to answer for debt of another) | Common Law |

## Contract Interpretation
| Rule | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Plain Meaning Rule** | Words given ordinary meaning |
| **Parol Evidence Rule** | Extrinsic evidence inadmissible to vary integrated writing |
| **Contra Proferentem** | Ambiguities construed against drafter |
| **Course of Performance** | Parties' conduct under this contract |
| **Course of Dealing** | Parties' conduct under prior contracts |
| **Usage of Trade** | Industry custom and practice |

## Performance and Breach
| Concept | Common Law | UCC |
|---------|------------|-----|
| **Perfect Tender** | Not required | Required (2-601) |
| **Substantial Performance** | Sufficient for recovery | Not applicable |
| **Material Breach** | Discharges other party | Same |
| **Anticipatory Repudiation** | Immediate action allowed | Same |
| **Cure** | Limited | Right to cure (2-508) |

## Remedies for Breach
| Remedy | Description | Availability |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Expectation Damages** | Put plaintiff in position as if contract performed | Standard remedy |
| **Reliance Damages** | Recover costs incurred in reliance | When expectation uncertain |
| **Restitution** | Return of benefit conferred | Unjust enrichment |
| **Specific Performance** | Compel performance | Unique goods/land; inadequate legal remedy |
| **Liquidated Damages** | Agreed sum | Must be reasonable, not penalty |
