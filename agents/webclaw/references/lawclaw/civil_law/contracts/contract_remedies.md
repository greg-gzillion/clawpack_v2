# Contract Remedies - Damages and Equitable Relief

## Expectation Damages
Damages intended to put the non-breaching party in the position they would have been in had the contract been performed.

- **Cornell LII - Expectation Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/expectation_damages
- **Restatement (Second) ｧ 347**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/347
- **UCC ｧ 2-713**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-713

## Reliance Damages
Damages intended to reimburse the non-breaching party for costs incurred in reliance on the contract.

- **Cornell LII - Reliance Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/reliance_damages
- **Restatement (Second) ｧ 349**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/349

## Restitution
Return of any benefit conferred to prevent unjust enrichment.

- **Cornell LII - Restitution**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/restitution
- **Restatement (Third) of Restitution**: https://www.ali.org/publications/show/restitution-and-unjust-enrichment-third/

## Consequential Damages
Damages for losses beyond the contract itself, such as lost profits, that were foreseeable at the time of contracting.

- **Cornell LII - Consequential Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/consequential_damages
- **UCC ｧ 2-715**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-715
- **Hadley v. Baxendale Rule**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/supremecourt/text/156/1

## Incidental Damages
Damages for expenses incurred as a result of the breach, such as costs to find substitute performance.

- **Cornell LII - Incidental Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/incidental_damages
- **UCC ｧ 2-710**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-710

## Liquidated Damages
An agreed-upon sum to be paid in the event of breach, provided it is reasonable and not a penalty.

- **Cornell LII - Liquidated Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/liquidated_damages
- **UCC ｧ 2-718**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-718
- **Restatement (Second) ｧ 356**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/356

## Specific Performance
A court order compelling a party to perform their contractual obligations, available when legal remedies are inadequate.

- **Cornell LII - Specific Performance**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/specific_performance
- **UCC ｧ 2-716**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-716
- **Restatement (Second) ｧ 357**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/357

## Mitigation of Damages
The non-breaching party must take reasonable steps to reduce their damages.

- **Cornell LII - Mitigation of Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/mitigation_of_damages
- **Restatement (Second) ｧ 350**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/350

## Nominal Damages
A small amount awarded when a breach occurred but no actual damages are proven.

- **Cornell LII - Nominal Damages**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/nominal_damages
