# Contract Performance and Breach

## Performance
Performance is the fulfillment of a contractual obligation.

- **Cornell LII - Performance**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/performance
- **Restatement (Second) § 235**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/235

## Substantial Performance
Performance that is nearly complete, with only minor defects, entitling the performing party to payment less damages.

- **Cornell LII - Substantial Performance**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/substantial_performance
- **Restatement (Second) § 237**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/237

## Perfect Tender Rule (UCC)
Buyer may reject goods that fail in any respect to conform to the contract.

- **Cornell LII - Perfect Tender Rule**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/perfect_tender_rule
- **UCC § 2-601**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-601

## Material Breach
A breach that substantially impairs the value of the contract, discharging the non-breaching party.

- **Cornell LII - Material Breach**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/material_breach
- **Restatement (Second) § 241**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/241

## Anticipatory Repudiation
A clear statement or action indicating a party will not perform when performance is due.

- **Cornell LII - Anticipatory Repudiation**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/anticipatory_repudiation
- **UCC § 2-610**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-610
- **Restatement (Second) § 250**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/250

## Cure
Seller's right to correct a non-conforming tender within the time for performance.

- **Cornell LII - Cure**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/cure
- **UCC § 2-508**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-508

## Impossibility and Impracticability
Performance may be excused when it becomes impossible or impracticable due to unforeseen events.

- **Cornell LII - Impossibility**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/impossibility
- **UCC § 2-615**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2/2-615
- **Restatement (Second) § 261**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/261

## Frustration of Purpose
Performance may be excused when the principal purpose of the contract is substantially frustrated.

- **Cornell LII - Frustration of Purpose**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/frustration_of_purpose
- **Restatement (Second) § 265**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/restatement/second/contracts/265
