# Intellectual Property Law - Overview

## What is Intellectual Property Law?
Intellectual property (IP) law protects creations of the mind, including inventions, literary and artistic works, designs, symbols, names, and images used in commerce. It grants exclusive rights to creators and owners to encourage innovation and creativity.

## Comparison of IP Rights
| Type | Subject Matter | Duration | Registration | URL |
|------|----------------|----------|--------------|-----|
| Patent | Inventions (utility, design, plant) | 20 years from filing (utility) | Required | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/patent |
| Trademark | Source identifiers (words, logos, sounds) | Renewable indefinitely | Optional (but beneficial) | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/trademark |
| Copyright | Original works of authorship | Life + 70 years (generally) | Optional (but required for suit) | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/copyright |
| Trade Secret | Valuable confidential information | Indefinite (while secret) | No registration | https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/trade_secret |
