# Challenging Agency Action - Judicial Review

## Threshold Requirements
| Requirement | Standard | Key Case |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| **Standing** | Injury in fact, causation, redressability | Lujan v. Defenders of Wildlife (1992) |
| **Ripeness** | Fitness for review, hardship | Abbott Labs v. Gardner (1967) |
| **Exhaustion** | Complete administrative remedies | McKart v. US (1969) |
| **Final Agency Action** | Consummation of decision-making | Bennett v. Spear (1997) |
| **Statute of Limitations** | 6 years from injury (APA) | Corner Post v. FRB (2024) |

## Remedy Options
| Remedy | Standard | Effect |
|--------|----------|--------|
| **Vacatur** | Default remedy | Rule set aside |
| **Remand Without Vacatur** | Allied-Signal factors | Rule stays, agency corrects error |
| **Injunction** | Irreparable harm, likelihood of success | Pre-enforcement relief |
| **Declaratory Judgment** | Actual controversy | Rights declared |
| **Mandamus** | Clear duty, no discretion | Compel agency action |

## Nationwide Injunctions Debate
- **Universal Vacatur**: Default in D.C. Circuit
- **Party-Specific Relief**: Preferred by some Justices
- **Key Cases**: DHS v. Regents (2020), United States v. Texas (2023)
- **Pending SCOTUS Review**: Scope of APA vacatur

## Key Strategic Considerations
1. **Venue Choice**: D.C. Circuit vs. regional circuit
2. **Standing Development**: Identify concrete injuries
3. **Record Preservation**: Ensure administrative record complete
4. **Exhaustion**: Complete all agency appeals
5. **Timing**: Corner Post expands limitations for new plaintiffs
6. **Remedy Request**: Vacatur vs. remand without vacatur
