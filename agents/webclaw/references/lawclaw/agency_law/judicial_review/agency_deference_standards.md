# Agency Deference Standards - Judicial Review

## Overview
Judicial deference to agency interpretations has undergone radical change in 2024. Courts now exercise independent judgment on statutory questions.

## Skidmore Deference (Skidmore v. Swift & Co., 323 U.S. 134 (1944))
**Factors for Persuasive Weight:**
1. **Thoroughness** of agency's consideration
2. **Validity** of agency's reasoning
3. **Consistency** with earlier/later pronouncements
4. **Other factors** giving power to persuade

**Application**: Post-Loper Bright, Skidmore is the primary deference doctrine for agency interpretations.

## Auer/Kisor Deference (Narrowed)
- **Auer v. Robbins (1997)**: Deference to agency interpretation of own ambiguous regulations
- **Kisor v. Wilkie (2019)**: Narrowed Auer - requires genuine ambiguity after exhausting interpretive tools
- **Survives Loper Bright**: But limited to true regulatory ambiguities

## Major Questions Doctrine
**West Virginia v. EPA (2022)**: Clear congressional authorization required for:
- Actions of "vast economic and political significance"
- Agency claims of "unheralded" regulatory authority
- Transformative expansion of agency power

**Key Cases Applying MQD:**
| Case | Agency | Action Struck Down |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| West Virginia v. EPA (2022) | EPA | Clean Power Plan |
| Biden v. Nebraska (2023) | Education | Student loan forgiveness |
| NFIB v. OSHA (2022) | OSHA | Vaccine-or-test mandate |
| Alabama Ass'n of Realtors v. HHS (2021) | CDC | Eviction moratorium |

## Arbitrary and Capricious Review (State Farm)
**Motor Vehicle Mfrs. Ass'n v. State Farm (1983)**:
- Agency must provide "reasoned explanation" for policy changes
- Must consider relevant factors and obvious alternatives
- Cannot ignore factual findings underlying prior policy

**Agency Policy Change Requirements:**
1. Acknowledge policy change
2. Provide reasoned explanation
3. Consider reliance interests
4. Address factual premises of prior policy
5. Consider obvious alternatives

## Practice Implications Post-Loper Bright
- Challenge agency statutory interpretations directly
- Argue "best reading" of statute, not reasonableness
- Use Skidmore to argue agency lacks persuasive power
- Raise Major Questions Doctrine for expansive claims
- Emphasize State Farm for policy reversals
