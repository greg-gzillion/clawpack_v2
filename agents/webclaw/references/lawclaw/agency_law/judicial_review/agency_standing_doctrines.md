# Standing in Agency Law - Judicial Review

## Constitutional Standing (Lujan Test)
**Lujan v. Defenders of Wildlife (1992)**:
1. **Injury in Fact**: Concrete, particularized, actual or imminent
2. **Causation**: Fairly traceable to agency action
3. **Redressability**: Likely judicial relief will remedy injury

## Agency-Specific Standing Issues
| Issue | Standard | Key Case |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **Procedural Injury** | Lower threshold, but must show concrete interest | Lujan footnote 7 |
| **Informational Standing** | Statutory right to information | FEC v. Akins (1998) |
| **Competitor Standing** | Increased competition from agency action | Association of Data Processing v. Camp (1970) |
| **Regulated Entity Standing** | Compliance costs sufficient | Abbott Labs (1967) |
| **Beneficiary Standing** | Denial of benefits confers standing | Mathews v. Eldridge (1976) |
| **State Standing** | "Special solicitude" for quasi-sovereign interests | Massachusetts v. EPA (2007) |

## Zone of Interests Test
- **Prudential Standing**: Plaintiff's interest must be within "zone of interests" protected by statute
- **Not Constitutional**: Congress can override
- **Key Cases**: 
  - Association of Data Processing v. Camp (1970)
  - Clarke v. Securities Industry Ass'n (1987)
  - Lexmark Int'l v. Static Control (2014) - zone of interests applies to all statutory causes of action

## Associational Standing
**Hunt v. Washington Apple Advertising Comm'n (1977)**:
1. Members would have standing individually
2. Interests at stake germane to organization's purpose
3. Individual members' participation not required

## State Standing - Special Solicitude
**Massachusetts v. EPA (2007)**:
- States receive "special solicitude" in standing analysis
- Parens patriae standing for quasi-sovereign interests
- Procedural rights lower standing threshold

## Recent Standing Developments
| Case | Holding | Impact |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Spokeo v. Robins (2016)** | Statutory violation alone insufficient for concrete injury | Limits procedural standing |
| **TransUnion v. Ramirez (2021)** | Class members without concrete injury lack standing | Class action limits |
| **FDA v. Alliance for Hippocratic Medicine (2024)** | Doctor associations lacked standing to challenge mifepristone approval | Limits associational standing |
| **Murthy v. Missouri (2024)** | No standing for jawboning claims against social media | Government persuasion not coercion |

## Strategic Standing Considerations
1. **Identify Concrete Injury**: Beyond statutory violation
2. **Trace to Agency Action**: Causation chain
3. **Demonstrate Redressability**: Specific relief would help
4. **Consider Multiple Plaintiffs**: Different injuries
5. **Develop Administrative Record**: Document harm during comment period
