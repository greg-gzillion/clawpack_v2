# Administrative Procedure Act (APA) - 5 U.S.C. §§ 551-559, 701-706

## Overview
The APA is the foundational statute for federal agency law, governing rulemaking, adjudication, and judicial review of agency actions.

## Key Provisions for Agency Law
- **§ 551**: Definitions of "agency," "rule," "adjudication," "person," "party"
- **§ 552**: Public information (FOIA) - agency transparency requirements
- **§ 552a**: Privacy Act - agency handling of personal information
- **§ 553**: Rulemaking - notice-and-comment procedures
- **§ 554**: Adjudication - formal hearing requirements
- **§ 555**: Ancillary matters - right to counsel, subpoenas
- **§§ 556-557**: Formal hearings and ALJ decisions
- **§§ 701-706**: Judicial review of agency action

## Agency Creation and Authority
- **Enabling Statutes**: Each agency created by organic statute defining its mission
- **Delegation of Authority**: Congress delegates rulemaking/enforcement authority
- **Executive Orders**: Presidential directives shaping agency operations

## Types of Federal Agencies
| Type | Examples | Characteristics |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| Executive Departments | DOJ, DHS, HHS, DOL | Cabinet-level, headed by Secretary |
| Independent Agencies | SEC, FTC, FCC, NLRB | Multi-member commissions, fixed terms |
| Government Corporations | USPS, FDIC, TVA | Business-like operations |
| Regulatory Commissions | FERC, NRC, CFTC | Rate-setting, licensing |

## Agency Powers
- **Rulemaking**: Quasi-legislative power to issue binding regulations
- **Adjudication**: Quasi-judicial power to decide individual cases
- **Enforcement**: Investigate and prosecute violations
- **Licensing**: Grant, deny, revoke permits and licenses
- **Guidance**: Issue interpretive rules and policy statements
