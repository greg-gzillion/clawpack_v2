# Privacy Act of 1974 - Agency Law

## Overview
The Privacy Act (5 U.S.C. Ï 552a) regulates agency collection, maintenance, use, and dissemination of personal information about U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents.

## Agency Privacy Act Obligations
| Requirement | Description | Citation |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| **System of Records Notice (SORN)** | Publish in Federal Register | Ï 552a(e)(4) |
| **Collect Directly from Individual** | When possible | Ï 552a(e)(2) |
| **Maintain Accuracy** | Ensure records are accurate, relevant, timely, complete | Ï 552a(e)(5) |
| **Accounting of Disclosures** | Track who accessed records | Ï 552a(c) |
| **Provide Access** | Individuals can access own records | Ï 552a(d) |
| **Allow Amendment** | Individuals can request corrections | Ï 552a(d)(2) |

## Key Privacy Act Cases - Agency Context
| Case | Agency | Issue | Holding |
|------|--------|-------|---------|
| **Doe v. Chao** (2004) | DOL | Actual damages | Must prove actual damages for recovery |
| **FAA v. Cooper** (2012) | FAA | Mental/emotional damages | Not "actual damages" |
| **Mayo v. HHS** (D.C. Cir. 2020) | HHS | SORN adequacy | SORN must provide meaningful notice |
