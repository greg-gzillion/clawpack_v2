# Agency Organic Acts - Enabling Legislation

## Overview
Each federal agency is created by an organic statute that defines its mission, structure, and authority. These statutes are the foundation of agency law.

## Agency Creation Process
1. **Congress passes organic statute** defining mission, structure, and powers
2. **President signs** or Congress overrides veto
3. **Leadership appointed**: President nominates, Senate confirms
4. **Regulations promulgated**: Agency implements statutory mandate
5. **Funding appropriated**: Congress controls agency budget

## Key Organic Statute Provisions
| Provision | Purpose | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Statement of Purpose** | Agency mission | FTC: "prevent unfair methods of competition" |
| **Rulemaking Authority** | Scope of regulatory power | SEC: rules "necessary or appropriate" |
| **Enforcement Powers** | Investigation, penalties, injunctions | CFPB: CID authority, civil penalties |
| **Adjudication Procedures** | Formal/informal hearing requirements | NLRB: ULP hearings |
| **Judicial Review** | Standards and venue | SEC: D.C. Circuit or residence |
| **Removal Provisions** | Leadership tenure | FTC: "inefficiency, neglect, or malfeasance" |

## Constitutional Challenges to Agency Structure
| Case | Agency | Issue | Outcome |
|------|--------|-------|---------|
| **Humphrey's Executor** (1935) | FTC | For-cause removal | Constitutional |
| **Morrison v. Olson** (1988) | Independent Counsel | Inter-branch appointment | Constitutional |
| **Free Enterprise Fund** (2010) | PCAOB | Dual for-cause removal | Unconstitutional |
| **Seila Law** (2020) | CFPB | Single-director for-cause removal | Unconstitutional |
| **Collins v. Yellen** (2021) | FHFA | Single-director removal | Unconstitutional |
