# Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) - Agency Law

## Overview
FOIA (5 U.S.C. § 552) provides public access to federal agency records. Each agency has unique FOIA procedures and portals.

## FOIA Exemptions - Agency Application
| Exemption | Subject | Common Agency Use |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| **1** | Classified info | DOD, CIA, FBI, State, NSA |
| **2** | Internal personnel rules | All agencies |
| **3** | Exempted by other statutes | IRS (tax returns), Census (confidential data) |
| **4** | Trade secrets | SEC, FTC, EPA, FDA, USPTO |
| **5** | Privileged communications | All agencies (deliberative process, attorney-client) |
| **6** | Personal privacy | SSA, HHS, VA, OPM |
| **7** | Law enforcement | FBI, DEA, ATF, SEC Enforcement, ICE |
| **8** | Financial institution reports | FDIC, Fed, OCC, NCUA |
| **9** | Geological information | USGS, BLM |

## Key FOIA Cases - Agency Context
| Case | Agency | Issue | Holding |
|------|--------|-------|---------|
| **DOJ v. Reporters Committee** (1989) | FBI | Privacy interests | Rap sheets exempt under Exemption 7(C) |
| **Milner v. Dept of Navy** (2011) | Navy | Exemption 2 limits | Narrow construction of exemptions |
| **Food Marketing Inst. v. Argus Leader** (2019) | USDA | Confidential business info | SNAP retailer data exempt |
| **ACLU v. CIA** (D.C. Cir. 2013) | CIA | Glomar response | Can neither confirm nor deny |

| **Other** | First 2 hours free | General public |
