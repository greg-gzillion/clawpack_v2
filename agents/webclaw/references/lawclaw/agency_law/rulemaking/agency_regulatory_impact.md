# Regulatory Impact Analysis - Agency Law

## Executive Order 12866 Requirements
| Requirement | Applies To | Exempt Agencies |
|-------------|------------|-----------------|
| **Cost-Benefit Analysis** | Executive agencies | Independent agencies (SEC, FTC, FCC, NLRB, CFPB) |
| **OIRA Review** | Significant rules | Independent agencies |
| **Regulatory Agenda** | All executive agencies | Independent agencies |

## Independent Agency Exception
| Agency | EO 12866 Applicable? | Voluntary Compliance? |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **SEC** | No | Yes - conducts economic analysis |
| **FTC** | No | Yes - cost-benefit for some rules |
| **FCC** | No | Partial - public interest analysis |
| **NLRB** | No | No - no substantive rulemaking |
| **CFPB** | No | Yes - statutory cost-benefit required |

## Agency-Specific Economic Analysis Requirements
| Agency | Statutory Requirement | Citation |
|--------|----------------------|----------|
| **SEC** | Economic analysis required for rulemaking | 15 U.S.C. § 77b(b) |
| **FTC** | Magnuson-Moss rules require analysis | 15 U.S.C. § 57a(d) |
| **CFPB** | Cost-benefit analysis mandated | 12 U.S.C. § 5512(b)(2) |
| **EPA** | EO 12866 + statutory requirements | Various |

## SEC Economic Analysis (Post-Business Roundtable)
- Must consider economic consequences of rule
- Quantify costs and benefits where feasible
- Address comments on economic analysis
- **Key Case**: Business Roundtable v. SEC (D.C. Cir. 2011)
- **Guidance**: https://www.sec.gov/divisions/riskfin/rsfi-guidance

## CFPB Cost-Benefit Analysis
- Consider potential benefits and costs to consumers and covered persons
- Quantify impacts where feasible
- Consult with appropriate Federal agencies
- **Statute**: 12 U.S.C. § 5512(b)(2)

## Paperwork Reduction Act - Agency Compliance
- OMB clearance required for information collections
- 10+ respondents triggers PRA
- Control number must display on forms
- **PRA Violation**: No penalty for non-response

## Post-Loper Bright Impact on Regulatory Analysis
- Economic analysis still required by statute/EO
- Statutory interpretation of authorizing statutes reviewed de novo
- Agency cost-benefit determinations may receive Skidmore weight
- Major Questions Doctrine applies to economically significant rules
