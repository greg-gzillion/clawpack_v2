# Agency Rulemaking Process - Agency Law

## Overview
Agency rulemaking is the primary mechanism by which federal agencies create binding regulations. Each agency has unique statutory requirements layered on the APA Ï 553 framework.

| Agency | Unique Requirement | Statute/Regulation |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **SEC** | Economic analysis required | 15 U.S.C. Ï 77b(b); 17 CFR Ï 202.3 |
| **FTC** | Magnuson-Moss procedures for certain rules | 15 U.S.C. Ï 57a |
| **EPA** | Scientific Advisory Board review | 42 U.S.C. Ï 4365; 40 CFR Part 22 |
| **FCC** | Public interest findings required | 47 U.S.C. Ï 151 |

## Post-Loper Bright Rulemaking Strategy
- Agency interpretations no longer receive Chevron deference
- Courts exercise independent judgment on statutory interpretation
- Agency must show "best reading" of statute, not just "reasonable"
- Skidmore persuasiveness based on thoroughness, validity, consistency
- Major Questions Doctrine requires clear congressional authorization
