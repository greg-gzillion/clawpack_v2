# Agency Creation and Congressional Oversight - Agency Law

## Agency Creation Process
1. **Congress passes organic/enabling statute**
   - Defines agency mission and powers
   - Delegates rulemaking and enforcement authority
   - Establishes agency structure and leadership
2. **President signs into law** (or veto override)
3. **Leadership appointed**
   - Executive agencies: Secretary confirmed by Senate
   - Independent commissions: Commissioners confirmed, fixed terms
4. **Appropriations**: Congress funds agency operations
5. **Regulations promulgated**: Agency implements statutory mandate

## Congressional Oversight Mechanisms
| Mechanism | Authority | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Appropriations Hearings** | Power of the purse | Annual |
| **Oversight Hearings** | House/Senate committees | As needed |
| **Inspector General Reports** | IG Act of 1978 | Semi-annual |
| **GAO Audits** | 31 U.S.C. § 712 | Agency request or congressional mandate |
| **Congressional Review Act** | 5 U.S.C. §§ 801-808 | 60 legislative days after rule |
| **Subpoena Power** | House/Senate rules | Investigations |
| **Confirmation Process** | Advice and Consent | Vacancies |

## Inspector General System
- **IG Act of 1978**: Established independent IGs in major agencies
- **Duties**: Audit, investigate, prevent waste/fraud/abuse
- **Reports**: Semi-annual to Congress
- **Independence**: President can remove but must notify Congress
- **Key Case**: Free Enterprise Fund v. PCAOB (2010) - removal protections

## Government Accountability Office (GAO)
- **"Congressional Watchdog"**: Independent, non-partisan agency
- **Audit Authority**: Review agency programs and expenditures
- **Legal Decisions**: Bid protests, appropriations law
- **CRA Reports**: Major rule determinations under CRA
- **Website**: https://www.gao.gov

## Agency Reorganization
- **Reorganization Act**: President can submit reorganization plans
- **Congressional Disapproval**: Simple resolution within 60 days
- **Recent Examples**: DHS creation (2002), FEMA reorganization
- **Limitations**: Cannot abolish departments or create new executive departments

## Agency Termination
- **Sunset Provisions**: Statutory expiration dates
- **Funding Cutoff**: Congress defunds agency operations
- **Repeal of Organic Act**: Congress eliminates statutory authority
- **Historical Examples**: ICC (abolished 1995), OTS (abolished 2010)
