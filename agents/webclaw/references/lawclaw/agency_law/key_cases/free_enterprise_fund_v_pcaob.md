# Free Enterprise Fund v. PCAOB, 561 U.S. 477 (2010)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 561 U.S. 477 (2010)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: June 28, 2010
**Vote**: 5-4 (Roberts writing for majority)

## Issue
Whether the dual for-cause removal protections for PCAOB members violate Article II by insulating them from presidential control.

## Facts
- PCAOB created by Sarbanes-Oxley Act (2002) to oversee public company auditors
- PCAOB members appointed by SEC, removable only for cause
- SEC Commissioners removable only for cause
- President cannot remove PCAOB members directly (two layers of for-cause protection)

## Holding
**Yes. Dual for-cause removal protections unconstitutionally insulate PCAOB members from presidential control. The removal provision is severed; PCAOB continues operating.**

## Key Quote
> "The President cannot 'take Care that the Laws be faithfully executed' if he cannot oversee the faithfulness of the officers who execute them... The diffusion of power carries with it a diffusion of accountability."

## The Removal Chain Problem
| Level | Officer | Removal Protection |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| 1 | President | Elected, no removal |
| 2 | SEC Commissioners | For-cause only (Humphrey's Executor) |
| 3 | PCAOB Members | For-cause only (Sarbanes-Oxley) |
| **Result** | President cannot remove PCAOB members | Two layers of insulation |

## Severability Analysis
- **Removal Provision Severed**: PCAOB members now removable at will by SEC
- **PCAOB Survives**: Agency continues operating
- **SEC Oversight**: SEC retains full supervisory authority

## Distinction from Prior Cases
| Case | Layers | Result |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Humphrey's Executor** | 1 (President ? FTC) | Constitutional |
| **Morrison v. Olson** | 1 (President ? Independent Counsel) | Constitutional |
| **Free Enterprise Fund** | 2 (President ? SEC ? PCAOB) | Unconstitutional |

## Impact on Other Agencies
- **No other agency had dual for-cause protection**
- **Limited to unique PCAOB structure**
- **Multi-member commissions with for-cause protection remain constitutional**

## Subsequent Cases
| Case | Issue | Outcome |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Seila Law v. CFPB (2020)** | Single-director for-cause removal | Unconstitutional |
| **Collins v. Yellen (2021)** | FHFA single-director removal | Unconstitutional |
| **CFSA v. CFPB (2024)** | CFPB funding mechanism | Constitutional |

## Practical Implications
- PCAOB continues operating with at-will removal
- SEC retains oversight authority
- Agency accountability enhanced
- Future agency designs must consider removal chain
