# Lucia v. Securities and Exchange Commission, 138 S. Ct. 2044 (2018)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 138 S. Ct. 2044 (2018)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: June 21, 2018
**Vote**: 7-2 (Kagan writing for majority)

## Issue
Whether SEC Administrative Law Judges are "Officers of the United States" subject to the Appointments Clause, requiring appointment by the President, courts, or department heads.

## Facts
- SEC ALJ presided over enforcement action against Raymond Lucia
- ALJ imposed $300,000 fine and lifetime industry bar
- Lucia challenged, arguing ALJ not properly appointed under Appointments Clause
- SEC ALJs hired through OPM competitive process, not appointed by Commission

## Holding
**Yes. SEC ALJs are "Officers of the United States" under the Appointments Clause. They must be appointed by the President, courts, or department heads.**

## Key Quote
> "SEC ALJs hold a continuing office established by law and exercise significant discretion... They are officers of the United States and must be appointed under the Appointments Clause."

## Appointments Clause Analysis (Article II, ｧ 2, cl. 2)
| Factor | Application to SEC ALJs |
|--------|------------------------|
| **Continuing Office** | ALJs hold permanent positions |
| **Established by Law** | Created by statute |
| **Significant Authority** | Take testimony, conduct trials, rule on evidence |
| **Discretion** | Issue initial decisions with factual findings and legal conclusions |

## Remedy
- New hearing before properly appointed ALJ required
- Different ALJ must preside
- Ratification by Commission insufficient

## Impact on Other Agencies
| Agency | ALJ Status | Action Required |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **SEC** | Officers | Commission appointed ALJs |
| **FTC** | Officers | Commission appointed ALJs |
| **NLRB** | Officers | Board appointed ALJs |
| **SSA** | Officers | Commissioner appointed ALJs |
| **EPA** | Officers | Administrator appointed ALJs |
| **DOL** | Officers | Secretary appointed ALJs |

## Post-Lucia Developments
- **Executive Order 13843 (2018)**: Moved ALJs from competitive service to excepted service
- **Carr v. Saul (2021)**: Appointments Clause claims need not exhaust administrative remedies
- **SEC v. Jarkesy (2024)**: SEC fraud actions require jury trials (separate constitutional issue)

## Practical Implications
- Thousands of ALJ decisions potentially voidable
- Agencies rushed to ratify ALJ appointments
- New ALJ hiring through agency appointment, not OPM examination
- Appointments Clause challenges available even without exhaustion
