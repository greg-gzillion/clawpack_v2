# SEC v. Jarkesy, 144 S. Ct. ___ (2024)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 144 S. Ct. ___ (2024)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: June 27, 2024
**Vote**: 6-3 (Roberts writing for majority)

## Issue
Whether SEC administrative enforcement actions seeking civil penalties for securities fraud violate the Seventh Amendment right to jury trial.

## Facts
- SEC brought administrative enforcement action against George Jarkesy for securities fraud
- Sought civil penalties (disgorgement, fines)
- ALJ found fraud, imposed $300,000 penalty and industry bar
- Jarkesy challenged, arguing Seventh Amendment required jury trial in federal court

## Holding
**Yes. SEC fraud actions seeking civil penalties are "suits at common law" under the Seventh Amendment. Defendants have right to jury trial in Article III court.**

## Key Quote
> "The SEC's antifraud provisions replicate common law fraud. The remedy of civil penalties is a type of remedy at common law that could only be enforced in courts of law. The Seventh Amendment requires a jury trial."

## Seventh Amendment Analysis
| Factor | SEC Fraud Action |
|--------|------------------|
| **Nature of Claim** | Analogous to common law fraud |
| **Remedy Sought** | Civil penalties (punitive, not remedial) |
| **Historical Treatment** | Common law fraud tried to juries |
| **Public Rights Exception** | Does not apply - involves private rights |

## Public Rights Exception Limited
- **Atlas Roofing v. OSHRC (1977)**: Agency adjudication of public rights (OSHA violations) constitutional
- **Jarkesy Distinction**: SEC fraud actions involve "private rights" requiring jury trial
- **NLRB, FTC, EPA**: May be affected depending on nature of claim

## What Survives SEC ALJ Jurisdiction
- **Cease-and-desist proceedings** (forward-looking relief)
- **Industry/Officer bars** (regulatory, not punitive)
- **Censure** (reputational sanction)
- **Non-fraud enforcement** (some may still be administrative)

## Practical Implications
- SEC must bring fraud actions in federal district court
- Defendants entitled to jury trial
- SEC ALJ docket dramatically reduced
- Other agencies reviewing enforcement procedures
- Potential flood of federal court cases
