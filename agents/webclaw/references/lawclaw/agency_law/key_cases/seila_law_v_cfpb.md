# Seila Law LLC v. Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, 140 S. Ct. 2183 (2020)

## Case Summary
**Citation**: 140 S. Ct. 2183 (2020)
**Court**: Supreme Court of the United States
**Date**: June 29, 2020
**Vote**: 5-4 (Roberts writing for majority)

## Issue
Whether the CFPB's single-Director structure, removable only for "inefficiency, neglect of duty, or malfeasance," violates separation of powers.

## Facts
- CFPB created by Dodd-Frank Act (2010) with single Director
- Director serves 5-year term, removable only "for cause"
- Seila Law challenged CFPB civil investigative demand
- Argued CFPB structure unconstitutional

## Holding
**Yes. The CFPB's single-Director for-cause removal protection violates Article II. The Director is removable at will by the President. The removal provision is severable - CFPB continues operating.**

## Key Quote
> "The CFPB's single-Director structure is an innovation with no foothold in history or tradition... The Director occupies a reserved echelon in the Executive Branch, and the President must be able to supervise and remove that officer."

## Distinction from Humphrey's Executor
| Factor | Humphrey's Executor (FTC) | Seila Law (CFPB) |
|--------|--------------------------|------------------|
| Structure | Multi-member Commission | Single Director |
| History | Long tradition (1887 ICC) | "Innovation" |
| Accountability | Diffused among commissioners | Concentrated in one person |
| Removal | For-cause protection UPHELD | For-cause protection STRUCK DOWN |

## Reasoning
1. **Article II Vesting**: Executive power vested in President alone
2. **Removal as Supervision**: President must be able to supervise executive officers
3. **Humphrey's Executor Exception**: Limited to multi-member expert agencies, not single directors
4. **Historical Practice**: No tradition of single-director independent agencies
5. **Accountability**: Single director concentrates power without accountability

## Severability
- **Removal Provision Severed**: Struck from statute
- **CFPB Continues**: Agency operates with Director removable at will
- **Prior Actions Valid**: Ratification by confirmed Director cures constitutional defect

## Subsequent Cases
| Case | Holding |
|------|---------|
| **Collins v. Yellen (2021)** | FHFA single-director structure similarly unconstitutional |
| **CFSA v. CFPB (2024)** | CFPB funding mechanism (Federal Reserve) constitutional |

## Practical Implications
- CFPB Director serves at President's pleasure
- Other single-director agencies vulnerable (FHFA, OCC)
- Multi-member commissions retain removal protection (SEC, FTC, FCC, NLRB)
- Agency design matters for constitutional analysis
