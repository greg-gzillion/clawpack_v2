# Central Panel vs. Agency ALJ Systems - Agency Law

## Overview
States and federal government differ on whether ALJs work for specific agencies (agency-specific) or a centralized corps (central panel).

## Federal System: Agency-Specific ALJs
- **ALJs employed by individual agencies**
- Hired through OPM competitive examination
- **Key Case**: Lucia v. SEC (2018) - ALJs are "Officers" requiring Appointments Clause compliance
- **Removal Protection**: APA ｧ 7521 - only for "good cause"

## Central Panel Advantages
- **Independence**: No agency pressure on ALJs
- **Efficiency**: Shared resources across agencies
- **Consistency**: Uniform procedures and training
- **Specialization**: ALJs develop expertise across areas

## Agency-Specific Advantages
- **Expertise**: Deep knowledge of agency's mission
- **Integration**: Close coordination with agency staff
- **Efficiency**: Faster resolution for routine matters
- **Accountability**: Agency head sets policy

## Constitutional Issues
- **Appointments Clause**: Lucia v. SEC (2018) requires proper appointment
- **Removal Protection**: Free Enterprise Fund v. PCAOB (2010) limits multiple layers
- **Seventh Amendment**: SEC v. Jarkesy (2024) - jury trial for fraud actions
