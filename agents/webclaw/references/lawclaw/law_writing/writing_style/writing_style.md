## Common Grammar Issues
| Issue | Example | Correction |
|-------|---------|------------|
| Subject-Verb Agreement | The court have ruled | The court has ruled |
| Pronoun Agreement | Each judge signed their opinion | Each judge signed his or her opinion |
| Dangling Modifier | Walking into court, the brief was filed | Walking into court, the lawyer filed the brief |
| Parallel Structure | The lawyer drafted, reviewed, and was filing | The lawyer drafted, reviewed, and filed |
| That vs. Which | The brief which was filed (restrictive) | The brief that was filed |
| Who vs. Whom | The lawyer who/whom I hired | The lawyer whom I hired |

## Word Choice
| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|------------|
| Legalese (hereinafter, aforesaid, wherein) | Plain English |
| Nominalizations (make a determination) | Strong verbs (determine) |
| Wordy phrases (due to the fact that) | Because |
| Redundancies (null and void) | Null (or void) |
| Vague terms (reasonable time) | Specific (within 30 days) |
| Double negatives | Positive statements |
| Passive voice (the brief was filed by counsel) | Active voice (counsel filed the brief) |

## Punctuation
| Mark | Legal Usage |
|------|-------------|
| Commas | Oxford comma preferred; use with nonrestrictive clauses |
| Semicolons | Separate items in complex lists; join independent clauses |
| Colons | Introduce lists; emphasize following clause |
| Hyphens | Compound adjectives (well-reasoned opinion) |
| Em Dashes | Emphasis; parenthetical information |
| Quotation Marks | Exact quotations; signal case names in text |
| Apostrophes | Possessives; avoid for plurals of acronyms |
