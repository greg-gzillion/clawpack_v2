# Civil Procedure - Overview

## What is Civil Procedure?
Civil procedure governs the process by which civil cases are adjudicated in federal and state courts. The Federal Rules of Civil Procedure (FRCP) provide the framework for federal civil litigation.

## Stages of Civil Litigation
| Stage | Description | FRCP Rules |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Pleading | Complaint, answer, motions | 7-15 |
| Discovery | Exchange of information | 26-37 |
| Pretrial | Summary judgment, conferences | 16, 56 |
| Trial | Jury selection, evidence, verdict | 38-53 |
| Post-Trial | JMOL, new trial, relief from judgment | 50, 59-60 |
| Appeal | Appellate review | FRAP |

## The Erie Doctrine
Federal courts sitting in diversity must apply state substantive law and federal procedural law.

- **Cornell LII - Erie Doctrine**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/erie_doctrine
- **Erie Railroad v. Tompkins (1938)**: https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/304/64/
- **Guaranty Trust v. York (1945)**: https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/326/99/
- **Hanna v. Plumer (1965)**: https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/380/460/
