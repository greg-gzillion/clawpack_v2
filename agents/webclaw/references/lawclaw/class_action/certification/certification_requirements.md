## Commonality Post-Wal-Mart
- **Wal-Mart v. Dukes (2011)**: https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/564/338/
- Commonality requires class members to have suffered the "same injury"
- Claims must depend on common contention capable of class-wide resolution
- Dissimilarities within proposed class may defeat commonality

## Ascertainability
- Class must be objectively defined and ascertainable
- Implied requirement in most circuits
- **Carrera v. Bayer Corp. (3d Cir. 2013)**: Ascertainability requires administrative feasibility

## Class Certification Standards
| Circuit | Standard | Key Case |
|---------|----------|----------|
| 1st Circuit | Rigorous analysis required | In re New Motor Vehicles |
| 2nd Circuit | Preponderance of evidence | In re IPO |
| 3rd Circuit | Ascertainability required | Carrera v. Bayer |
| 9th Circuit | Merits may be considered | Dukes v. Wal-Mart |

## Notice Requirements (Rule 23(c)(2))
- **Best notice practicable under circumstances**
- Individual notice to all members identifiable through reasonable effort
- Must state: nature of action, class definition, claims/issues/defenses, right to appear, opt-out rights (for b)(3)

## Issue Classes (Rule 23(c)(4))
- Court may certify class with respect to particular issues
- **URL**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_23#c_4
