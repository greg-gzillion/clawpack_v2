# Residential Electrical Plan Standards
## How to draw residential electrical layouts per NEC

## Electrical Plan Symbols (NEC / NCS)
| Symbol | Represents | Drawing Note | Source |
|--------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Circle with two parallel lines | Duplex receptacle outlet | Height noted: "18" AFF" standard, "42" AFF" for counter | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Circle with "GFCI" | GFCI-protected receptacle | Required: bathroom, kitchen counter, garage, outdoor, crawl, basement, within 6' of sink | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Circle with "WP" | Weatherproof receptacle (outdoor) | GFCI protected, in-use cover required per NEC 406.9(B)(1) | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Circle with "240V" | 240-volt receptacle (dryer, range, EV charger) | Note circuit amperage: 30A (dryer), 40A/50A (range), 50A (EV) | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| "S" in circle | Single-pole switch | Mounted 48" to center above floor at latch side of door | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| "S3" in circle | Three-way switch (two locations) | Locate at each end of stair, each entry to room with two doors | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| "S4" in circle | Four-way switch (three or more locations) | Between two 3-way switches for long halls or large rooms | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| "D" in circle | Dimmer switch | Note load type: LED/CFL compatible, wattage rating | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Circle with cross | Recessed ceiling light | Note fixture type and trim size in legend | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Circle with "P" | Pendant light | Note mounting height and fixture in schedule | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Circle with "F" | Ceiling fan with light | Show separate switch legs for fan and light if separate control | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Rectangle with "SMK" | Smoke detector | Per IRC R314: each sleeping room, outside sleeping areas, each story. Interconnected. | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1 |
| Rectangle with "CO" | Carbon monoxide detector | Per IRC R315: outside sleeping areas on each floor with fuel-burning appliances | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1 |

## Circuit Numbering Convention
| Label | Meaning | Source |
|-------|---------|--------|
| "CKT 1" | Circuit number corresponding to panel schedule | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Arrow from switch to light | Shows which lights a switch controls ("switch leg") | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Dashed line from switch | Indicates switch leg wiring to controlled fixture | Standard drafting convention |
| Home run arrow with circuit number(s) | Shows homerun back to panel; label with panel designation and circuit numbers | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |

## Standard Outlet Locations (NEC 210.52)
| Location | Spacing Requirement | Source |
|----------|-------------------|--------|
| Any wall 2' or wider | Require outlet; no point more than 6' from outlet along wall line | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Kitchen counter (12" or wider) | Require outlet; no point more than 24" from outlet | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Island/peninsula counter | One outlet for first 9 sq ft, second for next 18 sq ft | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Bathroom | One outlet within 3' of sink basin edge | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Hallway 10' or longer | Require at least one outlet | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Laundry area | At least one outlet, dedicated 20A circuit required | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Exterior (front and back) | At least one outlet at front and back of dwelling | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Garage | At least one outlet per car bay | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
