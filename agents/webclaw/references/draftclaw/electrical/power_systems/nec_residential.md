# National Electrical Code (NEC) References - Residential
## Primary Authority: NFPA 70 - National Electrical Code 2023
## Adopted by: All 50 states (with state/local amendments)

## Kitchen Circuits (NEC Article 210)
| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Small appliance branch circuits | NEC 210.52(B)(1) | "In the kitchen, pantry, breakfast room, dining room, or similar area of a dwelling unit, the two or more 20-ampere small-appliance branch circuits required by 210.11(C)(1) shall serve all wall and floor receptacle outlets covered by 210.52(A)." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Countertop receptacle spacing | NEC 210.52(C)(1) | "Receptacle outlets shall be installed so that no point along the wall line is more than 24 inches measured horizontally from a receptacle outlet in that space." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Countertop receptacles required | NEC 210.52(C)(1) | "Wall countertop spaces 12 inches or wider shall be provided with a receptacle outlet." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| GFCI protection in kitchens | NEC 210.8(A)(6) | "All 125-volt through 250-volt receptacles installed in kitchens...shall have ground-fault circuit-interrupter protection for personnel." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Dishwasher circuit | NEC 210.8(D) | " GFCI protection shall be provided for the branch circuit supplying the dishwasher." (2023 expansion) | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |

| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Bathroom receptacle required | NEC 210.52(D) | "At least one receptacle outlet shall be installed in bathrooms...within 3 feet of the outside edge of each basin." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| GFCI protection | NEC 210.8(A)(1) | "All 125-volt through 250-volt receptacles installed in bathrooms shall have GFCI protection." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |

## General Receptacle Requirements (NEC 210.52)
| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| General receptacle spacing | NEC 210.52(A)(1) | "Receptacles shall be installed such that no point measured horizontally along the floor line of any wall space is more than 6 feet from a receptacle outlet." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Wall space definition | NEC 210.52(A)(2)(1) | "Any space 2 feet or more in width...unbroken along the floor line by doorways and similar openings, fireplaces, and fixed cabinets." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Floor receptacles | NEC 210.52(A)(3) | "Floor receptacle outlets located closer than 18 inches from the wall shall be counted as required receptacles." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Hallway receptacles | NEC 210.52(H) | "Hallways of 10 feet or more in length shall have at least one receptacle outlet." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |

## Panel and Service Requirements (NEC Article 230, 408)
| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Working space (depth) | NEC 110.26(A)(1) | "For equipment operating at 150 volts or less to ground, the minimum depth of working space...shall be 3 feet." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Working space (width) | NEC 110.26(A)(2) | "The width of the working space in front of the electrical equipment shall be the width of the equipment or 30 inches, whichever is greater." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Working space (height) | NEC 110.26(A)(3) | "The workspace shall be clear and extend from the grade, floor or platform to a height of 6-1/2 feet." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |

## Jurisdictional Notes
- **California**: Adopts NEC with California Electrical Code amendments (Title 24, Part 3) - https://www.dgs.ca.gov/BSC/Codes
- **New York City**: NYC Electrical Code with amendments - https://www.nyc.gov/site/buildings/codes/electrical-code.page
- **Chicago**: Chicago Electrical Code (not NEC-based, separate code) - https://www.chicago.gov/city/en/depts/bldgs.html
- **Texas**: Texas Electrical Safety and Licensing Act adopts NEC with state amendments
- **Most jurisdictions**: Adopt NEC directly or with minimal local amendments. Check local building department.
