# Wiring Methods and Materials - NEC Reference
## All URLs verified May 2026

## Conduit Types (NEC Chapter 3)
| Type | NEC Article | Material | Typical Application | Source |
|------|-----------|----------|-------------------|--------|
| EMT (Electrical Metallic Tubing) | NEC 358 | Thin-wall galvanized steel | Exposed indoor commercial/industrial; most common conduit type | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_conduit |
| RMC (Rigid Metal Conduit) | NEC 344 | Thick-wall galvanized steel or aluminum | Hazardous locations, service masts, where physical protection needed | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_conduit |
| IMC (Intermediate Metal Conduit) | NEC 342 | Medium-wall galvanized steel | Same as RMC but lighter; allowed by NEC for same uses as RMC | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_conduit |
| PVC (Schedule 40/80) | NEC 352 | Rigid nonmetallic PVC | Underground, encased in concrete, corrosive environments; Sch 80 where exposed to physical damage | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_conduit |
| FMC (Flexible Metal Conduit/Greenfield) | NEC 348 | Interlocking spiral-wound metal strip | Connections to motors, vibrating equipment; limited to 6' lengths unless with ground wire | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_conduit |
| LFMC (Liquidtight Flexible Metal Conduit) | NEC 350 | FMC with waterproof plastic coating | Outdoor equipment connections, wet locations; common for A/C disconnects | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_conduit |
| ENT (Electrical Nonmetallic Tubing/Smurf Tube) | NEC 362 | Corrugated plastic (blue or orange) | Concealed in walls; not for exposed use or outdoors | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_conduit |

## Cable Types (NEC Chapter 3)
| Type | NEC Article | Construction | Typical Use | Source |
|------|-----------|-------------|------------|--------|
| NM-B (Nonmetallic Sheathed/Romex) | NEC 334 | Thermoplastic jacket over 2 or 3 conductors + ground | Residential interior wiring; not for wet locations or exposed below 8' in basements | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_wiring |
| UF (Underground Feeder) | NEC 340 | Solid thermoplastic jacket, moisture-resistant | Direct burial, outdoor outlets, post lights; can be buried 24" without conduit | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_wiring |
| MC (Metal-Clad) | NEC 330 | Interlocking metal armor over THHN conductors | Commercial/industrial branch circuits; can be exposed; faster than conduit | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_wiring |
| AC (Armored Cable/BX) | NEC 320 | Metal armor with bonding strip | Older residential/commercial; limited use in new construction in favor of MC | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_wiring |
| SE/SER (Service Entrance) | NEC 338 | Reinforced jacket over multiple conductors | Service entrance cable (from meter to panel); SER for interior subpanel feeds | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_wiring |

## Box Fill Calculation (NEC 314.16)
| Component | Volume Allowance | Source |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| Each conductor (originating outside box) | 1 x conductor fill (varies by AWG: #14 = 2.00 in^3, #12 = 2.25 in^3, #10 = 2.50 in^3) | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Each conductor passing through unbroken | 1 x conductor fill | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Each conductor terminating in box | 1 x conductor fill | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| All internal cable clamps (count once) | 1 x largest conductor in box | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Each yoke or strap (device) | 2 x largest conductor connected to device | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
| Each equipment grounding conductor (all count as 1) | 1 x largest grounding conductor | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70-standard-development/70 |
