# Fire Alarm Systems - NFPA 72 Reference
## All URLs verified May 2026

## Detection Devices
| Device | NFPA 72 Section | Application | Source |
|--------|----------------|-------------|--------|
| Smoke detector (ionization) | NFPA 72 17.7 | Fast response to flaming fires with small particles; may cause false alarms from cooking | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smoke_detector |
| Smoke detector (photoelectric) | NFPA 72 17.7 | Better at detecting smoldering fires with large particles; fewer nuisance alarms; preferred for residential near kitchens | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smoke_detector |
| Heat detector (fixed temperature) | NFPA 72 17.6 | Activates at preset temperature (135F or 194F typical); used in kitchens, garages, attics where smoke detectors would false alarm | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_detector |
| Heat detector (rate-of-rise) | NFPA 72 17.6 | Activates if temperature rises >15F per minute; faster response than fixed temp alone | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_detector |
| Duct smoke detector | NFPA 72 17.7.5 | Installed in HVAC ducts >2,000 CFM; shuts down air handler on alarm to prevent smoke spread | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-72-standard-development/72 |
| Beam smoke detector | NFPA 72 17.7.3 | Projected beam across large open areas (atriums, warehouses); transmitter and receiver or reflector type | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-72-standard-development/72 |

## Notification Appliances
| Device | Standard | Source |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Horn (audible) | 15 dBA above ambient noise level; temporal pattern (T3) for evacuation | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fire_alarm_notification_appliance |
| Strobe (visual) | Per NFPA 72 Chapter 18; 15 cd minimum in sleeping areas; synchronization required in rooms with multiple strobes | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fire_alarm_notification_appliance |
| Speaker (voice evacuation) | High-rise and assembly occupancies; provides voice instructions instead of tone-only | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fire_alarm_notification_appliance |
| Bed shaker (tactile) | For hearing-impaired sleeping areas; per ADA requirements | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-72-standard-development/72 |

## Manual Pull Stations (NFPA 72 17.14)
| Requirement | Standard | Source |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| Mounting height | 42"-48" to center above floor (ADA-compatible) | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-72-standard-development/72 |
| Location | At each exit, within 5' of exit door opening; maximum 200' travel distance to nearest pull station | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-72-standard-development/72 |
| Color | Red; contrasting background | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-72-standard-development/72 |

## Fire Alarm Control Panel (FACP)
| Feature | Standard | Source |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Primary power | Dedicated circuit, mechanically protected; labeled "FIRE ALARM CIRCUIT" in red | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-72-standard-development/72 |
| Secondary power (battery) | 24 hours standby + 5 minutes alarm (voice systems: 24 hours + 15 minutes alarm at maximum connected load) | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-72-standard-development/72 |
| Location | In normally occupied area (lobby, office, security desk); not in locked rooms inaccessible to firefighters | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-72-standard-development/72 |
| Annunciator | Remote annunciator at main building entrance for fire department access | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-72-standard-development/72 |
