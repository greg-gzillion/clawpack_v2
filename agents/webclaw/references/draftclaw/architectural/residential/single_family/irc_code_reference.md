# Residential Code References - Single Family Dwellings
## Primary Authority: International Residential Code (IRC) 2021
## Adopted by: All 50 states (with state-specific amendments)

## Room Dimensions (IRC Chapter 3 - Building Planning)
| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Habitable room minimum area | IRC R304.1 | "Every dwelling unit shall have at least one habitable room that shall have not less than 120 square feet of gross floor area." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Other habitable rooms minimum | IRC R304.2 | "Other habitable rooms shall have a floor area of not less than 70 square feet." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Minimum ceiling height | IRC R305.1 | "Habitable spaces...shall have a ceiling height of not less than 7 feet." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Basement ceiling height | IRC R305.1.1 | "Portions of basements that do not contain habitable spaces...shall have a ceiling height of not less than 6 feet 8 inches." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Bathroom ceiling height exception | IRC R305.1 Exception 1 | "Beams and girders spaced not less than 4 feet on center may project not more than 6 inches below the required ceiling height." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Minimum room width | IRC R304.3 | "The minimum dimension of any habitable room shall be not less than 7 feet." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Kitchen minimum area | IRC R304.4 | "The kitchen shall have a floor area of not less than 50 square feet." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |

## Light and Ventilation (IRC R303)
| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Natural light (glazing) | IRC R303.1 | "Aggregate glazing area shall be not less than 8 percent of the floor area of the room served." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Natural ventilation | IRC R303.1 | "Natural ventilation...shall be through windows, doors, louvers or other openings...openable area shall be not less than 4 percent of the floor area." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Artificial light | IRC R303.2 | "Where natural light is not provided...artificial light shall be provided." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Bathroom ventilation | IRC R303.3 | "Where the air infiltration rate of a dwelling unit is less than 5 air changes per hour...mechanical ventilation shall be provided at 50 cfm intermittent or 20 cfm continuous." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |

## Egress Requirements (IRC R310-R311)
| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Emergency escape opening (sleeping rooms) | IRC R310.1 | "Basements, habitable attics and every sleeping room shall have not less than one operable emergency escape and rescue opening." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Egress window minimum opening | IRC R310.2.1 | "Minimum net clear opening of 5.7 square feet." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Egress window sill height | IRC R310.2.2 | "Maximum height of 44 inches above the floor." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Window well requirements | IRC R310.2.3 | "Minimum horizontal area of 9 square feet, minimum horizontal projection and width of 36 inches." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Egress door size | IRC R311.2 | "Not less than 3 feet in width and 6 feet 8 inches in height." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Stairway width | IRC R311.7.1 | "Minimum width of 36 inches above the handrail height." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Stair riser height (max) | IRC R311.7.5.1 | "Maximum riser height of 7-3/4 inches." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Stair tread depth (min) | IRC R311.7.5.2 | "Minimum tread depth of 10 inches." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Handrail height | IRC R311.7.8.1 | "Not less than 34 inches and not more than 38 inches above the nosing of the treads." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Guardrail height (residential) | IRC R312.1.1 | "Porches, balconies, ramps or raised floor surfaces located more than 30 inches above the floor or grade shall have guards not less than 36 inches in height." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |

## Garage Separation (IRC R309)
| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Garage/dwelling separation | IRC R302.6 | "The garage shall be separated from the residence and its attic area by not less than 1/2-inch gypsum board applied to the garage side." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Garage ceiling separation | IRC R302.6 | "Where there is habitable space above the garage, separation shall be not less than 5/8-inch Type X gypsum board." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Door between garage and dwelling | IRC R302.5.1 | "Solid wood door not less than 1-3/8 inches in thickness, solid or honeycomb-core steel door not less than 1-3/8 inches thick, or 20-minute fire-rated door, equipped with a self-closing device." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Garage floor slope | IRC R309.1 | "Garage floors shall be sloped to facilitate the movement of liquids to a drain or toward the vehicle entry door." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |

## Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Alarms (IRC R314-R315)
| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Smoke alarm locations | IRC R314.3 | "Smoke alarms shall be installed in each sleeping room, outside each separate sleeping area in the immediate vicinity of the bedrooms, and on each additional story of the dwelling." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| CO alarm locations | IRC R315.3 | "Carbon monoxide alarms shall be installed outside of each separate sleeping area in the immediate vicinity of the bedrooms. Where a fuel-burning appliance is located within a bedroom or its attached bathroom, a carbon monoxide alarm shall be installed within the bedroom." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |
| Interconnection required | IRC R314.4 | "Where more than one smoke alarm is required...the alarm devices shall be interconnected." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IRC2021P1/chapter-3-building-planning |

## Jurisdictional Notes
- **California**: California Residential Code (CRC) based on IRC with amendments - https://www.dgs.ca.gov/BSC/Codes
- **Florida**: Florida Residential Code based on IRC with wind/hurricane provisions - https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/FLRC2020P1
- **Texas**: IRC adopted with minimal amendments by Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation
- **Colorado**: IRC adopted at state level; local jurisdictions may amend. Check local building department.
- **New York**: IRC adopted with NY-specific amendments - https://www.dos.ny.gov/dcea/
