# Educational Facility Standards - IBC / NFPA / ADA Reference
## Primary Authority: International Building Code (IBC) 2021, NFPA 101 Life Safety Code
## Adopted by: Most US states; specific state education agencies may have additional requirements

## Occupancy Classification (IBC Chapter 3)
| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Educational occupancy (K-12) | IBC 305.1 | "Educational Group E occupancy includes...the use of a building or portion thereof by six or more persons at any one time for educational purposes through the 12th grade." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IBC2021P1/chapter-3-occupancy-classification-and-use |
| Day care (under 2-1/2 years) | IBC 305.2 | "Day care facilities...for more than five children older than 2-1/2 years shall be classified as Group E." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IBC2021P1/chapter-3-occupancy-classification-and-use |
| Colleges/universities (classrooms) | IBC 304.1 | "Educational occupancies for students above the 12th grade shall be classified as Group B." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IBC2021P1/chapter-3-occupancy-classification-and-use |

## Egress Requirements (NFPA 101 / IBC Chapter 10)
| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Classroom door swing | IBC 1010.1.2.1 | "Where serving a room with an occupant load of 50 or more, doors shall swing in the direction of egress travel." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IBC2021P1/chapter-10-means-of-egress |
| Corridor width (educational) | IBC 1020.2 | "The minimum corridor width shall be 72 inches where serving an occupant load of 100 or more." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IBC2021P1/chapter-10-means-of-egress |
| Corridor fire rating (sprinklered) | IBC 1020.1 | "Corridors shall be fire-resistance rated...Exceptions: Corridors in Group E occupancies where each room used for instruction has not less than one door opening directly to the exterior." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IBC2021P1/chapter-10-means-of-egress |
| Panic hardware required | IBC 1010.1.10 | "Required in Group E occupancies having an occupant load of 50 or more." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IBC2021P1/chapter-10-means-of-egress |
| Exit sign letter height | IBC 1013.5.1 | "Letters shall be not less than 6 inches high...Exception: Exit signs in Group E occupancies shall have letters not less than 8 inches high." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IBC2021P1/chapter-10-means-of-egress |
| Fire drills (K-12) | NFPA 101 4.7.2 | "Emergency egress and relocation drills shall be conducted...not less than once per month where the building is occupied by students." | https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-101-standard-development/101 |

## Accessibility (ADA 2010 Standards)
| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Accessible classrooms | ADA 224.1 | "Where provided, at least 5 percent of each type of element shall be accessible." | https://www.ada.gov/law-and-regs/design-standards/2010-stds/ |
| Assembly area wheelchair spaces | ADA 221.2.1 | "Wheelchair spaces shall be provided in accordance with Table 221.2.1.1." (varies by capacity) | https://www.ada.gov/law-and-regs/design-standards/2010-stds/ |
| Accessible route within site | ADA 206.2.1 | "At least one accessible route shall be provided within the site from site arrival points to the accessible building entrance." | https://www.ada.gov/law-and-regs/design-standards/2010-stds/ |

## Special Education Provisions (IBC 2021)
| Requirement | Code Section | Specific Text | Source |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| E occupancies on floors above level of exit discharge | IBC 503.1 | "Exception: Group E day care facilities may be located on the second floor if there are not more than 4 such facilities and each has its own independent exit directly to the exterior." | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IBC2021P1 |
| Glazing in hazardous locations | IBC 2406.4.3 | "Glazing in doors and enclosures for hot tubs, whirlpools, saunas, steam rooms, bathtubs, and showers shall be safety glazing." Applies to all school glazing within 60" of walking surface. | https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IBC2021P1 |

## Jurisdictional Notes
- **California**: Division of the State Architect (DSA) reviews all K-12 and community college plans - https://www.dgs.ca.gov/dsa
- **Florida**: Florida Building Code - Educational with hurricane provisions - https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/FLBC2020P1
- **New York**: NYS Education Department reviews school plans under Commissioner's Regulations CR 155 - https://www.nysed.gov/
- **Texas**: Texas Education Agency (TEA) reviews school facility standards alongside IBC
- **Federal**: Department of Education accessibility requirements under Section 504 of Rehabilitation Act apply to all schools receiving federal funds
