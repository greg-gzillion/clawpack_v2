# Site Grading and Drainage - Reference
## All URLs verified May 2026

## Grading Fundamentals
| Concept | Standard/Practice | Source |
|---------|------------------|--------|
| Finished grade | Final ground surface after all construction; slopes away from buildings at 5% minimum for 10 feet (IRC R401.3) | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grading_(engineering) |
| Rough grade | Initial earthwork to establish general contours; typically within +/- 0.5 feet of finished grade | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grading_(engineering) |
| Cut and fill balance | Earthwork calculation: cut volume should approximately equal fill volume to minimize import/export | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grading_(engineering) |

## Drainage Elements
| Element | Design Standard | Source |
|---------|----------------|--------|
| Swale | Shallow vegetated channel; 2% minimum longitudinal slope; 4:1 maximum side slopes | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swale_(landform) |
| Culvert | Pipe or box under roadway/driveway; sized for 25-year storm minimum; headwalls at inlet and outlet | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culvert |
| Retention basin | Detains stormwater; releases slowly; 1% minimum bottom slope toward outlet | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retention_basin |
| Storm drain inlet | Catch basin or area drain; maximum 400-foot spacing on continuous grade roadways | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Storm_drain |
| Curb and gutter | 6" reveal, 18" width typical; 2% cross-slope toward gutter; type per local DOT standard | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curb |

## Hardscape
| Element | Standard | Source |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Sidewalk | 4" thick concrete, 1.5% max cross-slope; 5' minimum width in commercial zones, 4' residential | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sidewalk |
| ADA curb ramp | 1:12 max running slope; 36" min clear width; detectable warning surface (truncated domes) at street edge | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sidewalk |
